name: The Interns problem
description: The problem involves scheduling three interns at a hospital, with certain constraints on their workdays. Each intern has specific days off, and there are rules about consecutive workdays and shared days off. The goal is to determine the day of the week when all three interns are on call.
variables: $interns_{i,j}$, where $i \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ and $j \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7\}$, representing whether intern $i$ is working ($1$) or not ($0$) on day $j$ of the week.
constraints: 
$\sum_{j=1}^{7} (\sum_{i=1}^{3} interns_{i,j} = 3) = 1$,
$\forall i \in \{1, 2, 3\}, \forall j \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}, \sum_{k=0}^{2} interns_{i,j+k} \leq 2$,
$\forall i_1, i_2 \in \{1, 2, 3\} \text{ where } i_1 < i_2, \sum_{j=1}^{7} (interns_{i_1,j} = 0 \wedge interns_{i_2,j} = 0) = 1$,
$interns_{1,1} = 0, interns_{1,3} = 0, interns_{1,5} = 0$,
$interns_{2,5} = 0, interns_{2,7} = 0$,
$interns_{3,1} = 0$, 
explaining what they are in English: The constraints ensure that all three interns are on call exactly once a week, no intern works three consecutive days, no two interns are off on the same day more than once a week, and each intern has their specified days off.
objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, meaning the objective is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
