name: Finite Element Problem (Temperature in a Grid)
description: The problem models the temperature distribution in a grid, where the temperature at each point is the average of the temperatures at its neighboring points. The grid has fixed temperatures at its boundaries, and the goal is to find the temperature at each point in the grid.
variables: $grid_{i,j}$, where $i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}$ and $j \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}$. These variables represent the temperature at each point $(i, j)$ in the grid.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{2, \ldots, 5\}, j \in \{2, \ldots, 6\}: grid_{i,j} = \frac{grid_{i,j-1} + grid_{i-1,j} + grid_{i+1,j} + grid_{i,j+1}}{4.0}$ and $grid_{i,j} \geq 0.0$. These constraints ensure that the temperature at each point is the average of its neighboring points and that the temperature is non-negative.
objective: The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, effectively finding the steady-state temperature distribution in the grid.
