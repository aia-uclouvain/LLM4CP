name: Minimum Subsequence Sum Problem
description: The Minimum Subsequence Sum Problem is a classical problem in constraint programming that involves finding a subsequence of a given sequence with a specific length, such that the sum of the elements in the subsequence is minimized. A subsequence is a sequence that can be derived from another sequence by deleting some elements without changing the order of the remaining elements.
variables: $x = (x_1, x_2, ..., x_n)$, $x_s = (x_{s_1}, x_{s_2}, ..., x_{s_m})$, $ss_sum$, where $x$ is the master sequence, $x_s$ is the subsequence, and $ss_sum$ is the sum of the elements in the subsequence. $x$ and $x_s$ are arrays of decision variables, where each element in $x$ and $x_s$ represents an integer value, and $ss_sum$ is an integer decision variable representing the sum of the subsequence.
constraints: $cp1d(x, [5,4,1,5,2,1])$, $subsequence_sum(x, x_s, ss_sum)$, where $cp1d(x, [5,4,1,5,2,1])$ ensures that the master sequence $x$ is equal to the given sequence $[5,4,1,5,2,1]$, and $subsequence_sum(x, x_s, ss_sum)$ ensures that $x_s$ is a subsequence of $x$ and $ss_sum$ is the sum of the elements in $x_s$. Additionally, there are implicit constraints that the elements of $x_s$ are in increasing order of their positions in $x$.
objective: minimize $ss_sum$
