The problem is called "Among" or "Among Low Up". 
It is about finding values for two collections of variables, x and y, such that a certain number of values from x are present in y, within a specified range.

The main decision variables are x and y, which are arrays of variables representing the two collections of values. The variables low and up represent the lower and upper bounds of the range, respectively.

The key constraints are that the values of x must be present in the given array [9,2,4,6], the values of y must be present in the given array [0,2,4,6,8], and the number of values from x that are also in y must be between low and up.

The problem's objective is to satisfy these constraints, specifically to find values for x and y such that the number of common values is within the specified range. In this case, the problem is looking for a solution where between 1 and 3 values from x are also in y. The goal is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a feasible solution that meets the given constraints.
