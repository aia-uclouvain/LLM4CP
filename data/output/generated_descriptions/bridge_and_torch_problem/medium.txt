Bridge and Torch Problem
The Bridge and Torch problem is a classic logic puzzle that involves four people trying to cross a narrow bridge at night with only one torch available. The bridge can only hold two people at a time, and each person takes a different amount of time to cross the bridge. The goal is to find a sequence of crossings that allows all four people to cross the bridge in 15 minutes or less.

- The bridge can only hold two people at a time.
- The torch must be used when crossing the bridge, and it must be carried by one of the people.
- When two people cross the bridge together, they must move at the slower person's pace.
- The total time taken for all crossings must be minimized.
- All people must end up on the other side of the bridge.
