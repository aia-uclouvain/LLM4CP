name: Rostering Problem
description: The Rostering Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves assigning workers to different tasks or shifts while satisfying certain constraints. The goal is to create a fair schedule where each worker works approximately the same number of days per week, and the total workload is distributed evenly among the workers.
variables: $on\_duty_{i,j}, num\_workdays_w$ 
These are decision variables where $on\_duty_{i,j}$ represents the worker assigned to shift $j$ on day $i$, and $num\_workdays_w$ represents the total number of days worked by worker $w$.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{mon..sun\}, \forall j, k \in \{1..num\_shifts\} \text{ where } j < k: on\_duty_{i,j} \neq on\_duty_{i,k}$, 
$\forall w \in \{1..num\_workers\}: num\_workdays_w = \sum_{j \in \{mon..sun\}, k \in \{1..num\_shifts\}} (bool2int(on\_duty_{j,k} == w))$, 
$\forall w \in \{1..num\_workers\}: num\_workdays_w \geq \frac{(num\_shifts \times 7)}{num\_workers}$, 
$\forall w \in \{1..num\_workers\}: \sum_{j \in \{mon..sun\}, k \in \{1..num\_shifts\}} (workload_k \times bool2int(on\_duty_{j,k} == w)) \leq 13$
These constraints ensure that each shift is assigned to a different worker on the same day, the total number of days worked by each worker is calculated, each worker works at least a certain number of days, and the total workload for each worker does not exceed a certain limit.
objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
