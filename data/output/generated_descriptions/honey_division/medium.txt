Honey Division Puzzle
The Honey Division Puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem that involves dividing a certain quantity of honey among multiple sons, with specific constraints on the amount each son receives and the capacity of the barrels used.

The main decision variables in this problem are `x[i,j]`, which represent the number of barrels of capacity `j` that son `i` receives, and `howfull[j]`, which represents the amount of honey that a barrel of capacity `j` can hold. 

The key constraints in this problem are: each son must receive exactly 7 barrels, each son must receive exactly 3.5 units of honey (with the amount of honey in each barrel capacity being predefined), and each barrel capacity must be used exactly 7 times. 

The objective of the Honey Division Puzzle is not to minimize or maximize a specific quantity, but rather to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints, which is achieved by using the `solve satisfy` statement in the MiniZinc model. In other words, the goal is to determine how the honey can be divided among the sons in a way that meets all the requirements.
