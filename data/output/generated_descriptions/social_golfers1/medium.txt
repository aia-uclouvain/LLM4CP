Social Golfer Problem
The Social Golfer Problem is about scheduling golf games for a group of golfers over several weeks, with the constraint that no two golfers can play in the same group more than once. 

The main decision variables in this problem are the `assign` variables, which represent the group assignment for each golfer in each week. Specifically, `assign[g,w]` represents the group number that golfer `g` is assigned to in week `w`.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- Each group must have exactly `groupSize` players, ensuring that all groups are fully populated. 
- Each pair of players can only meet at most once, meaning that no two golfers can be in the same group in more than one week. 
- Additional symmetry-breaking constraints are used to reduce the search space by fixing the group assignments for the first two weeks.

The objective of the Social Golfer Problem is to find a schedule for as many weeks as possible, given the number of groups and the group size, such that the constraints are satisfied. In other words, the goal is to maximize the number of weeks for which a valid schedule can be found.
