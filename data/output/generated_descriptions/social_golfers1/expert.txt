name: Social Golfer Problem
description: The Social Golfer Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves scheduling golfers into groups over several weeks, with the constraint that no two golfers can play in the same group more than once.
variables: $assign_{g,w}$, where $g \in \{1, \ldots, golfers\}$ and $w \in \{1, \ldots, weeks\}$. These variables represent the assignment of each golfer $g$ to a group in each week $w$.
constraints: 
$\forall gr \in Group, w \in Week: \sum_{g \in Golfer} (assign_{g,w} = gr) = groupSize$,
$\forall g1, g2 \in Golfer, w1, w2 \in Week, g1 \neq g2, w1 \neq w2: (bool2int(assign_{g1,w1} = assign_{g2,w1}) + bool2int(assign_{g1,w2} = assign_{g2,w2})) \leq 1$,
$\forall g \in Golfer: assign_{g,1} = \lfloor \frac{g-1}{groupSize} \rfloor + 1$,
$\forall g \in Golfer, g \leq groupSize: assign_{g,2} = g$. 
These constraints ensure that each group has exactly $groupSize$ players, no two golfers play in the same group more than once, and introduce symmetry-breaking constraints to reduce the search space.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a schedule for as many weeks as possible, i.e., to maximize the number of weeks $weeks$ for which a valid scheduling exists.
