Social Golfer Problem
The problem is about organizing golf games for a group of people. 
The main variables are the golfers, the groups they play in, and the weeks they play. 
In simple terms, the golfers are the people playing golf, the groups are the teams they play in, and the weeks are the time periods when they play. 
The basic restrictions are that each group must have the same number of players, and no two players can be in the same group more than once. 
The goal is to schedule the golf games for as many weeks as possible without violating these rules.
