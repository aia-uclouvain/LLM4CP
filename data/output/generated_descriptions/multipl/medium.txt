The problem is called the "Unknown Multiplication Problem". 
It is about finding the values of digits in a multiplication equation where each digit from 0 to 9 appears exactly twice, and the equation holds true. 
The main decision variables are X1 to X20, which represent the digits in the multiplication equation. 
The key constraints are that each digit appears exactly twice, the multiplication equation holds true, and the sum of the partial products equals the final product. 
The objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
