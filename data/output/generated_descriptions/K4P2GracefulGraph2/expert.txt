name: K4P2 Graceful Graph
description: The K4P2 Graceful Graph problem is a graph theory problem that involves finding a labeling of the nodes and edges of a given graph such that the labels of the edges are the absolute differences between the labels of the nodes they connect, and all labels are unique. The graph in question is a specific combination of two complete graphs K4 and P2.
variables: $nodes = (nodes_1, nodes_2, ..., nodes_8)$, $graph = (graph_{i,1}, graph_{i,2})_{i=1}^{16}$, $edges = (edges_1, edges_2, ..., edges_{16})$, where $nodes_i$ represents the label of node $i$, $graph_{i,1}$ and $graph_{i,2}$ represent the nodes connected by edge $i$, and $edges_i$ represents the label of edge $i$, which is the absolute difference between the labels of the nodes it connects.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 16\}, |nodes_{graph_{i,1}} - nodes_{graph_{i,2}}| = edges_i$, $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 16\}, edges_i \in \{1, 2, ..., 16\}$, $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., 16\}, i \neq j, edges_i \neq edges_j$, $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., 8\}, i \neq j, nodes_i \neq nodes_j$, where the first constraint ensures that the label of each edge is the absolute difference between the labels of the nodes it connects, the second constraint ensures that all edge labels are within the range $\{1, 2, ..., 16\}$, the third constraint ensures that all edge labels are unique, and the fourth constraint ensures that all node labels are unique.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
