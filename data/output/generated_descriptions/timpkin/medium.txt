The problem is called Mrs. Timpkin's Age problem. 
It is about determining the current age of Mrs. Timpkin based on the information provided about her age and her husband's age at the time of their marriage and at present. 
The main decision variables are T, representing Mr. Timpkin's current age, and W, representing Mrs. Timpkin's current age. 
The key constraints are that eighteen years ago, Mr. Timpkin was three times as old as his wife, and now he is twice as old as she. 
The problem's objective is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a solution that satisfies the given constraints, which will provide the current age of Mrs. Timpkin.
