Fixed-Charge Transportation Problem
The Fixed-Charge Transportation Problem is a classic problem in operations research and management science that involves finding the most cost-effective way to transport goods from a set of sources to a set of customers. 

The main decision variables in this problem are x[i,j], which represents the amount shipped from source i to customer j, and y[i,j], which is a binary variable that equals 1 if some amount is shipped from i to j and 0 otherwise.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- The total amount shipped from each source must equal the supply at that source.
- The total amount shipped to each customer must equal the demand at that customer.
- If y[i,j] is 0, then x[i,j] must also be 0, meaning that if no shipment is made from i to j, then the amount shipped is 0.
- The amount shipped from i to j cannot exceed the minimum of the supply at i and the demand at j if a shipment is made.

The objective of the Fixed-Charge Transportation Problem is to minimize the total transportation cost, which consists of two parts: a variable cost per unit shipped and a fixed cost for each shipment made. The variable cost is calculated as the sum of the variable cost per unit shipped from each source to each customer multiplied by the amount shipped, while the fixed cost is calculated as the sum of the fixed cost for each shipment made.
