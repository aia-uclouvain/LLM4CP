name: Crossword Puzzle Problem

description: The problem is to fill in a crossword puzzle with a given set of words. The puzzle has a grid with some numbers indicating the starting position of each word. The words must be placed horizontally or vertically in the grid, and the letters in the overlapping positions must be the same. The goal is to find a valid placement of all the words in the grid.

variables: $E_1, E_2, ..., E_8$ 
These are decision variables representing the index of the word to be placed at each starting position in the grid. Each $E_i$ is an integer between 1 and 15, corresponding to one of the 15 given words.

constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, ..., 12\}: A[E_{overlapping[i,1]}, overlapping[i,2]] = A[E_{overlapping[i,3]}, overlapping[i,4}]$ and $all\_different(E)$
These constraints ensure that the letters in the overlapping positions are the same and that each word is used at most once. The first constraint checks all overlapping positions, and the second constraint ensures that all the $E_i$ values are distinct.

objective: satisfy
The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., a valid placement of all the words in the grid. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
