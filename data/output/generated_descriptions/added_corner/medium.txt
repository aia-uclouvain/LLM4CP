The Added Corner Puzzle problem. 
This problem involves placing the numbers 1 through 8 in a specific arrangement of circles and squares such that the number in each square is equal to the sum of the numbers in the adjacent circles.

The main decision variables are A, B, C, D, E, F, G, and H, which represent the numbers to be placed in the circles and squares. Each variable is assigned a value between 1 and 8.

The key constraints are that each number from 1 to 8 is used exactly once, and the numbers in the squares are equal to the sum of the numbers in the adjacent circles. Specifically, the constraints are: the number in the top-middle square (B) is the sum of the numbers in the top-left and top-right circles (A and C), the number in the middle-left square (D) is the sum of the numbers in the top-left circle and the bottom-left circle (A and F), the number in the middle-right square (E) is the sum of the numbers in the top-right circle and the bottom-right circle (C and H), and the number in the bottom-middle square (G) is the sum of the numbers in the bottom-left and bottom-right circles (F and H).

The objective of the problem is to find an assignment of numbers to the circles and squares that satisfies all the constraints, which is a satisfaction problem rather than an optimization problem, meaning that the goal is to find a feasible solution that meets all the requirements rather than to minimize or maximize a specific objective function.
