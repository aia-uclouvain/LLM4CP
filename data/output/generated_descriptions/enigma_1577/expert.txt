name: Happy New Year
description: The problem is to find three 3-digit numbers that use nine different digits, where one of the numbers is a perfect square, another is a triangular number, and the sum of the three numbers is 2010.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_9, num1, num2, num3$ - These variables represent the digits of the three 3-digit numbers and the numbers themselves, where $x_i$ are the digits and $num1, num2, num3$ are the constructed numbers from these digits.
constraints: $x_1 \neq x_2 \neq ... \neq x_9, num1 = 100x_1 + 10x_2 + x_3, num2 = 100x_4 + 10x_5 + x_6, num3 = 100x_7 + 10x_8 + x_9, num1 \leq num2 \leq num3, num1 + num2 + num3 = 2010, num1 \in squares \lor num2 \in squares \lor num3 \in squares, num1 \in triangular \lor num2 \in triangular \lor num3 \in triangular$ - These constraints ensure that the digits are unique, the numbers are constructed correctly, they are in ascending order, their sum is 2010, and one of the numbers is a perfect square and another is a triangular number.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, which means finding the three numbers that meet all the conditions, there is no explicit value to minimize or maximize.
