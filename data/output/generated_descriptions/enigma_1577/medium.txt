The Happy New Year problem.
This problem is about finding three 3-digit numbers that use nine different digits, where one number is a perfect square, another is a triangular number, and the sum of the three numbers is 2010.
The main decision variables are `x`, an array of nine variables representing the digits of the three numbers, and `num1`, `num2`, and `num3`, which represent the three numbers themselves. The `x` array is used to ensure that each digit is used only once, while `num1`, `num2`, and `num3` are used to satisfy the conditions of the problem.
The key constraints are: each digit is used only once, the numbers are in ascending order, the sum of the three numbers is 2010, one of the numbers is a perfect square, and another is a triangular number. In plain language, this means that we need to find three numbers that meet these conditions and use nine different digits without repeating any.
The objective of this problem is to find the three numbers that satisfy all the given conditions, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. The goal is to determine the values of `num1`, `num2`, and `num3` that meet the constraints, which are then output in ascending order.
