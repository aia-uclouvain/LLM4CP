name: Strimko
description: Strimko is a puzzle game that combines the rules of Latin squares with an additional constraint. The game is played on an nxn grid, where each row and column must contain the numbers 1 to n exactly once, forming a Latin square. Additionally, the grid is divided into "streams" or connected paths, each of which must also contain the numbers 1 to n exactly once. The goal is to fill in the grid such that all rows, columns, and streams satisfy these constraints.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i \in [1,n]$ and $j \in [1,n]$. These variables represent the value of the cell at row $i$ and column $j$ in the grid, and can take on any integer value from 1 to $n$.
constraints: $\forall i \in [1,n]: \text{all\_different}([x_{i,j} | j \in [1,n]]) \land \text{all\_different}([x_{j,i} | j \in [1,n]])$, $\forall i \in [1,n]: \text{all\_different}([x_{\text{streams}_{i,2j+1}, \text{streams}_{i,2j+2}} | j \in [0,n-1]])$, $\forall i \in [1,\text{num\_placed}]: x_{\text{placed}_{i,1}, \text{placed}_{i,2}} = \text{placed}_{i,3}$. These constraints ensure that each row and column forms a Latin square, each stream contains the numbers 1 to $n$ exactly once, and any predefined values are respected.
objective: satisfy. The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
