Car Talk Odometer problem 
The problem is about a person named Tommy who notices that his car's odometer reading is the same when read forwards and backwards, which is called a palindrome, both when he starts his car and when he arrives at work after a short drive. 
The main variables are the start and end odometer readings, which are numbers between 100,000 and 999,999, and the difference between these two readings, which represents the distance driven. 
The basic restrictions are that both the start and end odometer readings must be palindromes, and the distance driven must be greater than zero. 
The goal of the problem is to find the smallest possible distance that Tommy could have driven, which is the difference between the end and start odometer readings.
