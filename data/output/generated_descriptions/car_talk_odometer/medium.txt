The Car Talk Odometer problem
The Car Talk Odometer problem is about finding the minimum distance driven by a person, given that the odometer readings at the start and end of the trip are palindromic numbers.
The main decision variables are `start` and `end`, which represent the odometer readings at the start and end of the trip, respectively. These variables are further broken down into arrays `start_a` and `end_a`, which represent the individual digits of the odometer readings.
The key constraints are that the start and end odometer readings must be palindromic numbers, meaning they read the same forwards and backwards, and that the difference between the end and start readings must be greater than 0. In plain language, this means that the odometer readings at the start and end of the trip must be the same when reversed, and the car must have actually moved.
The objective of the problem is to minimize the difference between the end and start odometer readings, which represents the distance driven. In other words, the goal is to find the smallest possible distance that could have been driven, given the palindromic odometer readings at the start and end of the trip.
