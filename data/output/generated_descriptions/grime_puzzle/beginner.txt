The problem is called "abcdef". 
It's about finding a six-digit number, with digits a, b, c, d, e, and f, that meets certain conditions. 
The main variables are the six digits (a, b, c, d, e, f) of the number and a variable y that is used to check these conditions. 
The basic restrictions are that the sum of the digits equals y, the sum of pairs of digits (where each pair is considered as a two-digit number) equals 10 times y, and the sum of the first three and last three digits (again, considered as three-digit numbers) equals 100 times y. 
The goal is to determine if such a six-digit number exists, and if so, to find it, by satisfying all the given conditions.
