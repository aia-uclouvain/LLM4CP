The problem is known as the "abcdef" problem. 
It is about finding a six-digit number, represented as abcdef, where each letter stands for a digit, such that three specific conditions are met regarding the sums and concatenated values of its digits. 
The main decision variables are a, b, c, d, e, and f, which represent the individual digits of the six-digit number, and y, which is a variable used to express the constraints. 
The key constraints are: the sum of all digits equals y, the sum of the two-digit numbers formed by consecutive pairs of digits (ab, cd, ef) equals 10y, and the sum of the three-digit numbers formed by consecutive triples of digits (abc, def) equals 100y. 
The objective of the problem is to determine if such a six-digit number exists, which is achieved by finding a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
