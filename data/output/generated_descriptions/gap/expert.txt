name: Generalized Assignment Problem (GAP)
description: The Generalized Assignment Problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves assigning a set of jobs to a set of agents, where each job must be assigned to exactly one agent, and the total resources consumed by all jobs assigned to an agent must not exceed the agent's capacity.
variables: $x_{i,j}, obj$ where $x_{i,j}$ is a binary variable that equals 1 if job $j$ is assigned to agent $i$, and $obj$ is an integer variable representing the total cost of the assignment. These variables represent the assignment of jobs to agents and the total cost of the assignment.
constraints: $\sum_{i \in I} x_{i,j} = 1 \forall j \in J$, $\sum_{j \in J} a_{i,j} \cdot x_{i,j} \leq b_i \forall i \in I$, $obj \geq 0$, $obj = \sum_{i \in I} \sum_{j \in J} c_{i,j} \cdot x_{i,j}$ where the first constraint ensures that each job is assigned to exactly one agent, the second constraint ensures that the total resources consumed by all jobs assigned to an agent do not exceed the agent's capacity, and the third and fourth constraints define the objective function.
objective: minimize $obj$ which represents the total cost of the assignment, although the problem can also be formulated as a maximization problem.
