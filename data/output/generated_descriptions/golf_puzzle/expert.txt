name: Golf Puzzle
description: The Golf Puzzle is a classic problem where a golfer wants to find the optimal lengths of two golf strokes, a drive and an approach, to achieve the lowest score on a nine-hole course with given hole lengths. The golfer can play the strokes in any order, and the ball must go the full length of each stroke, but can go beyond the hole and then play back towards it.
variables: $club1, club2, holes_{i,j}, z$ where $club1$ and $club2$ are the lengths of the two golf strokes, $holes_{i,j}$ is the number of times stroke $j$ is used on hole $i$, and $z$ is the total number of strokes used. These variables represent the decision variables in the problem, where $club1$ and $club2$ determine the lengths of the strokes, $holes_{i,j}$ determines the strategy used on each hole, and $z$ measures the total number of strokes.
constraints: $data_i = holes_{i,1} \cdot club1 + holes_{i,2} \cdot club2, z = \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^2 |holes_{i,j}|, club1 \leq club2$ where the first constraint ensures that the total length of the strokes used on each hole equals the length of the hole, the second constraint calculates the total number of strokes used, and the third constraint breaks symmetry by requiring the length of the first stroke to be less than or equal to the length of the second stroke.
objective: minimize $z$
