Golf Puzzle
The problem is about finding the best way to play golf on a 9-hole course with two different golf clubs. 
The main variables are the lengths of the two golf clubs (club1 and club2) and the number of times each club is used for each hole (holes). 
The basic rules are that each hole has a certain distance, and the golfer must use a combination of the two clubs to cover that distance, with the option to go beyond the hole and then play back towards it. 
The goal is to find the lengths of the two clubs that will result in the lowest total number of strokes (z) to complete the 9-hole course.
