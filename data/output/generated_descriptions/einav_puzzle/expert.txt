name: Sign Flip Problem
description: The problem involves flipping the signs of rows and columns in a given matrix to make all row and column sums positive or zero, while minimizing the total sum of the matrix elements.
variables: $row\_signs, col\_signs, data\_x, row\_sums, col\_sums, total\_sum$ 
These are decision variables where $row\_signs$ and $col\_signs$ represent the signs of the rows and columns, $data\_x$ is the resulting matrix after flipping signs, $row\_sums$ and $col\_sums$ are the sums of the rows and columns, and $total\_sum$ is the total sum of the matrix elements.
constraints: 
$\forall i \in 1..rows, j \in 1..cols: data\_x[i,j] = data[i,j] \* row\_signs[i] \* col\_signs[j]$
$\forall i \in 1..rows: row\_sums[i] = \sum_{j=1}^{cols} (row\_signs[i] \* col\_signs[j] \* data[i,j])$
$\forall j \in 1..cols: col\_sums[j] = \sum_{i=1}^{rows} (row\_signs[i] \* col\_signs[j] \* data[i,j])$
$total\_sum = \sum_{i=1}^{rows} \sum_{j=1}^{cols} data\_x[i,j]$
These constraints ensure that the signs of the rows and columns are applied correctly, the row and column sums are calculated correctly, and the total sum is calculated correctly.
objective: Minimize $total\_sum$
