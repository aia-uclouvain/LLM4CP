name: 30 Bottles Problem
description: The problem involves distributing a certain number of bottles, each containing a specific amount of oil, among a specified number of sons. The goal is to divide the bottles in such a way that each son receives the same number of bottles and the same amount of oil, without pouring oil from one bottle to another.
variables: $x_{s,j}$, where $x_{s,j}$ represents the number of bottles of type $j$ assigned to son $s$. These decision variables determine how the bottles are distributed among the sons.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{s,j} = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{n} b_j}{num\_sons}$, $\sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{s,j} \cdot t_j = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{n} b_j \cdot t_j}{num\_sons}$, $\sum_{s=1}^{num\_sons} x_{s,j} = b_j$, and $lex\_lesseq(x_{s,..},x_{s+1,..})$. These constraints ensure that each son receives the same number of bottles, the same amount of oil, and that the total number of bottles of each type is distributed exactly.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find all possible distributions of bottles that satisfy the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find all feasible solutions.
