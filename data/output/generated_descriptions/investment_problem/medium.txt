Investment Problem
The investment problem is a classic constraint programming problem where a portfolio manager has to allocate a fixed budget of $100 million among eight investment opportunities to maximize the total annual return.

The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `x[i]`: the amount invested in opportunity `i`, ranging from $0 to $40 million, discretized to intervals of $10 million.
- `the_returns[i]`: the return from investment opportunity `i`.
- `not_invested`: the amount of budget not invested in the eight opportunities.
- `z`: the total annual return.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The total amount invested in all opportunities plus the amount not invested should not exceed the budget.
- The return from each investment opportunity is a function of the amount invested, as given in Table 1.
- The amount invested in each opportunity and the amount not invested should be integers.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the total annual return, `z`, which is the sum of the returns from all investment opportunities plus the return from the amount not invested. The return from the amount not invested is 5% per year, or $0.5 million for each $10 million invested.
