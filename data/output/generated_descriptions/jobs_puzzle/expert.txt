name: Jobs Puzzle
description: The Jobs Puzzle is a classic problem in constraint programming where four people - Roberta, Thelma, Steve, and Pete - hold eight different jobs, with each person holding exactly two jobs. The jobs are chef, guard, nurse, clerk, police officer, teacher, actor, and boxer. The problem provides several clues about the jobs held by each person, and the goal is to determine who holds which jobs.
variables: $Roberta, Thelma, Steve, Pete, chef, guard, nurse, clerk, police\_officer, teacher, actor, boxer, Jobs$ 
These variables represent the people and the jobs, where $Roberta, Thelma, Steve, Pete$ are constants representing the people, and $chef, guard, nurse, clerk, police\_officer, teacher, actor, boxer$ are decision variables representing the jobs held by each person. $Jobs$ is an array of decision variables representing the jobs.
constraints: $\begin{aligned}
&global\_cardinality(Jobs,set2array(index\_set([2,2,2,2])), [2,2,2,2]) \\
&nurse = Steve \lor nurse = Pete \\
&clerk = Steve \lor clerk = Pete \\
&chef = Roberta \lor chef = Thelma \\
&chef \neq clerk \\
&Roberta \neq boxer \\
&Pete \neq teacher \land Pete \neq police\_officer \land Pete \neq nurse \\
&Roberta \neq chef \land chef \neq police\_officer \land Roberta \neq police\_officer \\
&actor = Steve \lor actor = Pete
\end{aligned}$ 
These constraints represent the given clues, including each person holding exactly two jobs, the nurse being a male, the husband of the chef being the clerk, Roberta not being a boxer, Pete having no education past the ninth grade, and Roberta, the chef, and the police officer going golfing together.
objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of jobs to people that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
