**Shopping Basket Optimization Problem**
The Shopping Basket Optimization Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the optimal way to purchase a set of items from multiple shops with different prices and delivery costs.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variable is `x`, an array of variables where `x[i]` represents the shop from which to buy item `i`. The value of `x[i]` is an integer between 1 and `num_shops`, where `num_shops` is the total number of shops.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each item can only be bought from one shop.
- The total cost of buying all items from a shop, plus the delivery cost if the total cost is below a certain limit, must be calculated.
- The delivery cost is only added if the total cost of buying from a shop is greater than 0 and less than the delivery cost limit.

**Objective**
The objective of this problem is to minimize the total cost of buying all items, which includes the cost of the items themselves and any applicable delivery costs. The total cost is calculated by summing the costs of buying each item from the chosen shop and adding the delivery costs for each shop.
