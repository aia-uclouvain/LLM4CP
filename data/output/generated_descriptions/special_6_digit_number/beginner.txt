The problem is called the "Special 6 digit number" problem. 
It's about finding a 6-digit number where, when you multiply it by 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6, you get a special kind of table. 
The main variables are the digits of the 6-digit number (a, b, c, d, e, f) and the numbers you get when you multiply the original number by 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6. 
The basic restrictions are that each row and each column of the table must have the digits 0-9 without repeating any digit, and each row is the original number multiplied by a different number. 
The goal is to find a 6-digit number that meets these conditions.
