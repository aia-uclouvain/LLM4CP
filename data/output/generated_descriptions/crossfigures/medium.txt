Crossfigure problem
The Crossfigure problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding a solution to a numerical crossword puzzle. The puzzle consists of a grid with some given numbers and clues that need to be filled in. The clues come in different forms, such as simple arithmetic operations or more complex relationships between numbers.

Main decision variables
The main decision variables in the Crossfigure problem are the values of the unknown numbers in the grid. These variables are typically represented as integers or strings of digits, depending on the specific formulation of the problem.

Key constraints
The key constraints in the Crossfigure problem are the relationships between the numbers in the grid, as specified by the clues. These constraints can be simple arithmetic operations, such as addition or multiplication, or more complex relationships, such as "this number is a square" or "this number is prime". The constraints also include the requirement that each number in the grid must be a valid digit string.

Objective
The objective of the Crossfigure problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., to fill in the grid with numbers that meet all the conditions specified by the clues. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize; the goal is simply to find a feasible solution.
