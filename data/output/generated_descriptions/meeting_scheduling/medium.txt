The problem is the Nurse Scheduling Problem. 
It is about creating schedules for nurses to work shifts at a hospital, ensuring that all shifts are covered and that each nurse works a reasonable number of hours. 
The main decision variables represent which nurse is assigned to which shift, and they can be thought of as a matrix where each row corresponds to a nurse and each column corresponds to a shift. 
The key constraints include ensuring that each shift is covered by at least one nurse, that no nurse works more than a certain number of hours per week, and that nurses get at least a certain amount of time off between shifts. 
The objective is typically to minimize the total number of nurses required to cover all shifts, while also ensuring that the schedule is fair and reasonable for the nurses, which can involve maximizing the satisfaction of the nurses with their assigned shifts or minimizing the variation in hours worked among nurses.
