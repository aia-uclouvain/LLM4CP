Sudoku Pi Day 2015
The problem is about filling in a special Sudoku grid with numbers so that each row, column, and block has the numbers 1-9 exactly once, and also making sure that certain areas of the grid contain the digits of the mathematical constant Pi (3.1415926) in the correct order.
The main variables are the grid cells (x), which represent the numbers to be filled in, and the sequences (seqs), which define the areas where the Pi digits should appear.
The basic restrictions are that each row, column, and block must have the numbers 1-9 exactly once, and that the areas defined by the sequences must contain the Pi digits in the correct order.
The goal of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all these restrictions, effectively filling in the entire grid with numbers while meeting the special Pi-themed conditions.
