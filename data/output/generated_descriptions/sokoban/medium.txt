Sokoban
Sokoban is a transport puzzle where a porter needs to move crates into designated target positions in a warehouse laid out on a rectangular grid, while avoiding walls and following certain movement rules.

The main decision variables are: 
- `sokPosn`: the position of the porter at each time step, 
- `move`: the direction of movement made by the porter at each time step, 
- `crateGoalPosns`: the position of each crate at each time step.

The key constraints are: 
- the porter cannot move into a wall, 
- the porter can only push one crate at a time, 
- each crate must be moved into a target position, 
- the porter and crates cannot occupy the same position at the same time, 
- the crates cannot be pulled, only pushed.

The objective of the problem is to find a sequence of moves that allows the porter to move all the crates into their target positions in a minimal number of steps, while satisfying all the constraints. The problem does not explicitly specify an objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
