name: Sokoban
description: Sokoban is a transport puzzle where a porter needs to move crates into designated target positions in a warehouse laid out on a rectangular grid. The porter can move freely in empty cells, but crates can only be pushed one at a time. The goal is to find a sequence of moves that will move all crates into their target positions.
variables: $sokPosn, move, crateGoalPosns$ - These are the decision variables, where $sokPosn$ represents the position of the porter at each time step, $move$ represents the direction of movement made by the porter at each time step, and $crateGoalPosns$ represents the position of each crate at each time step. 
constraints: $sokPosn[0] = pInit, sokPosn[i] \neq walls[wll], sokPosn[i+1] = sokPosn[i] + move[i], crateGoalPosns[0,ck] = crates[ck], alldifferent([crateGoalPosns[s,j]  | j in CRATEINDICES]), sokPosn[k+1] = crateGoalPosns[k,c]  \rightarrow crateGoalPosns[k+1,c] = crateGoalPosns[k,c] + move[k], sokPosn[i+1] = crateGoalPosns[i,c]  \vee crateGoalPosns[i+1,c] = crateGoalPosns[i,c], exists(g in GOALINDICES) (crateGoalPosns[stps-1,c] = goals[g])$ - These constraints ensure that the porter starts at the initial position, does not move into walls, moves in the specified direction, crates start at their initial positions, all crates are in different positions at each time step, crates are moved when the porter pushes them, and each crate ends up at a target position.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a sequence of moves that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., to find a feasible solution that moves all crates into their target positions.
