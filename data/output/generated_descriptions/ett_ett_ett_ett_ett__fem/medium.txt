The name of the problem is the Swedish Puzzle, also known as ETT+ETT+ETT+ETT+ETT=FEM. 
This problem is about finding a solution to an equation where the letters E, T, F, and M represent digits from 0 to 9, such that when replaced in the equation, it holds true. 
The main decision variables are E, T, F, and M, which represent the digits to be replaced in the equation ETT+ETT+ETT+ETT+ETT=FEM. 
The key constraints are that each letter must be a unique digit, the first digit of each number (E and F) must be greater than 0, and when the numbers are added together, the equation must hold true. 
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement.
