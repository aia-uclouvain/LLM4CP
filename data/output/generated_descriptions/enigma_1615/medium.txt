The Enigma problem. 
It is about finding a six-digit number, ENIGMA, where all digits are different and non-zero, such that when the first two digits are moved to the end, the resulting number, IGMAEN, is 20% larger than the original number.

The main decision variables are e, n, i, g, m, and a, which represent the individual digits of the ENIGMA number, and enigma and igmaen, which represent the complete six-digit numbers.

The key constraints are that all digits must be different, and the digits must be non-zero. Additionally, the constraint that the resulting number IGMAEN is 20% larger than ENIGMA is represented by the equation 10*igmaen = 12*enigma, which means that IGMAEN is 1.2 times ENIGMA.

The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, specifically to find the value of the ENIGMA number. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a feasible solution that meets all the given conditions.
