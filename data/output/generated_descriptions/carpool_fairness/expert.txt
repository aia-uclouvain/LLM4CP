name: Carpool Fairness Problem
description: The Carpool Fairness Problem is a discrete optimization problem where a group of people share a carpool for a certain number of days. Each person can choose whether to participate in the carpool on each day. The goal is to allocate the daily driving responsibilities fairly among the participants, based on the number of people using the carpool on each day.
variables: $d, num\_drives_p, diffs_p, z, pdiff$ where $d \in \{1, \ldots, num\_days\}, p \in \{1, \ldots, num\_people\}$, $driver_d$ is the decision variable representing the person driving on day $d$, $num\_drives_p$ is the number of days person $p$ drives, $diffs_p$ is the absolute difference between the obligation of person $p$ and the actual number of days they drive, $z$ is the total difference between the obligations and actual driving days, and $pdiff$ is the sum of the absolute differences between the differences of each pair of people.
constraints: $\forall p \in \{1, \ldots, num\_people\}: num\_drives_p = \sum_{d=1}^{num\_days} bool2int(driver_d = p) \leq obligation_p$, $\forall d \in \{1, \ldots, num\_days\}: driver_d \in \{p \mid p \in \{1, \ldots, num\_people\} \wedge participation_{p,d} = 1\}$, $z = \sum_{p=1}^{num\_people} diffs_p$, $pdiff = \sum_{p_1 < p_2} |diffs_{p_1} - diffs_{p_2}|$ where $participation_{p,d}$ is a given parameter indicating whether person $p$ participates in the carpool on day $d$, and $obligation_p$ is the calculated obligation of person $p$ based on their participation. These constraints ensure that each person drives no more than their obligation, only participating people can drive, and define the total difference and pairwise differences.
objective: minimize $pdiff$
