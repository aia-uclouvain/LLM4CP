The Monkey and the Coconuts problem. 
This problem is about a group of men who take turns dividing a pile of coconuts, with some coconuts being given to a monkey at each step. 
The main variables are the number of men (n), the number of coconuts left at each step (left), and the number of coconuts removed by each man (removed). 
The basic restrictions are that each man takes a fraction of the remaining coconuts, leaves some for the monkey, and the remaining coconuts must be divided evenly among the men at the end. 
The goal is to find the original number of coconuts, which is the value of left[0], given the number of men (n) and the fact that the same number of coconuts is left for the monkey at each step.
