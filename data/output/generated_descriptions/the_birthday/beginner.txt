The Birthday Problem. 
It's about a person named Frank who is trying to decide whether to buy flowers for his friend Kitty, thinking it might be her birthday. 
The main variables are Frank's actions: either buying flowers or doing nothing, and the outcome: whether it's Kitty's birthday or not. 
The basic rules are: if Frank buys flowers and it's not Kitty's birthday, Kitty might be suspicious, and if Frank doesn't buy flowers and it's Kitty's birthday, he'll be in trouble. 
The goal is to find the best strategy for Frank to follow, which maximizes his payoff, or the good outcome, in this situation.
