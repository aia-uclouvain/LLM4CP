Talent Scheduling Problem
The problem is about scheduling actors for scenes in a movie or play, trying to minimize the cost of idle time for the actors.
The main variables are: 
- Actors: the people performing in the scenes
- Scenes: the parts of the movie or play
- Slot: the order in which the scenes are performed
- First and last slot for each actor: the first and last scene each actor performs in
- Actor wait time: the time each actor spends waiting for their next scene
The basic restrictions are: 
- Each scene can only be performed once
- Each actor can only perform in the scenes they are assigned to
- The order of the scenes must be followed
The goal is to minimize the total cost of idle time for all actors, which is calculated by multiplying the pay of each actor by the time they spend waiting for their next scene.
