name: Kakuro puzzle
description: The Kakuro puzzle is a mathematical puzzle where the objective is to fill in white cells with digits from 1 to 9 such that the sum of the numbers in each entry matches the clue associated with it and no digit is duplicated in any entry.
variables: $X_1, X_2, ..., X_{20}$, where $X_i$ represents the digit to be filled in the $i^{th}$ white cell, and is a decision variable that can take any integer value from 1 to 9.
constraints: $word([X_1,X_2],3) \wedge word([X_3,X_4],6) \wedge word([X_5,X_6,X_7,X_8,X_9],18) \wedge word([X_{10},X_{11},X_{12}],23) \wedge word([X_{13},X_{14}],9) \wedge word([X_{15},X_{16}],6) \wedge word([X_{17},X_{18}],15) \wedge word([X_{19},X_{20}],12) \wedge word([X_1,X_5],4) \wedge word([X_{13},X_{17}],17) \wedge word([X_2,X_6,X_{10},X_{14},X_{18}],22) \wedge word([X_7,X_{11}],16) \wedge word([X_3,X_8,X_{12},X_{15},X_{19}],16) \wedge word([X_4,X_9],3) \wedge word([X_{16},X_{20}],14)$, where each $word$ constraint ensures that the sum of the numbers in the corresponding entry matches the clue associated with it and that no digit is duplicated in the entry.
objective: satisfy, which means the objective is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
