Euler problem 52
This problem is about finding the smallest positive whole number that, when multiplied by 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6, results in numbers that have the same digits, just in a different order.
The main variables are x, which represents the number we're looking for, and digits, which represents the individual digits of the numbers x, 2x, 3x, 4x, 5x, and 6x. 
The basic restrictions are that x must be a positive whole number, and when multiplied by 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6, the resulting numbers must have the same digits as x, just in a different order.
The goal of the problem is to find the smallest x that satisfies these conditions, which means we want to minimize x.
