name: Marathon Puzzle
description: The Marathon Puzzle is a constraint satisfaction problem where six runners - Dominique, Ignace, Naren, Olivier, Philippe, and Pascal - have arrived at the finish line of the Paris marathon, and we need to reconstruct their arrival order based on given clues about their relative positions.
variables: $arrive_{r,p}, place_r$ where $r \in \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}$ and $p \in \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}$. These variables represent the assignment of runners to positions, where $arrive_{r,p}$ is a binary variable indicating whether runner $r$ arrives at position $p$, and $place_r$ is an integer variable representing the position at which runner $r$ arrives.
constraints: $\forall r \in RUNNERS: place_r = \sum_{p \in POS} p \cdot arrive_{r,p}$, $\forall p \in POS: \sum_{r \in RUNNERS} arrive_{r,p} = 1$, $\forall r \in RUNNERS: \sum_{p \in POS} arrive_{r,p} = 1$, $arrive_{4,6} = 0$, $\sum_{p \in 5..6} (arrive_{1,p} + arrive_{6,p} + arrive_{2,p}) = 0$, $\sum_{p \in 1..3} (arrive_{3,p} + arrive_{4,p}) = 0$, $arrive_{1,1} + arrive_{1,2} = 1$, $\sum_{p \in 1..4} arrive_{5,p} = 1$, $arrive_{2,2} + arrive_{2,3} = 0$, $\sum_{p \in 4..6} arrive_{6,p} = 0$, $\sum_{p \in 1..3} arrive_{3,p} = 0$, $arrive_{2,4} + arrive_{1,4} = 0$. These constraints ensure that each runner is assigned to exactly one position, each position is assigned to exactly one runner, and the given clues about the relative positions of the runners are satisfied.
objective: satisfy
