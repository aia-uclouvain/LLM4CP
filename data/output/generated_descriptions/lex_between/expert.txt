name: Lexicographic Bounds
description: The problem is to demonstrate the use of the lexicographic between constraint, which ensures that a given vector is lexicographically greater than or equal to a lower bound vector and lexicographically less than or equal to an upper bound vector.
variables: $lower\_bound = (lower\_bound_1, lower\_bound_2, ..., lower\_bound_n)$, $vector = (vector_1, vector_2, ..., vector_n)$, $upper\_bound = (upper\_bound_1, upper\_bound_2, ..., upper\_bound_n)$, where $n$ is the length of the vectors and each $lower\_bound_i$, $vector_i$, $upper\_bound_i$ is a decision variable representing the $i^{th}$ element of the respective vector, with $1 \leq i \leq n$ and $1 \leq lower\_bound_i, vector_i, upper\_bound_i \leq 9$.
constraints: $cp1d(lower\_bound, [5, 2, 3, 9])$, $cp1d(vector, [5, 2, 6, 2])$, $cp1d(upper\_bound, [5, 2, 6, 3])$, $lex\_between(lower\_bound, vector, upper\_bound)$, where $cp1d$ is a constraint that ensures two vectors are equal and $lex\_between$ is the lexicographic between constraint.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, with no explicit minimization or maximization objective, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the model.
