name: Newspaper Problem
description: The problem involves four students, Algy, Bertie, Charlie, and Digby, who share a flat and read four different newspapers, the Financial Times, the Guardian, the Daily Express, and the Sun. Each student has a specific order in which they read the newspapers and spends a certain amount of time reading each one. The students get up at different times, and the goal is to find the earliest time they can all leave for college.
variables: $reading\_start_{p,m}, reading\_end_{p,m}, earliest\_end\_time$ where $p \in \{1,2,3,4\}$ represents the students and $m \in \{1,2,3,4\}$ represents the newspapers. These variables represent the start and end times of each student reading each newspaper, and the earliest end time at which all students can leave for college.
constraints: 
$\forall p \in \{1,2,3,4\}, \forall m \in \{1,2,3,4\}: reading\_start_{p,m} \geq start\_times_p$
$\forall p \in \{1,2,3,4\}, \forall m \in \{1,2,3,4\}: reading\_end_{p,m} = reading\_start_{p,m} + reading\_times_{p,m}$
$\forall m \in \{1,2,3,4\}: cumulative([reading\_start_{p,m} | p \in \{1,2,3,4\}], [reading\_times_{p,m} | p \in \{1,2,3,4\}], one, 1)$
$\forall p \in \{1,2,3,4\}, \forall m1,m2 \in \{1,2,3,4\} where m1 < m2: if reading\_order_{p,m1} < reading\_order_{p,m2} then before(reading\_end_{p,m1}, reading\_start_{p,m2}) else after(reading\_start_{p,m1}, reading\_end_{p,m2})$. 
These constraints ensure that each student starts reading a newspaper after they wake up, that the end time of reading a newspaper is the start time plus the reading time, that no two students read the same newspaper at the same time, and that each student reads the newspapers in the specified order.
objective: minimize $earliest\_end\_time$
