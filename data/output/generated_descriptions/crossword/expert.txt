name: Crossword Puzzle Problem
description: The problem is to fill in a crossword puzzle with a given set of words, where each word corresponds to a specific position in the puzzle. The words must overlap at certain positions, and the goal is to find an assignment of words to positions that satisfies all the overlap constraints.
variables: E1, E2, E3, E4, E5, E6, E7, E8, A 
These are the decision variables, where E1 to E8 represent the indices of the words to be assigned to each position in the crossword puzzle, and A is a 2D array representing the words, with each row corresponding to a word and each column corresponding to a letter in the word.
constraints: A[E1, 3] = A[E2, 1], A[E1, 5] = A[E3, 1], A[E4, 2] = A[E2, 3], A[E4, 3] = A[E5, 1], A[E4, 4] = A[E3, 3], A[E7, 1] = A[E2, 4], A[E7, 2] = A[E5, 2], A[E7, 3] = A[E3, 4], A[E8, 1] = A[E6, 2], A[E8, 3] = A[E2, 5], A[E8, 4] = A[E5, 3], A[E8, 5] = A[E3, 5] 
These constraints represent the overlap requirements between the words, ensuring that the letters at the specified positions are the same.
objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of words to positions, meaning that all the overlap constraints are met, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
