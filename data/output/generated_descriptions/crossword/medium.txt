Crossword Puzzle Problem
The Crossword Puzzle Problem is a classic constraint programming problem where the goal is to fill in a crossword puzzle grid with a set of given words.
The main decision variables in this problem are the positions of the words in the grid, represented by the variables E1 to E8, which correspond to the numbers 1 to 8 in the crossword puzzle. Each variable Ei represents the index of the word that will start at the corresponding position in the grid.
The key constraints in this problem are the overlapping positions in the crossword puzzle, where the letters must be the same. For example, the third letter of the word at position E1 must be the same as the first letter of the word at position E2. These constraints are represented by the equations A[E1, 3] = A[E2, 1], A[E1, 5] = A[E3, 1], and so on.
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of words to positions in the grid, such that all the constraints are met. In other words, the goal is to fill in the crossword puzzle grid with the given words, ensuring that the overlapping letters are the same. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
