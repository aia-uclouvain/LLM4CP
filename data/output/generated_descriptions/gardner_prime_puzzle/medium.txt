**Gardner's Prime Puzzle**
The problem is about finding two numbers, `mP` and `nP`, composed of prime digits (2, 3, 5, or 7), such that their product equals the concatenation of `mP` and `nP`.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables are:
- `mP` and `nP`: the two numbers to be found, represented as integers.
- `mP_a` and `nP_a`: arrays of prime digits that make up `mP` and `nP`, respectively.
- `mnP_a`: an array of prime digits that represents the product of `mP` and `nP`.
- `M` and `N`: the lengths of `mP` and `nP`, respectively.

**Constraints**
The key constraints are:
- `mP` and `nP` must be composed of prime digits (2, 3, 5, or 7).
- The product of `mP` and `nP` must equal the concatenation of `mP` and `nP`.
- The numbers `mP`, `nP`, and their product must be represented correctly as arrays of digits.

**Objective**
The objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. In other words, the goal is to find two numbers `mP` and `nP` that meet the conditions of the puzzle.
