The problem is called Lexicographically Strictly Greater. 
It is about comparing two vectors of integers to determine if one is lexicographically strictly greater than the other. 
The main decision variables are vector1 and vector2, which represent the two vectors being compared. 
The key constraints are that vector1 must be equal to [5,2,7,1] and vector2 must be equal to [5,2,6,2], and vector1 must be lexicographically strictly greater than vector2. 
The problem's objective is to satisfy these constraints, which means finding values for vector1 and vector2 that meet the conditions, and in this case, it is already given that vector1 = [5,2,7,1] and vector2 = [5,2,6,2], and the model will verify if vector1 is indeed lexicographically strictly greater than vector2.
