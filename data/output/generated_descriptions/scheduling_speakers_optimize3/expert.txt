name: Scheduling Speakers
description: The problem involves scheduling a set of speakers to give talks at a conference, where each speaker has a specific duration and availability. The goal is to find a schedule that minimizes the total duration of the conference.
variables: starting_slot, ending_slot, m 
These variables represent the starting and ending time slots for each speaker, and a 0-1 matrix indicating whether a speaker is giving a talk at a particular time slot.
constraints: 
∀i ∈ 1..num_speakers (ending_slot[i] = starting_slot[i] + app_durations[i] - 1) 
∧ cumulative(starting_slot, app_durations, [1 | i in 1..num_speakers], 1) 
∧ ∀s ∈ 1..num_speakers (starting_slot[s] in speaker_availability[s]) 
∧ ∀t ∈ 1..num_slots (sum([m[t,s] | s in 1..num_speakers]) ≤ 1)
These constraints ensure that the ending time slot for each speaker is calculated correctly, that the talks do not overlap, that each speaker starts at an available time slot, and that at most one speaker can give a talk at a particular time slot.
objective: minimize z = max(i in 1..num_speakers) (ending_slot[i])
The objective is to minimize the maximum ending time slot among all speakers, which represents the total duration of the conference.
