name: 1D Rubik's Cube
description: The 1D Rubik's Cube is a puzzle where a line of numbers is scrambled and the goal is to restore the original order by applying a sequence of rotations to groups of four numbers. The rotations can be applied in three different ways, and the task is to find the shortest sequence of rotations to solve the puzzle.
variables: $n, num\_moves, moves, data, steps, ops, min\_step$ 
These variables represent the following: $n$ is the length of the line of numbers, $num\_moves$ is the number of possible rotations, $moves$ is a 2D array representing the possible rotations, $data$ is the input sequence of numbers, $steps$ is a 2D array representing the sequence of rotations applied to the input sequence, $ops$ is an array representing the operations applied at each step, and $min\_step$ is the minimum number of steps required to solve the puzzle.
constraints: $\forall j \in 1..n (steps[1,j] = data[j]) \land ops[1] = 0 \land \forall i \in 2..max\_steps (alldifferent([steps[i,j] | j \in 1..n]) \land (ops[i-1] > 0 \rightarrow ops[i] \neq ops[i-1])) \land \forall i \in 2..max\_steps (\exists m \in 0..num\_moves (\forall k \in 1..n (steps[i-1,k] = moves[m, steps[i,k]]) \land ops[i] = m)) \land \forall j \in 1..n (steps[min\_step,j] = j) \land \forall k \in 2..max\_steps (k > min\_step \rightarrow (\forall j \in 1..n (steps[k,j] = j) \land ops[k] = 0)) \land \forall i \in 2..max\_steps (i < min\_step \rightarrow ops[i] > 0)$
These constraints ensure that the input sequence is initialized correctly, that each step applies a valid rotation, that the sequence of rotations is valid, that the puzzle is solved at the minimum step, and that all further steps after the minimum step are the same as the solved state.
objective: minimize $min\_step$
