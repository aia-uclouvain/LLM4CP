name: Rotating Disc Problem
description: The problem involves four concentric discs divided into eight valued zones. The goal is to rotate the discs so that each octant counts up to the same value of 12.
variables: $rot_1, rot_2, rot_3, rot_4$ - These are decision variables representing the rotation of each disc, where $rot_i$ is the number of positions the $i^{th}$ disc is rotated.
constraints: $\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, 8\}: \sum_{i=1}^{4} v_{i, 1+((rot_i+j-1) \mod 8)} = 12$, $rot_1 = 0$ - The first constraint ensures that the sum of the values in each octant is 12 after rotation, and the second constraint is a symmetry-breaking constraint that fixes the rotation of the first disc to 0.
objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a rotation of the discs that satisfies the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
