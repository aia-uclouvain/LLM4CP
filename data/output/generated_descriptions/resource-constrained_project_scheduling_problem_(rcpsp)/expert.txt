name: Resource-Constrained Project Scheduling Problem (RCPSP)
description: The Resource-Constrained Project Scheduling Problem is a classic problem in operations research and project management that involves scheduling a set of jobs or activities subject to precedence constraints and limited resource availability. The goal is to find a schedule that minimizes the makespan, which is the maximum completion time of all jobs.
variables: $s = [s_1, s_2, ..., s_n]$, where $s_i$ represents the starting time of the $i^{th}$ job, and is a decision variable that determines when each job starts.
constraints: $s_i + d_i \leq s_j$ for $i \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}$ and $j \in successors_i$, and $\sum_{i=1}^n r_{i,k} \leq C_k$ for $k \in \{1, 2, ..., m\}$, where $d_i$ is the duration of job $i$, $successors_i$ is the set of jobs that must start after job $i$, $r_{i,k}$ is the amount of resource $k$ required by job $i$, and $C_k$ is the available capacity of resource $k$. These constraints ensure that jobs are scheduled in a valid order and that resource usage does not exceed capacity.
objective: Minimize $s_n$, which represents the makespan of the schedule, i.e., the maximum completion time of all jobs.
