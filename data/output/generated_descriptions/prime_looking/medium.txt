The problem is called the Prime Looking problem. 
It is about finding numbers that are composite, meaning they have more than two distinct positive divisors, but are not divisible by 2, 3, or 5. 
The main decision variables are x, d1, and d2, where x represents a prime-looking number, and d1 and d2 are its divisors, with d1 being less than or equal to d2. 
The key constraints are that x must be the product of d1 and d2, and x must not be divisible by 2, 3, or 5. 
The problem's objective is to find a prime-looking number, and the model uses a satisfiability solver to find such a number, with the search strategy focused on finding a solution that satisfies the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
