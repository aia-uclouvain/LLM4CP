The 0/1 Knapsack Problem
The 0/1 knapsack problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves finding the optimal way to pack a set of items of different sizes and values into a knapsack of limited capacity. 

The main decision variables in this problem are the array `x`, where `x[i]` represents the quantity of item `i` to include in the knapsack. 

The key constraints in this problem are that the total size of the items included in the knapsack cannot exceed its capacity, which is represented by the constraint `sum(i in 1..n) (x[i]*units[i]) <= c`. This means that the total size of all items packed must be less than or equal to the knapsack's capacity `c`. 

The objective of the 0/1 knapsack problem is to maximize the total value of the items included in the knapsack, which is represented by the variable `TotalProfit`. This is calculated by summing the product of the quantity of each item `x[i]` and its corresponding profit `profits[i]`. The goal is to find the combination of items that results in the highest total profit while not exceeding the knapsack's capacity.
