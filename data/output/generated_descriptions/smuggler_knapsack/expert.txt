name: Smuggler's Knapsack Problem
description: The Smuggler's Knapsack Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming and operations research. It involves a smuggler who has a knapsack with a limited capacity and wants to maximize the profit by selecting the optimal items to include in the knapsack. Each item has a certain unit size and profit, and the smuggler must decide how many units of each item to include in the knapsack without exceeding its capacity.
variables: $x_1, x_2, x_3$ - These are the decision variables representing the number of units of each item (whisky, perfume, and cigarettes) to include in the knapsack. They are integer variables that can take values between 0 and the knapsack capacity.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{3} x_i \cdot units_i \leq c$ - This is the capacity constraint, which ensures that the total size of the items included in the knapsack does not exceed its capacity. The $units_i$ represents the unit size of each item, and $c$ is the knapsack capacity.
objective: Maximize $TotalProfit = \sum_{i=1}^{3} x_i \cdot profits_i$ - The objective is to maximize the total profit by selecting the optimal items to include in the knapsack. The $profits_i$ represents the profit of each item, and the goal is to find the combination of items that yields the highest total profit without exceeding the knapsack capacity.
