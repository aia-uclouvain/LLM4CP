Magic Square Water Retention Problem
The Magic Square Water Retention problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding a magic square, which is a square grid filled with distinct positive integers in the range from 1 to n^2, such that each cell contains a different integer and the sum of the integers in each row, column, and diagonal is equal. The water retention problem adds an additional layer of complexity by introducing a "water" variable for each cell, which represents the amount of water that can be retained in that cell. The goal is to maximize the total amount of water retained in the square.

Main Decision Variables
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `magic[i,j]`: the value of the cell at row `i` and column `j` in the magic square.
- `water[i,j]`: the amount of water retained in the cell at row `i` and column `j`.

Key Constraints
The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each row, column, and diagonal must sum to the same total, which is `(n*(n*n+1)) div 2`.
- Each cell must contain a distinct positive integer between 1 and `n*n`.
- The "associative value" constraint, which states that `magic[i,j] + magic[n-i+1,n-j+1] = assoc` for all `i` and `j`.
- The water retention constraints, which state that the amount of water retained in each cell is the maximum of its own value and the minimum of the water retained in its neighboring cells.

Objective
The objective of this problem is to maximize the total amount of water retained in the square, which is represented by the variable `z`. This is calculated as the sum of the water retained in each cell minus the total sum of the magic square.
