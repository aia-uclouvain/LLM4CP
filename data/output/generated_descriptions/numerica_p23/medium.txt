The problem is known as the Robot Kinematic Application. 
It is about finding the values of sine and cosine of six joints in a robot arm that satisfy a set of given constraints, which are derived from the kinematic equations of the robot. 
The main decision variables are two arrays, s and c, where s[i] and c[i] represent the sine and cosine of the angle of the i-th joint in the robot arm. 
The key constraints are: 
- each sine and cosine value must be non-negative and their squares must sum up to 1 (which is a fundamental property of sine and cosine functions), 
- and a set of linear and nonlinear equations that relate the sine and cosine values of different joints, which are derived from the kinematic equations of the robot. 
These constraints can be thought of as restrictions on the possible movements of the robot arm. 
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding a set of sine and cosine values for the joints that make the robot arm reach a specific position or satisfy certain conditions. 
In other words, the goal is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a feasible solution that meets all the requirements.
