name: Ice Cream Production Problem
description: The Ice Cream Production Problem is a classic problem in operations research and constraint programming that involves finding the optimal production plan for an ice cream company to meet the monthly demands while minimizing the total cost, which includes production costs and storage costs.
variables: $x_i, s_i, y_i, z_i$ for $i = 0, 1, \ldots, 12$, where $x_i$ represents the production in month $i$, $s_i$ represents the surplus (inventory) at the end of month $i$, $y_i$ represents the increase in production from month $i-1$ to month $i$, and $z_i$ represents the decrease in production from month $i-1$ to month $i$. These variables are used to model the production and inventory levels over a 12-month period.
constraints: $x_0 = 0, s_0 = 0, s_{12} = 0, x_i + s_{i-1} - s_i = d_i$ for $i = 1, \ldots, 12$, $x_i - x_{i-1} = y_i - z_i$ for $i = 1, \ldots, 12$, $x_i \geq 0, s_i \geq 0, y_i \geq 0, z_i \geq 0$ for $i = 0, 1, \ldots, 12$, where $d_i$ represents the demand in month $i$. These constraints model the initial and final inventory levels, the balance between production and demand, the changes in production levels, and the non-negativity of the decision variables.
objective: Minimize the total cost $obj = 50\sum_{i=0}^{12} (y_i) + 50\sum_{i=0}^{12} (z_i) + 20\sum_{i=0}^{12} (s_i)$, which represents the sum of the production change costs and the storage costs over the 12-month period.
