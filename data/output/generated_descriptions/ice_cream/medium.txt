**Ice Cream Production Problem**
The Ice Cream Production Problem is about determining the optimal production and storage of ice cream to meet monthly demands while minimizing costs.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables are:
- `x`: the amount of ice cream produced each month
- `s`: the surplus of ice cream stored at the end of each month
- `y`: the increase in production from the previous month
- `z`: the decrease in production from the previous month

**Constraints**
The key constraints are:
- The initial production and surplus are zero
- The final surplus is zero
- The production and surplus each month must meet the demand
- The change in production from the previous month is the difference between the increase and decrease in production
- Production, surplus, increase, and decrease are all non-negative

**Objective**
The objective is to minimize the total cost, which consists of the cost of increasing production, the cost of decreasing production, and the cost of storing surplus ice cream. The goal is to find the optimal production and storage plan that meets the monthly demands while minimizing these costs.
