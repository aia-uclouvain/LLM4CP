name: Cutting Stock Problem
description: The Cutting Stock Problem is a classic problem in operations research and optimization, which involves cutting rolls of material into smaller pieces to meet customer demands while minimizing waste. The problem has been modeled using Minizinc to find the optimal way to cut the material.
variables: $a_1, a_2, ..., a_8, b$ 
These variables represent the number of times each term (or pattern) is used to cut the material, where $a_i$ is the number of times term $i$ is used, and $b$ is the minimum number of rolls required to meet the customer demands.
constraints: 
$\forall i \in terms: a_i \geq 0$
$b \geq 0$
$\forall i \in ceils: \sum_{j \in terms} a_{ceiling[i,j]} \leq b$
$\forall k \in roofs: \sum_{j \in terms} a_{roofing[k,j]} \geq b+1$
$\forall j \in terms: j < card(terms) \rightarrow a_j - a_{j+1} \geq 0$
These constraints ensure that the number of times each term is used is non-negative, the minimum number of rolls required is non-negative, the total amount of material cut does not exceed the available material, the total amount of material cut is at least the demand plus one, and the number of times each term is used is non-increasing.
objective: minimize $ss = \sum_{j \in terms} a_j \cdot j$ 
The objective of the problem is to minimize the sum of the products of the number of times each term is used and the term itself, which represents the total amount of material wasted. Alternatively, the problem can also be formulated to minimize the minimum number of rolls required, $rhs = b - a_3 - a_5$.
