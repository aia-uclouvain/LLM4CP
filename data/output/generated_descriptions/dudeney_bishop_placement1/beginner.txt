Dudeney's Bishop Placement problem 
This problem is about placing bishops on a chessboard in such a way that every square on the board is either occupied by a bishop or under attack by one. 
The main variables are x and a, where x represents whether a square is occupied by a bishop and a represents whether a square is under attack by a bishop. 
The basic restrictions are that each square must be either occupied or under attack, and that bishops can attack squares diagonally. 
The goal is to minimize the number of bishops needed to achieve this.
