Dudeney's Bishop Placement problem
The problem is about placing the minimum number of bishops on a chessboard such that every square is either occupied by a bishop or under attack by one.
The main decision variables are `x[i,j]` and `a[i,j]`, where `x[i,j]` represents whether a bishop is placed on square `(i,j)` and `a[i,j]` represents whether square `(i,j)` is under attack by a bishop.
The key constraints are that each square is either occupied by a bishop or under attack, and that a square is under attack if there is a bishop on a diagonally adjacent square. Additionally, the model ensures that each bishop attacks all squares on its diagonals.
The objective of the problem is to minimize the total number of bishops placed on the board, which is represented by the variable `sumx`. The model uses a constraint programming approach to find the minimum number of bishops required to cover all squares on the board.
