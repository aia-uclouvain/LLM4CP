Relative sizes puzzle 
is a classic constraint programming problem. 
The problem is about finding the value of the word "BEETLES" where each letter represents a unique digit, given certain conditions and comparisons between the totals of different words, also represented by unique digits.

The main decision variables are the digits a, b, e, f, g, i, l, n, s, and t, which represent the unique digits for each letter in the words. 
These variables are used to construct the values of words like "BEES", "SNAIL", "FLEAS", "ANT", "ANTS", "GNAT", "FLIES", "FLEA", and "BEETLES".

The key constraints are that each letter consistently stands for the same digit, leading digits cannot be zero, and the total of four "BEES" and a "SNAIL" is greater than the total of eight "FLEAS" and an "ANT". 
Additionally, the total of 18 "ANTS" and a "GNAT" is greater than the total of 18 "FLIES" and a "FLEA". 
All the digits used must be different.

The problem's objective is to find the value of "BEETLES" that satisfies all the given conditions, which is essentially a satisfaction problem rather than an optimization problem, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the MiniZinc model. 
The model aims to find a solution that meets all the constraints, and the output will be the value of "BEETLES" that fulfills these requirements.
