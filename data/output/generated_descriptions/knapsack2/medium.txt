0-1 Knapsack Problem
The 0-1 Knapsack Problem is a classic problem in computer science and operations research that involves finding the optimal way to pack a set of items of different weights and values into a knapsack of limited capacity.

The main decision variables in this problem are the `take` array, where `take[i]` represents whether the `i-th` item is included (1) or excluded (0) from the knapsack. The `profit` variable represents the total value of the items included in the knapsack.

The key constraints in this problem are that the total weight of the items included in the knapsack cannot exceed its capacity, and each item can either be included or excluded, but not partially included. This is enforced by the `knapsack` predicate, which ensures that the sum of the weights of the included items does not exceed the capacity, and the `forall` constraint, which ensures that each `take[i]` is either 0 or 1.

The objective of the 0-1 Knapsack Problem is to maximize the total value of the items included in the knapsack, which is represented by the `profit` variable. The problem is solved by finding the optimal subset of items to include in the knapsack, such that the total value is maximized while not exceeding the capacity.
