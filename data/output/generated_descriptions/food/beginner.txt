Food Manufacture Problem
The problem is about a company that produces a product using different types of oil. The company wants to decide how much of each oil to buy and use each month to maximize its profit.

The main variables are: 
- buys: the amount of each oil bought each month
- stock: the amount of each oil stored each month
- production: the amount of product produced each month
- useoil: the amount of each oil used to produce the product each month

The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- The company can only buy and store a certain amount of each oil
- The company must have a minimum amount of each oil in stock at the beginning and end of the period
- The company can only use the oil it has in stock or buys
- The product must have a certain level of hardness, which depends on the types of oil used
- There are limits on the amount of certain oils that can be used together

The goal of the problem is to maximize the total profit, which is the revenue from selling the product minus the cost of buying and storing the oil.
