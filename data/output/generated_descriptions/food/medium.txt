**Food Manufacture Problem**
The Food Manufacture Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves optimizing the production and storage of a food product over a period of time. 

The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `buys`: the amount of each oil bought in each month
- `stock`: the amount of each oil stored in each month
- `useoil`: the amount of each oil used in production in each month
- `production`: the total amount of product produced in each month

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- The initial stock of each oil is 500 units, and the final stock must also be at least 500 units.
- The amount of each oil bought, stored, and used in production must be non-negative.
- The total amount of each oil available (bought and stored) must be sufficient to meet the production requirements.
- The hardness of the product must be within a certain range, which is determined by the hardness of the oils used in production.
- There are limits on the amount of certain oils that can be used in production in each month.

The objective of this problem is to **maximize the total profit**, which is calculated as the revenue from selling the product minus the cost of buying and storing the oils. The revenue is calculated as the product of the price of the product and the total amount produced, while the cost is calculated as the sum of the buying prices of the oils and the storage costs.
