name: Office Blocked Problem
description: The problem involves assigning six employees - Arthur, Bella, Craig, Duncan, Elizabeth, and Francesca - to six offices on two floors of a building, with three offices on each floor. The goal is to determine the office assignments based on given constraints, such as which employees work above or next to each other, and which employees work in adjacent rooms.
variables: $arthur, bella, craig, duncan, elizabeth, francesca$ - These decision variables represent the office numbers assigned to each of the six employees, with values ranging from 1 to 6, where 1-3 correspond to offices on the lower floor and 4-6 correspond to offices on the upper floor.
constraints: $all\_different(x) \wedge above(arthur, bella) \wedge adjacent(bella, duncan) \wedge room\_number(elizabeth) < room\_number(francesca) \wedge adjacent(craig, francesca)$ - These constraints ensure that each employee is assigned a unique office, Arthur is directly above Bella, Bella works next to Duncan, Elizabeth's office number is smaller than Francesca's, and Craig and Francesca work in adjacent rooms.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of offices to employees that meets all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
