name: Blocked N-Queens Problem
description: The Blocked N-Queens Problem is a variation of the classic N-Queens Problem, where the goal is to place N queens on an NxN chessboard such that no two queens attack each other, with the added constraint that some squares on the board are blocked and cannot be occupied by a queen.
variables: $q_i$ (i = 1..n), where $q_i$ represents the column where the ith queen is placed. These variables are used to determine the positions of the queens on the board.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1..n\}, \forall j \in \{1..n\}, i \neq j: |q_i - q_j| \neq |i - j|$, which ensures that no two queens are on the same diagonal, and $\forall i \in \{1..n\}: q_i \neq block_{i,2}$, where $block_{i,2}$ represents the blocked column for the ith row, which ensures that no queen is placed on a blocked square. Additionally, $\forall i \in \{1..n\}: \exists j \in \{1..n\}: q_i = j$, which ensures that each queen is placed in a valid column.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution, i.e., to determine the positions of the queens such that all constraints are satisfied, rather than to minimize or maximize a specific value.
