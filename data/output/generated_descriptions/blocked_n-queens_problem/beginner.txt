N-Queens Problem with Blocked Squares

The problem is about placing queens on a chessboard in such a way that no two queens attack each other, with some squares on the board being blocked and unable to hold a queen. 

The main variables are the positions of the queens on the board, which can be represented as a matrix where each row and column corresponds to a row and column on the chessboard.

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- Each row and column can only hold one queen.
- No two queens can be on the same diagonal.
- Queens cannot be placed on blocked squares.

The goal of the problem is to find a placement of queens on the board such that all the rules are satisfied, or to determine that no such placement exists. 

In other words, the problem is trying to solve the classic N-Queens problem, but with the added complication of some squares being blocked and unavailable for queen placement.
