The Transportation Problem
The Transportation Problem is a classic problem in operations research and constraint programming that involves finding the most cost-effective way to transport goods from a set of sources (warehouses) to a set of destinations (customers) through a set of intermediate points (transshipment points).

The main decision variables in this problem are `amountWT` and `amountTC`, which represent the amount of goods to be transported from a warehouse to a transshipment point and from a transshipment point to a customer, respectively.

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows: 
- The total amount of goods transported into a transshipment point must be equal to the total amount of goods transported out of it, to preserve the flow of goods.
- The total amount of goods transported to a customer must meet the customer's demand.
- The total amount of goods transported from a warehouse must not exceed the available stock at that warehouse.

The objective of the Transportation Problem is to minimize the total cost of transportation, which is calculated by summing the costs of transporting goods from warehouses to transshipment points and from transshipment points to customers. The total cost is subject to a maximum cost constraint `maxCost`.
