The problem is called the Transportation Problem. 
It's about delivering items from warehouses to customers through transshipment points in the most cost-effective way. 
The main variables are: 
- the amount of items transported from a warehouse to a transshipment point (amountWT), 
- the amount of items transported from a transshipment point to a customer (amountTC), 
- the total cost of transportation (totalCost). 
These variables represent the quantity of goods being moved between locations. 
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- the amount of items transported through each transshipment point must be the same (to preserve the flow of goods), 
- each customer must receive the exact amount of items they demand, 
- no warehouse can send more items than it has in stock. 
The goal of the problem is to minimize the total cost of transportation (totalCost) while meeting all the demands and following the rules.
