Cashier Change Problem
The problem is about a cashier who has $1.15 in coins but cannot make change for $1, $0.50, $0.25, $0.10, or $0.05. 
The main variables are the number of each type of coin (1 cent, 5 cents, 10 cents, 25 cents, and 50 cents) the cashier has.
The basic restrictions are that the total value of the coins must be $1.15, and the cashier must not be able to make change for the given amounts using the available coins.
The goal is to find the combination of coins that satisfies these conditions.
