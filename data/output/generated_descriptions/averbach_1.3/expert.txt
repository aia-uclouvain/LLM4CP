name: Devonshire Polo Club Problem
description: The problem involves five senior members of the Devonshire Polo Club, each owning a pony named after the wife of one of the other members. The task is to determine the husband of Jasmine and the owner of Helene, given various clues about the names of the ponies and the wives.
variables: $X, wife = [wife_1, wife_2, wife_3, wife_4, wife_5], pony = [pony_1, pony_2, pony_3, pony_4, pony_5]$ where $X$ is the husband of Georgette, $wife_i$ is the name of the wife of the $i^{th}$ member, and $pony_i$ is the name of the pony owned by the $i^{th}$ member.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}: wife_i \neq pony_i, all\_different(wife), all\_different(pony), pony_{Alloway} = Georgette, wife_{Alloway} = Francine, pony_{Cunningham} = Jasmine, wife_{Cunningham} = Helene, pony_{Elmsby} = Inez, pony_{Dunstan} = Francine, (wife_X = Georgette \wedge pony_X = wife_{Bennington})$ which ensure that each wife and pony has a unique name, and the given clues are satisfied.
objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
