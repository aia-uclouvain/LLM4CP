The Devonshire Polo Club Problem
The problem is about determining the names of the wives and ponies owned by five senior members of the Devonshire Polo Club, given certain clues about their relationships.
The main decision variables are `wife` and `pony`, which are arrays representing the names of the wives and ponies owned by each of the five men. The variable `X` represents the husband of Georgette.
The key constraints are: each wife and pony has a unique name, no man's wife and pony have the same name, and specific clues about the names of the wives and ponies owned by each man, such as Mr. Alloway's pony being named Georgette and Col. Cunningham owning Jasmine. Additionally, there are constraints about the relationships between the wives and ponies, such as Georgette's husband owning a pony named after Mr. Bennington's wife.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of wives and ponies to the five men, given the constraints. The model uses the `solve satisfy` statement, which means that it aims to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, without optimizing any particular objective function. The solution is then output in the form of the `wife`, `pony`, and `X` arrays.
