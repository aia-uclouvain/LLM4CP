name: Dudeney's Queen Placement Problem
description: This problem is a classic puzzle in the field of constraint programming, where the goal is to place eight queens on a chessboard such that the number of squares attacked by the queens is minimized, with certain constraints on the placement of the queens.
variables: $x_{i,j}, a_{i,j}, suma$ where $x_{i,j}$ represents whether the square $(i,j)$ is occupied by a queen, $a_{i,j}$ represents whether the square $(i,j)$ is attacked by any queen, and $suma$ is the total number of squares attacked by the queens. These variables are used to model the placement of the queens and the attack status of each square on the chessboard.
constraints: $\sum_{i,j} x_{i,j} = 8, \sum_{j=3}^{8} (x_{8,j} + x_{7,8} + x_{6,8}) = 5, (\sum_{m \neq i} x_{m,m-i+j} + \sum_{m \neq i} x_{m,i+j-m} + \sum_{m \neq i} x_{m,j} + \sum_{n \neq j} x_{i,n}) \leq 99a_{i,j}$ which ensure that all eight queens are used, five of the original queens are untouched, and a square is marked as attacked if it is under attack by any queen.
objective: minimize $suma$ which represents the total number of squares attacked by the queens.
