The 3-Partition Problem
The 3-Partition problem is a classic problem in computer science and operations research that involves dividing a set of items into three groups, with the goal of minimizing the maximum sum of the items in any group.

The main decision variables in this problem are the array `x`, where `x[i]` represents the group that item `i` is assigned to, and the array `sums`, where `sums[j]` represents the sum of the items in group `j`.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each item must be assigned to exactly one group.
- The sum of the items in each group must be calculated correctly.
- The sum of the items in all groups must be equal to the total sum of all items.
- The sums of the items in the groups must be in increasing order.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the maximum sum of the items in any group, which is represented by the variable `max_sum`. This means that the goal is to find an assignment of items to groups such that the sum of the items in the group with the highest sum is as small as possible.
