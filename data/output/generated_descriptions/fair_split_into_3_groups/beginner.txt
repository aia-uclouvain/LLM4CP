The problem is called the 3-Group Split problem. 
It's about dividing a group of items into three smaller groups in a way that makes the group with the highest total value as small as possible. 
The main variables are the items (each with a distinct value) and the groups they will be divided into. 
The basic restrictions are that each item can only be in one group, and there are three groups. 
The goal is to minimize the total value of the group with the highest value, meaning we want to distribute the items in a way that makes the most valuable group as small as possible.
