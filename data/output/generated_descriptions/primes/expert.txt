name: Prime Number Identification
description: The problem is to identify whether a given number is prime or not by calculating its divisors and checking if the number of divisors is exactly two, which is a characteristic of prime numbers.
variables: $nn, num\_divisors, divisor, is\_prime$ - These are the decision variables where $nn$ is the number to be checked for primality, $num\_divisors$ is the number of divisors of $nn$, $divisor$ is the set of divisors of $nn$, and $is\_prime$ is a binary variable indicating whether $nn$ is prime or not.
constraints: $n > 1 \land (i \in divisor \leftrightarrow n \mod i = 0) \land 1 \in divisor \land n \in divisor \land num\_divisors = card(divisor) \land (is\_prime = 1 \leftrightarrow num\_divisors = 2) \land (is\_prime = 0 \leftrightarrow num\_divisors > 2)$ - These constraints ensure that $nn$ is greater than 1, that the divisors of $nn$ are correctly identified, that 1 and $nn$ are included in the set of divisors, that the number of divisors is calculated correctly, and that $is\_prime$ is set to 1 if and only if $nn$ has exactly two divisors.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy the given constraints, which essentially means finding a value of $nn$ that satisfies the primality condition, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
