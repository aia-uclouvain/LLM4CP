Dinner Problem
The problem is about determining the number of grandparents, parents, and children to invite to dinner under certain constraints. 
The main decision variables are Grandparents, Parents, and Children, which represent the number of individuals from each group attending the dinner.
The key constraints are that the total number of people at dinner must be 20, the total cost must be $20, and there must be at least one person from each group, with costs being $3 for grandparents, $2 for parents, and $0.50 for children.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables that meet all the given constraints, as it is a satisfaction problem rather than an optimization problem, meaning it aims to find a feasible solution that satisfies all conditions rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
