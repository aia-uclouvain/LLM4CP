name: Werewolves II
description: The problem is a classic puzzle where there are three people, and each person is either a knight (who always tells the truth) or a knave (who always lies). Additionally, one of the people is a werewolf. The puzzle provides a set of statements made by each person, and the goal is to determine who is a knight, who is a knave, and who is the werewolf.
variables: $x_1, x_2, x_3, y_1, y_2, y_3, z$ where $x_i$ represents whether person $i$ is a knight (1) or a knave (0), $y_i$ represents whether person $i$ is a werewolf (1) or not (0), and $z$ is an auxiliary variable used to model one of the statements.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{3} y_i = 1, y_1 - 9x_1 \leq 0, y_1 - x_1 \geq 0, y_2 - 9x_2 \leq 0, y_2 - x_2 \geq 0, \sum_{i=1}^{3} (x_i + 9x_3) \geq 2, \sum_{i=1}^{3} (x_i + 9x_3) \leq 28$ where the first constraint ensures that only one person is a werewolf, the next four constraints model the statements made by each person, and the last two constraints model the statement made by the third person.
objective: satisfy, meaning that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
