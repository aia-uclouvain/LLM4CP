name: Language Knowledge Problem
description: The problem is to determine which languages each person knows, given a summary of the number of people who know each language. There are 7 people and 5 languages, and the summary states that 7 people know English, 4 know Swedish, 2 know Persian, 1 knows Russian, and 1 knows Hindi. The goal is to find a possible assignment of languages to people that satisfies the given summary.
variables: $mat_{p,l}, num\_lang_p$ where $p \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}$ and $l \in \{1, \ldots, 5\}$. These variables represent the language knowledge of each person, where $mat_{p,l}$ is a binary variable that is 1 if person $p$ knows language $l$ and 0 otherwise, and $num\_lang_p$ is the total number of languages known by person $p$.
constraints: $\sum_{p=1}^{7} mat_{p,l} = num\_person\_per\_language_l$ for $l \in \{1, \ldots, 5\}$, $num\_lang_p = \sum_{l=1}^{5} mat_{p,l}$ for $p \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}$, and $num\_lang_p \geq num\_lang_{p+1}$ for $p \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}$. These constraints ensure that the number of people who know each language matches the given summary, that the total number of languages known by each person is calculated correctly, and that the number of languages known by each person is sorted in non-increasing order.
objective: maximize $num\_lang_1$, which means that the problem is trying to maximize the number of languages known by the first person.
