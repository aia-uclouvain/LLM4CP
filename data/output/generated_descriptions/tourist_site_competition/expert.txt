name: Tourist Site Competition Problem
description: The problem involves assigning judges to tourist sites such that every site is visited by a specified number of judges, every judge visits a specified number of sites, and every pair of sites has a specified number of common judges. The problem can also be interpreted as an airline competition problem where airlines are tested by judges.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ where $i \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}$ and $j \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}$, $judges\_where_j$ where $j \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}$. These variables represent the assignment of judges to tourist sites, where $x_{i,j} = 1$ if judge $j$ visits site $i$ and $0$ otherwise, and $judges\_where_j$ is the set of sites visited by judge $j$.
constraints: 
$\forall s \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}: \sum_{j=1}^{7} x_{s,j} = 3$, 
$\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}: \sum_{s=1}^{7} x_{s,j} = 3$, 
$\forall s_1, s_2 \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}: s_1 < s_2: \sum_{j=1}^{7} (x_{s_1,j} \wedge x_{s_2,j}) = 1$, 
$\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}: \forall s \in \{1, \ldots, 7\}: x_{s,j} = 1 \leftrightarrow s \in judges\_where_j$, 
$\forall s \in \{1, 2, 3\}: x_{s,1} = 1$. 
These constraints ensure that every site is visited by exactly 3 judges, every judge visits exactly 3 sites, every pair of sites has exactly 1 common judge, the judges\_where variable is consistent with the x variable, and the symmetry breaking constraint that the first judge visits the first three sites.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible assignment of judges to tourist sites that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize. The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
