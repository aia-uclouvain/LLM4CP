name: Binero (Binairo, Takuzu, Binary Puzzle)
description: Binero is a grid game, similar to Sudoku, where a 2n x 2n grid must be filled with ones and zeroes. Some of the symbols are given, and empty squares are represented with a blank space. The goal is to fill in the grid such that each line and column has an equal number of zeros and ones, no more than two consecutive zeros or ones are placed in a line or column, and no identical lines or columns are allowed.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ and $j$ are indices ranging from 1 to $2n$, representing the value (0 or 1) of the cell at position $(i, j)$ in the grid. These variables are the decision variables of the problem, determining the final state of the grid.
constraints: 
$\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}: \sum_{j=1}^{2n} x_{i,j} = n$,
$\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}: \sum_{i=1}^{2n} x_{i,j} = n$,
$\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}: \text{max\_2\_in\_row\_regular}([x_{i,j} | j \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}])$,
$\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}: \text{max\_2\_in\_row\_regular}([x_{i,j} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}])$,
$\forall i, j \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\} \text{ where } i < j: \text{not\_same2}([x_{i,k} | k \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}], [x_{j,k} | k \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}])$,
$\forall i, j \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\} \text{ where } i < j: \text{not\_same2}([x_{k,i} | k \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}], [x_{k,j} | k \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}])$,
$\forall i, j \in \{1, \ldots, 2n\}: x_{i,j} = \text{data}_{i,j}$ if $\text{data}_{i,j} \neq X$. 
These constraints ensure that each line and column has an equal number of zeros and ones, no more than two consecutive zeros or ones are placed in a line or column, no identical lines or columns are allowed, and the given values in the grid are respected.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, i.e., to find a feasible solution that fills in the grid according to the rules of the game. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
