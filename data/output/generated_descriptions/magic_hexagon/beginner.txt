Magic Hexagon
The Magic Hexagon problem is about arranging the numbers 1 to 19 in a special hexagonal pattern so that all rows, columns, and diagonals add up to the same number, which is 38. 
The main variables are the numbers 1 to 19, which are represented by letters A to S in the hexagonal pattern. 
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are that each number can only be used once, and all rows, columns, and diagonals must add up to 38. 
The goal of the problem is to find an arrangement of the numbers 1 to 19 that satisfies all the rules and restrictions.
