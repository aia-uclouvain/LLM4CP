name: Number Cross Puzzle
description: The Number Cross Puzzle is a constraint satisfaction problem where the goal is to place numbers from 1 to N in a cross-shaped structure such that no two adjacent cells in any direction (up, down, left, right, or diagonal) contain adjacent numbers. The problem is defined by a specific cross-shaped structure with a given number of cells, and the task is to find all possible assignments of numbers to cells that satisfy the adjacency constraint.
variables: $x = (x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)$, where $x_i$ represents the number assigned to cell $i$ in the cross-shaped structure, and $n$ is the total number of cells.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}: \forall j \in g_i: |x_i - x_j| > 1$, where $g_i$ is the set of cells adjacent to cell $i$, and $|x_i - x_j| > 1$ ensures that the numbers in adjacent cells are not consecutive. Additionally, the constraints $\forall i \in \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}: x_i \in \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ and $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}: i \neq j \Rightarrow x_i \neq x_j$ ensure that each cell is assigned a unique number between 1 and $n$.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find all possible assignments of numbers to cells that satisfy the constraints, i.e., to find all solutions to the constraint satisfaction problem. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
