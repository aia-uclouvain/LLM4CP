Mr. Greenguest puzzle. 
This problem is about a man named Mr. Greenguest who wants to attend a dress party where men have to wear green dresses. He needs to decide what clothes to buy to follow the party's rules without spending too much money. 
The main variables are: 
- t (whether Mr. Greenguest wears a green tie)
- h (whether he wears a green hat)
- r (whether he wears a green shirt, which he already owns)
- s (whether he wears green socks)
- n (whether he does not follow the rules and has to pay a fine)
- cost (the total amount of money Mr. Greenguest spends). 
The basic restrictions are: 
- If Mr. Greenguest wears a green tie, he must wear a green shirt.
- He can only wear green socks and a green shirt if he wears a green tie or a green hat.
- If he wears a green shirt, a green hat, or no green socks, he must wear a green tie.
- If he breaks any of these rules, he has to pay a fine.
The goal is to minimize the cost, which means finding the cheapest way for Mr. Greenguest to attend the party by buying the necessary clothes or paying the fine if he doesn't follow the rules.
