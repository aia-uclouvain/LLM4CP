name: Mr Greenguest Puzzle
description: The problem is about Mr. Greenguest who wants to participate in a dress party where male guests must wear green dresses, and there are specific rules to follow regarding the color of the tie, shirt, hat, and socks. Mr. Greenguest only owns a green shirt and needs to decide what other items to buy to participate in the party at the lowest cost.
variables: $t, h, r, s, n, cost$ - These variables represent the decision variables: $t$ (whether to buy a green tie), $h$ (whether to buy a green hat), $r$ (whether to wear a green shirt), $s$ (whether to buy green socks), $n$ (whether the outfit does not follow the rules), and $cost$ (the total cost of the outfit).
constraints: $(t \rightarrow r) \vee n, ((s \vee r) \rightarrow (t \vee h)) \vee n, ((r \vee h \vee \neg s) \rightarrow t) \vee n, cost = 10 \cdot bool2int(t) + 2 \cdot bool2int(h) + 12 \cdot bool2int(s) + 11 \cdot bool2int(n)$ - These constraints represent the rules of the party: if a green tie is worn, a green shirt must be worn; a guest can only wear green socks and a green shirt if a green tie or a green hat is worn; a guest wearing a green shirt, a green hat, or not wearing green socks must wear a green tie; and the total cost is calculated based on the items purchased.
objective: minimize $cost$ - The objective of the problem is to minimize the total cost of the outfit while following the rules of the party.
