Mr. Greenguest Puzzle
The problem is about finding the cheapest way for Mr. Greenguest to participate in a dress party with specific rules regarding the color of his attire.
The main decision variables are: 
- t (bool): whether to buy a green tie
- h (bool): whether to buy a green hat
- r (bool): whether Mr. Greenguest is wearing a green shirt (he already owns one)
- s (bool): whether to buy green socks
- n (bool): whether Mr. Greenguest is not dressed according to the rules
These variables represent the choices Mr. Greenguest has to make in order to participate in the party.
The key constraints are: 
- If Mr. Greenguest wears a green tie, he must wear a green shirt, unless he is not following the rules.
- A guest can only wear green socks and a green shirt if he wears a green tie or a green hat, unless he is not following the rules.
- A guest wearing a green shirt or a green hat, or not wearing green socks, must wear a green tie, unless he is not following the rules.
The objective of the problem is to minimize the total cost, which includes the cost of buying a green tie ($10), a green hat ($2), and green socks ($12), as well as an entrance fee of $11 if Mr. Greenguest does not follow the rules.
