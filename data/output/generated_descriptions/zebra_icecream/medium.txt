Zebra Ice Cream Problem
The Zebra Ice Cream Problem is a classic constraint programming problem where we have to find the favorite ice cream flavor of four boys standing side by side, given certain clues about their ages, shirt colors, and positions.

The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `FirstName` and `LastName`: arrays representing the first and last names of the boys
- `Shirt`: an array representing the shirt colors of the boys
- `Icecream`: an array representing the favorite ice cream flavors of the boys
- `Age`: an array representing the ages of the boys
- `Position`: an array representing the positions of the boys

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- Each boy has a unique first name, last name, shirt color, ice cream flavor, age, and position
- The oldest boy is wearing a blue shirt
- Nicholas is standing next to the boy who likes strawberry ice cream
- Harris is at the fourth position
- Miller's favorite ice cream is chocolate
- The youngest boy is standing next to the boy wearing a green shirt
- Young is at the third position
- The 9-year-old boy is standing to the left of Young
- Kevin is standing next to the 9-year-old boy
- The boy wearing a black shirt is at the second position
- Daniel likes strawberry ice cream
- The boy who likes vanilla ice cream is at the fourth position
- The 7-year-old boy is standing between the boy wearing a blue shirt and the 8-year-old boy

The objective of this problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, which means finding a solution that assigns a unique first name, last name, shirt color, ice cream flavor, age, and position to each boy, while also satisfying all the given conditions. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
