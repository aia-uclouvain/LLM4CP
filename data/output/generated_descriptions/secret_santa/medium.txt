Secret Santa Problem
The Secret Santa problem is about assigning a Secret Santa to each person in a group, ensuring that no person is their own Secret Santa and that people from the same family are not assigned to each other.

The main decision variables in this problem are represented by the array `x`, where `x[i]` denotes the person assigned as the Secret Santa to person `i`. The `family` array represents the family affiliation of each person.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- each person must have a unique Secret Santa (all_different(x)), 
- no person can be their own Secret Santa (no_fix_points(x)), 
- and people from the same family cannot be assigned to each other (family[i] != family[x[i]]).

The objective of the Secret Santa problem is to find a valid assignment of Secret Santas to each person in the group, satisfying the given constraints. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that meets all the constraints.
