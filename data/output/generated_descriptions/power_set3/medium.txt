In 'ps2', each row also represents a subset, but the value in each column is the position of the corresponding element in the subset, scaled by the element's position in the set. 
The key constraints are that 'ps1' and 'ps2' must both represent valid powersets of the given set, meaning that each row in 'ps1' must be a unique binary number from 1 to 2^n - 1, and each row in 'ps2' must be a unique permutation of the numbers from 1 to n, where the presence of a number indicates the presence of the corresponding element in the subset.
