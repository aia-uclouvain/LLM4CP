The Coloured Painting problem. 
This problem is about figuring out the correct arrangement of colors in a 6x6 painting, where each square can be one of three colors: red, blue, or green. 
The main variables are the rows and columns of the painting, and the order in which they appear. 
The basic restrictions are that each row and column can only be used once, and that the colors in each row must match the colors in each column. 
The goal is to find the correct arrangement of rows and columns that satisfies these restrictions, effectively reproducing the original painting.
