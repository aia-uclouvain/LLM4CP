name: Prime Multiplication Problem
description: The problem is to find prime numbers (2, 3, 5, or 7) for each digit in a multiplication equation such that the equation is correct. The equation is a multiplication of two numbers, where the first number is a three-digit number (PPP) and the second number is a two-digit number (PP), resulting in a five-digit number (PPPPP).
variables: $abc, de, fghi, jklm, nopqr, abc_a, de_a, fghi_a, jklm_a, nopqr_a$ - These variables represent the digits in the multiplication equation, where $abc$, $de$, $fghi$, $jklm$, and $nopqr$ are the numerical values of the corresponding digits, and $abc_a$, $de_a$, $fghi_a$, $jklm_a$, and $nopqr_a$ are arrays of prime numbers representing the individual digits.
constraints: $abc = to\_num(abc\_a), de = to\_num(de\_a), fghi = to\_num(fghi\_a), jklm = to\_num(jklm\_a), nopqr = to\_num(nopqr\_a), abc \* de\_a[2] = fghi, abc \* de\_a[1] = jklm, fghi + 10 \* jklm = nopqr$ - These constraints ensure that the numerical values of the digits are calculated correctly from the prime number arrays, and that the multiplication equation is satisfied.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., to find prime numbers for each digit such that the multiplication equation is correct. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
