name: Shopping with Delivery Cost Problem
description: The problem involves buying different kinds of parts from a set of stores, where each store may or may not have each specific part, and the goal is to minimize the total cost of buying all parts, including the shipping cost for each store.
variables: $x_{p} \in \{1, 2, ..., num\_stores\}$ for $p \in \{1, 2, ..., num\_parts\}$, $part\_cost \in \mathbb{R}^+$, $delivery\_cost \in \mathbb{R}^+$, $total\_cost \in \mathbb{R}^+$. These variables represent the store from which to buy each part ($x_p$), the total cost of the parts ($part\_cost$), the total delivery cost for all stores ($delivery\_cost$), and the total cost including parts and delivery ($total\_cost$).
constraints: $total\_cost \geq 0$, $product\_stores_{p, x_p} > 0$ for all $p$, $part\_cost = \sum_{p=1}^{num\_parts} product\_stores_{p, x_p}$, $delivery\_cost = \sum_{s=1}^{num\_stores} store\_delivery\_costs_s \cdot \mathbb{I}(\sum_{p=1}^{num\_parts} \mathbb{I}(x_p = s) > 0)$. These constraints ensure that the total cost is non-negative, that each part is bought from a store that has it, that the total part cost is calculated correctly, and that the delivery cost is calculated correctly based on which stores are used.
objective: Minimize $total\_cost = part\_cost + delivery\_cost$
