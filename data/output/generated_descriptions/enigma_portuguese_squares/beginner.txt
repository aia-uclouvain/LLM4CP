Portuguese Squares problem. 
This problem is about decoding a statement where numbers are written with letters, and these letters represent digits. The statement says that four specific numbers, ZERO, UM, NOVE, and CEM, are perfect squares. 
The main variables are the letters Z, E, R, O, U, M, N, V, and C, which represent the digits 0 through 9, with each letter being a different digit. 
The basic restrictions are that each letter must be a unique digit, and no number can start with zero. Also, when these letters are replaced with their corresponding digits and formed into numbers, these numbers must be perfect squares. 
The goal is to find the square root of the product of these four perfect squares, ZERO, UM, NOVE, and CEM, and calculate its numerical value.
