name: Enigma Puzzle 1476: Portuguese Squares
description: The problem is to find the numerical value of the square root of the product of four perfect squares, ZERO, UM, NOVE, and CEM, where each letter represents a digit and different letters represent different digits, and no number starts with zero.
variables: $Z, E, R, O, U, M, N, V, C, x1, x2, x3, x4, square\_root$ - These variables represent the digits to be replaced in the words ZERO, UM, NOVE, and CEM, with $Z, E, R, O, U, M, N, V, C$ being the individual digits, $x1, x2, x3, x4$ being the perfect squares, and $square\_root$ being the square root of the product of these perfect squares.
constraints: $all\_different(x) \wedge x1^2 = 1000Z + 100E + 10R + O \wedge x2^2 = 10U + M \wedge x3^2 = 1000N + 100O + 10V + E \wedge x4^2 = 100C + 10E + M \wedge square\_root = x1 \* x2 \* x3 \* x4 \wedge Z \geq 1 \wedge U \geq 1 \wedge N \geq 1 \wedge C \geq 1$ - These constraints ensure that all digits are different, each word is a perfect square, and the square root of the product of these perfect squares is calculated correctly, with the additional constraints that no number starts with zero.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find the value of $square\_root$, which is the square root of the product of the four perfect squares.
