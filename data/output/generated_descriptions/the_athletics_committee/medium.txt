The Athletics Committee problem
The Athletics Committee problem is about selecting a committee from a group of people such that no one on the committee has three or more friends on the committee, but everyone not on the committee has exactly three friends on it.
The main decision variables are `x` and `friends`. `x[i]` represents whether person `i` is on the committee (1) or not (0), and `friends[i,j]` represents whether person `i` and person `j` are friends (1) or not (0).
The key constraints are: 
- A person on the committee cannot have three or more friends on the committee.
- A person not on the committee must have exactly three friends on the committee.
- A person cannot be friends with themselves.
- The friendship relation is symmetric, meaning if person `i` is friends with person `j`, then person `j` is also friends with person `i`.
- There must be at least one person on the committee and one person not on the committee.
The objective of the problem is to find a valid assignment of people to the committee that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. In other words, the goal is to determine whether it is possible to form a committee under the given conditions.
