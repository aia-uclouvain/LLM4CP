name: The Athletics Committee problem

description: The problem is about selecting a committee from a group of people, where each person has a certain number of friends. The goal is to choose the committee such that no one on the committee has three or more friends on the committee, but everyone outside the committee has exactly three friends on the committee.

variables: $x_i, friends_{i,j}$, where $x_i$ represents whether person $i$ is on the committee (1 if on, 0 if off), and $friends_{i,j}$ represents whether person $i$ and person $j$ are friends (1 if friends, 0 if not). 

constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{n} friends_{i,j} \cdot x_j < 3 \text{ if } x_i = 1, \sum_{j=1}^{n} friends_{i,j} \cdot x_j = 3 \text{ if } x_i = 0, friends_{i,i} = 0, friends_{i,j} = friends_{j,i}, \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i > 0, \sum_{i=1}^{n} (1 - x_i) > 0$, which ensure that a person on the committee has less than three friends on the committee, a person off the committee has exactly three friends on the committee, a person is not a friend of themselves, the friendship relation is symmetric, at least one person is on the committee, and at least one person is off the committee.

objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
