Building Design Problem
The problem is about designing a rectangular building that is partially buried underground to save energy costs for heating and cooling. 
The main variables are: 
- the number of stories in the building (n), 
- the depth of the building below ground (d), 
- the height of the building above ground (h), 
- the length of the building (l), and 
- the width of the building (w).
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- the total floor space must be at least 20,000 square meters, 
- the length and width of the building cannot be more than 50 meters, 
- the ratio of the length to the width must be the golden ratio (1.618), 
- each story must be 3.5 meters high, and 
- the annual energy costs for heating and cooling must not exceed $225,000.
The goal is to minimize the cost of excavating the building, which is directly related to the volume of the building below ground (z = d*l*w).
