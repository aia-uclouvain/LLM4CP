The Building Design Problem
The Building Design problem is about designing a partially buried rectangular building to minimize the cost of excavation while meeting certain constraints on energy costs, floor space, and lot size.

The main decision variables are:
- `d`: the depth of the building below ground
- `h`: the height of the building above ground
- `l`: the length of the building in plan
- `w`: the width of the building in plan
- `n`: the number of stories

These variables represent the dimensions of the building and the number of stories, which determine the volume of excavation required and the energy costs associated with the building.

The key constraints are:
- The total floor space must be at least 20,000 m^2
- The ratio of the plan dimensions (length to width) must be equal to the golden ratio (1.618)
- Each story must be 3.5 m high
- The building plan dimensions must not exceed 50 m
- The annual energy costs must not exceed $225,000, which is estimated based on the exposed surface area of the building

The objective of the problem is to minimize the cost of excavation, which is represented by the variable `z`, calculated as the product of the depth, length, and width of the building (`d*l*w`). The goal is to find the optimal building dimensions that satisfy all the constraints while minimizing the excavation cost.
