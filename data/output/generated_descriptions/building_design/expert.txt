name: Building Design Problem
description: The problem is to design a partially buried rectangular building to minimize the cost of excavation while meeting certain constraints such as total floor space, lot size, and energy costs. The building's dimensions, including depth below ground, height above ground, length, and width, need to be determined to achieve the minimum excavation cost.
variables: $d, h, l, w, n$ - These variables represent the depth of the building below ground ($d$), the height of the building above ground ($h$), the length of the building in plan ($l$), the width of the building in plan ($w$), and the number of stories ($n$), respectively.
constraints: $n \geq 1, d \geq 0, h \geq 0, l \geq 0, w \geq 0, n \leq 100, d \leq 100, h \leq 100, l \leq 100, w \leq 100, \frac{d+h}{n} = 3.5, l = 1.618w, nlw \geq 20000, l \leq 50, w \leq 50, 100(2hl + 2hw + lw) \leq 225000$ - These constraints ensure that the number of stories is a positive integer, all dimensions are non-negative and within certain limits, the ratio of the plan dimensions is the golden ratio, the total floor space meets the requirement, and the energy costs do not exceed the specified limit.
objective: Minimize $z = dlw$, which represents the volume of the building to be excavated, thereby minimizing the cost of excavation.
