The name of the problem is the Fixed-charge problem. 
This problem is about a company that makes three products: shirts, shorts, and pants. The company wants to make as much money as possible from selling these products. 
The main variables are: 
- Rent: whether or not to rent a machine to make each product
- Produce: how many of each product to make
- These variables mean that the company needs to decide which machines to rent and how many products to make with those machines.
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- The company only has a certain amount of labor and cloth to use, so it can't make too many products
- Each product can only be made with a specific machine, so if the company wants to make a product, it needs to rent the right machine
- The company can't make more products than it has the capacity to make, even if it rents the right machine.
The goal of the problem is to maximize the company's profit, which is the total amount of money it makes from selling the products, minus the cost of renting the machines.
