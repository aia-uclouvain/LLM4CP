name: Fixed-Charge Problem

description: The Fixed-Charge Problem is a classic problem in integer programming and operations research. It involves a company that manufactures multiple products, each requiring a certain amount of labor and cloth, and limited by the available capacity of these resources. The company must also rent machines to produce each product, incurring a fixed renting cost. The goal is to determine the optimal production levels of each product and the machines to rent in order to maximize the total profit.

variables: $Rent = (Rent_1, Rent_2, ..., Rent_{num\_machines})$, $Produce = (Produce_1, Produce_2, ..., Produce_{num\_products})$
These are the decision variables, where $Rent_i$ is a binary variable indicating whether machine $i$ is rented or not, and $Produce_j$ is the quantity of product $j$ to produce.

constraints: $\sum_{p=1}^{num\_products} use_{p,r} \cdot Produce_p \leq Capacity_r$ for $r = 1, 2$, $Produce_p \leq MaxProduction \cdot Rent_{mm}$ for $p = 1, ..., num\_products$ and $mm \in ProductMachines_p$
These constraints ensure that the total usage of each resource does not exceed its available capacity, and that the production of each product does not exceed the maximum possible production if the required machine is rented.

objective: Maximize $z = \sum_{p=1}^{num\_products} ProductProfit_p \cdot Produce_p - \sum_{m=1}^{num\_machines} RentingCost_m \cdot Rent_m$
The objective is to maximize the total profit, which is the sum of the profits from producing each product minus the total renting cost of the machines.
