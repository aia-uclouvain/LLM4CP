**Hidato Puzzle**
The Hidato puzzle is a logic-based puzzle where the goal is to connect numbered tiles in a grid to form a continuous path. The puzzle starts with some numbered tiles already filled in, and the objective is to fill in the remaining tiles with consecutive numbers, ensuring that each number is connected to the next one horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are represented by the 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` represents the number that should be placed in the cell at row `i` and column `j`. The values of `x` range from 1 to `r*c`, where `r` and `c` are the number of rows and columns in the grid, respectively.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- Each cell in the grid must contain a unique number between 1 and `r*c`.
- The numbers in the grid must be consecutive, meaning that the difference between the numbers in two adjacent cells (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) must be 1.
- The puzzle starts with some numbered tiles already filled in, and these numbers must remain in their original positions.
- The connections between numbers can only be horizontal, vertical, or diagonal, and not diagonal in a way that wraps around the grid (i.e., it's a non-torus grid).

**Objective**
The objective of the Hidato puzzle is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, effectively connecting the numbered tiles in a continuous path. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize; instead, it aims to find a feasible solution that meets all the constraints. The solution is typically presented as a completed grid with consecutive numbers, where each number is connected to the next one according to the rules of the puzzle.
