The problem is known as the Topcoder Problem or the Integer Programming Problem. 
It is about finding the optimal values of three unique integers, a, b, and c, within the range of 1 to 10, such that the expression ab + bc is maximized.

The main decision variables are a, b, and c, which represent the three unique integers. 

The key constraints are that a, b, and c must be within the range of 1 to 10 and must be unique, meaning they cannot be equal to each other.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the expression ab + bc, which is achieved by finding the optimal combination of a, b, and c that satisfies the given constraints. In other words, the goal is to find the values of a, b, and c that result in the largest possible value of the expression ab + bc.
