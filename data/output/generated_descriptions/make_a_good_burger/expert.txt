name: Make a Good Burger problem

description: The problem is to create a burger recipe that meets certain health requirements and taste quality standards while minimizing or maximizing the total cost. The burger can include various ingredients such as beef patty, bun, cheese, onions, pickles, lettuce, ketchup, and tomato, each with its own nutritional values and cost. The goal is to find the optimal combination of ingredients that satisfies the given constraints and achieves the desired objective.

variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_8$, where $x_i$ represents the number of servings of each ingredient, with $x_1$ being the beef patty, $x_2$ being the bun, and so on. These decision variables determine the quantity of each ingredient to include in the burger.

constraints: 
$\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot items[i,1] < 3000$ (sodium constraint)
$\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot items[i,2] < 150$ (fat constraint)
$\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot items[i,3] < 3000$ (calories constraint)
$x_6 = x_7$ (ketchup and lettuce servings must be the same)
$x_5 = x_8$ (pickles and tomatoes servings must be the same)
$1 \leq x_i \leq 5$ for $i = 1, 2, ..., 8$ (each ingredient can have at least 1 and at most 5 servings)
These constraints ensure that the burger meets the health requirements, taste quality standards, and ingredient availability.

objective: The objective is to either minimize or maximize the total cost of the burger, which is given by the expression $\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot items[i,4]$. The minimize objective aims to find the cheapest burger that meets the constraints, while the maximize objective aims to find the most expensive burger that meets the constraints.
