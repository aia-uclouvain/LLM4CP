**Make a Good Burger Problem**
The Make a Good Burger problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves creating a burger with a combination of ingredients while meeting certain health and taste requirements.

**Main Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are the quantities of each ingredient to include in the burger, represented by the array `x`. Each element `x[i]` represents the number of servings of the `i-th` ingredient, which can range from 1 to 5.

**Key Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
* The total sodium content of the burger must be less than 3000 mg.
* The total fat content of the burger must be less than 150 grams.
* The total calorie content of the burger must be less than 3000 calories.
* The number of servings of ketchup and lettuce must be the same.
* The number of servings of pickles and tomatoes must be the same.
* Each ingredient must be included at least once and no more than five times.

**Problem Objective**
The problem has two objectives:
* Minimize the total cost of the burger.
* Maximize the total cost of the burger.
The goal is to find the most expensive and the least expensive burger that meets all the constraints.
