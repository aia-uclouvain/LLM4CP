Water Jug Problem
The Water Jug Problem is a classic problem in the field of operations research and constraint programming, which involves finding the minimum number of steps to measure a certain amount of water using jugs of different capacities.
The main decision variables in this problem are the flow of water between the jugs, represented by the array `x`, where `x[i,j]` denotes the amount of water flowing from jug `i` to jug `j`. The `inFlow` and `outFlow` variables represent the total amount of water flowing into and out of each jug.
The key constraints in this problem are: each jug can only hold a certain amount of water, the total amount of water flowing into a jug cannot exceed its capacity, and the total amount of water flowing out of a jug cannot exceed the amount of water in the jug. Additionally, the problem has a constraint on the total cost, which is the total number of steps required to measure the desired amount of water.
The objective of the Water Jug Problem is to minimize the total cost, which is the total number of steps required to measure the desired amount of water. In other words, the goal is to find the shortest sequence of steps that will result in the desired amount of water being measured.
