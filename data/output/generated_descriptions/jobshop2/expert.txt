name: Job-Shop Scheduling Problem

description: The Job-Shop Scheduling Problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves scheduling a set of jobs on a set of machines to minimize the total time taken to complete all jobs. Each job consists of a sequence of operations, each of which must be performed on a specific machine. The problem requires finding a schedule that satisfies the constraints of the jobs and machines, such as the order of operations within each job and the availability of machines.

variables: $num\_machines, num\_jobs, job\_times, job\_order, one, order\_add, max\_time, job\_start, job\_end, earliest\_end\_time$ 
These variables represent the number of machines and jobs, the time taken by each job on each machine, the order of operations within each job, a helper array, a compensation value for the job order, the maximum time, the start and end times of each job on each machine, and the earliest end time of all jobs.

constraints: 
$job\_end[job,machine] = job\_start[job,machine] + job\_times[job,machine]$ 
$\forall machine \in 1..num\_machines: cumulative([job\_start[job,machine] | job in 1..num\_jobs], [job\_times[job,machine] | job in 1..num\_jobs], one, 1)$ 
$\forall job \in 1..num\_jobs, \forall machine1,machine2 \in 1..num\_machines: if job\_order[job,machine1] < job\_order[job,machine2] then before(job\_end[job,machine1], job\_start[job,machine2]) else after(job\_start[job,machine1], job\_end[job,machine2])$ 
$\forall job \in 1..num\_jobs: all\_different([job\_start[job,machine] | machine in 1..num\_machines]) \wedge all\_different([job\_end[job,machine] | machine in 1..num\_machines])$ 
These constraints ensure that the end time of each job on each machine is the start time plus the job time, that the jobs on each machine do not overlap, that the jobs are performed in the correct order, and that the start and end times of each job on each machine are distinct.

objective: minimize $earliest\_end\_time$ 
The objective of the problem is to minimize the earliest end time of all jobs, which represents the total time taken to complete all jobs.
