Enigma Puzzle Eight Times
The problem is about finding a 5-digit number with different digits, not starting with zero, which when multiplied by 8 produces another 5-digit number with the remaining 5 digits. Additionally, the sum of the digits of the original number must be greater than the sum of the digits of the product.

The main decision variables are x and y, which represent the 5-digit numbers before and after multiplication by 8, respectively. The arrays x_a and y_a represent the individual digits of x and y, respectively.

The key constraints are: 
- all digits in x and y must be different, 
- x and y must be 5-digit numbers, 
- y must be the result of multiplying x by 8, 
- the sum of the digits of x must be greater than the sum of the digits of y.

The objective is to find a 5-digit number x that satisfies these constraints, with the goal of identifying the specific number that meets the given conditions. The problem does not involve explicit minimization or maximization, but rather finding a feasible solution that meets all the constraints.
