The Arch Friends puzzle 
is a classic constraint programming problem about determining the order in which Harriet bought four different pairs of shoes and the stores where she bought them. 
The main decision variables are the order of the shoes (ecru espadrilles, fuchsia flats, purple pumps, and suede sandals) and the order of the stores (Foot Farm, Heels in a Handcart, The Shoe Palace, and Tootsies). 
The key constraints are: Harriet bought fuchsia flats at Heels in a Handcart, the store she visited after buying purple pumps was not Tootsies, Foot Farm was her second stop, and she bought suede sandals two stops after The Shoe Palace. 
The objective is to find a satisfying assignment of shoes to stores, which means finding an order that satisfies all the given constraints, with no specific minimization or maximization goal.
