name: Arch Friends Puzzle
description: The problem is about determining the order in which Harriet bought four different pairs of shoes (ecru espadrilles, fuchsia flats, purple pumps, and suede sandals) and the store where she bought each pair (Foot Farm, Heels in a Handcart, The Shoe Palace, or Tootsies) based on given clues.
variables: $ecru\_espadrilles, fuchsia\_flats, purple\_pumps, suede\_sandals, Foot\_Farm, Heels\_in\_a\_Handcart, The\_Shoe\_Palace, Tootsies, shoes = [ecru\_espadrilles, fuchsia\_flats, purple\_pumps, suede\_sandals], shops = [Foot\_Farm, Heels\_in\_a\_Handcart, The\_Shoe\_Palace, Tootsies]$ - These variables represent the order of the shoes and stores, where each shoe and store is assigned a position from 1 to 4, with $shoes$ and $shops$ being arrays of these positions.
constraints: $all\_different(shoes) \wedge all\_different(shops) \wedge fuchsia\_flats = Heels\_in\_a\_Handcart \wedge purple\_pumps + 1 \neq Tootsies \wedge Foot\_Farm = 2 \wedge The\_Shoe\_Palace + 2 = suede\_sandals$ - These constraints ensure that each shoe and store is assigned a unique position, that fuchsia flats were bought at Heels in a Handcart, that the store after buying purple pumps is not Tootsies, that Foot Farm is the second stop, and that suede sandals were bought two stops after The Shoe Palace.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of positions to shoes and stores, there is no explicit value to minimize or maximize.
