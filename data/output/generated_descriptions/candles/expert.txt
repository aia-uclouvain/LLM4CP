name: Candles problem
description: The problem describes a situation where Marshall and Lily have 7 children born on 6 consecutive Thanksgivings. Uncle Barney prepares a birthday cake with candles every year and has bought 2 more candles this year than last year. The problem asks to determine the ages of the children and the number of candles Barney bought.
variables: $varsnow_1, varsnow_2, ..., varsnow_7, varsthen_1, varsthen_2, ..., varsthen_7, now, lastyear$. These variables represent the ages of the children this year ($varsnow_i$) and last year ($varsthen_i$), and the total number of candles bought this year ($now$) and last year ($lastyear$).
constraints: $\forall i \in \{2, ..., 7\}: (varsnow_{i-1} = varsnow_i + 1) \vee (varsnow_{i-1} = varsnow_i)$, $\forall i \in \{1, ..., 7\}: varsnow_i = varsthen_i + 1$, $nvalue(6, varsnow) = 6$, $nvalue(6, varsthen) = 6$, $lastyear + 2 = now$, $now = \sum_{i=1}^{7} varsnow_i$, $lastyear = \sum_{i=1}^{7} varsthen_i$. These constraints ensure that the ages of the children this year are either the same or one more than their ages last year, the ages of the children last year are one less than their ages this year, there are 6 consecutive years, the number of candles this year is 2 more than last year, and the total number of candles is equal to the sum of the ages of the children.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the variables, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement, which means that the problem is a decision problem where the goal is to determine whether a solution exists, rather than optimizing a specific objective function.
