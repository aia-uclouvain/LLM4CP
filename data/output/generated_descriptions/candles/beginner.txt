Candles problem
The problem is about figuring out how old Marshall and Lily's 7 children are and how many candles their uncle Barney bought for their birthday cake this year and last year. 
The main variables are the ages of the children this year and last year, and the number of candles Barney bought this year and last year. 
The basic restrictions are that the children were born in 6 consecutive years, each child is one year older than they were last year, and Barney bought 2 more candles this year than last year. The total number of candles is equal to the sum of the children's ages. 
The goal is to find a solution that satisfies all these conditions, which will give the ages of the children and the number of candles Barney bought.
