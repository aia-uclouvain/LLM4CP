Contiguity Constraint Problem
The problem is about finding a sequence of decisions (yes or no) that minimizes a certain value, given some random weights and rules about consecutive decisions.
The main variables are: 
- w: an array of random weights (0 or 1) 
- y: an array of decisions (0 for no, 1 for yes) 
- z: the total value of the decisions, calculated by multiplying each decision by its corresponding weight and summing them up.
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- The total value (z) must be greater than or equal to a certain threshold (A) 
- If a decision is made (y[i] is 1), then all subsequent decisions must also be made (consecutive ones).
The goal of the problem is to minimize the total value (z) while satisfying the rules and restrictions.
