The Hundred Fowls puzzle. 
This problem is about finding the number of cocks, hens, and chicks that can be bought with 100 coins, given their respective prices. 
The main decision variables are cock, hen, and chicks, which represent the number of cocks, hens, and chicks to be bought. 
The key constraints are that the total cost of the birds should equal 100 coins, the total number of birds should be 100, and the number of chicks should be a multiple of 3, as they are sold in groups of three. 
The problem's objective is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
