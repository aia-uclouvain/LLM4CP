name: Divisible by 9 through 1 problem

description: The problem is to find a number consisting of 9 digits in which each of the digits from 1 to 9 appears only once, and the number must satisfy certain divisibility requirements. Specifically, the number should be divisible by 9, and when the rightmost digit is removed, the remaining number should be divisible by 8, and so on, until there's only one digit left, which should be divisible by 1.

variables: $x = (x_1, x_2, ..., x_9)$, $t = (t_1, t_2, ..., t_9)$ 
These are the decision variables, where $x_i$ represents the $i^{th}$ digit of the number, and $t_i$ represents the number formed by the first $i$ digits. The $x_i$ variables are integers between 1 and 9, and the $t_i$ variables are integers between 0 and $10^9 - 1$.

constraints: $all\_different(x)$, $toNum([x_j | j in 1..base-i], t[i], base)$, $int\_mod(t[base-i], i, 0)$ for $i = 1..9$ 
These constraints ensure that all digits are different, that the number formed by the first $i$ digits is correctly calculated, and that the number formed by the first $i$ digits is divisible by $i$, respectively.

objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, meaning that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
