**Divisible by 9 through 1 Problem**

The Divisible by 9 through 1 problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding a number consisting of 9 digits, where each digit from 1 to 9 appears only once. The number must satisfy a set of divisibility requirements, where removing the rightmost digit repeatedly results in a number that is divisible by a decreasing sequence of integers from 9 to 1.

The array `t` of size 9 represents the numbers formed by removing the rightmost digit repeatedly, where each element `t[i]` is a variable that takes a value from 0 to a large integer `m`.

**Key Constraints**

The key constraints in this problem can be described in plain language as follows:

* Each digit from 1 to 9 appears only once in the number.
* The number formed by the first 9 digits is divisible by 9.
* The number formed by the first 8 digits is divisible by 8.
* The number formed by the first 7 digits is divisible by 7, and so on.
* The single digit remaining after removing all other digits is divisible by 1.

**Problem Objective**

The objective of this problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function. The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a feasible solution that meets all the requirements.
