name: No-three-in-line problem
description: The no-three-in-line problem is a classic problem in discrete geometry that asks for the maximum number of points that can be placed in an n × n grid so that no three points are collinear, meaning they do not lie on the same line. The goal is to find the optimal placement of points in the grid to achieve this maximum number.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ where $i \in 1..n$ and $j \in 1..n$, and $tot$. These are binary decision variables where $x_{i,j} = 1$ if a point is placed at position $(i, j)$ in the grid, and $x_{i,j} = 0$ otherwise. $tot$ is an integer variable representing the total number of points placed in the grid.
constraints: $tot = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{i,j}$, $\forall i \in 1..n: \sum_{a=1}^{n} x_{a,i} < 3$, $\forall i \in 1..n: \sum_{a=1}^{n} x_{i,a} < 3$, $tot = 2n$, and $lex2(x)$. These constraints ensure that the total number of points is calculated correctly, no three points are in the same row or column, the total number of points is equal to $2n$, and the solution is lexicographically non-decreasing to break symmetry.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy the given constraints, which implicitly maximizes the total number of points $tot$ to $2n$.
