name: Good Burger Problem
description: The problem is to create the most expensive burger possible using a set of ingredients, each with its own nutritional content and cost, while adhering to certain health and taste requirements, including limits on sodium, fat, and calories, as well as constraints on the servings of certain ingredients.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_8$, where $x_i$ represents the number of servings of the $i^{th}$ ingredient, ranging from 1 to 5, and corresponds to the ingredients Beef Patty, Bun, Cheese, Onions, Pickles, Lettuce, Ketchup, and Tomato, respectively.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot sodium_i < 3000$, $\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot fat_i < 150$, $\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot calories_i < 3000$, $x_7 = x_6$, $x_5 = x_8$, $1 \leq x_i \leq 5$ for $i = 1, 2, ..., 8$, where $sodium_i$, $fat_i$, and $calories_i$ represent the sodium, fat, and calorie content of the $i^{th}$ ingredient, respectively, and the last two constraints ensure that the servings of Ketchup and Lettuce are the same, and the servings of Pickles and Tomatoes are the same.
objective: Maximize the total cost of the burger, given by $\sum_{i=1}^{8} x_i \cdot cost_i$, where $cost_i$ is the cost of the $i^{th}$ ingredient.
