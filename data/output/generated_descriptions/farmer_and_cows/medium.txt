The Farmer and Cow Problem
The Farmer and Cow problem is a classic constraint programming problem where a farmer has a certain number of cows, each producing a different amount of milk, and wants to distribute them among his sons so that each son gets the same amount of milk.

The main decision variables in this problem are `sons` and `cows`. The `sons` variable represents the total amount of milk each son should get, and the `cows` variable represents which cow is assigned to which son.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- Each son should get the same amount of milk, which is achieved by setting the total amount of milk for each son to be the same.
- The total amount of milk for all sons should add up to the total amount of milk produced by all cows.
- Each son should get exactly 9 cows.
- The assignment of cows to sons should be such that the total amount of milk for each son is equal to the sum of the milk produced by the cows assigned to him.

The objective of this problem is to find a distribution of cows among the sons such that each son gets the same amount of milk, which is the total amount of milk produced by all cows divided by the number of sons. In other words, the problem is trying to divide the total amount of milk into equal parts, with each part being the total amount of milk assigned to a son.
