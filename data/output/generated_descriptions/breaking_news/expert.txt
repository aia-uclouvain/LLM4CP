name: Breaking News
description: The problem is about matching four reporters (Corey, Jimmy, Lois, and Perry) with four locations (Bayonne, New Hope, Port Charles, and South Amboy) and four news events (30-pound baby, blimp launching, skyscraper dedication, and beached whale) based on given clues.
variables: $Bayonne, New\_Hope, Port\_Charles, South\_Amboy, baby, blimp, skyscraper, whale$ - These are decision variables representing the location and event assigned to each reporter, where $Bayonne, New\_Hope, Port\_Charles, South\_Amboy$ are the locations and $baby, blimp, skyscraper, whale$ are the events.
constraints: $all\_different(locations) \wedge all\_different(events) \wedge baby \neq South\_Amboy \wedge baby \neq New\_Hope \wedge Jimmy \neq Port\_Charles \wedge (blimp = Lois \wedge skyscraper = Port\_Charles \vee skyscraper = Lois \wedge blimp = Port\_Charles) \wedge South\_Amboy \neq whale \wedge South\_Amboy \neq skyscraper \wedge (bool2int(Bayonne = Corey) + bool2int(Bayonne = whale) \geq 1)$ - These constraints ensure that each location and event is assigned to exactly one reporter, and the given clues are satisfied.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of locations and events to reporters, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
