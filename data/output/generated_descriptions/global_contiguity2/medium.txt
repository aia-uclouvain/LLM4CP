Global Contiguity Problem
The Global Contiguity problem is about enforcing a specific condition on a sequence of binary variables, where all variables assigned to value 1 must appear contiguously. 

The main decision variables in this problem are the array `x` of binary variables, where each `x[i]` represents whether the `i-th` position in the sequence is assigned to 0 or 1.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- all variables in the sequence must be assigned to either 0 or 1, 
- all variables assigned to 1 must appear contiguously, meaning there can be at most one group of consecutive 1's in the sequence, and 
- if there are no 1's in the sequence, the constraint still holds.

The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of the variables, meaning an assignment that meets the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function. The goal is to demonstrate that the sequence can be arranged such that all 1's appear together, or to show that no such arrangement exists.
