name: Shift Scheduling Problem

description: The Shift Scheduling Problem is a constraint programming problem that involves scheduling tasks into shifts. The goal is to assign tasks to shifts such that the difference between the end of the last task of a shift and the origin of the first task of the shift does not exceed a certain maximum range. Two tasks are considered to be in the same shift if they are adjacent, overlap, or are separated by a break that is less than or equal to a certain minimum break time.

variables: $tasks_{i,j}, shifts_i, min\_break, max\_range$ 
These variables represent the following: $tasks_{i,j}$ is a 2D array where $tasks_{i,1}$ is the start time of task $i$ and $tasks_{i,2}$ is the end time of task $i$, $shifts_i$ is the shift assigned to task $i$, $min\_break$ is the minimum break time between tasks in the same shift, and $max\_range$ is the maximum range of a shift.

constraints: $tasks_{i,1} < tasks_{i,2}, min\_break > 0, max\_range \geq 0, max\_range < max\_time, shifts_i = shifts_j \leftrightarrow (tasks_{j,1} - tasks_{i,2} \leq min\_break \wedge tasks_{j,1} - tasks_{i,2} \geq 0 \vee tasks_{i,1} - tasks_{j,2} \leq min\_break \wedge tasks_{i,1} - tasks_{j,2} \geq 0 \vee tasks_{i,2} \geq tasks_{j,1} \wedge tasks_{i,2} \leq tasks_{j,2} \vee tasks_{j,2} \geq tasks_{i,1} \wedge tasks_{j,2} \leq tasks_{i,2}), max\_end - min\_start \leq max\_range$ 
These constraints ensure that each task starts before it ends, the minimum break time is positive, the maximum range is non-negative and less than the maximum time, and tasks are assigned to the same shift if they are adjacent, overlap, or are separated by a break that is less than or equal to the minimum break time. Additionally, the range of each shift is constrained to be less than or equal to the maximum range.

objective: Satisfy 
The objective of this problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
