name: Family Riddle

description: The problem involves two families, each with three girls and three boys. The youngest child in Leo's family is a girl, and in Rene's family, there is a newborn girl. The ages of the children in both families are distinct and range from 0 to 9. The sum of the ages of the girls is equal to the sum of the ages of the boys in each family. The total sum of the ages of all children in both families is 60.

variables: $leo\_boys = (leo\_boys_1, leo\_boys_2, leo\_boys_3), leo\_girls = (leo\_girls_1, leo\_girls_2, leo\_girls_3), rene\_boys = (rene\_boys_1, rene\_boys_2, rene\_boys_3), rene\_girls = (rene\_girls_1, rene\_girls_2, rene\_girls_3)$, where $leo\_boys_i$ and $leo\_girls_i$ represent the ages of the boys and girls in Leo's family, and $rene\_boys_i$ and $rene\_girls_i$ represent the ages of the boys and girls in Rene's family.

constraints: 
$\begin{aligned}
& all\_different(leo\_boys) \wedge all\_different(leo\_girls) \wedge all\_different(rene\_boys) \wedge all\_different(rene\_girls) \\
& \wedge \: minimum(rene\_girls) = 0 \wedge minimum(leo\_girls) < minimum(leo\_boys) \\
& \wedge \: sum(leo\_boys) = sum(leo\_girls) \wedge sum(rene\_boys) = sum(rene\_girls) \\
& \wedge \: sum(leo\_boys \cup leo\_girls \cup rene\_boys \cup rene\_girls) = 60 \\
& \wedge \: increasing(leo\_boys) \wedge increasing(leo\_girls) \wedge increasing(rene\_boys) \wedge increasing(rene\_girls)
\end{aligned}$, 
which represent the constraints that all ages are distinct, the youngest child in each family is a girl, the sum of the ages of the girls equals the sum of the ages of the boys in each family, the total sum of ages is 60, and the ages are increasing within each family.

objective: The objective of the problem is to find the ages of the children in both families that satisfy all the given constraints, with no specific value to minimize or maximize.
