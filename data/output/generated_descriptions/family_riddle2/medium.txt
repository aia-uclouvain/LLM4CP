The Family Riddle problem. 
This problem is about determining the ages of children in two families, each with three girls and three boys, given certain conditions about their ages. 
The main decision variables are the ages of the boys and girls in each family, represented by the arrays `leo_boys`, `leo_girls`, `rene_boys`, and `rene_girls`. 
The key constraints are that all children in each family have different ages, the youngest child in each family is a girl, one family has a newborn girl, the sum of the ages of the girls equals the sum of the ages of the boys in each family, and the total sum of all children's ages is 60. 
The problem's objective is to find all possible age combinations for the children in the two families that satisfy these conditions, with no specific objective function being minimized or maximized, as the problem is a satisfaction problem.
