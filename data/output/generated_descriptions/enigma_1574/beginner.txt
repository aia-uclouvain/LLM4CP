Doubly Square Dates
The problem is about finding specific dates where the numbers representing the day, month, and year can be arranged in two different ways to form perfect squares. 
The main variables are day, month, and year, which represent the parts of a date. 
The basic restrictions are that the day, month, and year must be valid (e.g., day between 1 and 31, month between 1 and 12), and when the numbers are arranged in the two different ways, they must form perfect squares. 
The goal is to find the next two dates after 4 April 2001 that meet these conditions.
