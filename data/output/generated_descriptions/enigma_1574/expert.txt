name: Doubly Square Dates Problem
description: The problem is to find two dates in the 21st century that are doubly square, meaning that when written in the format day.month.year or month.day.year (with two digits for each element), they form a square number, but the square numbers formed by the two different orders are not the same.
variables: $d, m, y, day\_month\_year\_a, month\_day\_year\_a, day\_month\_year, month\_day\_year, y1, y2$ where $d$ is the day of the month, $m$ is the month, $y$ is the year, $day\_month\_year\_a$ and $month\_day\_year\_a$ are arrays representing the day, month, and year in the two different orders, $day\_month\_year$ and $month\_day\_year$ are the numbers formed by concatenating the day, month, and year in the two different orders, and $y1$ and $y2$ are the square roots of these numbers.
constraints: $day = \sum_{i=1}^{2} 10^{2-i} \cdot day\_month\_year\_a[i]$, $month = \sum_{i=3}^{4} 10^{4-i} \cdot day\_month\_year\_a[i]$, $year = \sum_{i=5}^{6} 10^{6-i} \cdot day\_month\_year\_a[i]$, $month = \sum_{i=1}^{2} 10^{2-i} \cdot month\_day\_year\_a[i]$, $day = \sum_{i=3}^{4} 10^{4-i} \cdot month\_day\_year\_a[i]$, $year = \sum_{i=5}^{6} 10^{6-i} \cdot month\_day\_year\_a[i]$, $month\_day\_year = \sum_{i=1}^{6} 10^{6-i} \cdot month\_day\_year\_a[i]$, $day\_month\_year = \sum_{i=1}^{6} 10^{6-i} \cdot day\_month\_year\_a[i]$, $m \neq d$, $y1 > 0$, $y2 > 0$, $y1^2 = month\_day\_year$, $y2^2 = day\_month\_year$ where these constraints ensure that the day, month, and year are correctly extracted from the arrays, that the numbers formed by concatenating the day, month, and year are correctly calculated, and that these numbers are square.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find the values of $y$, $m$, and $d$ that satisfy the given constraints, with no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
