The problem is called the "Global Constraint Arith" problem. 
It's about a set of light switches, where each switch can be either on (1) or off (0), and a master switch that controls all of them. 
The main variables are the individual light switches (x) and the master switch (y). 
The basic restrictions are that the master switch is on only if all the individual light switches are on, and the first two individual switches must be on. 
The goal of the problem is to find a combination of light switch settings that satisfy these rules.
