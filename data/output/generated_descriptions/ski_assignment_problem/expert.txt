name: Ski Assignment Problem
description: The Ski Assignment Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming where the goal is to assign skis to skiers in a way that minimizes the total disparity between the height of the skier and the height of the assigned skis. The disparity is defined as the absolute difference between the height of the skier and the height of the skis.
variables: $x_i, z$ - $x_i$ represents the index of the ski assigned to the $i^{th}$ skier, and $z$ represents the total disparity between the heights of the skiers and their assigned skis. $x_i$ is a decision variable that takes a value from the set of available skis, and $z$ is a variable that depends on the values of $x_i$.
constraints: $x_i \in \{1, 2, ..., num\_skis\}, all\_different(x)$ - The first constraint ensures that each skier is assigned a ski from the available set of skis. The $all\_different(x)$ constraint ensures that each ski is assigned to at most one skier.
objective: Minimize $z = \sum_{i=1}^{num\_skiers} \sum_{j=1}^{num\_skis} (bool2int(x_i = j) * |ski\_heights_j - skier\_heights_i|)$ - The objective is to minimize the total disparity $z$ between the heights of the skiers and their assigned skis.
