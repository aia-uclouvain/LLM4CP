Lights on puzzle 
The problem is about turning on lights in a grid, where each light can affect other lights. 
The main variables are: 
- x, which represents whether a light is turned on or off at a certain position in the grid
- d, which represents the number of times a light is turned on at a certain position
- moves, which represents the total number of times a light is turned on in the grid
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- each light can only be turned on or off
- the number of times a light is turned on must be non-negative
- the total number of times a light is turned on and the number of lights that affect it must follow a specific rule based on the grid's connections
The goal of the problem is to minimize the total number of times a light is turned on in the grid, which is represented by the variable "moves".
