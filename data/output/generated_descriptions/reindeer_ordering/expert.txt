name: Reindeer Ordering Problem
description: The problem is to determine the order in which nine reindeer will pull Santa's sleigh, given a set of rules that specify the relative positions of the reindeer. The rules are based on which reindeer must be in front of or behind others.
variables: $x = [Blitzen, Comet, Cupid, Dancer, Dasher, Donder, Prancer, Rudolph, Vixen]$, $order = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_9]$ where $x_i$ represents the reindeer at position $i$ and $order$ represents the final order of the reindeer. 
constraints: $all\_different(x)$, $inverse(x, order)$, $Blitzen \in\_front\_of \{Donder, Vixen, Dancer\}$, $Donder \behind \{Vixen, Dasher, Prancer\}$, $Rudolph \in\_front\_of \{Donder, Dancer, Dasher\}$, $Vixen \in\_front\_of \{Dancer, Comet\}$, $Dancer \behind \{Donder, Rudolph, Blitzen\}$, $Prancer \in\_front\_of \{Cupid, Donder, Blitzen\}$, $Dasher \in\_front\_of \{Vixen, Dancer, Blitzen\}$, $Donder \behind \{Comet, Cupid\}$, $Cupid \in\_front\_of \{Rudolph, Dancer\}$ where $\behind$ and $\in\_front\_of$ are predicates that represent the relative positions of the reindeer. 
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of reindeer to positions, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
