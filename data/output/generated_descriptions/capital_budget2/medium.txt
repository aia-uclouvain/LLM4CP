Capital Budgeting Problem
The Capital Budgeting Problem is about allocating a limited budget to a set of investment projects to maximize the total return, considering the cash flow requirements and potential returns of each project.

The main decision variables are the array `x`, where `x[i]` represents whether investment `i` is selected (1) or not (0). The `x` variables determine which investments to undertake.

The key constraints are:
- The total cash flow of the selected investments must not exceed the available budget.
- Each investment's cash flow must be non-negative.
- The budget must be non-negative.
Additionally, there are optional constraints that can be applied, such as:
- Limiting the number of investments to two.
- Requiring that if investment 2 is selected, then investment 1 must also be selected.
- Requiring that if investment 2 is selected, then investment 4 must not be selected.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the total return, represented by the variable `z`, which is the sum of the Net Present Values (NPV) of the selected investments. The NPV represents the potential return of each investment. By maximizing `z`, the model aims to find the optimal set of investments that yields the highest total return while satisfying the budget and other constraints.
