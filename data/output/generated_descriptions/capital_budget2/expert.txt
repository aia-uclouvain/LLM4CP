name: Capital Budgeting Problem
description: The Capital Budgeting Problem is a classic problem in operations research and finance that involves selecting a subset of investment projects to undertake, given a limited budget. The goal is to maximize the total net present value (NPV) of the selected projects, subject to the constraint that the total cash outlay does not exceed the available budget. The problem also considers additional constraints, such as limits on the number of projects that can be undertaken or dependencies between specific projects.
variables: $x_i, i = 1, 2, 3, 4$ - These are binary decision variables, where $x_i = 1$ indicates that investment project $i$ is selected, and $x_i = 0$ indicates that it is not.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{4} x_i \cdot cash\_flow_i \leq budget$, $x_i \in \{0, 1\}, i = 1, 2, 3, 4$, $budget \geq 0$, $cash\_flow_i \geq 0, i = 1, 2, 3, 4$ - These constraints ensure that the total cash outlay does not exceed the available budget, that each project is either selected or not, that the budget is non-negative, and that the cash flow for each project is non-negative. Additional constraints, such as $\sum_{i=1}^{4} x_i = 2$, $x_2 = 1 \rightarrow x_1 = 1$, or $x_2 = 1 \rightarrow x_4 = 0$, may also be considered to model specific requirements or dependencies between projects.
objective: Maximize $z = \sum_{i=1}^{4} x_i \cdot npv_i$ - The objective is to maximize the total net present value (NPV) of the selected projects.
