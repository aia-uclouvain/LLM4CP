Collatz Conjecture Problem
The Collatz Conjecture problem is about finding the sequence of numbers generated by iteratively applying a simple transformation to a given starting number, and determining the length of this sequence until it reaches 1.

The main decision variables in this problem are `x`, an array of integers representing the sequence of numbers, and `len`, an integer representing the length of the sequence until it reaches 1. The `x` array represents the sequence of numbers generated by the Collatz transformation, where each element `x[i]` is the result of applying the transformation to the previous element `x[i-1]`. The `len` variable represents the number of steps it takes for the sequence to reach 1.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The sequence starts with the given number `n`.
- Each subsequent number in the sequence is generated by applying the Collatz transformation to the previous number: if the previous number is even, the next number is half of it, and if the previous number is odd, the next number is 3 times the previous number plus 1, divided by 2.
- The sequence ends when it reaches 1.
- The length of the sequence is the number of steps it takes to reach 1.

The objective of this problem is to find the sequence of numbers and its length, given a starting number `n`. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a feasible solution that satisfies the constraints. In other words, the goal is to determine the sequence of numbers generated by the Collatz transformation and the number of steps it takes for the sequence to reach 1, given a starting number `n`.
