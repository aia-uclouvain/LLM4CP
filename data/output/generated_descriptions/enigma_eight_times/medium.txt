The name of the problem is the Enigma puzzle "Eight times" (#1317). 
This problem is about finding a 5-digit number consisting of five different digits, not starting with zero, which when multiplied by 8 produces another 5-digit number consisting of the other 5 digits. 
The main decision variables are x1, x2, x3, x4, x5, and y1, y2, y3, y4, y5, which represent the digits of the original 5-digit number and the digits of the product when the original number is multiplied by 8, respectively. 
The key constraints are that all digits must be different, the first digit of both numbers must be greater than zero, the product of the original number and 8 must be equal to the second 5-digit number, and the sum of the digits of the original number must be greater than the sum of the digits of the product. 
The problem's objective is to find a 5-digit number that satisfies all these constraints, with no specific minimization or maximization goal, but rather to find a solution that meets the given conditions.
