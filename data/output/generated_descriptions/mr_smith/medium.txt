The Mr. Smith Problem
The problem is about determining which members of the Smith family will visit, given certain constraints on their availability and relationships.
The main decision variables are boolean values representing whether each family member will come: Mr_Smith, Mrs_Smith, Matt, John, and Tim. These variables indicate the presence or absence of each individual.
The key constraints are:
- If Mr. Smith comes, his wife will also come.
- At least one of the two sons, Matt or John, will come.
- Either Mrs. Smith or Tim will come, but not both.
- Either both Tim and John will come, or neither will come.
- If Matt comes, then both John and Mr. Smith will also come.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, such that all constraints are met, which is achieved by using the "solve satisfy" statement in the MiniZinc model. In other words, the goal is to determine a valid combination of family members who will visit, given the specified conditions.
