name: Word Design for DNA Computing on Surfaces
description: The problem involves designing a set of DNA words, each consisting of 8 symbols from the set {A, C, G, T}, such that each word has exactly 4 symbols from {C, G}, each pair of words differs in at least 4 positions, and the reverse of each word and the Watson-Crick complement of every other word differ in at least 4 positions.
variables: $x_{i,k}, y_{i,k}$, where $x_{i,k}$ represents the kth letter of the ith word and $y_{i,k}$ represents the kth letter of the Watson-Crick complement of the ith word. $x_{i,k}$ and $y_{i,k}$ are decision variables that take values from the set {0, 1, 2, 3} representing the letters {A, C, G, T} respectively.
constraints: $x_{i,k} + y_{i,k} = 3$, $x_{i,k} \in \{0, 1, 2, 3\}$, $\sum_{k=1}^{8} (x_{i,k} \neq x_{j,k}) \geq 4$, $\sum_{k=1}^{8} (x_{i,8-k} \neq y_{j,k}) \geq 4$, where $i \neq j$. These constraints ensure that each word has a valid Watson-Crick complement, each pair of words differs in at least 4 positions, and the reverse of each word and the Watson-Crick complement of every other word differ in at least 4 positions.
objective: maximize $n$, where $n$ is the number of words in the set.
