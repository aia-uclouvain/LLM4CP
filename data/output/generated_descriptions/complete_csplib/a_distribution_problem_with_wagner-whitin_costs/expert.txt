name: Wagner-Whitin Distribution Problem
description: The Wagner-Whitin Distribution Problem is a classic problem in operations research and management science that involves finding the optimal production and inventory policy for a multi-period, multi-product system with time-varying demand. The problem is defined on a tree-structured network, where each node represents a product or a production facility, and the edges represent the flow of goods between nodes. The goal is to minimize the total cost of production, inventory holding, and ordering over a finite horizon, subject to demand satisfaction and inventory capacity constraints.
variables: $orders_{i,t}, stock_{i,t}$, where $orders_{i,t}$ represents the amount of product ordered at node $i$ in period $t$, and $stock_{i,t}$ represents the inventory level at node $i$ in period $t$. These variables determine the production and inventory policy for each node and period.
constraints: $stock_{i,0} = 0, stock_{i,t} = stock_{i,t-1} + orders_{i,t} - \sum_{m \in children[i]} orders_{m,t}, stock_{i,t} = stock_{i,t-1} + orders_{i,t} - demand_{i,t}$, where the first constraint initializes the inventory level to zero at the beginning of the horizon, the second constraint represents the inventory balance equation for non-leaf nodes, and the third constraint represents the inventory balance equation for leaf nodes. These constraints ensure that the inventory levels are consistent with the ordering and demand satisfaction decisions.
objective: Minimize $\sum_{t=1}^{T} \sum_{i=1}^{N} (holdingCost_i \cdot stock_{i,t} + procCost_i \cdot \mathbb{I}(orders_{i,t} > 0))$, where $holdingCost_i$ is the inventory holding cost per unit at node $i$, $procCost_i$ is the ordering cost at node $i$, and $\mathbb{I}(orders_{i,t} > 0)$ is an indicator function that equals 1 if an order is placed at node $i$ in period $t$ and 0 otherwise. The objective is to minimize the total cost of inventory holding and ordering over the horizon.
