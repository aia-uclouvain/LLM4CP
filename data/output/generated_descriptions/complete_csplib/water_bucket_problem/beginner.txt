Water Bucket Problem
The Water Bucket Problem is about dividing a certain amount of water into two or more buckets using a set of rules. 
The main variables are the amount of water in each bucket at any given time and the number of transfers (or steps) needed to reach the goal.
The basic restrictions are that each bucket has a limited capacity, and water can only be poured from one bucket to another if the source bucket has enough water and the target bucket has enough space. 
The goal is to find the minimum number of transfers required to achieve a specific distribution of water among the buckets.
