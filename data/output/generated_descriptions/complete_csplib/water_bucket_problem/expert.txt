name: Water Buckets Problem
description: The Water Buckets Problem is a classic problem in the field of constraint programming and operations research. It involves a set of buckets with different capacities, and the goal is to find a sequence of pouring operations to transform an initial state into a target state, where the amount of water in each bucket is specified. The problem requires finding the minimum number of pouring operations to achieve the target state.
variables: $x_{i,j}, goal\_step, state_{s,b}, z, actions, states, nbActions$ 
These variables represent the following: $x_{i,j}$ is the amount of water in bucket $j$ at time step $i$, $goal\_step$ is the time step at which the goal state is reached, $state_{s,b}$ is the amount of water in bucket $b$ at time step $s$, $z$ is the number of transfers of water, $actions$ is a sequence of pouring operations, $states$ is a sequence of states, and $nbActions$ is the number of actions.
constraints: $state_{1,b} = start_b, state_{goal\_step,b} = goal_b, \sum_{b \in buckets} state_{s,b} = \sum_{b \in buckets} state_{s+1,b}, \sum_{b \in buckets} (state_{s,b} \neq state_{s+1,b}) = 2, state_{s+1,b1} = 0 \lor state_{s+1,b1} = capacity_{b1} \lor state_{s+1,b2} = 0 \lor state_{s+1,b2} = capacity_{b2}$ 
These constraints ensure that the initial state is set correctly, the goal state is reached, the total amount of water is preserved at each time step, exactly two buckets change at each time step, and after each pouring operation, either the source bucket is empty or the target bucket is full.
objective: minimize $goal\_step$ or $z$ or $nbActions$ 
The objective is to minimize the number of pouring operations, which is represented by the variable $goal\_step$, $z$, or $nbActions$, depending on the formulation.
