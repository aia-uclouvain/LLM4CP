Water Bucket Problem
The Water Bucket Problem is a classic problem in the field of constraint programming. 

It involves a set of buckets with different capacities, and the goal is to find a sequence of pouring operations to achieve a specific distribution of water among the buckets.

The main decision variables in this problem are the amount of water in each bucket at each time step. 

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The amount of water in each bucket cannot exceed its capacity.
- The total amount of water remains constant throughout the process.
- At each time step, exactly two buckets change their water levels.
- After each pouring operation, either the source bucket is empty or the target bucket is full.
- The initial and final states of the buckets are specified.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the number of pouring operations required to reach the target distribution of water. 

In other words, the goal is to find the shortest sequence of pouring operations that transforms the initial state into the final state while satisfying all the constraints.
