**Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem (CVRP)**
The Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves finding the most efficient routes for a fleet of vehicles to visit a set of locations and return to a depot, while not exceeding the capacity of each vehicle.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in CVRP are the routes taken by each vehicle, which are represented as a sequence of locations to visit. Each route starts and ends at the depot, and the sequence of locations determines the order in which they are visited.

**Key Constraints**
The key constraints in CVRP are:
* Each location must be visited exactly once by one of the vehicles.
* The total demand of the locations visited by each vehicle must not exceed the capacity of the vehicle.
* The vehicles must start and end at the depot.
* The total distance traveled by each vehicle is calculated based on the sequence of locations visited.

**Objective**
The objective of CVRP is to minimize the total distance traveled by all vehicles, which is equivalent to minimizing the total cost of the routes. This is achieved by finding the most efficient routes that satisfy all the constraints and minimize the total distance traveled. The problem can be formulated as a minimization problem, where the objective function is the total distance traveled by all vehicles.
