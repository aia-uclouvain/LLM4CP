The problem is the Nurse Scheduling Problem. 
It is about creating schedules for nurses to work shifts at a hospital, ensuring that all shifts are covered and that each nurse works a reasonable number of hours. 
The main decision variables represent which nurse is assigned to which shift, and they can be binary (1 if a nurse is assigned to a shift, 0 otherwise) or integer (the nurse ID assigned to a shift). 
The key constraints include ensuring every shift is covered by at least one nurse, not overworking any nurse by assigning them too many shifts in a row or too many hours in a week, and respecting the availability and qualifications of each nurse. 
The objective is typically to minimize the total number of nurses required to cover all shifts, or to maximize the satisfaction of the nurses based on their preferences for certain shifts or days off.
