name: Fractions Problem
description: The problem is to find distinct non-zero digits such that the equation A/BC + D/EF + G/HI = 1 holds, where A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, and I are the digits.
variables: A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, D1, D2, D3, Vars = [A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I], where A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, and I are decision variables representing the digits, D1, D2, and D3 are auxiliary variables representing the values of BC, EF, and HI respectively, and Vars is an array of the digit variables.
constraints: all_different(Vars) ∧ D1 = 10*B+C ∧ D2 = 10*E+F ∧ D3 = 10*H+I ∧ A*D2*D3 + D*D1*D3 + G*D1*D2 = D1*D2*D3 ∧ A*D2 ≥ D*D1 ∧ D*D3 ≥ G*D2 ∧ 3*A ≥ D1 ∧ 3*G ≤ D2, which are constraints that ensure the digits are distinct, calculate the values of D1, D2, and D3, satisfy the equation, break symmetries, and add redundant constraints to help the solver.
objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the objective is to find an assignment of values to the variables that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
