Low Autocorrelation Binary Sequences
The problem is about finding a special sequence of numbers that has a low autocorrelation. In everyday terms, imagine a sequence of coins where each coin can either be heads or tails, represented by -1 or 1. The goal is to arrange these coins in a way that the similarity between the sequence and its shifted versions is as low as possible.

The main variables are:
- `x` or `Bits`: an array of variables representing the sequence of coins, where each variable can be either -1 (tails) or 1 (heads).
- `y` or `products`: an array of variables representing the products of pairs of coins in the sequence, used to calculate the autocorrelation.
- `c` or `Correlations`: an array of variables representing the autocorrelation values at different shifts.

The basic restrictions or rules are:
- Each coin in the sequence can only be -1 or 1.
- The autocorrelation values are calculated based on the products of pairs of coins in the sequence.
- The sequence should have a low autocorrelation, meaning that the similarity between the sequence and its shifted versions should be minimized.

The goal of the problem is to minimize the sum of the squares of the autocorrelation values. In other words, the goal is to find a sequence of coins that has the lowest possible autocorrelation, making it as dissimilar as possible from its shifted versions.
