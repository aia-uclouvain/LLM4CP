name: Social Golfer Problem
description: The Social Golfer problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves scheduling golf games between groups of golfers over a period of weeks. The problem requires that each week, all golfers must be assigned to a different game, and each game must have a different set of golfers. Additionally, each golfer must play with a different group of golfers each week.
variables: $r_{i,w}, e_{i,w}, rg_{i,w}, eg_{i,w}$, where $r_{i,w}$ represents the visitor (golfer) assigned to the $i^{th}$ visitee (game) at week $w$, $e_{i,w}$ represents the visitee (game) assigned to the $i^{th}$ visitor (golfer) at week $w$, $rg_{i,w}$ represents the visitor group (golfer group) assigned to the $i^{th}$ visitee (game) at week $w$, and $eg_{i,w}$ represents the visitee group (game group) assigned to the $i^{th}$ visitor (golfer) at week $w$. These variables are used to model the assignments of golfers to games and groups over the weeks.
constraints: $\forall w: Alldifferent(r_{:,w}), \forall w: Alldifferent(e_{:,w}), \forall i: Alldifferent(rg_{i,:}), \forall i: Alldifferent(eg_{i,:}), \forall w: Channel(r_{:,w}, e_{:,w}), (rg_{i,w}, r_{i,w}) \in vr\_table, (eg_{i,w}, e_{i,w}) \in ve\_table$, which represent the constraints that each week, all visitors must be different, all visitees must be different, the visitor groups must be different for each visitee, the visitee groups must be different for each visitor, the channeling constraints between $r$ and $e$, and the linking constraints between visitors and their groups, and between visitees and their groups.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible assignment of golfers to games and groups over the weeks that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
