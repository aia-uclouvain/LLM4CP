name: Peaceably Co-existing Armies of Queens
description: The problem involves placing two armies of queens on a chessboard of size n x n, such that the queens from different armies do not attack each other, and the number of queens in each army is equal. The goal is to maximize the number of queens in each army.
variables: $b_{i,j}, w_{i,j}, x_{i,j}, nb, nw$ 
These variables represent the placement of queens on the board: $b_{i,j}$ and $w_{i,j}$ are binary variables indicating the presence of a black or white queen at position (i, j), $x_{i,j}$ is a variable that can take values 0, 1, or 2, indicating the absence of a queen, the presence of a black queen, or the presence of a white queen, respectively, and $nb$ and $nw$ are variables representing the number of black and white queens.
constraints: $|black| = |white|, \forall (rb,cb) \in black, \forall (rw,cw) \in white: (rb \neq rw) \land (cb \neq cw) \land (|rw - rb| \neq |cw - cb|), b_{i,j} + w_{i,j} \leq 1, x_{i,j} + x_{k,l} \neq 3$ if $i = k$ or $j = l$ or $|i - k| = |j - l|$
These constraints ensure that the number of black and white queens is equal, that no two queens from different armies attack each other, and that each cell on the board can contain at most one queen.
objective: Maximize $|black| + |white|$ or equivalently, maximize $nb$ or $nw$ 
The objective is to maximize the total number of queens on the board, which is equivalent to maximizing the number of queens in one of the armies, since the number of queens in each army is equal.
