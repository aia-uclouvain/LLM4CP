name: Balanced Academic Curriculum Problem
description: The Balanced Academic Curriculum Problem is a scheduling problem where a set of courses with prerequisites must be assigned to a limited number of periods, while satisfying constraints on the number of courses and credits per period, and minimizing the maximum load per period.
variables: $s_c, x_{pi}, load_p, objective$ - These variables represent the period assigned to each course ($s_c$), whether a course is assigned to a period ($x_{pi}$), the total load for each period ($load_p$), and the optimization target ($objective$), which is the maximum load per period.
constraints: $\forall p \in periods: \sum_{i \in courses} x_{pi} \geq courses\_per\_period\_lb \land \sum_{i \in courses} x_{pi} \leq courses\_per\_period\_ub$, $\forall p \in periods: load_p = \sum_{c \in courses} x_{pc} \* course\_load_c \land load_p \geq load\_per\_period\_lb \land load_p \leq load\_per\_period\_ub$, $prerequisite(a, b) \rightarrow course\_period_b < course\_period_a$ - These constraints ensure that each period has a valid number of courses, the total load per period is within bounds, and that courses are scheduled after their prerequisites.
objective: minimize $objective$ (the maximum load per period)
