name: Killer Sudoku
description: Killer Sudoku is a puzzle that combines elements of Sudoku and Kakuro, where the goal is to fill a 9x9 grid with numbers from 1 to 9, such that each row, column, and 3x3 sub-grid contains each number exactly once, and the sum of all numbers in a "cage" (a group of cells) matches the given sum for that cage.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ and $j$ are the row and column indices, respectively, and $x_{i,j}$ represents the value in the cell at position $(i,j)$ in the grid. These variables are the decision variables, and their values are the numbers from 1 to 9 that will be placed in the grid.
constraints: 
$\forall i \in \{1,2,...,9\}: \forall j \in \{1,2,...,9\}: x_{i,j} \in \{1,2,...,9\}$, 
$\forall i \in \{1,2,...,9\}: \forall j,k \in \{1,2,...,9\}: x_{i,j} \neq x_{i,k}$ if $j \neq k$, 
$\forall j \in \{1,2,...,9\}: \forall i,k \in \{1,2,...,9\}: x_{i,j} \neq x_{k,j}$ if $i \neq k$, 
$\forall i,j \in \{1,2,3\}: \forall r \in \{1,2,3\}: \forall c \in \{1,2,3\}: x_{(i-1)*3+r, (j-1)*3+c} \in \{1,2,...,9\}$, 
$\forall p \in \{1,2,...,29\}: \sum_{k=1}^{4} x_{P[p,2k-1],P[p,2k]} = P[p,9]$, 
where $P$ is a given matrix representing the cages and their corresponding sums. These constraints ensure that each row, column, and 3x3 sub-grid contains each number exactly once, and the sum of all numbers in each cage matches the given sum.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, as there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
