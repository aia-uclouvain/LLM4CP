Traveling Tournament Problem with Predefined Venues
The Traveling Tournament Problem with Predefined Venues is a classic constraint programming problem that involves scheduling a tournament between multiple teams while minimizing the total travel distance. 

The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `opponent[i,k]`: the opponent of team `i` in round `k`
- `venue[i,k]`: whether team `i` plays at home (`1`) or away (`2`) in round `k`
- `travel[i,k]`: the distance traveled by team `i` to play in round `k` (including traveling back home after the last round)

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each team plays every other team exactly once
- The venue of each game is predefined (i.e., which team plays at home and which team plays away)
- A team cannot play against itself
- If team `i` plays team `j` in round `k`, then team `j` plays team `i` in round `k`
- Each team has at most 3 consecutive home games and at most 3 consecutive away games
- The total travel distance is minimized

The objective of this problem is to minimize the total travel distance of all teams. This is achieved by finding a schedule that satisfies all the constraints while minimizing the sum of the distances traveled by each team. The distance traveled by a team between two rounds depends on the venues of the current and next rounds. If a team plays at home in both rounds, it does not travel. If it plays away in both rounds, it travels from the previous opponent's venue to the next opponent's venue. If it plays at home in one round and away in the next, it travels from its home venue to the opponent's venue or vice versa.
