Traveling Tournament Problem with Predefined Venues
The problem is about scheduling a tournament where several teams play against each other in a series of rounds. Each team has to play against every other team, and the venue of each game (home or away) has already been decided. The goal is to find a schedule that minimizes the total distance traveled by all teams.

The main variables are:
- opponent: which team each team plays against in each round
- venue: whether each team plays at home or away in each round
- travel: the distance each team travels in each round
- distance: the distance between two teams

The basic restrictions or rules are:
- each team plays against every other team exactly once
- a team cannot play against itself
- if team A plays against team B, then team B plays against team A
- each team can have at most 3 consecutive home games and at most 3 consecutive away games
- the venue of each game has already been decided

The goal is to minimize the total distance traveled by all teams.
