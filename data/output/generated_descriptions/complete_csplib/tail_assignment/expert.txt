name: Tail Assignment Problem
description: The Tail Assignment Problem is a classical problem in constraint programming that involves assigning a sequence of flights to each aircraft in a fleet, subject to various constraints such as maintenance requirements, flight networks, and aircraft capabilities. The goal is to find a feasible assignment of flights to aircraft that satisfies all the given constraints.
variables: $route: function (total) Plane --> function int(1..n_flights) --> Flight$, which represents the route for each aircraft, i.e., the sequence of flights assigned to each aircraft.
constraints: 
- $forAll s in range(route) . forAll i in defined(s) . i=1 \/ (i-1) in defined(s)$: ensures that all elements of the range of the function 'route' are valid sequences.
- $(carry_in(p), route(p)(1)) in network$: ensures that the route for each aircraft begins with a flight that may follow on from the last flight that it made.
- $tuple (a) in can_fly(p,_)$: ensures that each aircraft is able to make the flights on its route.
- $(s(i),s(i+1)) in network$: ensures that flights are made in the correct order.
- $exists p : Plane . f in range(route(p))$: ensures that every flight is made exactly once.
- $|range(route(p1)) intersect range(route(p2))| = 0$: ensures that no two aircraft can make the same flight.
- $exists j : int(i..i+maint_freq) . s(j) in maint$: ensures that every aircraft visits a maintenance depot frequently enough.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
