The problem is the Nurse Scheduling Problem. 
It is about creating schedules for nurses to work shifts at a hospital, taking into account various constraints such as the number of nurses required for each shift, the maximum number of hours a nurse can work, and the minimum number of days off between shifts. 
The main decision variables represent whether a nurse is assigned to work a particular shift, and they are typically binary variables (0 or 1) where 1 indicates the nurse is working the shift and 0 indicates they are not. 
The key constraints include ensuring that each shift has the required number of nurses, that no nurse works more hours than allowed, and that each nurse gets the required days off. Other constraints might limit the number of consecutive shifts a nurse can work or ensure that a nurse is not scheduled to work a shift that immediately follows a night shift. 
The objective of the problem is typically to minimize the total cost of the schedule, which might include the cost of hiring temporary nurses, the cost of overtime, or the cost of violating certain constraints, such as not giving a nurse enough days off. Alternatively, the objective might be to maximize the satisfaction of the nurses with their schedules, which could be measured by the number of requests that are fulfilled or the fairness of the schedule.
