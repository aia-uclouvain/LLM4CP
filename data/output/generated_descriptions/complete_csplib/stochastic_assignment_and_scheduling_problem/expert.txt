name: Stochastic General Assignment Problem

description: The Stochastic General Assignment Problem is a complex scheduling problem that involves assigning tasks to machines and scheduling them to minimize the expected makespan. The problem is stochastic because the processing times of the tasks are uncertain and are given as a set of possible scenarios. The problem has two stages: in the first stage, tasks are assigned to machines, and in the second stage, the tasks are scheduled on their assigned machines based on the observed processing times. The goal is to find an assignment and schedule that minimizes the expected makespan.

variables: $start_{s,t}, dur_{s,t}, b_o$ 
These are the decision variables of the problem, where $start_{s,t}$ is the start time of task $t$ in scenario $s$, $dur_{s,t}$ is the duration of task $t$ in scenario $s$, and $b_o$ is a binary variable indicating whether optional task $o$ is executed.

constraints: 
$\forall s \in SCENARIOS, \forall j \in Jobs, \forall i \in tasks[j] (i < last\_task[j]) : start_{s,i} + dur_{s,i} \leq start_{s,i+1}$, 
$\forall o \in OptTs, s \in SCENARIOS : b_o \rightarrow dur_{s,t} = optt\_dur_{s,o}$, 
$\forall t \in Tasks (card(optts[t]) > 1) : \sum_{o \in optts[t]} b_o \leq 1 \land \exists o \in optts[t] (b_o)$, 
$\forall m \in Mach, s \in SCENARIOS : cumulative([start_{s,optt\_task[o]} | o \in MTasks], [optt\_dur_{s,o} | o \in MTasks], [b_o | o \in MTasks], 1)$, 
$\forall s \in SCENARIOS, \forall j \in Jobs : start_{s,last\_task[j]} + dur_{s,last\_task[j]} \leq de\_objective_s$ 
These constraints ensure that the tasks are scheduled according to their precedence relations, that the duration of each task is consistent with the observed processing times, that at most one optional task is executed for each task, that the cumulative resource constraints are satisfied, and that the makespan is calculated correctly.

objective: minimize $\sum_{s \in SCENARIOS} weights_s \* de\_objective_s$ 
The objective of the problem is to minimize the expected makespan, which is calculated as the weighted sum of the makespan in each scenario.
