name: Round Robin Tournament Scheduling
description: The problem involves scheduling a round-robin tournament for a given number of teams, where each team plays every other team exactly once, and the schedule is divided into weeks and periods. The goal is to find a schedule that satisfies certain constraints, such as each team playing at most two times in each period, and all matches being different.
variables: $x_{w,p}, y_{w,p}, m_{w,p}$, where $x_{w,p}$ and $y_{w,p}$ represent the teams playing in the match at week $w$ and period $p$, and $m_{w,p}$ represents the match number at week $w$ and period $p$. These variables determine the schedule of the tournament.
constraints: $\forall t \in T, \forall w \in W, \exists p \in P, (t \in \{x_{w,p}, y_{w,p}\})$, $\forall t \in T, \forall p \in P, (\sum_{w \in W} (t \in \{x_{w,p}, y_{w,p}\}) \leq 2)$, $\forall t_1, t_2 \in T, (t_1 \neq t_2 \rightarrow |\{(w,p) \in W \times P | \{x_{w,p}, y_{w,p}\} = \{t_1, t_2\}\}| = 1)$, where $T$ is the set of teams, $W$ is the set of weeks, and $P$ is the set of periods. These constraints ensure that each team plays every other team exactly once, each team plays at most two times in each period, and all matches are different.
objective: The problem does not have an explicit objective function, as it is a feasibility problem, where the goal is to find a schedule that satisfies all the constraints.
