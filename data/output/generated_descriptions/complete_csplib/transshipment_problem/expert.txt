name: Multi-Echelon Supply Chain Problem
description: The problem involves managing a supply chain with multiple warehouses, transshipment points, and customers. Each warehouse has a limited stock, and each customer has a certain demand. The goal is to transport items from warehouses to customers through transshipment points while minimizing the total transportation cost.
variables: $amountWT_{w,t}, amountTC_{t,c}$, where $amountWT_{w,t}$ represents the amount of items transported from warehouse $w$ to transshipment point $t$, and $amountTC_{t,c}$ represents the amount of items transported from transshipment point $t$ to customer $c$. These variables determine the flow of items through the supply chain.
constraints: $\sum_{w} amountWT_{w,t} = \sum_{c} amountTC_{t,c} \forall t$, $\sum_{t} amountTC_{t,c} = demand_c \forall c$, $\sum_{t} amountWT_{w,t} \leq stock_w \forall w$, where the first constraint ensures that the flow of items into a transshipment point equals the flow out, the second constraint ensures that the demand of each customer is met, and the third constraint ensures that the stock of each warehouse is not exceeded.
objective: The objective is to minimize the total transportation cost, $totalCost = \sum_{w,t} costWT_{w,t} \cdot amountWT_{w,t} + \sum_{t,c} costTC_{t,c} \cdot amountTC_{t,c}$, which is the sum of the costs of transporting items from warehouses to transshipment points and from transshipment points to customers.
