name: Bookshelves Problem
description: The Bookshelves Problem is a combinatorial optimization problem that involves cutting planks of wood into pieces to build bookshelves. The goal is to maximize the total width of the shelves while satisfying various constraints such as the length of the planks, the thickness of the planks, the vertical gap between shelves, and the maximum width of the shelves.
variables: $pieces, pfrom$ - $pieces$ is a matrix that stores the length of each piece, and $pfrom$ is a matrix that stores the index of the plank from which each piece is cut. These decision variables determine how the planks are cut and assembled into bookshelves.
constraints: $pieces[1] = pieces[2], pieces[i] = pieces[3] \lor pieces[i] = 0 \forall i > 3, \sum_{i} pieces[i] \cdot (pfrom[i] = x) + CUTCOST \cdot (\sum_{i} (pieces[i] > 0) \cdot (pfrom[i] = x) - 1) \leq lengths[x] \forall x, pieces[1] \geq \sum_{i > 2} (pieces[i] > 0) \cdot (thickness + vertical\_gap)$ - These constraints ensure that the first two pieces are equal (uprights), all other pieces are of equal length (shelves) or zero, each plank is not overused, and each shelf has enough vertical room.
objective: maximize $\sum_{i} pieces[i] - (pieces[1] + pieces[2])$ - The objective is to maximize the total width of the shelves, which is the sum of the lengths of all pieces minus the lengths of the two uprights. In the satisfaction version, the objective is to find a solution that satisfies the constraint $\sum_{i} pieces[i] - (pieces[1] + pieces[2]) = width$.
