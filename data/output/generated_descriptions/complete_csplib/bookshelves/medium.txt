**Cutting Stock Problem**
The Cutting Stock Problem is a classic problem in operations research and constraint programming. It involves cutting a set of planks of given lengths into smaller pieces to maximize the total width of the shelves that can be constructed from these pieces.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `pieces`: a matrix representing the length of each piece cut from the planks. The first two pieces are the uprights, and the rest are the shelves.
- `pfrom`: a matrix representing the index of the plank from which each piece is cut.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- Each plank can be cut into multiple pieces, but the total length of the pieces cut from a plank cannot exceed the length of the plank, taking into account a fixed cutting loss for each cut.
- The first two pieces (uprights) must be of equal length.
- All other pieces (shelves) must be of equal length.
- The uprights must be at least as long as the total vertical space required for the shelves, including the thickness of the planks and the vertical gap between the shelves.
- If a maximum width is specified, the width of the shelves cannot exceed this value.
- If a minimum number of shelves is specified, at least this number of shelves must be constructed.

**Objective**
The objective of the problem is to maximize the total width of the shelves that can be constructed from the given planks, minus the width of the two uprights. In the satisfaction version of the problem, the goal is to find a solution that satisfies a given total width constraint.
