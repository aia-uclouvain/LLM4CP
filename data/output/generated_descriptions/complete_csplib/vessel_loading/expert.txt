name: Vessel Loading
description: The Vessel Loading problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves loading containers of different sizes and classes onto a ship's deck, subject to certain constraints such as the deck's dimensions, the containers' dimensions, and minimum separation requirements between containers of different classes.
variables: $x_i, y_i, w_i, h_i, r_i$ for $i = 1, \ldots, n$, where $x_i$ and $y_i$ are the x and y coordinates of the bottom left corner of container $i$, $w_i$ and $h_i$ are the width and height of container $i$, and $r_i$ is a binary variable indicating whether container $i$ is rotated by 90 degrees. These variables represent the position, size, and orientation of each container on the deck.
constraints: $\begin{aligned}
&x_i + w_i \leq \text{deck width}, \\
&y_i + h_i \leq \text{deck height}, \\
&(r_i, w_i, h_i) \in \{(0, \text{width}_i, \text{height}_i), (1, \text{height}_i, \text{width}_i)\}, \\
&\text{no overlap between containers}, \\
&\text{minimum separation between containers of different classes}
\end{aligned}$, where the constraints ensure that each container is within the deck's boundaries, its size and orientation are consistent, containers do not overlap, and the minimum separation requirements between containers of different classes are met.
objective: The objective of the Vessel Loading problem is to find a feasible loading plan that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, the goal is to determine whether a given set of containers can be loaded onto the deck subject to the constraints.
