Vessel Loading problem
The Vessel Loading problem is about packing containers of different sizes and classes onto a ship's deck in a way that meets certain rules. 
The main variables are the positions and orientations of the containers on the deck. 
The basic restrictions are that each container must be placed within the deck's boundaries, containers cannot overlap, and certain classes of containers must be separated by a minimum distance. 
The goal of the problem is to find a valid arrangement of containers on the deck that satisfies all the rules.
