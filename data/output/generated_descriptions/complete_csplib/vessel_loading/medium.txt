Vessel Loading Problem
The Vessel Loading Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves loading containers of different sizes and classes onto a ship's deck while satisfying various constraints.

The problem revolves around finding the optimal placement of containers on the deck, considering factors such as container size, class, and separation requirements. The goal is to pack the containers efficiently while ensuring that the deck's capacity is not exceeded and that the separation constraints between different classes of containers are met.

The main decision variables in this problem are the positions (x and y coordinates) and orientations (0 or 90 degrees) of the containers on the deck. These variables determine how the containers are arranged and whether they are rotated to fit the available space.

The key constraints in the Vessel Loading Problem can be described in plain language as follows:
- Each container must be placed within the deck's boundaries.
- Containers cannot overlap with each other.
- The separation requirements between different classes of containers must be respected, meaning that containers of certain classes must be kept at a minimum distance from each other in either the horizontal or vertical direction.
- Containers can be rotated by 90 degrees to fit the available space, but their original dimensions (width and length) must be respected.

The objective of the Vessel Loading Problem is not explicitly stated in terms of minimization or maximization, but the implicit goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints, allowing for the efficient loading of containers onto the ship's deck. In other words, the problem aims to determine whether it is possible to load all the containers while meeting the specified requirements, rather than optimizing a specific objective function.
