name: N-Queens Problem
description: The N-Queens problem is a classic problem in the field of computer science and chess, where the goal is to place N queens on an NxN chessboard such that no two queens attack each other, meaning no two queens are in the same row, column, or diagonal.
variables: $q_1, q_2, ..., q_n$, where $q_i$ represents the column where the $i^{th}$ queen is placed, and $q_i$ is a decision variable that takes values from 1 to $n$. 
constraints: $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}, i < j: q_i \neq q_j \land q_i + i \neq q_j + j \land q_i - i \neq q_j - j$, which ensures that no two queens are in the same column or diagonal.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution, i.e., a placement of $n$ queens on the board such that all the constraints are satisfied, and there is no objective function to minimize or maximize.
