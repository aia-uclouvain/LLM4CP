The N-Queens problem.
The problem is about placing a certain number of queens on a square chessboard in such a way that no two queens attack each other. 
The main variables are the positions of the queens on the board, which can be represented by an array of integers, where each integer corresponds to the column where a queen is placed in a particular row.
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are that no two queens can be in the same column, and no two queens can be on the same diagonal. 
The goal of the problem is to find all possible configurations of the board where the queens do not attack each other.
