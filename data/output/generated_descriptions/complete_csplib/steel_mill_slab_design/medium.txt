Steel Mill Slab Design Problem
The Steel Mill Slab Design Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves designing the production of steel slabs to meet customer orders while minimizing waste. 
The main decision variables are the assignment of orders to slabs (sb) and the load (ld) and loss (ls) of each slab. 
The key constraints are: 
- each order is assigned to exactly one slab, 
- the total size of orders assigned to a slab does not exceed its capacity, 
- the loss of each slab is calculated based on its load, 
- and no more than two colors are assigned to each slab. 
The objective of the problem is to minimize the total loss of all slabs, which represents the amount of wasted steel.
