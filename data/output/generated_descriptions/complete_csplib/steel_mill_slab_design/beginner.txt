Steel Mill Slab Design Problem
The problem is about a steel mill that needs to cut steel slabs to fulfill customer orders. Each order requires a certain amount of steel, and the steel mill has a limited number of slabs with different capacities. The goal is to assign the orders to the slabs in a way that minimizes the amount of wasted steel.

The main variables are:
- Orders: the customer orders that need to be fulfilled
- Slabs: the steel slabs with different capacities
- Load of each slab: the total amount of steel assigned to each slab
- Loss of each slab: the amount of wasted steel for each slab
- Assignment of orders to slabs: which order is assigned to which slab

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- Each order can only be assigned to one slab
- The total amount of steel assigned to each slab cannot exceed its capacity
- Each slab can have at most two different colors (orders with different colors are considered distinct)
- The total amount of steel assigned to all slabs must equal the total amount of steel required by all orders

The goal of the problem is to minimize the total amount of wasted steel (loss) by finding the optimal assignment of orders to slabs.
