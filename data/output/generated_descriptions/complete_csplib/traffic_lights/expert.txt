name: Traffic Lights Problem
description: The Traffic Lights Problem is a constraint satisfaction problem that involves finding a consistent configuration of traffic lights at a four-way junction. The problem consists of eight traffic lights, four for vehicles and four for pedestrians, each with specific color options. The goal is to determine all possible globally consistent configurations of the traffic lights.
variables: $V_1, V_2, V_3, V_4, P_1, P_2, P_3, P_4$ where $V_i$ represents the color of the traffic light for vehicles at junction $i$ and can take values from the set $\{r, ry, g, y\}$ (red, red-yellow, green, and yellow), and $P_i$ represents the color of the traffic light for pedestrians at junction $i$ and can take values from the set $\{r, g\}$ (red and green).
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}, \exists a \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$ such that $(V_i, P_i, V_{i+1 \mod 4}, P_{i+1 \mod 4}) = (allowed_a[1], allowed_a[2], allowed_a[3], allowed_a[4])$ where $allowed = \{(r,r,g,g), (ry,r,y,r), (g,g,r,r), (y,r,ry,r)\}$. These constraints ensure that the traffic light configurations at adjacent junctions are consistent with the allowed combinations.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find all possible configurations of the traffic lights that satisfy the given constraints, with no specific value to minimize or maximize.
