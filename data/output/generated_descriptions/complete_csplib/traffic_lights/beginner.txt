Traffic Lights Problem
The problem is about finding the possible configurations of traffic lights at a four-way junction. 
There are eight traffic lights in total, with four for vehicles and four for pedestrians. 
The vehicle traffic lights can be red, red-yellow, green, or yellow, while the pedestrian traffic lights can only be red or green.

The main variables are:
- V1 to V4: the colors of the vehicle traffic lights
- P1 to P4: the colors of the pedestrian traffic lights

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- The traffic lights must follow certain allowed combinations, which are (r,r,g,g), (ry,r,y,r), (g,g,r,r), and (y,r,ry,r).
- The combinations must be applied to each pair of adjacent traffic lights (i.e., (Vi, Pi, Vj, Pj) for 1<=i<=4, j=(1+i)mod 4).

The goal of the problem is to find all possible assignments of colors to the traffic lights that satisfy the given constraints.
