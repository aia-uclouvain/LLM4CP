name: Social Golfer Problem
description: The Social Golfer Problem is a scheduling problem where a group of golfers need to be scheduled to play in groups of a certain size over a number of weeks, such that no two golfers play together more than once.
variables: $g_{w,p}, x_{w,g,p}, tw_{p1,p2,w}, twg_{p1,p2,w,g}, Assign_{G,W}, round\_group\_golfers_{r,g}, round\_place\_golfer_{r,p}, golfer\_golfer\_round_{a,b}, round\_group\_i\_golfer_{r,g,i}, assign_{g,w}$ 
These are decision variables that represent the group assignments for each golfer in each week. $g_{w,p}$ represents the group that golfer $p$ is assigned to in week $w$. $x_{w,g,p}$ represents whether golfer $p$ is assigned to group $g$ in week $w$. $tw_{p1,p2,w}$ and $twg_{p1,p2,w,g}$ represent whether golfers $p1$ and $p2$ play together in week $w$ and group $g$ respectively. $Assign_{G,W}$ represents the group that golfer $G$ is assigned to in week $W$. $round\_group\_golfers_{r,g}$ represents the golfers assigned to group $g$ in round $r$. $round\_place\_golfer_{r,p}$ represents the golfer assigned to place $p$ in round $r$. $golfer\_golfer\_round_{a,b}$ represents the round in which golfers $a$ and $b$ play together. $round\_group\_i\_golfer_{r,g,i}$ represents the golfer assigned to group $g$ and place $i$ in round $r$. $assign_{g,w}$ represents the group that golfer $g$ is assigned to in week $w$.
constraints: $\sum_{g=1}^{nGroups} x_{w,g,p} = 1, \sum_{p=1}^{nPlayers} x_{w,g,p} = size, tw_{p1,p2,w} = \sum_{g=1}^{nGroups} twg_{p1,p2,w,g}, \sum_{w=1}^{nWeeks} tw_{p1,p2,w} \leq 1, card(round\_group\_golfers_{r,g}) = n\_per\_group, alldifferent(g in groups, i in group) (round\_group\_i\_golfer_{r,g,i}), sum (r in rounds, g in groups, i, j in group where i  j) (bool2int(round\_group\_i\_golfer_{r,g,i} = a /\ round\_group\_i\_golfer_{r,g,j} = b)) = 1$ 
These constraints ensure that each golfer is assigned to exactly one group in each week, each group has exactly the right number of players, and no two golfers play together more than once. The constraints also ensure that the groups are disjoint and that each pair of golfers plays together at most once.
objective: satisfy
