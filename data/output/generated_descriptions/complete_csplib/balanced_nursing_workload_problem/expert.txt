name: Nurse Workload Problem
description: The Nurse Workload Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves assigning patients to nurses in a way that minimizes the total workload of the nurses while ensuring that each nurse has a reasonable workload and each patient is assigned to a nurse. The problem takes into account the demand of each patient and the capacity of each nurse.
variables: $p = (p_1, p_2, ..., p_{nPatients})$, $w = (w_1, w_2, ..., w_{nNurses})$ 
These are decision variables where $p_i$ represents the nurse assigned to the $i^{th}$ patient and $w_k$ represents the workload of the $k^{th}$ nurse.
constraints: $\forall k \in [1, nNurses]: minPatientsPerNurse \leq |\{i: p_i = k\}| \leq maxPatientsPerNurse$, 
$\forall i, j \in [1, nPatients]: i \neq j \land patients[i][0] = patients[j][0] \rightarrow p_i \neq p_j$, 
$\forall k \in [1, nNurses]: w_k = \sum_{i=1}^{nPatients} patients[i][1] \cdot (p_i = k)$, 
$\forall z \in [1, nZones]: p_z = z$, 
$w_1 \leq w_2 \leq ... \leq w_{nNurses}$
These constraints ensure that each nurse has a workload within the allowed range, patients from the same zone are not assigned to the same nurse, the workload of each nurse is calculated correctly, the first patient in each zone is assigned to the corresponding nurse, and the workloads of the nurses are in non-decreasing order.
objective: minimize $\sum_{k=1}^{nNurses} w_k^2$ 
The objective is to minimize the sum of the squares of the workloads of all nurses, which represents the total workload of the nurses.
