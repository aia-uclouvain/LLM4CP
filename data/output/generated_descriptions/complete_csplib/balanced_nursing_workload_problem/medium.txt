The Nurse Workload Problem
The problem is about assigning patients to nurses in a way that balances the workload of each nurse, while ensuring that each patient is assigned to a nurse and that the workload of each nurse is within a specified range.

The main decision variables are `p` and `w`, where `p[i]` represents the nurse assigned to the `i-th` patient, and `w[k]` represents the workload of the `k-th` nurse.

The key constraints are:
- Each nurse must be assigned a number of patients within a specified range (between `minPatientsPerNurse` and `maxPatientsPerNurse`).
- Patients from the same zone must be assigned to different nurses.
- The workload of each nurse is calculated as the sum of the demands of the patients assigned to that nurse.
- The workload of each nurse must be within a specified range (between `lb` and `maxWorkloadPerNurse`).
- The workload of the nurses must be in increasing order, which is a symmetry-breaking constraint to reduce the search space.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the sum of the squares of the workloads of all nurses, which represents the overall imbalance of the workload distribution among nurses.
