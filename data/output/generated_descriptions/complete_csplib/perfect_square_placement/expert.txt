name: Perfect Square Placement
description: The problem involves placing a set of squares of different sizes into a larger square container in such a way that the squares do not overlap and the entire container is filled. The goal is to find a placement that satisfies these conditions.
variables: $x_i, y_i$ for $i = 1, 2, ..., n$, where $x_i$ and $y_i$ are the x and y coordinates of the bottom left corner of the $i^{th}$ square. These variables represent the position of each square in the container.
constraints: $\forall i: 0 \leq x_i \leq N - s_i$ and $0 \leq y_i \leq N - s_i$, where $N$ is the size of the container and $s_i$ is the size of the $i^{th}$ square. Additionally, $\forall i, j: (x_i + s_i \leq x_j \lor x_j + s_j \leq x_i \lor y_i + s_i \leq y_j \lor y_j + s_j \leq y_i)$, which ensures that the squares do not overlap. The constraint $\sum_{i=1}^{n} s_i^2 = N^2$ ensures that the entire container is filled.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, as there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
