Perfect Square Placement problem
The Perfect Square Placement problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves placing a set of squares of different sizes into a larger square container. 
The main decision variables in this problem are the x and y coordinates of the bottom-left corner of each square. 
The key constraints in this problem are that each square must be placed within the container, and no two squares can overlap. 
The objective of the problem is to find a placement of all the squares within the container such that no two squares overlap and all squares are placed within the container.
