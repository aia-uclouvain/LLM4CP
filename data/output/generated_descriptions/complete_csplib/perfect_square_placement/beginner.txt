Perfect Squares Problem
The problem is about packing squares of different sizes into a larger square without overlapping.
The main variables are the x and y coordinates of the bottom-left corner of each square, and the size of each square.
The basic restrictions are that each square must be fully contained within the larger square, and no two squares can overlap.
The goal is to find a packing that uses the least amount of empty space, or to determine if a given set of squares can be packed into a square of a certain size.
