name: Discrete Lot Sizing Problem

description: The Discrete Lot Sizing Problem is a production planning problem that involves determining a minimum cost production schedule to satisfy demands for multiple items without exceeding machine capacities. The problem considers a single machine with limited production capacity, sequence-dependent changeover costs, and storage costs. The goal is to find a production schedule that minimizes the sum of stocking costs and changeover costs while satisfying all demands.

variables: $d_{it}, x_{ij}, s_i, c$
$d_{it}$ represents the date of production for item $i$ at time $t$, $x_{ij}$ represents the successor of item $i$ in the production sequence, $s_i$ represents the storage cost for item $i$, and $c$ represents the total cost.
These variables are decision variables that determine the production schedule and the sequence of items to be produced.

constraints: $\sum_{t=0}^{T} d_{it} \leq 1, \forall i$, $x_{ij} \in \{0, 1\}, \forall i, j$, $\sum_{j=0}^{n} x_{ij} = 1, \forall i$, $d_{it} \leq t, \forall i, t$, $c = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{j=0}^{n} q_{ij} x_{ij} + \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{t=0}^{T} h_{it} d_{it}$
These constraints ensure that each item is produced at most once, the production sequence is valid, each item is produced before its due date, and the total cost is calculated correctly.

objective: Minimize $c = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{j=0}^{n} q_{ij} x_{ij} + \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{t=0}^{T} h_{it} d_{it}$
The objective is to minimize the total cost, which includes the changeover costs and the storage costs.
