Discrete Lot Sizing Problem
The problem is about planning the production of different items in a factory. Imagine you have a factory that produces multiple products, and you need to decide when to produce each product to meet customer demands while minimizing costs.

The main variables are:
- Production date: the date when each item is produced
- Demand date: the date when each item is needed by the customer
- Item type: the type of product being produced
- Successor: the next item to be produced after the current one

The basic restrictions or rules are:
- Each item must be produced before its demand date
- The factory can only produce one item at a time
- There are costs associated with storing items before they are needed, and with switching from producing one item to another

The goal of the problem is to minimize the total cost, which includes the cost of storing items and the cost of switching between different items. The problem aims to find the optimal production schedule that meets all the demands while keeping the total cost as low as possible.
