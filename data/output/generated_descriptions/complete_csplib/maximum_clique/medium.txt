**Maximum Clique Problem**
The Maximum Clique Problem is a classic problem in computer science and graph theory. 
It involves finding the largest subset of vertices in a graph such that every pair of vertices in the subset is connected by an edge.

**Main Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are the vertices that belong to the clique. 
In the given models, these variables are represented as `x` in the PyCSP3 model, `C` in the Essence model, and `c` in the Minizinc model. 
Each variable is a boolean value that indicates whether a vertex is included in the clique (1) or not (0).

**Key Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
- **Adjacency constraint**: For any two vertices that are not connected by an edge, at most one of them can be included in the clique. 
This constraint ensures that the selected vertices form a clique, where every pair of vertices is connected.
- **Clique size constraint**: The size of the clique is calculated as the sum of the vertices included in the clique.

**Objective**
The objective of the Maximum Clique Problem is to **maximize** the size of the clique, i.e., to find the largest subset of vertices that satisfy the adjacency constraint. 
This is achieved by maximizing the sum of the vertices included in the clique. 
In other words, the goal is to find the largest possible clique in the given graph.
