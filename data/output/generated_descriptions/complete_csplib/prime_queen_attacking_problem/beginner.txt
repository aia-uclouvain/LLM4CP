The problem is called the Queen Attacking problem. 
It's about placing a queen and a sequence of numbers on a chessboard in such a way that the queen attacks as many prime numbers as possible. 
The main variables are the position of the queen (q) and the positions of the numbers (x). 
The basic restrictions are that each number must be placed in a different cell, and each number must be placed in a cell that is a knight's move away from the previous number. 
The goal is to minimize the number of prime numbers that are not attacked by the queen.
