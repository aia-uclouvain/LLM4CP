name: Knight's Tour with Queen Attack
description: The problem involves finding a knight's tour on an n x n chessboard, where a knight's tour is a sequence of moves by a knight that visits every cell on the board exactly once. Additionally, a queen is placed on the board, and the goal is to minimize the number of prime numbers that are not attacked by the queen, where a prime number is associated with a cell on the board.

variables: $q, x_0, x_1, ..., x_{n^2-1}, p_0, p_1, ..., p_{m-1}$ 
These variables represent the following: $q$ is the cell where the queen is placed, $x_i$ represents the cell visited at the $i^{th}$ step of the knight's tour, and $p_j$ is a binary variable indicating whether the $j^{th}$ prime number is not attacked by the queen.

constraints: $\text{AllDifferent}(x), (d_1 == 1) \& (d_2 == 2) | (d_1 == 2) \& (d_2 == 1) \forall i \in \{0, 1, ..., n^2-2\}, (q, x_k, p_j) \in table2 \forall j, k$
These constraints ensure the following: all cells are visited exactly once by the knight, each move by the knight is an L-shape (two spaces in one direction, then one space to the side), and the queen's attack on prime numbers is correctly determined.

objective: Minimize $\sum_{j=0}^{m-1} p_j$ 
The objective is to minimize the number of prime numbers that are not attacked by the queen, which is equivalent to maximizing the number of prime numbers that are attacked by the queen.
