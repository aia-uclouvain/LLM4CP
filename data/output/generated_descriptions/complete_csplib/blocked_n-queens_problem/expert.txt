name: Blocked N-Queens Problem
description: The Blocked N-Queens Problem is a variation of the classic N-Queens problem, where the goal is to place N queens on an NxN chessboard such that no two queens attack each other, with the added constraint that some squares on the board are blocked and cannot be occupied by a queen.
variables: $q_i$ (i = 1 to n), where $q_i$ represents the column in which the ith queen is placed. These variables are used to determine the positions of the queens on the board.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, n\}, \forall j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}, i \neq j: q_i \neq q_j$ (no two queens in the same column), $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, n\}, \forall j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}, i \neq j: |q_i - q_j| \neq |i - j|$ (no two queens on the same diagonal), $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, given\_queens\}: q_{blocks[i,1]} \neq blocks[i,2]$ (queens cannot be placed on blocked squares). These constraints ensure that the queens are placed safely on the board, without any attacks between them, and while respecting the blocked squares.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution, i.e., to determine if it is possible to place the queens on the board while satisfying all the constraints. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
