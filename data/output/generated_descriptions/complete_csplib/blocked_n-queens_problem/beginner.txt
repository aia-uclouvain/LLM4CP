N-Queens Problem with Blocks

The problem is about placing queens on a chessboard in such a way that no two queens attack each other, with some squares on the board being blocked and unable to hold a queen.

The main variables are the positions of the queens on the board, with each queen being placed in a specific row and column. The variables can be thought of as a set of coordinates (x, y) where x is the row and y is the column.

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:

- Each row can only hold one queen.
- Each column can only hold one queen.
- No two queens can be on the same diagonal.
- Some squares on the board are blocked and cannot hold a queen.

The goal of the problem is to find a way to place the queens on the board such that all the rules are satisfied, or to determine that it is not possible to do so. In other words, the problem is trying to find a solution that maximizes the number of queens placed on the board while following the rules, or to prove that no such solution exists.
