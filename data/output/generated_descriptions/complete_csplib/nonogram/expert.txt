name: Nonogram
description: Nonogram is a picture logic puzzle where cells in a grid have to be colored or left blank according to numbers given at the side of the grid to reveal a hidden picture. The numbers measure how many unbroken lines of filled-in squares there are in any given row or column.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ is the row and $j$ is the column, $x_{i,j} = 1$ if the cell at row $i$ and column $j$ is colored in black, $x_{i,j} = 0$ otherwise. These variables represent the state of each cell in the grid.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, nRows\}, regular(x_{i,1..nCols}, automaton(rows[i]))$ and $\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, nCols\}, regular(x_{1..nRows,j}, automaton(cols[j]))$, where $automaton(pattern)$ is an automaton that accepts a sequence of 0s and 1s if it matches the given pattern. These constraints ensure that each row and column satisfies the given pattern.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., to find a coloring of the grid that matches the given patterns for all rows and columns. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
