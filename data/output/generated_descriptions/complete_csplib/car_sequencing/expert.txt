name: Car Sequencing
description: The Car Sequencing problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves scheduling the production of cars on an assembly line. Each car has a set of options, such as sunroofs or air conditioning, and there are constraints on the number of cars that can be produced with each option in a given time period. The goal is to find a sequence of cars that satisfies all the constraints.
variables: $c, o, seq, slot, setup$ - These are the decision variables in the problem. $c$ represents the class of each car, $o$ represents whether each car has a particular option, $seq$ represents the sequence of cars, $slot$ represents the class of each car in the sequence, and $setup$ represents whether each car has a particular option in the sequence. They are used to determine the sequence of cars that will be produced.
constraints: $\forall c \in Cars: \sum_{s \in Slots} (slot[s] = c) = demand[c]$, $\forall o \in Options, s \in 1..nbSlots - capacity[o].u + 1: \sum_{j \in s..s + capacity[o].u - 1} setup[o,j] \leq capacity[o].l$, $\forall o \in Options, s \in Slots: setup[o,s] = option[o,slot[s]]$, $\forall o \in Options, i \in 1..optionDemand[o]: \sum_{s \in 1..(nbSlots - i * capacity[o].u)} setup[o,s] \geq (optionDemand[o] - i * capacity[o].l)$ - These constraints ensure that the correct number of cars of each class are produced, that the capacity constraints for each option are satisfied, that each car has the correct options, and that the demand for each option is met.
objective: minimize $\sum_{s \in Cars} s*slot[s]$ - The objective is to minimize the total "cost" of the sequence, where the cost of each car is its class number. However, in some implementations, the objective is simply to find a feasible sequence, in which case there is no objective function.
