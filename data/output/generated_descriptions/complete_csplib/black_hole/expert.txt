name: Black Hole Solitaire
description: The problem is a solitaire card game where 17 stacks of 3 cards are given, and the objective is to remove all the cards from the stacks onto a central pile, called the black hole. The black hole accepts a card if its rank is one greater or one less than the rank of the card currently on top of it.
variables: $x, y, blackHole, cardSequence$ - These are the decision variables where $x$ and $y$ represent the value and position of each card in the built stack, $blackHole$ represents the sequence of cards played on the black hole, and $cardSequence$ represents the position of each card in the sequence.
constraints: $blackHole[0] = 0, cardSequence[0] = 0, allDiff(cardSequence), allDiff(blackHole), (blackHole[step-1], blackHole[step]) \in CONSECUTIVE\_CARD\_TABLE, cardSequence[initialStacks[card]] < cardSequence[initialStacks[card+1]]$ - These constraints ensure the initial state of the game, that all cards are played exactly once, that the sequence of cards on the black hole is valid, and that the cards are played in the correct order from each stack.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a valid sequence of cards that can be played on the black hole, effectively removing all cards from the stacks. The problem does not have an explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather it is a feasibility problem where the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
