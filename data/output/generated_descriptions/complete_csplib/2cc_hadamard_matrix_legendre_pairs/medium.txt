Hadamard Problem
The Hadamard problem is about constructing two sequences of values, each consisting of 1s and -1s, that have specific properties. 
The main decision variables are x and y, which represent the two sequences of values. Each x[i] and y[i] can take on the value of either -1 or 1, representing the ith element in the first and second sequences, respectively.
The key constraints are: the sum of all elements in each sequence must be equal to 1, and for each possible shift (from 1 to m) in the sequences, the sum of the products of corresponding elements in the sequences, considering the shift, must be equal to -2. 
The objective of the Hadamard problem is not explicitly stated as a minimization or maximization problem, but rather as a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find sequences that satisfy the given constraints.
