name: Hadamard Problem
description: The Hadamard problem is a classic problem in combinatorial design theory and constraint programming. It involves finding two sequences of values, either -1 or 1, such that the sequences satisfy certain properties. The problem is named after the French mathematician Jacques Hadamard, who first studied these sequences in the late 19th century. The goal is to find sequences that have optimal autocorrelation properties, which has applications in various fields such as coding theory, signal processing, and statistics.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_n, y_1, y_2, ..., y_n$ - These are the decision variables representing the values of the two sequences, where $x_i$ and $y_i$ can take values -1 or 1, and $n$ is the length of the sequences.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i = 1, \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i = 1, \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i \cdot x_{(i+k) \mod n} + \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i \cdot y_{(i+k) \mod n} = -2$ for $k = 1, 2, ..., \frac{n-1}{2}$ - These constraints ensure that the sum of the values in each sequence is 1, and the autocorrelation of the sequences at different lags is -2, which is a key property of Hadamard sequences.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find sequences that satisfy the given constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, the goal is to find feasible solutions that meet the constraints.
