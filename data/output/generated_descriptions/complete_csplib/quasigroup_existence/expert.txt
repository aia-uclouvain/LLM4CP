name: Quasigroup Existence Problem
description: The Quasigroup Existence Problem is a well-known problem in the field of constraint programming. It involves finding an m x m multiplication table of integers 1..m, where each element occurs exactly once in each row and column, and certain multiplication axioms hold. The problem is to determine whether such a quasigroup exists for a given order m.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i,j \in \{1,2,...,m\}$, $x_{i,j} \in \{1,2,...,m\}$. These variables represent the elements of the quasigroup table, where $x_{i,j}$ is the element at row $i$ and column $j$.
constraints: 
- $\forall i \in \{1,2,...,m\}, \forall j \in \{1,2,...,m\}, x_{i,j} \in \{1,2,...,m\}$ (domain constraint)
- $\forall i \in \{1,2,...,m\}, \text{allDifferent}(x_{i,1}, x_{i,2}, ..., x_{i,m})$ (each row is a permutation of 1..m)
- $\forall j \in \{1,2,...,m\}, \text{allDifferent}(x_{1,j}, x_{2,j}, ..., x_{m,j})$ (each column is a permutation of 1..m)
- $\forall i \in \{1,2,...,m\}, \forall j \in \{1,2,...,m\}, x_{x_{j,i},x_{i,j}} = i$ (quasigroup axiom)
- Additional axioms may be included, such as idempotence ($x_{i,i} = i$) or anti-Abelian ($x_{i,j} \neq x_{j,i}$ for $i \neq j$)
objective: The objective of the problem is to determine whether a quasigroup exists for a given order m, i.e., to find an assignment of values to the variables $x_{i,j}$ that satisfies all the constraints. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
