name: Warehouse Location Problem
description: The Warehouse Location Problem is a classic problem in operations research and management science that involves determining the optimal location of warehouses to supply a set of stores with goods. Each warehouse has a certain capacity and incurs a fixed construction cost, while the cost of transportation from warehouses to stores depends on their locations. The goal is to find the optimal assignment of warehouses to stores that minimizes the total cost, including construction and transportation costs.
variables: $w, o, c, \text{supplier}, \text{open}, \text{cost}, \text{tot}$ 
These variables represent the following: 
- $w$ is an array of decision variables representing the warehouse assigned to each store, 
- $o$ is an array of boolean decision variables indicating whether each warehouse is open or not, 
- $c$ is an array of decision variables representing the cost of supplying each store, 
- $\text{supplier}$ is an array of decision variables representing the warehouse assigned to each store, 
- $\text{open}$ is an array of boolean decision variables indicating whether each warehouse is open or not, 
- $\text{cost}$ is an array of decision variables representing the cost of supplying each store, 
- $\text{tot}$ is a decision variable representing the total cost.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{n} building\_cost \* o_i + \sum_{j=1}^{m} c_j = tot$, 
$\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, m\}, c_j = cost\_matrix_{j, supplier_j}$, 
$\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, n\}, \sum_{j=1}^{m} (supplier_j == i) \leq capacity_i$, 
$\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, n\}, (\exists j \in \{1, \ldots, m\}, supplier_j == i) == o_i$ 
These constraints ensure that: 
- the total cost is the sum of the construction costs of the open warehouses and the transportation costs, 
- the cost of supplying each store is equal to the cost of transportation from the assigned warehouse, 
- the capacity of each warehouse is not exceeded, 
- a warehouse is open if and only if it is assigned to at least one store.
objective: minimize $\text{tot}$, the total cost, which is the sum of the construction costs of the open warehouses and the transportation costs.
