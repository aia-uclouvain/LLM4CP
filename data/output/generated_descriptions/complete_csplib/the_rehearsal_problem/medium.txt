The Rehearsal Problem
The Rehearsal Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves scheduling a rehearsal for a concert or a film. The problem consists of a set of pieces of music or film scenes, each with a specific duration, and a set of players or actors, each with their own availability and requirements.

Main Decision Variables
The main decision variables in the Rehearsal Problem are:
- The order in which the pieces of music or film scenes are rehearsed, represented by the variable `rehearsal_order` or `o`.
- The arrival and departure times of each player or actor, represented by the variables `p_from` or `a` and `p_to` or `l`.
- The waiting time for each player or actor, represented by the variable `waiting_time`.

Key Constraints
The key constraints in the Rehearsal Problem are:
- Each piece of music or film scene must be rehearsed exactly once, represented by the constraint `all_different(rehearsal_order)`.
- A player or actor must be present when a piece of music or film scene requires them, represented by the constraint `(playing[p][o[i]] == 0) | (a[p] <= i) & (i <= l[p])`.
- The arrival and departure times of each player or actor must be consistent with the rehearsal order, represented by the constraints `p_from[p] < p_to[p]` and `i < p_from[p] -> (rehearsal[p, rehearsal_order[i]] = 0)`.

Objective
The objective of the Rehearsal Problem is to minimize the total waiting time of all players or actors, represented by the variable `total_waiting_time`. This is achieved by finding the optimal rehearsal order and arrival and departure times for each player or actor.
