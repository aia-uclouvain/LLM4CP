The Rehearsal Problem
The problem is about scheduling a rehearsal for a concert or a film with multiple pieces of music and a group of players. Each piece of music requires a specific combination of players, and each player can arrive and depart at different times. The goal is to find an order in which the pieces can be rehearsed to minimize the total time that players are waiting to play.

The main variables are:
- The order in which the pieces are played
- The arrival and departure times of each player
- The waiting time for each player

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- Each piece of music must be played exactly once
- A player must be present when a piece of music requires them
- A player can arrive immediately before the first piece they are required for and depart immediately after the last piece they are required for

The goal of the problem is to minimize the total waiting time of all players, which is the total time that players are present but not currently playing.
