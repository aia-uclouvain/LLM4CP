name: Student-Company Interview Assignment Problem
description: The problem involves assigning students to companies for interviews, where each student has a preference for the companies and each company has a limited capacity for interviews. The goal is to assign students to companies in a way that minimizes the total regret of the students, where regret is defined as the difference between the student's preference value and the best possible assignment.

variables: $x_{ijk}$, where $x_{ijk} = 1$ if student $i$ is assigned to company $j$ for interview $k$, and $0$ otherwise. These variables represent the assignment of students to companies for each interview.

constraints: 
$\sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{ijk} = 1 \quad \forall i, k$ (each student is assigned to exactly one company for each interview)
$x_{ijk} \neq x_{ij(k+1)} \quad \forall i, j, k$ (each student is assigned to a different company for each interview)
$\sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{k=1}^{p} x_{ijk} \leq c_j \quad \forall j$ (the total number of students assigned to each company does not exceed its capacity)
$x_{ijk} \in \{0,1\} \quad \forall i, j, k$ (the assignment variables are binary). 
These constraints ensure that each student is assigned to a company for each interview, that each student is assigned to a different company for each interview, and that the total number of students assigned to each company does not exceed its capacity.

objective: Minimize the total regret of the students, which is defined as the sum of the preference values of the students for their assigned companies. The objective function can be written as: $\min \sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{j=1}^{n} \sum_{k=1}^{p} p_{ij} x_{ijk}$, where $p_{ij}$ is the preference value of student $i$ for company $j$.
