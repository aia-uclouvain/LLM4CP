Rack Configuration Problem
The problem is about plugging electronic cards into racks with electronic connectors in a way that minimizes the total cost. 
The main variables are: 
- the model of rack used for each rack, 
- the number of cards of each type plugged into each rack, 
- the power and number of connectors of each rack model, 
- the cost of each rack model, 
- the power required and demand for each card type.
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- each rack can only hold a certain number of cards based on its model, 
- the total power of the cards in a rack cannot exceed the power of the rack model, 
- all cards of each type must be plugged into some rack, 
- each rack model has a limited number of connectors and power.
The goal of the problem is to minimize the total cost of the rack models used, while satisfying all the constraints and plugging all the cards into the racks.
