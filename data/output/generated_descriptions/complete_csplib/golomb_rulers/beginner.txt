The name of the problem is the Golomb Ruler problem. 
The Golomb Ruler problem is about finding the shortest ruler with a certain number of marks such that all the distances between the marks are different. 
The main variables are the positions of the marks on the ruler, which are typically represented as integers. 
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are that all the distances between the marks must be different, and the marks must be placed in increasing order. 
The goal of the problem is to minimize the length of the ruler, which is typically represented as the position of the last mark.
