Set Partition Problem
The problem is about dividing a set of numbers into two equal-sized groups, where the sum of the numbers in each group is the same, and the sum of the squares of the numbers in each group is also the same.
The main variables are the two sets A and B, which are the groups of numbers.
The basic restrictions are that A and B must be disjoint (i.e., they have no numbers in common), A and B must have the same number of elements, the sum of the numbers in A must be equal to the sum of the numbers in B, and the sum of the squares of the numbers in A must be equal to the sum of the squares of the numbers in B.
The goal of the problem is to find two sets A and B that satisfy these conditions.
