The problem is the Nurse Scheduling Problem. 
It is about creating schedules for nurses to work shifts at a hospital, taking into account various constraints such as the number of nurses required for each shift, the maximum number of hours a nurse can work, and the minimum number of days off between shifts. 
The main decision variables represent whether a nurse is assigned to work a particular shift, and they are typically binary variables (0 or 1) where 1 indicates the nurse is working the shift and 0 indicates they are not. 
The key constraints include ensuring that each shift has the required number of nurses, that no nurse works more hours than allowed, and that each nurse gets the required minimum days off between shifts. Additionally, constraints may be included to ensure that nurses are not overworked or underworked, and that the schedule is fair and balanced. 
The objective of the problem is typically to minimize the total cost of the schedule, which may include costs such as overtime pay, or to maximize the satisfaction of the nurses, which may be based on their preferences for certain shifts or days off.
