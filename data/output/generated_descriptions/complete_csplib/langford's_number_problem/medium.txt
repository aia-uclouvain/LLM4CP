Langford's Number Problem
The Langford's Number Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves arranging two sets of positive integers from 1 to k in a sequence, such that the first occurrence of each integer i is followed by the next occurrence of i after i+1 positions.

The main decision variables in this problem are the positions of the integers in the sequence. In some models, the positions are represented as an array of variables, where each variable represents the position of a specific integer. In other models, the positions are represented as a function that maps each integer to its position in the sequence.

The key constraints in this problem are:

* Each integer from 1 to k appears exactly twice in the sequence.
* The first occurrence of each integer i is followed by the next occurrence of i after i+1 positions.
* The positions of the integers in the sequence are distinct.

The objective of the problem is to find a sequence that satisfies all the constraints. There is no specific objective function to minimize or maximize, and the goal is simply to find a feasible solution.

In some models, symmetry breaking constraints are added to reduce the search space and improve the efficiency of the solver. These constraints typically involve imposing an order on the positions of the integers in the sequence.
