Langford's Number Problem
The problem is about arranging two sets of positive integers from 1 to k in a sequence, such that after the first occurrence of an integer i, each subsequent occurrence of i appears i+1 positions later.
The main variables are the sequence of numbers and the positions of the numbers in the sequence.
The basic restrictions are that each number from 1 to k appears exactly twice in the sequence, and the difference between the positions of two occurrences of the same number is equal to the number plus one.
The goal is to find a sequence that satisfies these conditions.
