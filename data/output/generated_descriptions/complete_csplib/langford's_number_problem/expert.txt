name: Langford's Number Problem
description: The problem is to arrange two sets of positive integers from 1 to k in a sequence such that the first occurrence of each integer i is followed by the next occurrence of i after i+1 positions.
variables: $x_{i,j}, pos_{i,j}, solution_i, position_i$ where $x_{i,j}$ represents the position of the jth occurrence of number i, $pos_{i,j}$ represents the position of the jth occurrence of number i, $solution_i$ represents the number at position i in the sequence, and $position_i$ represents the position of the ith number in the sequence. These variables are used to model the sequence and the positions of the numbers in the sequence.
constraints: $\forall i \in [1,k], \forall j \in [2,k], pos_{i,j} = pos_{i,j-1} + i + 1$, $\forall i \in [1,k], solution_{pos_{i,1}} = i$, $\forall i \in [1,k], solution_{pos_{i,k}} = i$, $alldifferent(position)$, $solution_1 < solution_{2k}$. These constraints ensure that the sequence is constructed correctly, the numbers are placed at the correct positions, and the positions are distinct.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
