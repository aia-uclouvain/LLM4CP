**Online Dating Decision Services Problem**
The Online Dating Decision Services problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves matching individuals based on their preferences, interests, and demographic information.

**Problem Description**
The problem involves a set of individuals, each with their own profile, which includes their name, gender, city, age, interests, and preferences for a potential match. The goal is to find the best matches between individuals, taking into account their preferences, interests, and demographic information.

**Main Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `x`: an array of variables that assigns each individual to their best match.
- `y`: an array of variables that represents the set of individuals that each person is matched to.
- `age_diff`: an array of variables that represents the age difference between each individual and their match.
- `common_interests`: an array of variables that represents the number of common interests between each individual and their match.
- `complete_match`: an array of variables that indicates whether each individual is in a complete match (i.e., they are matched to someone who is also matched to them).

**Key Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each individual can only be matched to someone of an acceptable gender.
- Each individual can only be matched to someone within their acceptable age range.
- Each individual can only be matched to someone who lives in the same city.
- Each individual can only be matched to someone with at least the minimum number of common interests specified in their profile.
- The age difference between each individual and their match is calculated.
- The number of common interests between each individual and their match is calculated.
- Each individual can only be matched to someone who is also matched to them (if the `reciprocal` variable is set to true).

**Problem Objective**
The objective of this problem is to maximize the total score of all matches, where the score is calculated as the number of common interests between two individuals minus the age difference between them. The problem also aims to find the maximum number of complete matches, where a complete match is a match between two individuals who are both matched to each other.
