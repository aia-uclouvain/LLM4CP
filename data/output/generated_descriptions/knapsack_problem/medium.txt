**Knapsack Problem**
The Knapsack problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the optimal way to pack a set of items of different weights and volumes into a knapsack of limited capacity.

**Main Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are the quantities of each item to be taken, represented by the array `x`. Each element `x[i]` represents the number of units of item `i` to be taken.

**Key Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
- The total weight of the items taken must not exceed 25 units.
- The total volume of the items taken must not exceed 0.25 cubic units.
- The quantity of each item taken must be a non-negative integer.
These constraints ensure that the knapsack's capacity is not exceeded and that only whole units of each item are taken.

**Objective**
The objective of the problem is to maximize the total value of the items taken, represented by the variable `z`. The value of each item is given, and the goal is to find the combination of items that yields the highest total value while satisfying the constraints.
