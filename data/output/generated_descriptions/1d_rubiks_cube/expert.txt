name: 1D Rubik's Cube
description: The 1D Rubik's Cube is a sequence of numbers that can be rotated in groups of four, with the goal of finding the shortest sequence of rotations to restore the original position from a scrambled line.
variables: $n, seq\_length, max\_steps, m, data, steps, ops, min\_step$ 
These variables represent the following: $n$ is the length of the sequence, $seq\_length$ is the length of each reverse sequence, $max\_steps$ is the maximum number of steps allowed, $m$ is a derived variable used for indexing, $data$ is the input sequence, $steps$ is a 2D array representing the sequence at each step, $ops$ is an array representing the operation performed at each step, and $min\_step$ is the minimum number of steps required to solve the puzzle.
constraints: 
$\forall j \in 1..n: steps[1,j] = data[j]$
$\forall i \in 2..max\_steps: alldifferent([steps[i,j] | j \in 1..n]) \wedge (ops[i-1] > 0 \rightarrow ops[i] \neq ops[i-1])$
$\forall i \in 2..max\_steps: (same([steps[i,j] | j \in 1..n], [steps[i-1,j] | j \in 1..n]) \wedge ops[i] = 0) \vee (\exists k \in 1..m: reverse([steps[i,j] | j \in 1..n], [steps[i-1,j] | j \in 1..n], k, k+seq\_length-1) \wedge ops[i] = k)$
$\exists i \in 1..max\_steps: \forall j \in 1..n: steps[i,j] = j \wedge min\_step = i \wedge \forall k \in i+1..max\_steps: same([steps[k,j] | j \in 1..n], [steps[k-1,j] | j \in 1..n])$
These constraints ensure that the initial sequence is set correctly, each step has a unique sequence, the operations are performed correctly, and the minimum number of steps is found.
objective: minimize $min\_step$
