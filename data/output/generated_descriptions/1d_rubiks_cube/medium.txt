1D Rubik's Cube
The 1D Rubik's Cube problem is about finding the shortest sequence of rotations to restore a scrambled line of numbers to its original position. 

The main decision variables are `steps` and `ops`. `steps` is a 2D array where `steps[i, j]` represents the value at position `j` after `i` rotations. `ops` is a 1D array where `ops[i]` represents the operation performed at step `i`, which can be either a reverse operation or no operation (same as previous step).

The key constraints are: 
- The initial state of the line is given by the `data` array.
- Each step can either be a reverse operation or the same as the previous step.
- A reverse operation can be performed on a sequence of four consecutive numbers, starting from positions 1, 2, or 3.
- The goal is to find a sequence of operations that restores the original position of the numbers.
- The model also includes constraints to break symmetries and to ensure that the operations are performed in a way that minimizes the number of steps.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the number of steps (`min_step`) required to restore the original position of the numbers. The model uses a constraint programming approach to find the shortest sequence of rotations that solves the problem.
