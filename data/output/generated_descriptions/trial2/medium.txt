The Second Trial puzzle
The Second Trial puzzle is about finding the correct arrangement of a lady and a tiger behind two doors, based on given statements. 
The main decision variables are x(i,j) and t(i), where x(i,j) represents whether door i hides prize j (1 for the lady, 2 for the tiger), and t(i) represents whether the statement on door i is true.
The key constraints are: each door hides one prize, the statement on door 1 is true if and only if the lady is behind either door 1 or door 2, the statement on door 2 is true if and only if the tiger is behind door 1, and the statements on both doors are either both true or both false.
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, such that all constraints are met, which is achieved by using the "solve satisfy" statement in the Minizinc model.
