Pandigital Numbers Problem
The problem is about finding a special arrangement of digits from 1 to 9 (or 0 to 9) that can be divided into three groups: two numbers and their product (or sum). 
The main variables are: 
- x: an array of digits from 1 to 9 (or 0 to 9)
- num1 and num2: the two numbers
- res: the product (or sum) of num1 and num2
- len1 and len2: the lengths of num1 and num2
- vop: the operation, either multiplication or addition
The basic restrictions are: 
- each digit can only be used once
- the numbers must be in a specific base (up to 10)
- the leading digit of each number must be non-zero
- the product (or sum) of the two numbers must be equal to the third group of digits
The goal is to find an arrangement of digits that satisfies these conditions, with a focus on multiplication.
