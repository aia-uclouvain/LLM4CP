name: Unbounded Knapsack Problem

description: The problem is about a traveller who has to leave a place and can take as many items as he likes, given that they fit in his knapsack and he can carry them. The items have different weights, volumes, and values, and the traveller wants to maximize the total value of the items he takes. The knapsack has a limited capacity in terms of weight and volume.

variables: $x_1, x_2, x_3$ 
These are the decision variables representing the number of units of each item (panacea, ichor, and gold) that the traveller takes.

constraints: $total\_weight = \sum_{i=1}^{3} x_i \* weight_i \leq 25.0$, $total\_volume = \sum_{i=1}^{3} x_i \* volume_i \leq 0.25$, $x_i \geq 0$ for $i = 1, 2, 3$, $total\_value \geq 54500.0$ 
These constraints ensure that the total weight and volume of the items taken do not exceed the knapsack's capacity, that the number of units of each item is non-negative, and that the total value of the items taken is at least 54500.0.

objective: Maximize $total\_value = \sum_{i=1}^{3} x_i \* value_i$ 
The objective is to maximize the total value of the items taken, which is the sum of the values of each item multiplied by the number of units of that item.
