name: Kidney Exchange Problem
description: The Kidney Exchange Problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves finding the maximum number of possible kidney exchanges between a set of patients and donors. In this problem, each patient has a set of compatible donors, and the goal is to find the maximum number of exchanges such that each patient receives a kidney from a compatible donor and each donor gives a kidney to a patient who is compatible with them.
variables: $x = (x_1, x_2, ..., x_{num\_people})$ and $y = (y_{i,j})_{i,j=1}^{num\_people}$, where $x_i$ represents the index of the donor who gives a kidney to patient $i$ (or 0 if patient $i$ does not receive a kidney), and $y_{i,j}$ is a binary variable that represents whether patient $i$ receives a kidney from patient $j$.
constraints: $\forall p \in \{1,2,...,num\_people\}: (x_p = 0 \land \sum_{i=1}^{num\_people} y_{p,i} = 0 \land \sum_{i=1}^{num\_people} y_{i,p} = 0) \lor (x_p \in compatible_p \land x_{x_p} > 0 \land \sum_{i=1}^{num\_people} y_{p,i} = 1 \land \sum_{i=1}^{num\_people} y_{i,p} = 1)$, $alldifferent\_except\_0(x)$, where the first constraint ensures that each patient either does not receive a kidney or receives a kidney from a compatible donor and the donor also receives a kidney, and the second constraint ensures that each patient receives a kidney from at most one donor.
objective: Maximize $z = \sum_{i=1}^{num\_people} bool2int(x_i > 0)$, which represents the total number of patients who receive a kidney.
