Touching Numbers puzzle
The Touching Numbers puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem where the goal is to arrange the digits 0 through 9 in a specific way to maximize a score based on the number of squares touching between adjacent digits.
The main decision variables are X(i,j) and Z(i). X(i,j) represents whether digit (i-1) is to the immediate left of digit (j-1), with a value of 1 indicating they are adjacent and 0 indicating they are not. Z(i) is a variable used to avoid circular sets in the arrangement.
The key constraints are: each digit can be to the immediate left of no more than one other digit, each digit can be to the immediate right of no more than one other digit, no circular sets are allowed, and exactly nine adjacencies are required. These constraints ensure that the arrangement is valid and that the digits are connected in a specific way.
The objective of the problem is to maximize the score, which is calculated based on the number of squares touching between adjacent digits. The score is calculated as the sum of the products of the indices of the adjacent digits and the number of squares touching between them, multiplied by the adjacency variable X(i,j). The goal is to find an arrangement that maximizes this score.
