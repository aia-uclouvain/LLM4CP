name: The Dyer Problem
description: The problem involves five gentlemen - Baker, Dyer, Farmer, Glover, and Hosier - seated around a circular table, where each gentleman is the namesake of the profession of one of the others. The task is to determine the name of the dyer based on given seating constraints.
variables: $Baker, Dyer, Farmer, Glover, Hosier, baker, dyer, farmer, glover, hosier$ - These variables represent the seating positions (0-4) of the gentlemen and their corresponding professions, where the uppercase variables denote the individuals and the lowercase variables denote their respective professions.
constraints: $all\_different(names) \wedge all\_different(profession) \wedge Baker = 1 \wedge baker \neq Baker \wedge dyer \neq Dyer \wedge farmer \neq Farmer \wedge glover \neq Glover \wedge hosier \neq Hosier \wedge dyer = (Hosier - 2) \mod 5 \wedge baker = (Baker + 2) \mod 5 \wedge farmer = (Farmer - 1) \mod 5 \wedge Dyer = (glover + 1) \mod 5$ - These constraints ensure that each gentleman has a unique seating position and profession, and that the given seating constraints are satisfied, including the relative positions of the dyer, baker, farmer, and glover.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of seating positions and professions for the gentlemen, thereby determining the name of the dyer.
