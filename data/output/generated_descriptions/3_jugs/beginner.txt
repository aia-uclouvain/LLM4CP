Three Jugs Problem
The problem is about finding the shortest way to get from one point to another in a network of connected nodes. 
The main variables are the nodes (or jugs), the connections between them (x), and the amount of "flow" going in and out of each node (inFlow and outFlow). 
The basic restrictions are that each node can only have a certain amount of flow going in and out, and that the total flow going into a node must be equal to the total flow going out. 
The goal is to minimize the total "distance" (or cost) of the flow through the network, which is represented by the variable z.
