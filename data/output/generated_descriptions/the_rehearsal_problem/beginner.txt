Rehearsal Problem
The Rehearsal Problem is about scheduling a rehearsal for a concert or a film. There are several pieces of music or scenes to be rehearsed, and each piece requires a different combination of players or actors. The goal is to find an order in which the pieces can be rehearsed to minimize the total time that players or actors are waiting to play.

The main variables are:
- The order in which the pieces are rehearsed (rehearsal_order)
- The time when each player arrives (arrive) and leaves (leave)
- The waiting time for each player (waiting_time)
- The total waiting time (total_waiting_time)

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- Each piece must be rehearsed exactly once
- A player must be present when a piece requires them
- A player can arrive immediately before the first piece they are required for and leave immediately after the last piece they are required for

The goal of the problem is to minimize the total waiting time, which is the sum of the waiting times for all players. The waiting time for a player is the total time they are present but not playing.
