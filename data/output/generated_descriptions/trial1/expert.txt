name: The First Trial (Smullyan) puzzle
description: The problem is a classic puzzle where there are two doors, behind one of which is a lady and behind the other is a tiger. The doors have statements on them, and the goal is to figure out which door hides the lady and which door hides the tiger, based on the given statements. The statements are: "The lady is behind the other door" and "The tiger is behind the other door". The puzzle is to determine the arrangement of the lady and the tiger behind the doors, given that one of the statements is true and the other is false.
variables: $x_{i,j}, t_i, d_i$ where $x_{i,j}$ represents whether door $i$ hides prize $j$ (1 = Lady, 2 = Tiger), $t_i$ represents whether the statement on door $i$ is true, and $d_i$ are dummy variables used to model the logical constraints.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{2} x_{i,j} = 1, \forall i \in D$, $x_{1,1} + x_{2,2} - t_1 \leq 1$, $x_{1,1} + x_{2,2} - 2t_1 \geq 0$, $x_{1,1} - x_{2,2} - 2d_1 \leq -1$, $x_{1,1} - x_{2,2} - d_1 \geq -1$, $x_{1,1} - x_{2,2} + 2d_2 \geq 1$, $x_{1,1} - x_{2,2} + 2d_2 \leq 2$, $d_1 + d_2 - t_2 \leq 1$, $d_1 + d_2 - 2t_2 \geq 0$, $t_1 + t_2 = 1$ which represent the constraints that each door hides one prize, the statements on the doors are modeled using logical constraints, and only one statement is true.
