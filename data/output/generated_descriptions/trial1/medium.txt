The Hardest Logic Puzzle Ever, also known as The First Trial puzzle. 
This problem is about determining which doors hide a lady and a tiger, based on statements on the doors. 
The main decision variables are x, which represents whether door i hides prize j (1 for lady, 2 for tiger), and t, which represents whether the statement on door i is true. 
The key constraints are: each door hides one prize, the statements on the doors are consistent with the arrangement of the lady and the tiger, and only one statement is true. 
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, such that all constraints are met, which is achieved by using the solve satisfy statement in the Minizinc model.
