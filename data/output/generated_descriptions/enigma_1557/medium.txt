The Reverse Division problem
The Reverse Division problem is about finding a specific integer that meets certain divisibility conditions and, when its digits are reversed, the resulting integer is larger and also meets the same divisibility conditions.
The main decision variables in this problem are `n1` and `n2`, which represent the two integers to be found. These variables are defined as integers within a specific range, from `10*n` to `max_num`, where `n` is the number of digits (4 in this case) and `max_num` is the maximum possible value for an `n`-digit number.
The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows: 
- The integers `n1` and `n2` must be divisible by specific pairs of numbers: `n1` by 11 and 13, and `n2` by 17 and 19.
- The reverse of each integer must also be divisible by the same pair of numbers.
- The reverse of each integer must be larger than the original integer.
- The first digit of each integer and its reverse must not be zero.
The objective of the problem is to find the values of `n1` and `n2` that satisfy all the given constraints. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function; instead, it aims to find specific integers that meet the specified conditions.
