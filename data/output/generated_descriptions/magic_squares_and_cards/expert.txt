name: Magic Square Problem with Cards
description: The problem is to find the largest constant sum for an order-3 magic square that can be formed with nine cards from a deck, allowing duplicates values. A magic square is a square grid filled with distinct or non-distinct numbers, such that each row, column, and diagonal sums to the same constant.
variables: $x_{r,c} \in \{1, 2, \ldots, 13\}$ for $r, c \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ and $s \in \{0, 1, \ldots, 13 \times 4\}$. These variables represent the values of the cards in the magic square, where $x_{r,c}$ is the value of the card in the $r$-th row and $c$-th column, and $s$ is the constant sum of each row, column, and diagonal.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, \ldots, 13\}: \sum_{r,c \in \{1,2,3\}} (x_{r,c} = i) \leq 4$, $\forall c \in \{1, 2, 3\}: \sum_{r \in \{1,2,3\}} x_{r,c} = s$, $\forall r \in \{1, 2, 3\}: \sum_{c \in \{1,2,3\}} x_{r,c} = s$, $\sum_{i \in \{1,2,3\}} x_{i,i} = s$, and $\sum_{i \in \{1,2,3\}} x_{i,4-i} = s$. These constraints ensure that each value appears at most 4 times in the magic square (since there are 4 cards of each value in the deck), and that the sum of each row, column, and diagonal is equal to the constant sum $s$.
objective: Maximize $s$
