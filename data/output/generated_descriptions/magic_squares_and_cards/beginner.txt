Magic Squares and Cards
The problem is about creating a special kind of square using cards from a deck, where each row, column, and diagonal adds up to the same number.
The main variables are the numbers on the cards (x) and the target sum (s) that each row, column, and diagonal should add up to.
The basic restrictions are that each number can be used at most four times (since there are four cards of each value in a deck), and the sum of each row, column, and diagonal must be the same.
The goal is to find the largest possible target sum (s) that can be achieved using the cards from the deck to fill in the square.
