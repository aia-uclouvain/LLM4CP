Magic Square Problem
The problem is about finding the largest constant sum for an order-3 magic square that can be formed with nine cards from a deck, allowing duplicates values. 
The main decision variables are x, a 2D array representing the values of the cards in the magic square, and s, a variable representing the constant sum of each row, column, and diagonal in the magic square. 
The key constraints are that each row, column, and diagonal in the magic square must have the same sum, and each value from 1 to 13 can appear at most four times in the magic square, since there are four cards of each value in a deck. 
The problem's objective is to maximize the constant sum s, meaning to find the largest possible sum that can be achieved in the magic square under the given constraints.
