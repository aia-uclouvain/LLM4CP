Magic Square Problem
The problem is about finding a square grid filled with distinct positive integers in the range 1 to n^2, where n is the number of rows (or columns) in the grid, such that the sum of the numbers in each row, column, and the two main diagonals is the same.

The main decision variables are the elements of the 2D array `magic`, where `magic[i, j]` represents the number in the i-th row and j-th column of the grid.

The key constraints are: 
- all numbers in the grid must be distinct, 
- the sum of the numbers in each row must be equal to the total sum, 
- the sum of the numbers in each column must be equal to the total sum, 
- the sum of the numbers in the main diagonal (from top-left to bottom-right) must be equal to the total sum, 
- the sum of the numbers in the other main diagonal (from top-right to bottom-left) must be equal to the total sum, 
- the number in the top-left corner must be the smallest of the four corner numbers, and 
- the number in the second column of the first row must be smaller than the number in the first column of the second row.

The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all these constraints, which is a classic constraint satisfaction problem. The model uses the `solve satisfy` statement to find a solution that meets all the constraints, without any specific objective function to minimize or maximize.
