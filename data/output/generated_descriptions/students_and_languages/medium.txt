**Students and Languages Problem**
The Students and Languages problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves determining the languages spoken by five students of different nationalities.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are represented by a 2D array `Students` where `Students[S, L]` is a binary variable that represents whether student `S` speaks language `L`. The students and languages are represented by integers from 1 to 5, where 1 corresponds to Polish, 2 to Hungarian, 3 to Finnish, 4 to Swedish, and 5 to German.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem can be described in plain language as follows:
- Each student is fluent in their own language and at least one other language.
- There is no single language spoken by all students.
- Each pair of students can communicate using at least one common language.
- On average, each student speaks two foreign languages.
- Specific constraints are imposed on the number of languages spoken by certain students, such as the Pole and the Hungarian speaking three foreign languages.
- Certain conditions must be met when some students are absent, such as the ability of the remaining students to communicate using a common language.
- Restrictions are placed on the number of students who can speak certain languages, such as Polish and Finnish being spoken by only two students as foreign languages.

**Objective**
The objective of this problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific variable but rather to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints. In other words, the goal is to determine which languages each student can speak, given the constraints provided. The problem is solved using a satisfaction approach, where the model is executed to find a solution that meets all the constraints, without optimizing a specific objective function.
