name: Students and Languages problem

description: The problem involves determining the languages spoken by five students of different nationalities (Pole, Hungarian, Finn, Swede, and German) who are on vacation together. Each student is fluent in one or more foreign languages, but only those that are native to some of the other students. The problem provides several conditions that must be satisfied, such as the average number of languages spoken by each student, the number of languages spoken by specific students, and the ability of certain students to communicate with each other in specific languages.

variables: $Students_{1..5,1..5}$, where $Students_{i,j}$ is a binary variable that represents whether student $i$ speaks language $j$. These variables represent the language proficiency of each student, with $Students_{i,j} = 1$ indicating that student $i$ speaks language $j$ and $Students_{i,j} = 0$ indicating that student $i$ does not speak language $j$.

constraints: 
$\forall i \in \{1..5\}: Students_{i,i} = 1$
$\forall i \in \{1..5\}: \sum_{j=1}^5 Students_{i,j} \geq 2$
$\forall j \in \{1..5\}: \sum_{i=1}^5 Students_{i,j} < 5$
$\forall i,j \in \{1..5\} \text{ where } i < j: \exists k \in \{1..5\}: Students_{i,k} + Students_{j,k} = 2$
$\sum_{i=1}^5 \sum_{j=1}^5 Students_{i,j} - 5 = 10$
$\sum_{j=1,j \neq 1}^5 Students_{1,j} = 3$
$\sum_{j=1,j \neq 2}^5 Students_{2,j} = 3$
$\exists k \in \{1..5\}: \sum_{i \in \{1,2,3,5\}} Students_{i,k} = 4$
$\exists k \in \{1..5\}: \sum_{i \in \{1,2,4,5\}} Students_{i,k} = 4$
$\sum_{i=1}^5 Students_{i,4} = 3$
$\sum_{i=1,i \neq 1}^5 Students_{i,1} + \sum_{i=1,i \neq 3}^5 Students_{i,3} = 2$
$\sum_{j=1}^5 (Students_{1,j} = Students_{3,j}) = 2$
$Students_{1,5} + Students_{3,5} < 2$
$\sum_{j=1}^5 (Students_{2,j} = Students_{4,j}) = 1$
These constraints represent the conditions provided in the problem, such as each student speaking at least two languages, no single language being spoken by all students, and specific students speaking certain numbers of languages.

objective: satisfy, which means that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
