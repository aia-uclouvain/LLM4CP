name: Assignment Problem
description: The assignment problem is a classic problem in combinatorial optimization and operations research that involves finding the optimal way to assign a set of agents to a set of tasks. In this specific model, it is illustrated with a swimming team example, where the goal is to assign swimmers to different events in a way that minimizes the total cost or time.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the assignment of the $i^{th}$ row (swimmer) to the $j^{th}$ column (event), with $x_{i,j} = 1$ if the $i^{th}$ swimmer is assigned to the $j^{th}$ event and $x_{i,j} = 0$ otherwise. These are binary decision variables that determine the assignment of swimmers to events.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{cols} x_{i,j} = 1 \forall i$, $\sum_{i=1}^{rows} x_{i,j} = 1 \forall j$, $x_{i,j} \in \{0,1\}$, which ensure that each swimmer is assigned to exactly one event, each event is assigned to exactly one swimmer, and the assignments are binary.
objective: Minimize $\sum_{i=1}^{rows} \sum_{j=1}^{cols} cost_{i,j} \cdot x_{i,j}$, where $cost_{i,j}$ is the cost or time associated with assigning the $i^{th}$ swimmer to the $j^{th}$ event. The objective is to find the assignment that minimizes the total cost or time.
