name: Building a House Scheduling Problem
description: This problem involves scheduling tasks for building a house, where some tasks must be completed before others due to precedence constraints, and the goal is to find a schedule that minimizes the makespan or an extra objective function.
variables: $start_1, start_2, ..., start_{10}, end_1, end_2, ..., end_{10}, limitx, makespan, z$ - These variables represent the start and end times of each task, the limit on the number of tasks that can be performed simultaneously, the makespan (the maximum end time of all tasks), and an extra objective function $z$.
constraints: $\forall t \in \{1, 2, ..., 10\}: end_t = start_t + duration_t$, $\forall p \in \{1, 2, ..., 14\}: start_{precedences_{p,1}} + duration_{precedences_{p,1}} \leq start_{precedences_{p,2}}$, $cumulative(start, duration, height, limitx)$, $makespan \geq \max(end_t)$ - These constraints ensure that the end time of each task is calculated correctly, that precedence constraints are satisfied, that the cumulative resource constraint is met, and that the makespan is calculated correctly.
objective: minimize $makespan$ or minimize $z = 400 \cdot \max(end_{moving} - 100, 0) + 200 \cdot \max(25 - start_{masonry}, 0) + 300 \cdot \max(75 - start_{carpentry}, 0) + 100 \cdot \max(75 - start_{ceiling}, 0)$ - The objective is to minimize either the makespan or the extra objective function $z$, which takes into account penalties for late completion of certain tasks.
