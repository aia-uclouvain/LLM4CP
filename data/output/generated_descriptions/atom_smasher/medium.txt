The Digital Atom Smasher problem. 
This problem is about finding values for the letters A, T, O, and M such that the equation sqrt(ATOM) = A + TO + M holds true, where each letter represents a digit from 0 to 9. 
The main decision variables are A, T, O, and M, which represent the digits for each letter in the equation. 
The key constraints are that the square of a number (z) must be equal to the value of ATOM, which is calculated by combining the digits A, T, O, and M. Additionally, z must also be equal to the sum of A, TO (where TO represents the two-digit number formed by T and O), and M. All the digits A, T, O, and M must be different from each other. 
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. In other words, the goal is to find values for A, T, O, and M that make the equation sqrt(ATOM) = A + TO + M true, with the additional condition that all the digits are distinct.
