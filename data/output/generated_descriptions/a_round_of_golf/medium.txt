A Round of Golf puzzle
The problem is about determining the full name, job, and golf score of four workers at a golf course, given certain clues about their scores and relationships between them.
The main decision variables are: 
- first_name: an array representing the first names of the four workers (Jack, Bill, Paul, Frank)
- last_name: an array representing the last names of the four workers (Green, Clubb, Sands, Carter)
- job: an array representing the jobs of the four workers (cook, maintenance man, clerk, caddy)
- score: an array representing the golf scores of the four workers, which must be between 70 and 85.
The key constraints are:
- Each worker has a unique last name, job, and score.
- Bill is not the maintenance man and had the lowest score.
- Mr. Clubb scored 10 strokes more than the pro-shop clerk.
- Frank and the caddy scored 4 and 7 strokes more than Mr. Sands, in some order.
- Mr. Carter scored 78, which is higher than Frank's score.
- None of the workers scored exactly 81 strokes.
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, which will determine the full name, job, and golf score of each worker. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a feasible solution that meets all the conditions.
