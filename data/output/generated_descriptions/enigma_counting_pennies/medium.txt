Enigma Problem (Counting Pennies)
The Enigma problem is about finding a 4-digit number that was subtracted from the sum of 24 different 4-digit numbers, which were formed by rearranging four distinct positive digits.
The main decision variables are: 
- `digits`: an array of four variables representing the four distinct positive digits chosen by Penny.
- `x`: an array of 24 variables representing the 24 different 4-digit numbers that can be formed using the four digits.
- `digits_sum`: a variable representing the sum of the 24 numbers.
- `digits_sum_a`: an array of six variables representing the digits of `digits_sum`.
The key constraints are:
- The four digits must be distinct and positive.
- The 24 numbers are formed by rearranging the four digits.
- The sum of the 24 numbers minus one of the numbers equals the given number 122**0.
- The digits of `digits_sum` match the given number 122**0, with the last two digits being unknown.
The objective of the problem is to find the 4-digit number that was subtracted from the sum of the 24 numbers, which is equivalent to finding the values of the `digits` and `x` variables that satisfy all the constraints.
