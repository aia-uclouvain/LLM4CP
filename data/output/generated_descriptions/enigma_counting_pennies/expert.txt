name: Enigma Problem (Counting Pennies)
description: Joe asked Penny to think of four different positive digits and add up the 24 different 4-digit numbers that could be made using the four digits. Then he asked her to subtract just one of the 4-digit numbers from the total and write down the new total. When asked what number she had written down, Penny replied: "122**0". Joe didn't quite hear the 4th or 5th digits, but he was able to work out the number Penny had subtracted. The problem is to find the number Penny had subtracted.
variables: $digits = [d_1, d_2, d_3, d_4]$, $x = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_{24}]$, $digits\_sum$, $digits\_sum\_a = [a_1, a_2, ..., a_6]$. These variables represent the four digits chosen by Penny, the 24 numbers formed by permuting the four digits, the sum of the 24 numbers minus one of the numbers, and the array representation of the sum, respectively.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}, d_i \in \{1, 2, ..., 9\}$, $d_1 > 0$, $x_i = \sum_{j=1}^{4} d_{perms[i,j]} \cdot 10^{4-j}$, $digits\_sum = \sum_{i=1}^{24} x_i - x_k$ for some $k \in \{1, 2, ..., 24\}$, $digits\_sum = \sum_{i=1}^{6} a_i \cdot 10^{6-i}$, $a_1 = 1$, $a_2 = 2$, $a_3 = 2$, $a_6 = 0$. These constraints ensure that the digits are distinct and positive, the numbers $x_i$ are formed by permuting the digits, the sum of the numbers minus one of the numbers is calculated, and the sum is represented as an array.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find the values of the decision variables that satisfy all the constraints, which will reveal the number Penny had subtracted. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
