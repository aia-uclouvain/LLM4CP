Five Element problem
The problem is about selecting five numbers from a 5x5 grid of numbers, such that no two numbers are from the same row or column, and the largest of these five numbers is as small as possible.
The main variables are x and y. The variable x is like a map that shows which numbers are selected from the grid, and the variable y represents the five selected numbers. 
The basic restrictions are that only one number can be chosen from each row and each column, and the chosen numbers must come from the given grid.
The goal is to minimize the largest of the five chosen numbers, meaning we want to find the smallest possible maximum value among the selected numbers.
