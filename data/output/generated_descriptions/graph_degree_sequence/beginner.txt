Graph Degree Sequence problem
The problem is about finding the degree sequence of a directed graph, which means determining how many connections each point in the graph has. 
The main variables are the degree sequence "d" which represents how many connections each point has, and the graph itself, represented as a matrix or a list of edges. 
The basic restrictions are that the degree sequence must match the actual connections in the graph, and the graph must be a valid directed graph. 
The goal of the problem is to find a graph that satisfies the given degree sequence, or to determine if such a graph exists, by using constraints to ensure that the degree sequence matches the graph.
