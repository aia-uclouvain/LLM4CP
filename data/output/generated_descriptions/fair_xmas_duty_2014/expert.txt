name: Fair Xmas Duty Problem
description: The problem involves scheduling a fair Christmas duty for a group of people over a period of 14 days, from December 24 to January 6, with certain days having higher points due to their significance, and constraints such as not having a person on duty for more than two days in a row and respecting forbidden assignments.
variables: $on\_duty_d, people\_points_p, people\_days_p, total\_diffs$ where $d \in \{1, \ldots, 14\}$ and $p \in \{1, \ldots, 4\}$. These variables represent the person on duty for each day, the total points for each person, the number of days each person is on duty, and the total differences in points between all pairs of people, respectively.
constraints: $\begin{aligned} total\_diffs &= \sum_{i < j} |people\_points_i - people\_points_j| \\ people\_points_p &= \sum_{d=1}^{14} points_d \cdot \mathbb{I}(on\_duty_d = p) \\ people\_days_p &= \sum_{d=1}^{14} \mathbb{I}(on\_duty_d = p) \\ on\_duty_d &\neq p \quad \forall p, d \in forbidden_p \\ \sum_{i=0}^2 \mathbb{I}(on\_duty_{d+i} = p) &\leq 2 \quad \forall p, d \in \{1, \ldots, 12\} \end{aligned}$ where the constraints ensure that the total differences in points are calculated correctly, each person's points and days are calculated based on their duty assignments, forbidden assignments are respected, and no person is on duty for more than two consecutive days.
objective: Minimize $total\_diffs$ to achieve the fairest possible distribution of Christmas duty among the group of people.
