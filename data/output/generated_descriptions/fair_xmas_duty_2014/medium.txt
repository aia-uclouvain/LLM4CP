Fair Duty Scheduling Problem
The Fair Duty Scheduling Problem is about scheduling duties for a group of people over a period of time, ensuring that the duties are distributed as fairly as possible. 

The main decision variables are `on_duty` and `people_points`. `on_duty` represents the person assigned to be on duty for each day, while `people_points` represents the total points for each person, calculated based on the points associated with each day they are assigned to be on duty.

The key constraints are: 
- Each day must have a person assigned to be on duty.
- A person cannot be assigned to be on duty for more than two consecutive days.
- Certain days are forbidden for certain people, meaning they cannot be assigned to be on duty on those days.
- The points for each day vary, with some days having higher points than others.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the total differences in points between each pair of people, ensuring that the duties are distributed as fairly as possible. This is achieved by minimizing the `total_diffs` variable, which calculates the sum of the absolute differences in points between each pair of people.
