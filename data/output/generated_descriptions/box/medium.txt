Box Design Problem
The Box Design Problem is about designing a box with minimum cost while meeting various requirements such as area, volume, marketing, and aesthetic constraints.

The main decision variables are `d`, `w`, and `h`, which represent the depth, width, and height of the box, respectively. These variables are floats, indicating that the dimensions of the box can be any real number.

The key constraints are:
- The surface area of the box must be at least 888.0, which is represented by the equation `2.0*(h*d + h*w + d*w) >= 888.0`.
- The volume of the box must be at least 1512.0, represented by `h*d*w >= 1512.0`.
- The box's height-to-width ratio must be between 0.518 and 0.718 for aesthetic purposes, enforced by `h/w <= 0.718` and `h/w >= 0.518`.
- The box's footprint, represented by `d*w`, must be less than or equal to 252.0 to meet marketing requirements.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the cost `z`, which is calculated as `2.0*(0.05*(d*w + d*h) + 0.1*w*h)`, representing the total cost of designing the box. The goal is to find the optimal dimensions (`d`, `w`, `h`) that result in the minimum cost `z` while satisfying all the given constraints.
