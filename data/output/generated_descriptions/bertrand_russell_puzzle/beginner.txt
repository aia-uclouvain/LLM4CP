Bertrand Russell Puzzle
The problem is about matching two sets of symbols, {a, b, c, d} and {w, x, y, z}, in the correct order. 
The main variables are the symbols a, b, c, d, w, x, y, and z, which represent the two sets of symbols to be matched.
The basic restrictions are: 
- If a is not matched with x, then c cannot be matched with y.
- If b is matched with either y or z, then a must be matched with x.
- If c is not matched with w, then b must be matched with z.
- If d is matched with y, then b cannot be matched with x.
- If d is not matched with x, then b must be matched with x.
The goal of the problem is to find the correct matching between the two sets of symbols, {a, b, c, d} and {w, x, y, z}.
