Voltage Divider Problem
The Voltage Divider Problem is about finding the optimal configuration of a voltage divider circuit, which consists of two resistors connected in series, given certain constraints on the input voltage, output voltage, and current.

The main decision variables are: 
- V: the input voltage 
- I: the total current 
- R1 and R2: the resistances of the two resistors 
- VD: the output voltage 
- ID: the current through the output resistor

The key constraints are: 
- The voltage divider equation must hold, which relates the input voltage, output voltage, and the two resistances 
- The output voltage must be within a certain range (5.4 to 5.5 volts) 
- The current through the output resistor is fixed at 0.1 amps 
- The input voltage must be one of two possible values (9 or 12 volts) 
- The resistances of the two resistors must be chosen from a set of three possible values (5, 9, or 14 ohms)

The objective of the problem is to find a configuration that satisfies all the constraints, with no specific objective function to minimize or maximize, as the goal is to find a feasible solution.
