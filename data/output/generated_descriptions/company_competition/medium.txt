**Team Formation Problem**
The problem is about forming teams of employees with certain constraints to ensure a balanced and diverse team composition.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables are:
- `team`: a set of employees assigned to each team
- `team_departments`: the number of employees from each department in each team
- `team_sex`: the number of employees of each sex in each team
- `team_size`: the size of each team
- `which_team`: the team assigned to each employee

**Constraints**
The key constraints are:
- Each team should have a similar size, with a maximum difference of one employee
- Each team should have an even distribution of sexes and departments
- Managers should be assigned to different teams if possible
- Employees can only be assigned to one team
- The number of employees in each team from each department and sex should be consistent with the overall team size

**Objective**
The objective is to minimize the number of violations of the constraints, specifically:
- The difference in the number of males and females in each team
- The difference in the number of employees from each department in each team
- The number of managers assigned to the same team

In other words, the goal is to form teams that are as balanced and diverse as possible, while also separating managers and respecting the constraints on team size and composition.
