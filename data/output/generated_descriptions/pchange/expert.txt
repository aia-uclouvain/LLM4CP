name: Price Change Puzzle
description: The Price Change Puzzle is a classic problem in constraint programming where three dealers sell a certain number of items at two different prices. The goal is to find the prices and the number of items sold by each dealer at each price, given the total number of items sold by each dealer and the constraint that the total revenue from the first dealer is equal to the total revenue from the other two dealers, with the additional constraint that the second price is at least one unit higher than the first price.
variables: $x_{i,j}, p_j$ where $i \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ and $j \in \{1, 2\}$. These variables represent the number of items sold by dealer $i$ at price $j$ ($x_{i,j}$) and the price $j$ ($p_j$).
constraints: $\forall i \in M, j \in N: x_{i,j} \leq nsold_i \wedge x_{i,j} \geq 0$, $\forall i \in M: nsold_i = \sum_{j \in N} x_{i,j}$, $\forall k \in Q: \sum_{j \in N} p_j \cdot x_{1,j} = \sum_{j \in N} p_j \cdot x_{k,j}$, $p_2 \geq p_1 + 1$. These constraints ensure that the number of items sold by each dealer at each price does not exceed the total number of items sold by that dealer, that the total number of items sold by each dealer is equal to the sum of the items sold at each price, that the total revenue from the first dealer is equal to the total revenue from the other dealers, and that the second price is at least one unit higher than the first price.
