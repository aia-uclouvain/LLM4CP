Price Change Puzzle
The problem is about determining the prices at which three dealers sold a certain number of items, given that the total number of items sold by each dealer is known, and that the total revenue from the first dealer is equal to the total revenue from the other two dealers, with the constraint that the second price is at least one more than the first price.

The main decision variables are x, a 2D array representing the number of items sold by each dealer at each price, and p, a 1D array representing the two possible prices. The variable x[i,j] represents the number of items sold by dealer i at price j, and p[j] represents the jth price.

The key constraints are: 
- each dealer can only sell a number of items at each price that is less than or equal to the total number of items they sold, 
- the total number of items sold by each dealer is the sum of the items sold at each price, 
- the total revenue from the first dealer is equal to the total revenue from each of the other dealers, 
- and the second price is at least one more than the first price.

The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, which is achieved by using the "solve satisfy" statement in the model. The goal is to determine the prices and the number of items sold by each dealer at each price, given the total number of items sold by each dealer and the constraints on the prices and revenues.
