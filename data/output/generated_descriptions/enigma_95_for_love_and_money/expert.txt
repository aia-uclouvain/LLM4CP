name: Enigma 95: For love and money
description: Amanda has seven suitors and wants to find the perfect one by evaluating them based on their passion and earning power. She assigns a score out of 50 for each criterion and then totals the scores. The problem states that there are no ties in either list, and when the scores are totalled, the suitors come out in alphabetical order. The task is to reproduce Amanda's marks for each suitor.
variables: $love = (l_1, l_2, ..., l_7), money = (m_1, m_2, ..., m_7), total = (t_1, t_2, ..., t_7)$, where $l_i, m_i, t_i$ are the scores for love, money, and total for each suitor, respectively. These variables represent the decision variables in the problem, which are the scores assigned to each suitor for love, money, and the total score.
constraints: $\begin{aligned}
&alldifferent(love) \\
&alldifferent(money) \\
&alldifferent(total) \\
&decreasing(total) \\
&total_i = love_i + money_i \quad \forall i \\
&placed(love, Aloysius, 4) \\
&placed(money, Aloysius, 4) \\
&total[Bertrand] = 40 \\
&placed(love, Cuthbert, 2) \\
&money[Desmond] = 11 \\
&love[Egbert] = 15 \\
&placed(love, Ferdie, 5) \\
&money[Ferdie] = 20 \\
&placed(money, Geoffrey, 6) \\
&\exists i \in \{1, ..., 7\} \quad money_i = love[Cuthbert]
\end{aligned}$, where these constraints represent the conditions given in the problem, such as no ties in either list, the total score being the sum of love and money scores, and the specific scores and rankings for each suitor.
objective: The objective of this problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, as it is a constraint satisfaction problem. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
