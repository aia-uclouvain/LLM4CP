name: The Day of the Week Problem
description: Seven friends have different opinions about the current day of the week. Each friend makes a statement about the day, and only one statement is correct. The task is to determine the day of the week when the friends met.
variables: $today \in \{mon, tue, wed, thu, fri, sat, sun\}, truth = (truth_1, truth_2, ..., truth_7)$, where $today$ represents the current day of the week and $truth_i$ represents whether the $i^{th}$ friend's statement is true or not.
constraints: $(truth_1 = 1 \leftrightarrow today = tue) \wedge (truth_2 = 1 \leftrightarrow today = enum\_prev(days, tue)) \wedge (truth_3 = 1 \leftrightarrow today = enum\_next(days, tue)) \wedge (truth_4 = 1 \leftrightarrow today = enum\_next(days, sat)) \wedge (truth_5 = 1 \leftrightarrow today \in \{thu, fri\}) \wedge (truth_6 = 1 \leftrightarrow today = sun) \wedge (truth_7 = 1 \leftrightarrow today \neq sun) \wedge \sum_{i=1}^{7} truth_i = 1$, which represent the truthfulness of each friend's statement and the constraint that only one friend's statement is correct.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, i.e., to find a day of the week that makes only one friend's statement true.
