name: Enigma 1576 (The holly and the ivy and the...)
description: The problem is about designing a 4x4 Christmas card with 16 squares, each containing one of four pictures (holly, ivy, mistletoe, and fir trees) and one of four background colors (gold, silver, red, and white). The goal is to find the arrangement of the squares such that each row and column contains one square of each picture and one square of each background color, and certain conditions are met, such as the holly on a gold background touching the ivy on a silver background, and no two mistletoe squares touching.
variables: $plants_{i,j}, colors_{i,j}, pairs_{i,j,k}$, where $plants_{i,j}$ and $colors_{i,j}$ are the decision variables representing the picture and background color of the square in the $i^{th}$ row and $j^{th}$ column, and $pairs_{i,j,k}$ is a auxiliary variable used to show the pairs of pictures and colors.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..n, \forall j \in 1..n: plants_{i,j} \in Plants \wedge colors_{i,j} \in Colors$, $latin\_square(plants)$, $latin\_square(colors)$, $all\_pairs(plants, colors)$, $cp1d([colors[1,j] | j in 1..n], [Gold,Silver,Red,White])$, $\exists i \in 1..n: plants[i,1] = FirTree \wedge colors[i,1] = White$, $\exists i,j \in 1..n, a,b \in \{-1,0,1\}: plants[i,j] = Holly \wedge colors[i,j] = Gold \wedge plants[i+a,j+b] = Ivy \wedge colors[i+a,j+b] = Silver$, $\forall i \in 2..n: \neg \exists j,k \in 1..n: plants[i,j] = Mistletoe \wedge plants[i-1,k] = Mistletoe \wedge |j-k| \leq 1$, which represent the constraints that each square contains a valid picture and color, each row and column contains one square of each picture and color, all pairs of pictures and colors are present, the top row has the specified colors, one row starts with a fir tree on a white background, the holly on a gold background touches the ivy on a silver background, and no two mistletoe squares touch.
objective: satisfy, which means the objective is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
