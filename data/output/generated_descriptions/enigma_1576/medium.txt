The problem is called the "Holly and the Ivy" problem. 
It is about arranging 16 squares, each with a picture of holly, ivy, mistletoe, or fir trees, and one of four background colors (gold, silver, red, or white), into a 4x4 grid such that each row and column contains one square of each plant and one square of each background color.

The main decision variables are `plants` and `colors`, which are 4x4 arrays representing the plant and background color of each square in the grid. 

The key constraints are: 
- each row and column must contain one square of each plant and one square of each background color, 
- the top row must have the colors gold, silver, red, and white from left to right, 
- one row must start with a fir tree on a white background, 
- the holly on a gold background must touch the ivy on a silver background, 
- no two mistletoe squares can touch, not even at their corners.

The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a feasible arrangement of the squares that satisfies all the given constraints.
