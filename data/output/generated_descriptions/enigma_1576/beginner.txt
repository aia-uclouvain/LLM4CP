The problem is called "The holly and the ivy and the..." or Enigma 1576. 
It's about arranging 16 squares on a Christmas card in a 4x4 grid. 
Each square has a picture of one of four plants (holly, ivy, mistletoe, or fir tree) and one of four background colors (gold, silver, red, or white). 

The main variables are the plants and colors on each square. 
The plants variable represents which plant is on each square, and the colors variable represents which background color is on each square.

The basic restrictions are that each row and column must have one square of each plant and one square of each background color. 
The top row must have the colors gold, silver, red, and white from left to right. 
The holly on a gold background must touch the ivy on a silver background, and no two mistletoe squares can touch. 
One row must start with a fir tree on a white background.

The goal is to find the arrangement of plants and colors that satisfies all these restrictions, specifically to determine the other three squares in the row that starts with a fir tree on a white background.
