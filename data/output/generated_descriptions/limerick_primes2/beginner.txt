Limerick Primes problem. 
This problem is about finding special prime numbers that follow the rhythm of a limerick, a type of humorous poem. 
The main variables are p (a prime number), x (a set of digits), A (an odd digit), and B (any digit that is not A). In simple terms, p is the prime number we're looking for, x is the set of digits that make up p, A is the repeating odd digit, and B is the other digit that appears in p. 
The basic restrictions are that p must be a prime number, it must have a specific pattern of digits (AABBA), A must be an odd digit, and B must be different from A. 
The goal of the problem is to find a prime number p that satisfies these conditions, specifically to find a number that is both prime and follows the limerick rhythm.
