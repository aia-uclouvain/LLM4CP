name: Survivor Problem
description: The Survivor Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming where a hiker needs to select one item from each of four categories (food, water, shelter, and defense) to take on a journey, given a limited backpack capacity of 25 kg. Each item has a weight and survival points associated with it. The goal is to maximize the total survival points while not exceeding the backpack capacity.
variables: $x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4, sum\_weight, sum\_survival\_points$ - These variables represent the decision variables in the problem. $x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4$ are the indices of the selected items from the food, water, shelter, and defense categories, respectively. $sum\_weight$ is the total weight of the selected items, and $sum\_survival\_points$ is the total survival points of the selected items.
constraints: $sum\_weight = food[x_1,1]+water[x_2,1]+shelter[x_3,1]+defence[x_4,1]$ and $sum\_weight \leq max\_cap$ and $sum\_survival\_points = food[x_1,2]+water[x_2,2]+shelter[x_3,2]+defence[x_4,2]$ - These constraints ensure that the total weight of the selected items does not exceed the backpack capacity, and that the total survival points are calculated correctly.
objective: Maximize $sum\_survival\_points$ - The objective of the problem is to maximize the total survival points of the selected items, which represents the hiker's chance of survival.
