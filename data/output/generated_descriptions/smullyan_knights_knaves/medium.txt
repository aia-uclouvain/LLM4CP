Smullyan Knights and Knaves problem
The Smullyan Knights and Knaves problem is a classic logic puzzle where some people are either knights, who always tell the truth, or knaves, who always lie. 
The main decision variables in this problem are the types of people, represented by the array `p`, where each element can be either a knight (1) or a knave (2).
The key constraints in this problem are based on the statements made by each person. For example, if a person says that another person is a knave, then either the speaker is a knight and the other person is indeed a knave, or the speaker is a knave and the other person is actually a knight. These constraints are modeled using the `says` predicate, which ensures that the statements made by each person are consistent with their type.
The objective of this problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a consistent assignment of types to each person that satisfies all the given constraints. In other words, the goal is to determine who is a knight and who is a knave, based on the statements they make about each other. The solution is then output in the form of an array `p`, where each element represents the type of the corresponding person.
