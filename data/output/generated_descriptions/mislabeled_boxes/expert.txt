name: Mislabeled Boxes Problem
description: The problem involves three boxes labeled as containing apples, oranges, and bananas. However, none of the boxes contain what their labels say. The label on box a says "apples", the label on box b says "oranges", and the label on box c says "bananas". It is given that box b contains apples. The goal is to determine what the other two boxes contain.
variables: $x = [x_1, x_2, x_3]$, where $x_i$ represents the content of box $i$, which can be either apples (1), oranges (2), or bananas (3).
constraints: $x_2 = 1 \land \text{all\_different}(x) \land \forall i \in \{1, 2, 3\} (x_i \neq \text{labels}_i) \land \neg \exists i \in \{1, 2, 3\} (x_i = i)$, where the first constraint ensures that box b contains apples, the second constraint ensures that all boxes contain different contents, the third constraint ensures that no box contains what its label says, and the fourth constraint ensures that there is no box that contains the content corresponding to its index.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of contents to the boxes, which is a feasibility problem and does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
