name: Elevator Puzzle
description: The Elevator Puzzle is a classic problem in the field of constraint programming. It involves finding the optimal sequence of stops for an elevator in a building with multiple floors, given a set of requests from passengers to travel between floors. The goal is to minimize the total time spent by the elevator in serving all the requests.
variables: $x_{ij}$, $y_{ij}$, $z_i$, where $x_{ij}$ represents whether the elevator travels from floor $i$ to floor $j$, $y_{ij}$ represents the number of passengers traveling from floor $i$ to floor $j$, and $z_i$ represents the time spent by the elevator at floor $i$. These variables determine the sequence of stops and the number of passengers served at each stop.
constraints: $\sum_{j} x_{ij} = 1$ for each floor $i$, $\sum_{i} x_{ij} = 1$ for each floor $j$, $y_{ij} \leq x_{ij}$ for each pair of floors $i$ and $j$, $\sum_{j} y_{ij} = \sum_{j} c_{ij}$ for each floor $i$, where $c_{ij}$ is the number of requests from floor $i$ to floor $j$. These constraints ensure that the elevator visits each floor exactly once, serves all the requests, and follows a valid sequence of stops.
objective: Minimize $\sum_{i} z_i$, which represents the total time spent by the elevator in serving all the requests. The objective is to find the sequence of stops that minimizes the total time spent by the elevator.
