name: Wine Cask Puzzle

description: The Wine Cask Puzzle is a classic problem in constraint programming. The problem states that there are 5 nephews and 5 types of casks, each with a different capacity. The goal is to distribute the casks among the nephews such that each nephew has a total volume of 4.5 units and each cask type is distributed evenly among the nephews. Additionally, the total volume of the casks for each nephew must be a unique integer value, and the volume for each subsequent nephew must be less than the previous one.

variables: $x_{i,j}, dtot_i$ 
These are the decision variables where $x_{i,j}$ represents the number of casks of type $j$ assigned to nephew $i$, and $dtot_i$ represents the total volume of casks assigned to nephew $i$.

constraints: 
$\forall i \in N: \sum_{j \in C} x_{i,j} = 9$
$\forall i \in N: \sum_{j \in C} howfull_j \cdot x_{i,j} = 4.5$
$\forall j \in C: \sum_{i \in N} x_{i,j} = 9$
$\forall i \in N: \sum_{j \in C} cdum_j \cdot x_{i,j} = dtot_i$
$\forall i \in 2..nephew: dtot_{i-1} - dtot_i \geq 1$
These constraints ensure that each nephew has 9 casks, the total volume for each nephew is 4.5 units, each cask type is distributed evenly, the total volume for each nephew is calculated correctly, and the total volume for each subsequent nephew is less than the previous one.

objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
