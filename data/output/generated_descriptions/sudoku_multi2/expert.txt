name: Multi Sudoku
description: The Multi Sudoku problem is a variant of the classic Sudoku puzzle where multiple Sudoku boards overlap. The goal is to fill in the missing numbers in each board such that each board is a valid Sudoku solution and the overlapping regions are consistent across all boards.
variables: $s1_{1..9,1..9}, s2_{1..9,1..9}$, where $s1$ and $s2$ are 9x9 matrices representing the two Sudoku boards, with each cell $s1_{i,j}$ and $s2_{i,j}$ being a decision variable that takes a value from 1 to 9, representing the number to be placed in the corresponding cell.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..9: \text{alldifferent}([s1_{i,j} | j \in 1..9]) \land \text{alldifferent}([s1_{j,i} | j \in 1..9]) \land \text{alldifferent}([s2_{i,j} | j \in 1..9]) \land \text{alldifferent}([s2_{j,i} | j \in 1..9]) \land \forall i,j \in 0..2: \text{alldifferent}([s1_{r,c} | r \in i*3+1..i*3+3, c \in j*3+1..j*3+3]) \land \text{alldifferent}([s2_{r,c} | r \in i*3+1..i*3+3, c \in j*3+1..j*3+3])$, which ensure that each row, column, and 3x3 sub-grid in both boards contains each number from 1 to 9 exactly once, and $s2_{7..9,7..9} = s1_{7..9,7..9}$, which ensures that the overlapping region is consistent between the two boards.
objective: satisfy, meaning that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, without any specific objective function to minimize or maximize.
