name: Muddle Management Problem
description: The Muddle Management Problem is a scheduling problem where six managers need to attend a series of meetings on different aspects of the company's activities. Each manager has to attend three or four meetings, and the meetings have certain precedence constraints. The goal is to schedule the meetings in the smallest possible number of timeslots.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_8, z$ - These variables represent the time slots for each meeting ($x_1$ to $x_8$) and the maximum timeslot ($z$) used in the schedule. The $x_i$ variables determine when each meeting will take place, and the $z$ variable keeps track of the maximum timeslot used.
constraints: $x_{sales} < x_{marketing}$, $x_{production} < x_{timetables}$, $x_{quality} < x_{timetables}$, $all\_different([x_m | m \in required_p])$ for each manager $p$ - These constraints ensure that the meetings are scheduled according to the given precedence constraints and that each manager attends their required meetings in different timeslots.
objective: Minimize $z$ - The objective is to minimize the maximum timeslot $z$ used in the schedule, which corresponds to the smallest possible number of timeslots needed to schedule all the meetings.
