Meeting Scheduling Problem
The Meeting Scheduling Problem is about scheduling a series of meetings for six managers with different attendance requirements, while respecting certain precedence constraints and minimizing the number of timeslots needed.

The main decision variables are the time slots for each meeting, represented by the array `x`, where `x[m]` denotes the time slot assigned to meeting `m`. The variable `z` represents the maximum time slot used, which is the objective to be minimized.

The key constraints are:
- Each manager must attend their required meetings in different time slots.
- The meeting on sales must happen before the one on marketing.
- Both production and quality control meetings must happen before the timetabling meeting.
- Each meeting can be assigned to any available time slot, but the same time slot cannot be used for two meetings that a manager is required to attend.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the maximum time slot used, `z`, which represents the smallest possible number of timeslots needed to schedule all the meetings while satisfying the constraints. In other words, the goal is to find a schedule that allows the managers to finish their meetings as early as possible, so they can attend their golf game after lunch.
