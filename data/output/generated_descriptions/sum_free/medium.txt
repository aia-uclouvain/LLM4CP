The problem is called the Sum-Free Set problem. 
It is about finding a set of numbers where no two numbers in the set add up to another number in the set. 
The main decision variables are the elements of the array x, which represent the numbers in the set. 
The key constraints are that for any two numbers in the set, their sum cannot be another number in the set, and for any two numbers in the set, their product cannot be another number in the set. Additionally, each number can appear at most three times in the set, and the sum of all numbers in the set must be 100. 
The problem's objective is to find a set that satisfies all these constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement.
