Nursing Workload Problem
The problem is about assigning nurses to patients in a way that balances the workload of each nurse, while ensuring that each patient is assigned to a nurse and that the number of patients per nurse is within a specified range.

The main decision variables are `p` and `w`, where `p[i]` represents the nurse assigned to the `i-th` patient, and `w[k]` represents the workload of the `k-th` nurse.

The key constraints are:
- Each nurse must be assigned a number of patients within a specified range (between `minPatientsPerNurse` and `maxPatientsPerNurse`).
- Patients from the same zone must be assigned to different nurses.
- The workload of each nurse is calculated as the sum of the demands of the patients assigned to that nurse.
- The workload of the nurses must be in increasing order, to ensure a balanced distribution of workload.
- The first `nZones` patients are assigned to the first `nZones` nurses, to break symmetry in the solution.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the sum of the squares of the workloads of all nurses, which represents a measure of the overall variability in nurse workloads. By minimizing this objective, the model aims to find an assignment of nurses to patients that balances the workload of each nurse as much as possible.
