Nursing Workload Problem
The problem is about assigning nurses to patients in a hospital while making sure that each nurse has a fair and manageable workload. 
The main variables are the nurse assigned to each patient and the workload of each nurse. In simple terms, these variables mean: 
- which nurse is taking care of a particular patient
- how much work a nurse has, based on the number and needs of the patients they are assigned to.
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- each nurse should have at least a minimum and at most a maximum number of patients
- patients from the same zone should have different nurses
- the workload of each nurse should be calculated based on the needs of the patients they are assigned to
- the workload of nurses should increase in a specific order.
The goal of the problem is to minimize the total workload of all nurses, which means finding an assignment of nurses to patients that keeps the workload as balanced and fair as possible.
