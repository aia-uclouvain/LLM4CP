War or Peace problem
The War or Peace problem is about determining the minimum number of peace treaties required between countries under certain conditions. 
The main decision variables in this problem are represented by a 2D array `x`, where `x[i,j]` denotes the relationship between country `i` and country `j`, which can be either at war (`war = 0`) or have a peace treaty (`peace = 1`). 
The key constraints are that each pair of countries is either at war or has a peace treaty, and if two countries have a common enemy, they must have a peace treaty. This means that for any two countries `i` and `j` that are at war, all other countries `k` must either have a peace treaty with `i` or `j`. 
The objective of the problem is to minimize the total number of peace treaties, represented by the variable `countpeaces`, which is the sum of all `x[i,j]` values that equal `peace`.
