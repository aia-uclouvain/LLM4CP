The problem is known as the Circle Packing or Intersection Problem. 
It is about finding the points of intersection between two circles, given their equations. 
The main decision variables are X and Y, which represent the coordinates of the point of intersection. 
The key constraints are that the point (X, Y) lies on both circles, meaning the distance from the center of each circle to the point is equal to the radius of the circle. 
The problem's objective is to find a point that satisfies both constraints, which is a feasibility problem, not an optimization problem, hence the "solve satisfy" statement, indicating that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a particular objective function.
