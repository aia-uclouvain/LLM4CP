name: Sortedness Problem
description: The problem is to find a permutation of a given array of integers such that the resulting array is sorted, while also satisfying certain constraints on the original and sorted arrays.
variables: $x = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_n], s = [s_1, s_2, ..., s_n], p = [p_1, p_2, ..., p_n]$ where $x$ is the original array, $s$ is the sorted array, and $p$ is the permutation of the indices of $x$ to obtain $s$.
constraints: $\text{sortedness}(x, s, p) \land \text{alldifferent}(x) \land x_2 = 5 \land s_4 = 10 \land \text{cp1d}(x, [29, 5, 21, 27, 10, 8, 23, 12, 9, 11])$ where $\text{sortedness}(x, s, p)$ enforces that $s$ is the sorted version of $x$ and $p$ is the permutation of the indices of $x$ to obtain $s$, $\text{alldifferent}(x)$ ensures that all elements of $x$ are distinct, $x_2 = 5$ and $s_4 = 10$ are specific constraints on the elements of $x$ and $s$, and $\text{cp1d}(x, [29, 5, 21, 27, 10, 8, 23, 12, 9, 11])$ is a constraint that checks if $x$ is equal to a specific array.
objective: satisfy
