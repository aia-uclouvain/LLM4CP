The problem is called Sortedness. 
It is about finding a permutation of an array of integers such that the resulting array is sorted, while also satisfying certain constraints on the original and sorted arrays. 
The main decision variables are x, s, and p, where x represents the original array of integers, s represents the sorted version of x, and p represents the permutation of the indices in x to obtain the sorted version. 
The key constraints are that s must be the sorted version of x, the array p must be a valid permutation of the indices in x, and certain elements in x and s must have specific values. 
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, with no specific objective function being minimized or maximized, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement.
