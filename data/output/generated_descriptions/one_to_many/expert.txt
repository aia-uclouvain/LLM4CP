name: One-To-Many Relationship Extraction Problem
description: The problem involves extracting one-to-many relationships between mothers and children from a set of projects, where each project has a list of mothers and children, but the relationships between them are not explicitly stated. The goal is to assign each child to its corresponding mother based on the given projects.
variables: $x[c]$ for $c \in \text{children}$, where $x[c]$ represents the mother of child $c$. These variables are used to determine the one-to-many relationships between mothers and children.
constraints: 
$\forall m \in \text{mothers} \exists c \in \text{children} (x[c] = m)$ 
$\text{check}(\text{mothers}_p, \text{children}_p)$ for each project $p$, 
where $\text{check}(\text{mothers}_p, \text{children}_p)$ ensures that each child in the project is assigned to a mother in the project, and all mothers in the project are assigned to a child. 
These constraints ensure that each mother has at least one child, and the relationships between mothers and children are consistent across all projects.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of mothers to children, such that all constraints are met. Since this is a satisfaction problem, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
