The Four Number Problem
The Four Number Problem is about finding three numbers that can be added together in different combinations to generate four given numbers.
The main decision variables are `x`, an array of three variables representing the three numbers to be found, and `r`, an array of four given numbers.
The key constraints are that each of the four given numbers can be expressed as a sum of a combination of the three numbers `x`, and that the three numbers `x` are distinct and in increasing order.
The objective of the problem is to find a set of three numbers `x` that satisfy the constraints, with no specific minimization or maximization goal, as the problem is a satisfaction problem.
