The Einstein's Puzzle or Houses Problem
The problem is about determining the residence of five pairs of people in eight houses, based on given constraints about their relative house positions and relationships.
The main decision variables are the house numbers of each person: allison, belinda, adrienne, benito, cheri, crawford, daryl, and don, which represent the house where each person lives.
The key constraints are: 
- Allison lives next to Adrienne and in a house greater than 2.
- Belinda lives two doors away from Benito and in a house greater than 5.
- Cheri lives three doors away from Crawford and in a house greater than Benito's.
- Daryl lives four doors away from Don and in a house less than 4.
- Each person lives in a different house.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of people to houses, such that all constraints are met, which is achieved by using the "solve satisfy" statement in the Minizinc model.
