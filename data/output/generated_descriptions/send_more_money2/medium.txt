The Send More Money problem.
This problem is about finding a solution to a cryptarithm, where letters represent digits, and the goal is to make a mathematical equation valid.
The main decision variables are S, E, N, D, M, O, R, and Y, which represent the digits from 0 to 9 that each letter corresponds to.
The key constraints are that each letter must represent a unique digit, and when the numbers represented by the letters are added together, they must satisfy the equation: SEND + MORE = MONEY. This means that when adding the corresponding digits, any carry-over from one column to the next must be taken into account. Additionally, the first letter of each number (S and M) must not be zero.
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding a valid assignment of digits to letters that makes the equation true. The solve statement "solve satisfy" indicates that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
