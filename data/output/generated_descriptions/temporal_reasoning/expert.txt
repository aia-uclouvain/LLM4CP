name: Meeting Scheduling Problem
description: The problem describes a meeting scenario where several people, including Mr. Jones, Ms. White, Director Smith, and Mr. Brown, attend a meeting that runs non-stop for a whole day. Each person stays for a continuous period, and there are specific constraints on their arrival and departure times relative to each other and the meeting. The goal is to determine if Mr. Jones and Ms. White could have talked during the meeting.
variables: $J1, J2, M1, M2, B1, B2, S1, S2, W1, W2$ - These variables represent the start and end times of each person's attendance and the meeting, where $J1$ and $J2$ are the start and end times of Mr. Jones, $M1$ and $M2$ are the start and end times of the meeting, $B1$ and $B2$ are the start and end times of Mr. Brown, $S1$ and $S2$ are the start and end times of Director Smith, and $W1$ and $W2$ are the start and end times of Ms. White.
constraints: $(J1 < M1 \wedge M1 < J2) \wedge overlaps(M1, M2, W1, W2) \wedge real\_overlap(M1, M2, S1, S2) \wedge before(J1, J2, S1, S2) \wedge real\_overlap(B1, B2, S1, S2) \wedge real\_overlap(B1, B2, W1, W2) \wedge real\_overlap(S1, S2, W1, W2) \wedge weak\_overlap(J1, J2, W1, W2)$ - These constraints represent the relationships between the start and end times of each person and the meeting, including the meeting and Jones, the meeting and White, the meeting and Smith, Jones and Smith, Brown and Smith, Brown and White, Smith and White, and the possibility of Jones and White talking during the meeting.
objective: minimize $J2 + W2$ - The objective is to minimize the sum of the end times of Mr. Jones and Ms. White, which represents the total time they spend at the meeting.
