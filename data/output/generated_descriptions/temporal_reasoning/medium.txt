Meeting Scheduling Problem
The problem is about determining whether two individuals, Jones and White, could have talked during a meeting, given certain constraints on the arrival and departure times of various people, including Jones, White, Brown, Smith, and the meeting itself.

The main decision variables are the start and end times of each person's presence at the meeting, represented by variables such as J1, J2, M1, M2, B1, B2, S1, S2, W1, and W2. These variables represent the time intervals during which each person is present at the meeting.

The key constraints are:
- Jones arrives before the meeting starts and leaves before the meeting ends.
- White arrives after the meeting starts and is present when the meeting ends.
- Smith arrives after Jones leaves.
- Brown talks to White in the presence of Smith, implying that their time intervals overlap.
- The meeting starts while Jones is present and ends while White is present.
- Jones and White may have talked during the meeting if their time intervals overlap.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the time interval during which Jones and White could have talked, represented by the variables J2 and W2. The problem is solved using a satisfaction or minimization approach, where the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints or minimizes the time interval for Jones and White.
