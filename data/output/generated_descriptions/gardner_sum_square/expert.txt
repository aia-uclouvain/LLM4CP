name: Gardner's Sum Square problem
description: The problem is to find the minimum possible value of the sum of the squares of the sums of two sets of four distinct numbers, where the numbers are chosen from the set {-7,-5,-3,-2,2,4,6,13}. The goal is to partition the set of numbers into two subsets, each containing four numbers, such that the sum of the squares of the sums of the numbers in each subset is minimized.
variables: a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h, x = [a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h], V - These are the decision variables, where a, b, c, d, e, f, g, and h are distinct elements in the set {-7,-5,-3,-2,2,4,6,13}, x is an array of these variables, and V is the objective value to be minimized, representing the sum of the squares of the sums of the two sets of numbers.
constraints: alldifferent(x), V = (a+b+c+d)*(a+b+c+d) + (e+f+g+h)*(e+f+g+h), increasing([a,b,c,d]), increasing([e,f,g,h]), a < e - These constraints ensure that the numbers in each subset are distinct, the objective value V is calculated correctly, the numbers in each subset are in increasing order to break symmetry, and the first number in the first subset is less than the first number in the second subset to further break symmetry.
objective: minimize V, which represents the sum of the squares of the sums of the two sets of numbers.
