name: Student-Company Interview Assignment Problem
description: The problem involves assigning students to companies for interviews, taking into account the students' preferences and the companies' capacity limits. The goal is to find an assignment that minimizes the total regret of the students, where regret is defined as the difference between the student's preference value and the best possible assignment.
variables: $x_{ijk}$, where $x_{ijk} = 1$ if student $i$ is assigned to company $j$ for interview $k$, and $0$ otherwise. These variables represent the assignment of students to companies for each interview.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{ijk} = 1 \forall i, k$ (each student is assigned to exactly one company for each interview), $x_{ijk} \neq x_{ij(k+1)} \forall i, j, k$ (each student is assigned to a different company for each interview), $\sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{k=1}^{p} x_{ijk} \leq c_j \forall j$ (the number of students assigned to each company does not exceed its capacity limit), $x_{ijk} \in \{0,1\} \forall i, j, k$ (the assignment variables are binary). 
objective: Minimize the total regret of the students, which is calculated as the sum of the preference values of the assigned companies minus the best possible assignment. The objective function is a weighted sum of the regret and a secondary objective that minimizes the maximum regret across all students.
