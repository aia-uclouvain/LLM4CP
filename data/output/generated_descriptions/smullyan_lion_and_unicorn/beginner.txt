Smullyan's Lion and Unicorn problem. 
This problem is about figuring out what day of the week it is, based on statements made by a lion and a unicorn. The lion lies on Mondays, Tuesdays, and Wednesdays, while the unicorn lies on Thursdays, Fridays, and Saturdays. 
The main variables are "today" which represents the current day of the week, "lion" and "unicorn" which represent whether the lion or unicorn is telling the truth on each day. 
The basic restrictions are that the lion and unicorn have specific days when they lie, and they make statements about what happened yesterday or what will happen tomorrow. 
The goal is to find the day of the week that satisfies the statements made by the lion, which involves determining the value of "today".
