Smullyan's Lion and Unicorn problem
The problem is about determining the current day of the week based on statements made by a lion and a unicorn, who have different lying days.
The main decision variable is "today", which represents the current day of the week, with Monday being day 0 and Sunday being day 6. The arrays "lion" and "unicorn" represent the truthfulness of the lion and unicorn on each day of the week.
The key constraints are based on the statements made by the lion and the unicorn. For example, the lion says "Yesterday was one of my lying days" and the unicorn says the same. The constraints ensure that the statements are consistent with the truthfulness of the lion and unicorn on each day. In plain language, the constraints check if the statements made by the lion and unicorn are true or false based on their lying days.
The problem's objective is to find a day of the week that satisfies the given constraints, which means finding a day when the statements made by the lion and unicorn are consistent with their lying days. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective, but rather finding a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
