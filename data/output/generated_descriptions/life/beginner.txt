Conway's Game of Life
The problem is about simulating the behavior of living cells in a grid, where each cell can be either alive or dead. 
The main variables are the grid size (n), the number of generations (m), and the state of each cell (x) at each generation. 
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- a live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies, 
- a live cell with more than three live neighbors dies, 
- a live cell with two or three live neighbors stays alive, 
- a dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes alive. 
The goal of the problem is to simulate the evolution of the grid over multiple generations, following these rules.
