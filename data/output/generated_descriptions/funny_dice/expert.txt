name: Funny Dice Problem
description: The problem involves two non-standard cubic dice, one red and one blue, each with positive whole numbers on each face. The total of the six numbers on the red die is higher than the same total for the blue die. The problem requires finding the numbers on the red dice given the probabilities of the sums of the numbers on the two dice when thrown.
variables: $r_1, r_2, ..., r_6, b_1, b_2, ..., b_6, p_2, p_3, ..., p_12$ - These variables represent the numbers on each face of the red and blue dice ($r_i$ and $b_i$) and the probabilities of the sums of the numbers on the two dice ($p_k$), where $r_i$ and $b_i$ are the numbers on the $i^{th}$ face of the red and blue dice, and $p_k$ is the probability of getting a sum of $k$ when the two dice are thrown.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{6} r_i > \sum_{i=1}^{6} b_i, p_k = \sum_{i=1}^{6} \sum_{j=1}^{6} (r_i + b_j == k), p_i = p_{12-i+2}, p_i < p_{i+1}, p_7 = \max(p_k), r_i \geq 1, b_i \geq 1, p_k \geq 1$ - These constraints ensure that the total of the numbers on the red die is higher than the total on the blue die, calculate the probabilities of the sums of the numbers on the two dice, enforce the given relationships between the probabilities, and ensure that all numbers are positive.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
