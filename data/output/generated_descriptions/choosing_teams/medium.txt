Team Formation Problem
The Team Formation Problem is about dividing a group of people into teams of equal size, where the order of people within each team and the order of teams do not matter.
The main decision variables are represented by a 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` represents the person assigned to the `j-th` position in the `i-th` team. The value of `x[i, j]` ranges from 1 to `p`, where `p` is the total number of people.
The key constraints are: 
- all people must be assigned to a team, and no person can be in more than one team, which is ensured by the `all_different` constraint.
- the order of people within each team does not matter, which is addressed by the `increasing` constraint that ensures people in each team are in ascending order.
- the order of teams does not matter, which is handled by the `lex2` constraint that ensures teams are in lexicographic order.
The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find all possible ways to form teams of equal size from a given group of people, while satisfying the given constraints. The model uses the `solve satisfy` statement to find all feasible solutions.
