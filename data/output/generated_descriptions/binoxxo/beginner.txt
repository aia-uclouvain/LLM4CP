Binoxxo problem
The Binoxxo problem is about filling a grid with X's and O's in a specific way. 
The main variables are the X's and O's in the grid, which can be thought of as a big piece of paper with many squares. 
The basic restrictions are that no more than two X's or O's can be together in a row or column, and each row and column must have the same number of X's and O's. Also, no row or column can be exactly the same as another one. 
The goal of the problem is to fill the grid in a way that satisfies all these rules, without trying to minimize or maximize anything, just finding a solution that works.
