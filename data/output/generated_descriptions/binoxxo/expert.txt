name: Binoxxo problem

description: The Binoxxo problem is a constraint satisfaction problem where the goal is to fill a square board with X's and O's in such a way that there are no more than two consecutive X's or O's in a row or a column, the number of X's is the same as the number of O's in each row and column, and all rows and columns are unique.

variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ and $j$ are the row and column indices, respectively. These variables represent the decision variables, which can take on the value 0 (representing an O) or 1 (representing an X), and are used to determine the placement of X's and O's on the board.

constraints: 
$\forall i \in 1..n: \sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{i,j} = m \land \sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{j,i} = m$ 
$\forall i \in 1..n: \text{sliding\_sum}(0,2,3,[x_{i,j} | j \in 1..n]) \land \text{sliding\_sum}(0,2,3,[x_{j,i} | j \in 1..n])$ 
$\forall i,j \in 1..n, i < j: \sum_{k=1}^{n} (x_{i,k} \neq x_{j,k}) > 0 \land \sum_{k=1}^{n} (x_{k,i} \neq x_{k,j}) > 0$
$\forall i \in 1..n, j \in 1..n-2: \sum_{k=0}^{2} (x_{i,j+k} = 0) \leq 2 \land \sum_{k=0}^{2} (x_{j+k,i} = 0) \leq 2$. 
These constraints ensure that each row and column has the same number of X's and O's, that there are no more than two consecutive X's or O's in a row or column, and that all rows and columns are unique.

objective: satisfy, meaning that the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
