name: Talisman Square
description: The Talisman Square problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves finding an n×n array of integers from 1 to n^2, where the difference between any integer and its neighbor (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) is greater than or equal to a given value k.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ for $i, j \in 1..n$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the integer value assigned to the cell at row $i$ and column $j$ in the n×n array.
constraints: $\bigwedge_{i,j \in 1..n} x_{i,j} \in [1..n^2] \wedge \text{all\_different}(x) \wedge \bigwedge_{i \in 2..n, j \in 1..n} |x_{i,j} - x_{i-1,j}| \geq k \wedge \bigwedge_{i \in 1..n, j \in 2..n} |x_{i,j} - x_{i,j-1}| \geq k \wedge \bigwedge_{i \in 1..n-1, j \in 1..n} |x_{i,j} - x_{i+1,j}| \geq k \wedge \bigwedge_{i \in 1..n, j \in 1..n-1} |x_{i,j} - x_{i,j+1}| \geq k$, which ensure that all integers from 1 to n^2 are used exactly once, and the difference between any integer and its neighbor is greater than or equal to k.
objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
