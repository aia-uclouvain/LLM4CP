Uniform Dice Problem
The problem is about creating two special dice that, when rolled together, produce a uniform distribution of sums. 
The main variables are: 
- x1 and x2, which represent the numbers on each die, 
- dist, which represents the distribution of sums when the dice are rolled together. 
The basic restrictions are: 
- each die can have numbers between 1 and a maximum value (m), 
- the distribution of sums should be as uniform as possible, 
- the total number of possible outcomes when rolling the two dice should be evenly distributed among the possible sums. 
The goal is to find a set of numbers for each die that achieves a uniform distribution of sums, with the fewest number of different sums (z) being greater than 0.
