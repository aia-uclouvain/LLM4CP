Uniform Dice Problem
The Uniform Dice Problem is about designing two dice such that the sums of the numbers on the faces of the two dice have a uniform distribution.
The main decision variables are x1 and x2, which represent the numbers on the faces of the two dice, and dist, which represents the distribution of the sums.
The key constraints are that the sum of the numbers on each face of the two dice must be between 2 and 2m, the total number of possible outcomes (n*n) must be distributed evenly among the possible sums, and the distribution of the sums must be uniform, meaning that either a sum has zero probability or the same probability as the previous sum.
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, with the goal of minimizing the number of different sums that have a non-zero probability, represented by the variable z.
