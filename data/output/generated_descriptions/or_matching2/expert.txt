name: Stable Marriage Problem or Matching Problem
description: The problem is a classic example of a matching problem, where a set of individuals (in this case, OR'ers) need to be paired with each other based on their compatibility scores. The goal is to find the optimal matching that maximizes the total compatibility score. The problem also considers the case where one person may not be matched with anyone, in which case they are assigned to a "dummy" person.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}$, $x_{i,j} \in \{0, 1\}$. These variables represent the assignment of person $i$ to person $j$, where $x_{i,j} = 1$ if person $i$ is matched with person $j$, and $x_{i,j} = 0$ otherwise. $total$ is a variable that represents the total compatibility score of the matching.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{i,j} = 1$, $\sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{j,i} = 1$, $x_{i,i} = 0$, $x_{i,j} = x_{j,i}$. These constraints ensure that each person is matched with exactly one other person, a person cannot be matched with themselves, and the matching is symmetric.
objective: Maximize $total = \sum_{i < j} x_{i,j} \cdot scores_{i,j}$, where $scores_{i,j}$ is the compatibility score between person $i$ and person $j$. The objective is to find the matching that maximizes the total compatibility score.
