The Book Buy Puzzle
The problem is about determining the number of books bought by two fathers, Peter and Paul, and their sons, Tom and Dick, given certain constraints on the number of books and the total amount spent.
The main decision variables are w, x, and y. The variable w is a binary variable that represents whether Peter is Tom's father (w = 1) or not (w = 0). The arrays x and y represent the number of books bought by each father and son, respectively. Specifically, x[i] represents the number of books bought by father i, and y[j] represents the number of books bought by son j.
The key constraints are: Dick buys one book, Peter buys one more book than Tom, and each family spends $65. The $65 spent by each family is calculated as the square of the number of books bought by the father plus the square of the number of books bought by the son, taking into account the relationship between the fathers and sons represented by the variable w.
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the model. In other words, the goal is to determine the values of w, x, and y that meet all the conditions, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
