Magic Square Problem
The problem is about creating a special square grid where numbers are arranged in a specific way. 
The main variables are the grid cells (x) and the sum of each row, column, and diagonal (s), which represent the total of the numbers in each line. 
The basic restrictions are that each number can only be used once, and the sum of numbers in each row, column, and diagonal must be the same. 
The goal is to find a way to fill the grid with numbers from 1 to n*n (where n is the size of the grid) such that all these conditions are met, and the sum of each row, column, and diagonal is equal to the sum of the first n*n natural numbers divided by n.
