Interval Constraint Problem
The problem is about enforcing a variable to take a value within a specified interval.
The main decision variables are `v`, `low`, and `up`, which represent the value to be constrained, the lower bound of the interval, and the upper bound of the interval, respectively.
The key constraints are that the value `v` must be greater than or equal to the lower bound `low` and less than or equal to the upper bound `up`.
The problem's objective is to satisfy the constraint that `v` is within the interval `[low, up]`, which is achieved by setting `v` to a specific value and ensuring that it falls within the given interval, in this case, between 2 and 5.
