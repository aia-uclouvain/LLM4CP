The 3-Partition Problem
The 3-Partition problem is about dividing a set of numbers into equal-sized groups, where each group has the same sum. 
The main decision variables are `a` and `a_sum`, where `a` represents the groups of numbers and `a_sum` represents the sum of numbers in each group. 
The key constraints are that the numbers must be divided into `n` groups, each group must have `m` numbers, the sum of numbers in each group must be the same, and all numbers must be used exactly once. 
The objective of the problem is to determine whether it is possible to divide the numbers into groups with the same sum, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
