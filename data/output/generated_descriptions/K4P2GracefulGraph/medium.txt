The name of the problem is the K4P2 Graceful Graph problem. 
This problem is about finding a labeling of the nodes in two complete graphs (K4) that are connected by four edges, such that the absolute difference between the labels of two connected nodes is equal to the label of the edge between them, and all node and edge labels are unique.

The main decision variables are the arrays `nodes` and `edges`, where `nodes` represents the labels of the nodes in the graph, and `edges` represents the labels of the edges in the graph. 

The key constraints are that the absolute difference between the labels of two connected nodes must be equal to the label of the edge between them, and all node and edge labels must be unique. This means that for any two nodes connected by an edge, the difference in their labels must match the label assigned to that edge, and no two nodes or edges can have the same label.

The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all these constraints, which is a classic example of a constraint satisfaction problem. There is no explicit objective function being minimized or maximized; instead, the goal is to find a valid assignment of labels to nodes and edges that meets all the given conditions.
