name: K4P2 Graceful Graph

description: The K4P2 Graceful Graph problem is a graph theory problem that involves finding a labeling of the nodes and edges of a graph such that the labels of the edges are the absolute differences between the labels of the nodes they connect, and all labels are unique. The graph in question consists of two complete subgraphs of 4 nodes each, with the nodes of the two subgraphs connected in a one-to-one correspondence.

variables: $nodes = [n_1, n_2, ..., n_8]$, $edges = [e_1, e_2, ..., e_{16}]$
These are the decision variables of the problem, where $nodes$ represents the labels of the 8 nodes in the graph, and $edges$ represents the labels of the 16 edges in the graph.

constraints: 
$\begin{aligned}
&|n_1 - n_2| = e_1 \\
&|n_1 - n_3| = e_2 \\
&|n_1 - n_4| = e_3 \\
&|n_2 - n_3| = e_4 \\
&|n_2 - n_4| = e_5 \\
&|n_3 - n_4| = e_6 \\
&|n_5 - n_6| = e_7 \\
&|n_5 - n_7| = e_8 \\
&|n_5 - n_8| = e_9 \\
&|n_6 - n_7| = e_{10} \\
&|n_6 - n_8| = e_{11} \\
&|n_7 - n_8| = e_{12} \\
&|n_1 - n_5| = e_{13} \\
&|n_2 - n_6| = e_{14} \\
&|n_3 - n_7| = e_{15} \\
&|n_4 - n_8| = e_{16} \\
&\text{all\_different}(edges) \\
&\text{all\_different}(nodes)
\end{aligned}$
These constraints ensure that the labels of the edges are the absolute differences between the labels of the nodes they connect, and that all labels are unique.

objective: The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize. The goal is to find a labeling of the nodes and edges that meets the conditions of a graceful graph.
