name: N-Queens Problem with Degree of Diversity
description: The problem is a variation of the classic N-Queens problem, where the goal is to place N queens on an NxN chessboard such that no two queens attack each other. In this version, the objective is to diversify a set of solutions to the N-Queens problem, using constraints that promote diversity among the solutions.
variables: $x_{i,j}, soft\_cost_j, total\_cost$ where $x_{i,j}$ represents the position of the queen in the j-th column of the i-th solution, $soft\_cost_j$ represents the cost of not having all different values in the j-th column across all solutions, and $total\_cost$ represents the total cost of not having all different values across all columns.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, n-1\}: queens([x_{i,j} | j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}]) \land \forall j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}: soft\_cost_j = soft\_alldifferent([x_{i,j} | i \in \{1, \ldots, n-1\}]) \land lex\_chain\_less(x)$ where $queens(q)$ ensures that the queens in a single solution do not attack each other, $soft\_alldifferent(a)$ counts the number of pairs of values in $a$ that are equal, and $lex\_chain\_less(a)$ ensures that the rows of $a$ are lexicographically sorted.
objective: minimize $total\_cost$
