Lights Out Puzzle
The Lights Out Puzzle is about turning off all the lights in a grid by clicking on cells, where each click toggles the cell and its immediate neighbors.
The main variables are:
- `n`: the size of the grid
- `r`: the initial state of the grid, where 1 represents a light that is on and 0 represents a light that is off
- `x`: the decision variable, where 1 represents a click on a cell and 0 represents no click
- `d`: the number of times each cell is toggled
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- Each click on a cell toggles the cell and its immediate neighbors (up, down, left, right)
- The goal is to turn off all the lights, i.e., to have all cells in the `r` array equal to 0 after the clicks
The goal of the problem is to minimize the number of clicks, i.e., to find the smallest number of clicks required to turn off all the lights.
