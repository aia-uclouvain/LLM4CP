Lights Out problem
The Lights Out problem is a classic puzzle where a grid of cells, or lights, is given with some lights on and others off. The goal is to turn off all the lights by clicking on the cells. Each click toggles the state of the clicked cell and its immediate neighbors.

The main decision variables in this problem are x[i,j], which represent whether a click is made on cell (i,j) or not, and d[i,j], which represent the final state of cell (i,j).

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each cell's final state is determined by the number of clicks made on it and its neighbors.
- A cell is toggled (i.e., its state is changed) if it is clicked or if one of its neighbors is clicked.
- The final state of all cells should be off (i.e., 0).

The objective of the problem is to minimize the total number of clicks made, which is represented by the variable "moves". This is achieved by finding the smallest possible number of clicks that satisfy the constraints and result in all cells being turned off.
