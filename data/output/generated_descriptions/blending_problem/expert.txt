name: Blending Problem
description: The blending problem is a classic problem in operations research and optimization, where a farmer needs to determine the optimal mix of different feed types (corn, tankage, and alfalfa) to meet the nutritional requirements of pigs at a minimum cost. The problem involves finding the quantities of each feed type to include in the mix, subject to constraints on the nutritional content of each feed type, the daily nutritional requirements of the pigs, and the cost of each feed type.
variables: $mix = (mix_1, mix_2, mix_3), tot\_cost, daily\_req\_met = (daily\_req\_met_1, daily\_req\_met_2, daily\_req\_met_3)$, where $mix_i$ is the quantity of feed type $i$ in the mix, $tot\_cost$ is the total cost of the mix, and $daily\_req\_met_i$ is a binary variable indicating whether the $i^{th}$ daily nutritional requirement is met.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{3} mix_j \* ingredients\_per\_feed_{ij} \geq min\_daily\_requirements_i \leftrightarrow daily\_req\_met_i = 1, \forall i \in \{1, 2, 3\}$, $tot\_cost = \sum_{j=1}^{3} mix_j \* feed\_cost_j$, $\sum_{j=1}^{3} bool2int(mix_j > 0) \leq 2$, $\sum_{i=1}^{3} daily\_req\_met_i = 2$, where the first constraint ensures that the nutritional requirements are met, the second constraint calculates the total cost, the third constraint limits the number of feed types in the mix to at most 2, and the fourth constraint ensures that only two of the three daily nutritional requirements are met.
objective: Minimize $tot\_cost$
