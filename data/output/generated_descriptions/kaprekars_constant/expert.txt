name: Kaprekar's Constant Problem
description: The problem is based on a mathematical operation where a four-digit number is rearranged to form the largest and smallest possible numbers, and then the smaller number is subtracted from the larger one. This process is repeated, and it is observed that the sequence eventually reaches a fixed point, known as Kaprekar's constant, which is 6174 for four-digit numbers. The problem aims to find the sequence of numbers that leads to this fixed point.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_{rows}, pos$, where $x_i$ represents the number at each step of the sequence, and $pos$ represents the position in the sequence where the fixed point is reached. These variables are used to store the intermediate results of the Kaprekar operation.
constraints: $\forall i \in 2..rows: kaprekar(x_{i-1}, x_i, base)$, $x_1 = 3141$, $x_1 \notin nums[n]$, $pos = i$, $x_i \in nums[n]$, $i > 1 \rightarrow x_{i-1} \neq x_i$, where $kaprekar(s, t, base)$ is a predicate that performs the Kaprekar operation on $s$ to get $t$, and $nums[n]$ is a set of fixed points for a given $n$. These constraints ensure that the sequence is generated correctly, starts with a given number, and reaches the fixed point.
objective: The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a sequence of numbers that leads to Kaprekar's constant, and to determine the position in the sequence where this fixed point is reached. The problem is solved using a satisfaction-based approach, where the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
