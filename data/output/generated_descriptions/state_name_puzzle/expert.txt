name: State Name Puzzle
description: The problem is to find two pairs of U.S. state names such that when the letters of the two states in each pair are mixed together, they can be rearranged to form the names of two different U.S. states. The problem is case-insensitive and ignores spacing, and the state names do not have to be in any particular order.
variables: $x1, x2, gcc$ where $x1$ and $x2$ are arrays of decision variables representing the indices of the two pairs of U.S. states, and $gcc$ is an array of decision variables representing the global cardinality count of each letter in the mixed state names. These variables represent the choices of state names and the count of each letter in the mixed names.
constraints: $\text{alldifferent}(x1 ++ x2)$, $\text{global_cardinality}([states[x1[J],I] | J in 1..2, I in 1..max_len], array1d(0..num_letters, set2array(index_set(gcc))), gcc)$, $\text{global_cardinality}([states[x2[J],I] | J in 1..2, I in 1..max_len], array1d(0..num_letters, set2array(index_set(gcc))), gcc)$, $\text{increasing}(x1)$, $\text{increasing}(x2)$, $x1[1] < x2[1]$, and constraints to handle duplicates. These constraints ensure that the chosen state names are distinct, the mixed letters have the same distribution, and the solution satisfies the symmetry breaking conditions.
