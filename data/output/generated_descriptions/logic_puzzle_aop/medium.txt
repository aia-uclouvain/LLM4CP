The problem is called the "Logic Puzzle" from The Art of Prolog. 
It is about determining the first names, sports, nationalities, and rankings of three friends who participated in a programming competition. 
The main decision variables are: 
- `michael`, `simon`, and `richard`, which represent the rankings of the friends with these names, 
- `basketball`, `tennis`, and `cricket`, which represent the rankings of the friends who like these sports, 
- `america`, `israel`, and `australia`, which represent the rankings of the friends from these countries, and 
- `place`, which is an array representing the rankings of the friends. 
The key constraints are: 
- each friend has a unique first name, sport, nationality, and ranking, 
- Michael likes basketball and did better than the American, 
- Simon, the Israeli, did better than the tennis player, and 
- the cricket player came first. 
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, which will allow us to determine the first name, sport, nationality, and ranking of each friend.
