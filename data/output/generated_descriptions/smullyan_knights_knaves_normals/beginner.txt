Smullyan Knight, Knave, and Normal problems. 
This problem is about three people - a knight who always tells the truth, a knave who always lies, and a normal person who sometimes tells the truth and sometimes lies. 
The main variables are the types of people - knight, normal, and knave, which are represented by numbers 1, 2, and 3 respectively. 
The basic restrictions are that a knight always speaks the truth, a knave always lies, and a normal person may or may not tell the truth. 
The goal of the problem is to figure out the types of the people based on the statements they make about each other, and to find a solution that satisfies all the given conditions.
