name: Crowded Chessboard puzzle
description: The Crowded Chessboard puzzle is a classic problem in constraint programming where the goal is to place a set of chess pieces (queens, bishops, knights, and rooks) on a chessboard in such a way that no two pieces of the same type attack each other, and each square of the board is occupied by at most one piece.
variables: $x_{i,j,k}$, where $x_{i,j,k}$ is a binary variable that represents whether a piece of type $k$ (1 - Queen, 2 - Bishop, 3 - Knight, 4 - Rook) is placed on the square $(i,j)$ of the board. $res_{i,j}$ is an auxiliary variable used to calculate the output.
constraints: 
$\sum_{i,j} x_{i,j,k} = N_k$ (each piece type has a fixed number of occurrences), 
$\sum_{k} x_{i,j,k} \leq 1$ (each square can be occupied by at most one piece), 
$\sum_{j} x_{i,j,1} \leq 1$ and $\sum_{i} x_{i,j,1} \leq 1$ (no two queens attack each other), 
$\sum_{k} x_{k,i-k+1,1} \leq 1$ and $\sum_{k} x_{k,i+k-1,1} \leq 1$ (no two queens attack each other diagonally), 
similar constraints for bishops and rooks, 
$x_{i-2,j-1,3} + x_{i-1,j-2,3} + x_{i+1,j-2,3} + x_{i+2,j-1,3} + x_{i+2,j+1,3} + x_{i+1,j+2,3} + x_{i-1,j+2,3} + x_{i-2,i+1,3} + 99x_{i,j,3} \leq 99$ (no two knights attack each other), 
$\sum_{i,j,k} x_{i,j,k} = 0$ for dummy squares (squares outside the actual chessboard).
These constraints ensure that the pieces are placed on the board according to the rules of chess, and that each square is occupied by at most one piece.
objective: minimize $z = \sum_{i,j,k} x_{i,j,k}$, which represents the total number of pieces placed on the board.
