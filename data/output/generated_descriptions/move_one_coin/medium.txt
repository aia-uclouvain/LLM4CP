The Move One Coin problem. 
This problem involves rearranging a line of coins by moving exactly one coin to achieve a specific target configuration, where the number of collected coins from left to right is in decreasing order.
The main decision variables are `from` and `to`, which represent the position of the coin to be moved and the target position where the coin will be moved, respectively.
The key constraints are that the coin can only be moved from a position that is currently occupied by a coin, the target position must be empty, and the coin being moved cannot be moved to its current position. Additionally, all other positions must remain unchanged after the move.
The objective of this problem is to find a valid move that satisfies the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. In other words, the goal is to find a single move that transforms the initial configuration into the target configuration.
