Spinning Disks Problem
The Spinning Disks problem is about determining the arrangement of transparent and opaque sectors on two identical disks, each divided into twelve sectors. 
The main decision variables are `disk1` and `disk2`, where `disk1` represents the arrangement of transparent (1) and opaque (0) sectors on the first disk, and `disk2` represents the arrangement of sectors on the second disk for all possible rotations.
The key constraints are that the second disk is a rotation of the first disk, and the number of transparent sectors that can be seen when the two disks are overlaid must match a given sequence of numbers. This is achieved by reversing the first disk to get the first rotation of the second disk, and then rotating the second disk to get the remaining rotations. The number of transparent sectors for each rotation is calculated by summing the number of positions where both disks have a transparent sector.
The objective of the problem is to find an arrangement of sectors on the disks that satisfies the given sequence of numbers, which represents the number of transparent sectors that can be seen when the top disk is rotated. The problem is a satisfaction problem, meaning that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than optimizing a specific objective function.
