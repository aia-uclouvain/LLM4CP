Magic Square Problem
The problem is about creating a square grid where each cell contains a unique integer from 1 to n^2, and the sum of integers in each row, column, and diagonal is the same.
The main decision variables are the `magic` array, which represents the integers in each cell of the grid, where `magic[i,j]` is the integer in the cell at row `i` and column `j`.
The key constraints are: 
- each integer from 1 to n^2 is used exactly once, 
- the sum of integers in each row is equal to the total sum, 
- the sum of integers in each column is equal to the total sum, 
- the sum of integers in the main diagonal is equal to the total sum, and 
- the sum of integers in the other diagonal is equal to the total sum.
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all these constraints, which is a feasible solution, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function. The `solve satisfy` statement in the model indicates that the goal is to find a solution that meets all the constraints, rather than optimizing a particular value.
