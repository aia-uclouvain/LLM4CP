The problem is called Age-changing. 
It's about a married couple where if you take the wife's age and apply four simple math operations in a certain order, you get the husband's age. 
The main variables are the wife's age (called "m") and the husband's age (called "h"), which are the two unknown ages we're trying to find. 
The basic restrictions are that we have to apply four specific math operations (+2, /8, -3, *7) in some order to the wife's age to get the husband's age, and if we apply the same operations in a different order to the husband's age, we should get the wife's age. 
The goal is to find the two ages that satisfy these conditions.
