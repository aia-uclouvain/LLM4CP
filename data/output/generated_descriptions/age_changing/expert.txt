name: Age-changing problem
description: The problem is about finding the ages of a husband and wife, given that applying four operations (+2, /8, -3, *7) in some order to the wife's age results in the husband's age, and applying the same operations in a different order to the husband's age results in the wife's age.
variables: m, h, perm1, perm2, hlist, mlist. These are the decision variables where m and h represent the wife's and husband's ages respectively, perm1 and perm2 are permutations of the four operations, and hlist and mlist are lists of intermediate results for calculating the husband's and wife's ages.
constraints: all_different(perm1) ∧ all_different(perm2) ∧ ∑[perm1[i] ≠ perm2[i] | i ∈ 1..n] > 0 ∧ hlist[1] = m ∧ h = hlist[n+1] ∧ mlist[1] = h ∧ m = mlist[n+1] ∧ ∀i ∈ 1..n (check(perm1[i], hlist[i], hlist[i+1]) ∧ check(perm2[i], mlist[i], mlist[i+1])). These constraints ensure that perm1 and perm2 are different permutations of the operations, and that applying these operations to the wife's and husband's ages results in the husband's and wife's ages respectively.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find the values of m and h that satisfy all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
