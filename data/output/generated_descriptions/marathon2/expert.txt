name: Marathon Puzzle
description: The Marathon Puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem where six runners, Dominique, Ignace, Naren, Olivier, Philippe, and Pascal, have arrived as the first six at the Paris marathon. The task is to reconstruct their arrival order based on given information about their relative positions.
variables: $D, I, N, O, P, Pa, r = [D, I, N, O, P, Pa]$ where $D, I, N, O, P, Pa$ represent the arrival positions of Dominique, Ignace, Naren, Olivier, Philippe, and Pascal respectively, and $r$ is an array of these positions. These variables are used to determine the order in which the runners arrived.
constraints: $D \neq n, D < N, D < O, Pa < N, Pa < O, I < N, I < O, D < 3, P \leq 4, I \neq 2, I \neq 3, Pa + 3 = N, I \neq 4, D \neq 4, \text{all\_different}(r)$ which represent the given conditions: Olivier not last, Dominique, Pascal, and Ignace before Naren and Olivier, Dominique better than third, Philippe among the first four, Ignace not second or third, Pascal three places earlier than Naren, neither Ignace nor Dominique on fourth position, and all runners have distinct positions.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, i.e., to find a feasible solution that reconstructs the arrival order of the runners. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
