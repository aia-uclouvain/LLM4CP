**Arbitrage Loop Problem**
The Arbitrage Loop Problem is a classic problem in finance and computer science that involves finding a sequence of currency exchanges that results in a profit.

**Description**
The problem is about detecting arbitrage loops in a set of currency exchange rates. An arbitrage loop is a sequence of currency exchanges where the final amount of money is greater than the initial amount, resulting in a profit. The problem takes as input a set of currency exchange rates and a starting amount of money, and the goal is to find the most profitable sequence of exchanges.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `y`: an array of variables representing the sequence of currencies to exchange.
- `profit`: an array of variables representing the profit made at each exchange.
- `z`: a variable representing the total profit made by the sequence of exchanges.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
- The sequence of currencies must be different at each step (i.e., no repeated currencies).
- The sequence must start and end with the same currency.
- The profit made at each exchange is determined by the exchange rate between the two currencies.
- The total profit is the product of the profits made at each exchange.

**Objective**
The objective of the problem is to maximize the total profit `z`. The problem is solved by finding the sequence of exchanges that results in the highest total profit. The solution includes the sequence of currencies, the profit made at each exchange, and the total profit.
