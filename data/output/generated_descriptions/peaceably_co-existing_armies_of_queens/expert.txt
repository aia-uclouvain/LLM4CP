name: Peaceably Co-existing Armies of Queens

description: The problem involves placing two armies of queens on a square board of size n x n, such that no two queens from opposing armies attack each other, and the number of queens in each army is equal. The goal is to maximize the size of the armies.

variables: $b_{ij}, w_{ij}, x_{ij}, nb, nw$ 
These are decision variables where $b_{ij}$ and $w_{ij}$ represent the presence of a black or white queen at position (i, j) on the board, $x_{ij}$ represents the type of queen (black, white, or none) at position (i, j), and $nb$ and $nw$ represent the total number of black and white queens, respectively.

constraints: $|black| = |white|, \forall (rb,cb) \in black, \forall (rw,cw) \in white: (rb \neq rw) \land (cb \neq cw) \land (|rw - rb| \neq |cw - cb|), b_{ij} + w_{ij} \leq 1, x_{ij} + x_{kl} \neq 3$ 
These constraints ensure that the number of black queens is equal to the number of white queens, no two queens from opposing armies attack each other (i.e., they are not in the same row, column, or diagonal), and that each cell on the board can contain at most one queen.

objective: Maximize $|black| + |white|$ (or equivalently, maximize $nb$ or $|black|$ since $|black| = |white|$) 
The objective is to maximize the total number of queens on the board, which corresponds to maximizing the size of the armies.
