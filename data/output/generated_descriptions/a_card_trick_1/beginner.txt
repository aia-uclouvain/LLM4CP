Card Trick Problem
The problem is about arranging a deck of 52 playing cards into a rectangle with four rows and 13 columns in such a way that certain conditions are met.

The main variables are the suits and values of the cards. The suits are hearts, spades, clubs, and diamonds, and the values are Ace through King. 

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- Each suit must appear once in each column.
- Each value must appear once in each row.
- No two cards with the same value can be in the same column.
- Each row must have four cards of one suit and three cards of each of the other suits.
- Any two columns must share exactly one value, and any two values must appear together in exactly one column.

The goal of the problem is to find an arrangement of the cards that satisfies all these conditions.
