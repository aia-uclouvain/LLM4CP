name: Minesweeper
description: Minesweeper is a classic puzzle game where the player is presented with a grid of cells, some of which contain hidden mines. The goal is to determine the location of all mines based on given clues, which are numbers indicating how many mines are adjacent to each cell.
variables: $x_{i,j} \in \{0,1\}$ for $i \in \{1, \ldots, r\}$ and $j \in \{1, \ldots, c\}$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents whether a mine is present in cell $(i,j)$ or not.
constraints: $\sum_{(i',j') \in N(i,j)} x_{i',j'} = game_{i,j}$ for all $(i,j)$, where $N(i,j)$ denotes the set of neighboring cells of $(i,j)$, and $game_{i,j}$ is the given clue for cell $(i,j)$. Additionally, $x_{i,j} \in \{0,1\}$ for all $(i,j)$.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution, i.e., an assignment of mines to cells that satisfies all given clues, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
