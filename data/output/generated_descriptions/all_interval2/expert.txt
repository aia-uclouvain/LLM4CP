It involves finding a sequence of musical notes, represented by integers from 0 to n-1, such that each note appears exactly once and the differences between consecutive notes are all different. This problem has applications in music composition, as it can be used to generate melodies with unique and interesting properties.

series[i] != series[j] for all i, j in classes where i != j, 
abs(series[i+1] - series[i]) != abs(series[j+1] - series[j]) for all i, j in classes where j < n-1 and i < n-1 and i != j,

objective: The problem does not have an explicit objective function to minimize or maximize. Instead, the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., a sequence of notes that meets the conditions of the All Interval Problem.
