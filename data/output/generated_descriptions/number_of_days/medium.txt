0-1 Knapsack Problem
The 0-1 knapsack problem is about finding the optimal way to pack a set of items of different weights and values into a knapsack of limited capacity, such that the total value of the packed items is maximized. However, in this specific variation, the problem is slightly different and is referred to as the "Number of Days" problem, which can be viewed as a type of 0-1 knapsack problem where the goal is to find the combination of items (days and prices) that sums up to a target number of days while minimizing the total cost.

The main decision variables are `x`, an array of binary variables where `x[i]` represents whether the `i-th` item (a reservation of a certain number of days at a specific price) is included in the optimal solution or not, `days`, which represents the total number of days reserved, and `total_cost`, which represents the total cost of the reserved days.

The key constraints are: 
- The total cost is calculated by summing the costs of the included items.
- The total number of days is calculated by summing the number of days of the included items.
- The total number of days must equal a target number (13 in this case).

The objective of the problem is to minimize the `total_cost`, which means finding the combination of items that results in the lowest total cost while still summing up to the target number of days.
