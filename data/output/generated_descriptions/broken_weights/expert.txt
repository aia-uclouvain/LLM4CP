name: Broken Weights Problem
description: A merchant has a 40-pound measuring weight that broke into four pieces, and each piece weighs a whole number of pounds. The goal is to find the weights of the pieces such that they can be used to weigh every integral weight between 1 and 40 pounds using a balance scale.
variables: $n, m, weights, x$ 
weights is an array of var 1..m, and x is an array of var -1..1. 
$n$ is the number of different weights (4 in this case), $m$ is the original weight (40 pounds), $weights$ are the weights of the pieces, and $x$ represents the combinations of weights on either side of the scale.
constraints: $\forall i \in 2..n (weights[i-1] \leq weights[i]) \land \sum_{i=1}^{n} weights[i] = m \land \forall j \in 1..m (\sum_{i=1}^{n} x[j,i]*weights[i] = j)$
These constraints ensure that the weights are non-decreasing, their sum equals the original weight, and all weights from 1 to 40 can be made by combining the pieces on either side of the scale.
objective: minimize $weights[n]$ 
The objective is to minimize the largest weight, which means finding the smallest possible weights that satisfy the constraints.
