The Broken Weights problem. 
This problem is about finding the weights of four broken pieces of a 40-pound measuring weight, with each piece weighing a whole number of pounds, such that the four pieces can be used to weigh every integral weight between 1 and 40 pounds using a balance scale.

The main decision variables are the weights of the four pieces, represented by the array `weights` of size `n`, where `n` is 4. Each `weights[i]` represents the weight of the `i-th` piece.

The key constraints are: 
- the sum of the weights of the four pieces must equal the original weight of 40 pounds, 
- each piece's weight must be a whole number of pounds, 
- the weights of the pieces must be non-decreasing to avoid symmetry, 
- and the four pieces must be able to weigh every integral weight between 1 and 40 pounds using a balance scale, where each piece can be placed on either side of the scale.

The problem's objective is to minimize the weight of the heaviest piece, `weights[n]`, while still satisfying all the constraints, effectively finding the set of weights that allows for the most efficient weighing of objects between 1 and 40 pounds.
