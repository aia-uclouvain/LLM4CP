name: Furniture Moving Problem
description: The problem involves moving a set of furniture items, each requiring a certain number of people and taking a specific amount of time, within a limited timeframe and with a limited number of people available. The goal is to determine the optimal allocation of people and time to complete the task.
variables: $n, \text{Starts}_i, \text{Durations}_i, \text{Resources}_i, \text{EndTimes}_i, \text{numPersons}, \text{end\_time}$, where $n$ is the number of furniture items, $\text{Starts}_i$ is the start time of item $i$, $\text{Durations}_i$ is the duration of item $i$, $\text{Resources}_i$ is the number of people required for item $i$, $\text{EndTimes}_i$ is the end time of item $i$, $\text{numPersons}$ is the total number of people available, and $\text{end\_time}$ is the total time taken to complete all tasks.
constraints: $\text{cumulative}(\text{Starts}, \text{Durations}, \text{Resources}, \text{numPersons})$, $\text{EndTimes}_i = \text{Starts}_i + \text{Durations}_i$, $\text{end\_time} = \max(\text{EndTimes})$, $\text{numPersons} \leq 4$, where the cumulative constraint ensures that the total number of people used at any time does not exceed the available number of people, the second constraint calculates the end time of each task, the third constraint calculates the total time taken, and the fourth constraint limits the total number of people available.
objective: minimize $\text{end\_time}$, which means finding the minimum total time required to complete all tasks while satisfying the given constraints.
