The name of the problem is the Shortest Path Problem. 
This problem is about finding the shortest way to get from one point to another in a network of roads or paths. 
The main variables are the nodes (like cities or intersections), the edges (like roads between cities), and the length of each edge (like the distance between cities). There's also a variable that keeps track of which edges are used in the shortest path. 
The basic restrictions are that you have to start at a certain point, end at another certain point, and only use the edges that exist in the network. You can't just make up new roads or paths. 
The goal of the problem is to minimize the total distance traveled, which means finding the shortest possible path from the starting point to the ending point.
