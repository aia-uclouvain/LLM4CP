name: Sequence Puzzle
description: The sequence puzzle is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves finding a permutation of numbers from 1 to 10 such that certain conditions are met. The conditions are that the number 6 must be placed in the 7th position, and each subsequent number must be either 3 more or 2 less than the previous number.
variables: $x = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_{10}]$ where $x_i$ represents the number assigned to the $i^{th}$ position in the sequence, and is a decision variable that can take any value from 1 to 10.
constraints: $all\_different(x) \land x_7 = 6 \land \forall i \in \{2, ..., 10\} (x_i - x_{i-1} = 3 \lor x_i - x_{i-1} = -2)$, which means that all numbers in the sequence must be different, the number 6 must be in the 7th position, and the difference between any two consecutive numbers must be either 3 or -2.
objective: The objective of this problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, as it is a satisfaction problem (solve satisfy) and there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
