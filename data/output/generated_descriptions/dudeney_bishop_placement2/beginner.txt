Dudeney's Bishop Placement Problem II
This problem is about placing bishops on a chessboard in such a way that the maximum number of bishops can be placed without any bishop attacking another.
The main variables are x and a, where x represents whether a square on the chessboard is occupied by a bishop or not, and a represents whether a square is attacked by a bishop or not.
The basic restrictions are that a square can either be occupied by a bishop or be attacked by a bishop, but not both, and that a bishop attacks all squares on the same diagonal.
The goal is to maximize the number of bishops that can be placed on the chessboard, which means finding the largest possible number of non-attacking bishops.
