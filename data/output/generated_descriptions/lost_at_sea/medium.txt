Lost at Sea problem
The Lost at Sea problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding a lost ship in a grid of ocean cells with varying probabilities of the ship's location. The goal is to maximize the probability of finding the ship by searching a limited number of cells.

The main decision variables in this problem are the coordinates (x, y) of the cells to be searched, represented by the arrays x and y, and the path, represented by the array path. These variables determine the order in which the cells are searched.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each cell can only be searched once.
- The search can only move to adjacent cells (up, down, left, or right).
- The search is limited to a maximum of 10 cells.
- The probability of finding the ship is calculated by summing the probabilities of the searched cells.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the probability of finding the ship, which is calculated by summing the probabilities of the searched cells, represented by the variable sum_points. The problem aims to find the optimal sequence of cells to search in order to maximize this probability.
