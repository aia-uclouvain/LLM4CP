Lost at Sea problem
The problem is about finding a lost ship in the ocean by searching a grid of cells, where each cell has a probability of finding the ship. 
The main variables are the cells in the grid, the probability of finding the ship in each cell, and the path to be taken to search the cells. 
The basic restrictions are that only 10 cells can be searched, the search can only move to adjacent cells, and no cell can be revisited. 
The goal is to find the path that gives the highest probability of finding the lost ship by maximizing the sum of the probabilities of the cells in the path.
