name: Chain of Primes
description: The problem involves finding a chain of ten 2-digit prime numbers where each prime's first digit is the same as the previous prime's second digit, except for the first prime. The fourth prime is the reverse of the first prime, and the tenth prime is the reverse of the seventh prime.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_{10}$, where $x_i$ represents the $i^{th}$ 2-digit prime number in the chain. These variables are used to store the prime numbers that satisfy the given conditions.
constraints: $\bigwedge_{i=1}^{10} x_i \in \text{primes} \land \bigwedge_{i=2}^{10} \left\lfloor \frac{x_i}{10} \right\rfloor = x_{i-1} \mod 10 \land x_4 = \text{reversed}(x_1) \land x_{10} = \text{reversed}(x_7) \land \bigwedge_{i=1}^{10} \bigwedge_{j=i+1}^{10} x_i \neq x_j$, where $\text{primes}$ is the set of 2-digit prime numbers and $\text{reversed}(x)$ is a predicate that checks if $x$ is the reverse of another number. These constraints ensure that all $x_i$ are distinct 2-digit primes, the first digit of each prime is the same as the second digit of the previous prime, the fourth prime is the reverse of the first prime, and the tenth prime is the reverse of the seventh prime.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding a valid chain of ten 2-digit prime numbers. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing any value, but rather finding a feasible solution that meets all the conditions.
