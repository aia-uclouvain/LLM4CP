Chain of Primes
The Chain of Primes problem is about finding a sequence of ten distinct 2-digit prime numbers where each prime's first digit matches the previous prime's second digit, with additional conditions applied to the fourth and tenth primes.

The main decision variables are the array `x`, which represents the sequence of ten 2-digit prime numbers. Each element `x[i]` in the array corresponds to the `i-th` prime number in the sequence.

The key constraints are: 
- all primes in the sequence must be distinct, 
- each prime must be a 2-digit number, 
- the first digit of each prime (except the first one) must match the second digit of the previous prime, 
- the fourth prime must be the reverse of the first prime, and 
- the tenth prime must be the reverse of the seventh prime.

The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a sequence of primes that satisfies all the given constraints, specifically to determine the third, sixth, and ninth primes in the chain.
