Matchmaker problem
The Matchmaker problem is a classic constraint programming problem where the goal is to pair up men and women based on their characteristics, such as hair color and eye color, in a way that each pair has at least one trait in common.

The main decision variables in this problem are the array `x`, where `x[i]` represents the man assigned to woman `i`. The `num_matches` array is also a decision variable, where `num_matches[i]` represents the number of traits that woman `i` and her assigned man have in common.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- Each woman is assigned to a unique man (alldifferent constraint).
- Each pair of woman and man has at least one trait in common (num_matches[i] >= 1).
- The total number of matches is calculated by summing up the number of matches for each woman (total = sum(num_matches)).

The objective of the problem is to find a pairing of men and women such that each pair has at least one trait in common. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather it is a satisfaction problem where the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints. However, in the provided model, the total number of matches is calculated and can be used as an objective function to maximize, but the main goal is to find a feasible pairing.
