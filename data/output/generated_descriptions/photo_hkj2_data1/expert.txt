name: Photo Problem
description: The Photo Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming where a set of people need to be arranged in a line for a photo, subject to certain preferences. Each person has a list of other people they would like to stand next to, and the goal is to find an arrangement that satisfies as many of these preferences as possible.
variables: $n, positions, z, preferences$ - $n$ is the number of people, $positions$ is an array representing the position of each person in the line, $z$ is the number of satisfied preferences, and $preferences$ is a 2D array representing the preferences of each person.
constraints: $z \geq 10, positions \in 0..n-1, \forall i \in 1..n (positions[i] \neq positions[j] \forall j \in 1..n, i \neq j)$ - these constraints ensure that each person is assigned a unique position, and that the number of satisfied preferences is at least 10.
objective: satisfy - the objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than optimizing a specific value. However, the problem can also be formulated as a maximization problem, where the objective is to maximize the number of satisfied preferences ($z$).
