Photo Problem
The problem is about arranging people in a line for a photo, taking into account their preferences for who they want to stand next to.
The main variables are: 
- positions: the order in which people stand in the line
- preferences: a list of pairs of people who want to stand next to each other
- z: the number of satisfied preferences
- n: the number of people in the photo
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- each person can only stand in one position
- each preference is either satisfied (the two people stand next to each other) or not
The goal of the problem is to find an arrangement of people that satisfies as many preferences as possible, with a minimum of 10 satisfied preferences.
