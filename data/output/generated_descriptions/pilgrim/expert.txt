name: The Riddle of the Pilgrims
description: The problem describes a scenario where pilgrims are to be allocated to rooms in a 3x3 grid on two floors. The goal is to find two solutions that satisfy certain conditions, including the total number of pilgrims on each floor, the number of pilgrims on the front and back sides, and the number of pilgrims on the left and right sides. Additionally, there are constraints on the minimum and maximum number of pilgrims that can be allocated to each room.
variables: $x_{i,j,k,m}$, where $x_{i,j,k,m}$ represents the number of pilgrims allocated to the room at floor $j$, row $k$, and column $m$ in solution $i$. 
constraints: 
$\forall i \in N, j \in F, k \in R, l \in C: x_{i,j,k,l} \geq 0$,
$\sum_{j \in F, k \in R, m \in C} x_{1,j,k,m} + 3 = \sum_{j \in F, k \in R, m \in C} x_{2,j,k,m}$,
$\forall i \in N: \sum_{k \in R, m \in C} 2x_{i,1,k,m} = \sum_{k \in R, m \in C} x_{i,2,k,m}$,
$\forall i \in N, k \in R \text{ where } k \neq 2: \sum_{j \in F, m \in C} x_{i,j,k,m} = 11$,
$\forall i \in N, m \in C \text{ where } m \neq 2: \sum_{j \in F, k \in R} x_{i,j,k,m} = 11$,
$\forall i \in N, j \in F, k \in R, m \in C \text{ where } k \neq 2 \text{ or } m \neq 2: x_{i,j,k,m} \geq 1$,
$\forall i \in N, j \in F, k \in R, m \in C \text{ where } k \neq 2 \text{ or } m \neq 2: x_{i,j,k,m} \leq 3$,
$\forall i \in N, j \in F: x_{i,j,2,2} = 0$. 
These constraints ensure that the number of pilgrims is non-negative, the difference between the two solutions is 3, the number of pilgrims on the second floor is twice the number on the first floor, there are 11 pilgrims on the front and back sides, there are 11 pilgrims on the left and right sides, each room has at least one pilgrim and at most three pilgrims, and the central cell is empty.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the model, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
