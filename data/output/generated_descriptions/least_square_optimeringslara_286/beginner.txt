The problem is called Least Square. 
It's about finding the best way to draw a curve that fits a set of given points on a graph, where each point has a temperature and a percentage of gas. 
The main variables are: 
- t, which represents the temperatures at different points, 
- F, which represents the percentages of gas at those points, 
- a, which represents the coefficients of the curve equation that we're trying to find, 
- z, which represents how well the curve fits the points. 
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- the curve must be of a specific type, a polynomial of degree 4, 
- the curve must pass through or close to the given points, 
- the curve must satisfy two specific conditions at temperatures 20 and 700, 
- the slope of the curve must be non-negative at all points. 
The goal of the problem is to minimize z, which means finding the curve that best fits the given points.
