The name of the problem is the Bananas problem. 
This problem is about purchasing a combination of fruits, specifically bananas, oranges, mangoes, and apples, with certain constraints on their quantities and costs, while minimizing the total quantity of bananas and apples purchased.

The main decision variables are bananas, oranges, mangoes, and apples, which represent the quantities of each fruit to be purchased. 

The key constraints are that at least one of each fruit must be purchased, the total cost of the fruits must equal $100, and the total quantity of fruits must be 100. The cost constraint is calculated based on the given prices for each fruit: $3 for 5 bananas, $5 for 7 oranges, $7 for 9 mangoes, and $9 for 3 apples.

The problem's objective is to minimize the sum of the quantities of bananas and apples purchased, as the problem states a dislike for these fruits, while still fulfilling the other constraints.
