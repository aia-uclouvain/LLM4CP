Rotation Permutation Puzzle
The problem is about solving a puzzle where you have a 4x4 grid of numbers and you can perform four different operations, each of which involves rotating the numbers in a 3x3 square clockwise. The goal is to find the shortest sequence of operations to transform the initial grid into a grid where the numbers are in order from 1 to 16.

The main variables are:
- `x`: a 2D array representing the state of the grid at each step
- `init`: an array representing the initial state of the grid
- `ops`: an array representing the operations performed at each step
- `check_ix`: a variable representing the step at which the grid is in the desired state

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- The grid is a 4x4 square of numbers
- There are four possible operations, each involving a clockwise rotation of a 3x3 square
- The numbers maintain their orientation and do not rotate

The goal of the problem is to minimize `check_ix`, which represents the number of steps required to transform the initial grid into the desired state. In other words, the goal is to find the shortest sequence of operations to solve the puzzle.
