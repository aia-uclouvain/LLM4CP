name: Rotation Permutation Puzzle
description: The Rotation Permutation Puzzle is a problem where we have a 4x4 square of numbers and four possible operations, each involving rotating the numbers in a 3x3 square clockwise. The goal is to find a sequence of operations that transforms the initial configuration into a target configuration, which is the identity permutation.
variables: $x_{i,j}, init_j, ops_i, check\_ix$ 
These are the decision variables where $x_{i,j}$ represents the number in the $j^{th}$ position after the $i^{th}$ operation, $init_j$ represents the initial configuration, $ops_i$ represents the operation performed at the $i^{th}$ step, and $check\_ix$ represents the index of the operation where the target configuration is reached.
constraints: $\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, n\} : x_{1,j} = init_j$, 
$\forall i \in \{2, \ldots, rows\} : permutation3([x_{i-1,k} | k \in \{1, \ldots, n\}],[moves[m,k] | k \in \{1, \ldots, n\}],[x_{i,k} | k \in \{1, \ldots, n\}])$, 
$\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, n\} : x_{check\_ix,j} = j$, 
$\forall k \in \{2, \ldots, rows\} : k > check\_ix \rightarrow (\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, n\} : x_{k,j} = j \wedge ops_k = 0)$, 
$\forall i \in \{2, \ldots, rows\} : i < check\_ix \rightarrow ops_i > 0$
These constraints ensure that the initial configuration is set correctly, each operation is performed correctly, the target configuration is reached at $check\_ix$, and all operations after $check\_ix$ do not change the configuration.
objective: minimize $check\_ix$ 
The objective is to minimize the index of the operation where the target configuration is reached, which means finding the shortest sequence of operations to transform the initial configuration into the target configuration.
