Urban Planning Problem
The Urban Planning problem is about arranging residential and commercial lots in a 5x5 grid to maximize the quality of the layout, which is determined by a points system.

The main decision variables are the `x` array, where `x[i, j]` represents the type of lot (residential or commercial) at position `(i, j)` in the grid. The `row_scores` and `col_scores` arrays represent the scores for each row and column, respectively.

The key constraints are:
- Each row and column must have a score based on the number of residential lots it contains, with more residential lots resulting in higher scores.
- The total number of residential lots must be 12.
- The grid must be filled with either residential or commercial lots.
- A symmetry-breaking constraint is imposed to reduce the search space.

The objective is to maximize the total score, `z`, which is the sum of the scores for all rows and columns. The score for each row or column is determined by the number of residential lots it contains, with 5 residential lots resulting in a score of +5, 4 residential lots resulting in a score of +4, and so on. Conversely, having 5 commercial lots results in a score of -5, 4 commercial lots results in a score of -4, and so on.
