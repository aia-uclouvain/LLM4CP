name: Plotting
description: The Plotting problem is a combinatorial problem where the goal is to find a sequence of shots in a grid-based game to remove a certain number of blocks. The game starts with a grid filled with blocks of different colors, and the player has a hand that can hold one color at a time. The player can shoot down a column or along a row, and the shot will remove all blocks of the same color as the hand until it hits a block of a different color or an empty space. The blocks above the removed blocks will then fall down to fill the gap. The goal is to find a sequence of shots that will remove a certain number of blocks, while satisfying various constraints such as the number of steps allowed and the initial and final state of the grid.
variables: $fpRow, fpCol, grid, hand$ 
These are the decision variables of the problem. $fpRow$ and $fpCol$ represent the row and column where the player shoots, $grid$ represents the state of the grid at each step, and $hand$ represents the color of the block in the player's hand at each step.
constraints: 
$fpRow[step] * fpCol[step] = 0 \wedge fpRow[step] + fpCol[step] > 0$, 
$sum(flatten(grid[step-1,..,..])) > sum(flatten(grid[step,..,..]))$, 
$atleast(flatten(grid[noSteps,..,..]), [NOBLOCKS - goalBlocksRemaining], [EMPTY])$, 
and various other constraints that define the behavior of the game, such as the effect of shooting down a column or along a row, and the falling of blocks.
These constraints ensure that the sequence of shots is valid and satisfies the rules of the game, and that the final state of the grid is as desired.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a sequence of shots that satisfies all the constraints and removes the desired number of blocks, while minimizing the number of steps taken.
