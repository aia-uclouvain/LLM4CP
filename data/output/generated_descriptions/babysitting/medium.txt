Babysitting puzzle
The Babysitting puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem that involves determining the full names and ages of five children, given certain clues about their relationships.
The main decision variables in this problem are the first names, last names, and ages of the children. The first names are represented by the variables Keith, Libby, Margo, Nora, and Otto, while the last names are represented by the variables Fell, Gant, Hall, Ivey, and Jule. The ages of the children are represented by the array "age" of variables, where each variable is assigned a value between 2 and 6.
The key constraints in this problem can be described in plain language as follows: each child has a unique last name and age, one child is named Libby Jule, Keith is one year older than the Ivey child who is one year older than Nora, the Fell child is three years older than Margo, and Otto is twice as old as the Hall child. Additionally, certain children cannot have the same last name, such as Keith and Ivey, or Ivey and Nora, or Fell and Margo, or Otto and Hall.
The objective of this problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, thereby determining the full names and ages of the five children. The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find an assignment of values to the variables that makes all the constraints true.
