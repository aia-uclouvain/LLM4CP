Volsay problem
The problem is about a company that produces two products, gas and chloride, using three components: nitrogen, hydrogen, and chlorine. 
The main variables are the amount of each product to produce, called Production, and the total profit, called z. 
The basic restrictions are that the company cannot use more of each component than it has in stock. 
The goal is to maximize the total profit, z, by deciding how much of each product to produce, while not using more components than available.
