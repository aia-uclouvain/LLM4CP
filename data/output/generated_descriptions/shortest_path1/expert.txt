name: Car Replacement Problem
description: The Car Replacement Problem is a classic problem in operations research and decision analysis that involves determining when to replace a car based on its maintenance costs, trade-in value, and the cost of purchasing a new car. The goal is to minimize the total cost of owning and maintaining a car over a certain period of time.
variables: $x_{i,j}, cost_{i,j}, z$ where $x_{i,j}$ represents the decision to replace the car at time $i$ with a new one that will be used until time $j$, $cost_{i,j}$ is the cost associated with this decision, and $z$ is the total cost.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..timex, j \in 1..timex+1: cost_{i,j} \geq 0, x_{i,j} \geq 0$, $\forall i \in 1..timex, j \in i+1..timex+1: cost_{i,j} = new\_car + \sum_{k=1}^{j-i} maintenance_k - trade\_in_{j-i}$, $\forall i \in 1..timex, j \in 1..timex+1 \text{ where } j < i: cost_{i,j} = new\_car^2$, $\forall i \in 1..timex: \sum_{j=1}^{timex+1} x_{i,j} = supply_i$, $\forall j \in 1..timex+1: \sum_{i=1}^{timex} x_{i,j} = demand_j$ which represent the non-negativity constraints, the cost calculation, the transshipment constraints, and the supply and demand constraints respectively.
objective: Minimize $z = \sum_{i=1}^{timex} \sum_{j=1}^{timex+1} x_{i,j} \cdot cost_{i,j}$, which represents the total cost of owning and maintaining the car over the given period of time.
