name: Enigma 1535 (Back to front)

description: The problem is about finding two 6-digit numbers that have a unique property: when the order of their digits is reversed, the new number is a multiple of the original number. The task is to find the lower of these two 6-digit numbers.

variables: $x1, x2, x1_a, x2_a$ 
These are the decision variables where $x1$ and $x2$ represent the two 6-digit numbers, and $x1_a$ and $x2_a$ are arrays of variables representing the digits of $x1$ and $x2$ respectively.

constraints: $toNum(x1_a, x1), toNum(x2_a, x2), reverse(x1_a, x2_a), x1 > x2, x1 \mod x2 = 0$ 
These constraints ensure that $x1$ and $x2$ are converted correctly from their digit arrays, that the digits of $x2$ are the reverse of the digits of $x1$, that $x1$ is greater than $x2$, and that $x1$ is a multiple of $x2$.

objective: The objective of the problem is to find the lower 6-digit number $x2$ that satisfies the given constraints, which is achieved by using the int_search function with the first_fail and indomain_min options to find the smallest possible value for $x2$.
