The problem is called "Back to Front" or Enigma 1535. 
It is about finding two 6-digit numbers that have a unique property: when their digits are reversed, the new number is a multiple of the original number. 
The main decision variables are x1 and x2, which represent the two 6-digit numbers, and x1_a and x2_a, which are arrays of variables representing the digits of x1 and x2, respectively. 
The key constraints are that the digits of x1 reversed must be equal to the digits of x2, x1 must be greater than x2, and x1 must be a multiple of x2. 
The problem's objective is to find the lower of the two 6-digit numbers that satisfy these constraints, which is x2.
