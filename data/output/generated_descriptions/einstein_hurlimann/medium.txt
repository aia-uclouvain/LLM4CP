Einstein Puzzle
The Einstein puzzle is a classic logic puzzle that involves determining the characteristics of five houses, each occupied by a person of a different nationality, with a different favorite color, pet, drink, and brand of cigarettes.
The main decision variables in this problem are arrays A, B, C, D, and E, which represent the nationalities, colors, pets, drinks, and cigarette brands of the five houses, respectively. Each array is indexed by the house number, and the value at each index represents the corresponding characteristic of that house.
The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows: each house has a unique nationality, color, pet, drink, and cigarette brand; the person who owns a cat lives in the yellow house; the person who drinks milk lives next to the person who owns a cat; the person who owns a bird lives in the house next to the person who drinks juice; and so on. These constraints are used to deduce the characteristics of each house.
The objective of the Einstein puzzle is not to minimize or maximize a particular quantity, but rather to satisfy all the given constraints and determine the unique solution that meets all the conditions. In other words, the goal is to find the assignment of nationalities, colors, pets, drinks, and cigarette brands to each house that satisfies all the constraints.
