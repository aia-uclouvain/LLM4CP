The Alphametics Problem
The Alphametics problem is a classic constraint programming problem where letters in a mathematical equation are replaced by digits, and the goal is to find the correct assignment of digits to letters such that the equation holds true.
The main decision variables in this problem are the letters A, N, C, E, L, R, T, I, G, and F, which represent the digits 0-9. These variables are defined as var Domain: A; ... var Domain: F;, where Domain is a set of integers from 0 to 9.
The key constraints in this problem are that each letter must be assigned a unique digit (all_different(x)), and the mathematical equation represented by the letters must be satisfied. The equation is a complex expression involving the letters and their corresponding digit values, with the goal of finding an assignment that makes the equation true. In plain language, the constraint is that the sum of the values of the letters on the left-hand side of the equation must be equal to the sum of the values of the letters on the right-hand side.
The objective of the Alphametics problem is to find a satisfying assignment of digits to letters, which means finding a solution that satisfies all the constraints. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize; instead, the goal is to find a feasible solution that makes the equation true.
