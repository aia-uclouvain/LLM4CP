The Pairs Divide the Sum problem. 
This problem is about finding a set of distinct positive integers such that for every pair of them, their difference divides their sum. 
The main decision variables are x, an array of n variables representing the distinct positive integers, and z, a variable representing the sum of the integers in x. 
The key constraints are that all the integers in x must be different, the integers in x must be in increasing order, the sum of the integers z must be divisible by the number of integers n, and for every pair of integers in x, their difference must divide their sum. 
The problem's objective is to find sets of distinct positive integers that satisfy these constraints, with no specific minimization or maximization goal, as the problem is a satisfaction problem.
