name: Hadamard Problem
description: The Hadamard problem is a classic problem in combinatorial design theory, which involves constructing two sequences of values, each consisting of 1s and -1s, such that the sequences have certain properties. The problem is named after the French mathematician Jacques Hadamard, who first studied these sequences in the late 19th century. The goal is to find two sequences that satisfy certain quadratic constraints, which have applications in various fields such as coding theory, cryptography, and statistics.
variables: $x = (x_1, x_2, ..., x_n)$ and $y = (y_1, y_2, ..., y_n)$, where $x_i$ and $y_i$ are decision variables representing the ith value of the first and second sequences, respectively, which can take values of either -1 or 1.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i = 1$, $\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i = 1$, and $\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i \cdot x_{(i+k) \mod n} + \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i \cdot y_{(i+k) \mod n} = -2$ for $k = 1, 2, ..., m$, where $m = \frac{n-1}{2}$, which represent the constraints that the sum of the values in each sequence should be 1, and the quadratic constraints that the sequences should satisfy.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find two sequences that satisfy all the given constraints, with no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, as the goal is to construct sequences that meet the specified properties.
