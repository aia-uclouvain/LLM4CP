name: Speaker Scheduling Problem
description: The problem involves scheduling a set of speakers to present their talks in a conference with a limited number of time slots. Each speaker has a specific availability and a predetermined talk duration. The goal is to find a schedule that allows all speakers to present their talks without overlapping and finishes as early as possible.
variables: $starting\_slot[i], ending\_slot[i], z$ where $i \in 1..num\_speakers$. These variables represent the starting and ending time slots assigned to each speaker, and $z$ is the maximum ending time slot among all speakers, which represents the finish time of the conference.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..num\_speakers: ending\_slot[i] = starting\_slot[i] + app\_durations[i] - 1$ and $\forall i,j \in 1..num\_speakers, i < j: cumulative(starting\_slot, app\_durations, [1 | i \in 1..num\_speakers], 1)$ and $\forall i \in 1..num\_speakers, \forall j \in 1..app\_durations[i]: starting\_slot[i]+j-1 \in speaker\_availability[i]$. These constraints ensure that the ending time slot of each speaker is calculated correctly, that there are no overlapping talks, and that each speaker is assigned to a time slot where they are available.
objective: minimize $z$
