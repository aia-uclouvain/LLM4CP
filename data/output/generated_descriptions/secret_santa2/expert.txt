name: Secret Santa Problem
description: The Secret Santa problem is a classic problem in constraint programming where a group of people participate in a gift exchange, and each person is randomly assigned to give a gift to another person in the group. The problem has several constraints, including that no person can be assigned to give a gift to themselves, no person can be assigned to give a gift to their spouse, and no person can be assigned to give a gift to the same person two years in a row. The goal is to find an assignment that satisfies these constraints and maximizes the time since the last time each person was assigned to give a gift to the same person.
variables: $santas_i, santa\_distance_i, z$ where $i = 1..n$. These variables represent the decision variables in the problem: $santas_i$ is the person assigned to receive a gift from person $i$, $santa\_distance_i$ is the time since the last time person $i$ was assigned to give a gift to the same person, and $z$ is the total time since the last time each person was assigned to give a gift to the same person. 
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..n: santas_i \neq i \land (spouses_i = 0 \lor santas_i \neq spouses_i) \land santa\_distance_i = rounds_{i,santas_i} \land z = \sum_{i=1}^{n} santa\_distance_i \land \forall i \in 1..n: \forall j \in 1..n: rounds_{i,j} = 1 \rightarrow santas_i \neq j \land \forall i \in 1..n: santas_i \in 1..n \land \forall i \in 1..n: santa\_distance_i \in 1..n+1$. These constraints ensure that each person is assigned to give a gift to a different person, that no person is assigned to give a gift to their spouse, that the time since the last time each person was assigned to give a gift to the same person is calculated correctly, and that the total time since the last time each person was assigned to give a gift to the same person is calculated correctly.
objective: Maximize $z$, the total time since the last time each person was assigned to give a gift to the same person.
