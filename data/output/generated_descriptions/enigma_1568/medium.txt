The name of the problem is the Odd Puzzle, also known as Enigma problem 1568. 
This problem is about finding a six-figure odd number, referred to as PUZZLE, which is the result of a multiplication of two three-digit numbers, num1 and num2. 
The main decision variables in this problem are the digits O, D, P, U, Z, L, and E, which represent the individual digits of the numbers ODD and PUZZLE, as well as the array x, which contains these digits, and the variables num1, num2, num3, num4, and ODD, which represent the numbers in the multiplication. 
The key constraints in this problem are that different letters stand for different digits, the same letter stands for the same digit, each dot can be any digit, and leading digits cannot be zero. Additionally, the constraints ensure that the multiplication of num1 and num2 equals the sum of num3, num4, ODD, and PUZZLE, and that PUZZLE is an odd number. The "long multiplication" constraints break down the multiplication into smaller parts, ensuring that the digits are correctly calculated. 
The objective of this problem is to find the six-figure odd number PUZZLE that satisfies all the given constraints, with no specific minimization or maximization objective, but rather a satisfaction of the constraints to find a valid solution.
