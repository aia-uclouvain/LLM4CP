Tennis Tournament Scheduling Problem
The Tennis Tournament Scheduling Problem is about organizing a round-robin tennis tournament with a limited number of players and courts. Each player must play against every other player, and there must be a break between matches for each player.

The main decision variables in this problem are represented by a 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` denotes the round number when player `i` plays against player `j`. The objective is to find a schedule that minimizes the total number of rounds required to complete the tournament.

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows: 
- Each player can play at most one match per round.
- A player must rest for at least one round after playing a match.
- The number of matches played in each round cannot exceed the number of available courts.
- The tournament must be a round-robin, meaning each player plays against every other player exactly once.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the maximum round number, denoted by `z`, which represents the total number of rounds required to complete the tournament. In other words, the goal is to find a schedule that allows all players to play against each other in the fewest number of rounds possible, given the constraints on court availability and player rest.
