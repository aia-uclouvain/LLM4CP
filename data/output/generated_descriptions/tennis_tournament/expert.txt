name: Tennis Tournament Scheduling Problem
description: The problem involves scheduling a tennis tournament where each player plays against every other player, with a limited number of courts available. The goal is to find a schedule that minimizes the number of rounds required to complete all matches, ensuring that no player plays two rounds without a break.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ for $i,j = 1,\ldots,n$, $z$: The decision variables $x_{i,j}$ represent the round number at which player $i$ plays against player $j$, and $z$ represents the maximum round number (i.e., the total number of rounds required to complete all matches).
constraints: 
$\forall k \in \{1,\ldots,n\}: \sum_{i,j \in \{1,\ldots,n\}, i < j} (x_{i,j} = k) \leq \text{num\_courts}$, 
$x_{i,i} = 0$ for $i = 1,\ldots,n$, 
$x_{i,j} = x_{j,i}$ for $i,j = 1,\ldots,n$, 
$x_{i,j} > 0$ for $i,j = 1,\ldots,n$, $i \neq j$, 
$|x_{p,p_2} - x_{p,p_3}| \geq 2$ for $p,p_2,p_3 = 1,\ldots,n$, $p_2 \neq p$, $p_3 \neq p$, $p_2 < p_3$, 
$x_{1,2} = 1$, 
$\text{increasing}([x_{1,j}]_{j=1}^n)$: 
These constraints ensure that the number of matches played in each round does not exceed the number of available courts, that each player plays against every other player, that no player plays two rounds without a break, and that the schedule is symmetric and sorted.
objective: Minimize $z$, the maximum round number, which represents the total number of rounds required to complete all matches.
