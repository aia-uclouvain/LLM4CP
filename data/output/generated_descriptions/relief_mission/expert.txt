name: Relief Mission
description: The problem involves coordinating relief efforts after a disaster by dropping two identical relief packages from the air to minimize the total distance that villagers must travel to pick up the supplies. The villagers are located in a grid, and each package can be dropped on any cell. The distance between cells is calculated using a direct line, and each villager will walk to the nearest drop location to pick up relief supplies.
variables: $x_{i,j}, distances_{i,j}, total\_dist$ 
These variables represent the following: $x_{i,j}$ is a 2x2 decision variable matrix where $x_{1,1}, x_{1,2}$ and $x_{2,1}, x_{2,2}$ are the coordinates of the two drop locations, $distances_{i,j}$ is a matrix representing the distance from each cell to the nearest package, and $total\_dist$ is the total distance that all villagers must travel.
constraints: $\forall i, j \in 1..n (huts_{i,j} = 1 \rightarrow distances_{i,j} = min(dist(x_{1,1}, x_{1,2}, i, j), dist(x_{2,1}, x_{2,2}, i, j))) \wedge \forall i, j \in 1..n (huts_{i,j} = 0 \rightarrow distances_{i,j} = 0) \wedge total\_dist = \sum_{i,j \in 1..n} distances_{i,j} \wedge total\_dist \geq 0 \wedge x_{1,1} \leq x_{2,1}$ 
These constraints ensure that the distance from each cell with a hut to the nearest package is calculated correctly, cells without huts have a distance of 0, the total distance is the sum of all distances, the total distance is non-negative, and the symmetry is broken by ensuring that the first drop location is to the left of or at the same position as the second drop location.
objective: minimize $total\_dist$
