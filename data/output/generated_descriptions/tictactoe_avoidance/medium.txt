Tic Tac Toe Avoidance Problem
The problem is about placing a certain number of X's on a Tic Tac Toe board without creating three X's in a row in any direction, including horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

The main decision variables are the elements of the 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` represents the value of the cell at row `i` and column `j` on the board, with 0 indicating an empty cell or an O, and 1 indicating an X.

The key constraints are:
- The total number of X's on the board must be equal to `n*n - n`, where `n` is the size of the board.
- Each row and column can have at most `n-1` X's.
- The main diagonal and the anti-diagonal can have at most `n-1` X's.
- A symmetry-breaking constraint is also included to reduce the number of equivalent solutions.

The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of X's and O's on the board, such that all constraints are met, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function. The model uses the `solve satisfy` statement, which means that it aims to find a feasible solution that satisfies all constraints, without optimizing a specific objective.
