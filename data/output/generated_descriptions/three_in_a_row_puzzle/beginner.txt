3-in-a-row puzzle
The problem is about filling a grid with two colors, blue and white, in such a way that no three squares of the same color are in a row or column, and each row and column has an equal number of blue and white squares.
The main variables are the squares in the grid, which can be either blue (represented by 1) or white (represented by 0).
The basic restrictions are that no three squares of the same color can be in a row or column, and each row and column must have an equal number of blue and white squares.
The goal of the problem is to find a way to fill the grid that satisfies these restrictions.
