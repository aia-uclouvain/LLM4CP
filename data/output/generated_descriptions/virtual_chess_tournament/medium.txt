Virtual Chess Tournament
The Virtual Chess Tournament problem is about determining the outcome of a series of chess matches between three players, Fisher, Kasparov, and Karpov, given certain constraints on their performance.
The main decision variables in this problem are: 
- `x`: an array representing the outcome of each match, 
- `total`: an array representing the total points earned by each player, 
- `winners` and `losers`: arrays representing the winner and loser of each match, 
- `num_wins` and `num_losses`: arrays representing the number of wins and losses for each player.
The key constraints in this problem are: 
- each match has one of three possible outcomes (win, loss, or draw), 
- the total points earned by each player is calculated based on the outcomes of their matches, 
- the number of wins and losses for each player is calculated based on the outcomes of their matches, 
- Kasparov wins the most games, 
- Karpov loses the least games, and 
- Fisher wins the tournament.
The objective of this problem is to find a set of match outcomes that satisfy all the given constraints, thereby determining the final scores of the tournament.
