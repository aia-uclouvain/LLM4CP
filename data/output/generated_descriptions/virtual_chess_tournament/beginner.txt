Virtual Chess Tournament
The problem is about a chess tournament between three world champions: Fisher, Kasparov, and Karpov. Each player plays 7 games against the other two players, and the goal is to determine the final scores and the winner of the tournament.

The main variables are:
- x: the outcome of each match (win, loss, or draw)
- total: the total points for each player
- winners and losers: the winner and loser of each match
- num_wins and num_losses: the number of wins and losses for each player
- num_draws_total: the total number of draws in the tournament

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- Each player plays 7 games against the other two players
- A win is worth 2 points, a draw is worth 1 point, and a loss is worth 0 points
- Kasparov wins the most games
- Karpov loses the least games
- Fisher wins the tournament

The goal of the problem is to find the final scores and the winner of the tournament, given the restrictions and rules. The problem aims to minimize or maximize nothing, but rather to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints and determines the winner of the tournament.
