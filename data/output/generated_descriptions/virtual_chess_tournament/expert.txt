name: Virtual Chess Tournament
description: The Virtual Chess Tournament problem involves determining the outcome of a series of chess matches between three players - Fisher, Kasparov, and Karpov. Each player plays 7 games against the other two players, with 2 points awarded for a win, 1 point for a draw, and 0 points for a loss. The problem aims to find a possible outcome where Kasparov wins the most games, Karpov loses the least games, and Fisher wins the tournament.
variables: $x_{1..21}, total_{1..3}, winners_{1..21}, losers_{1..21}, num\_wins_{1..3}, num\_losses_{1..3}, num\_draws\_total$ - These decision variables represent the outcome of each match ($x$), the total points earned by each player ($total$), the winner and loser of each match ($winners$ and $losers$), the number of wins and losses for each player ($num\_wins$ and $num\_losses$), and the total number of draws ($num\_draws\_total$).
constraints: $\forall i \in 0..20 (x_{i+1} \in valid\_outcome_{1+(i \mod 3)})$, $winners_i = W_{x_i}$, $losers_i = L_{x_i}$, $total_p = \sum_{i=1}^{21} M_{x_i, p}$, $num\_wins_p = \sum_{i=1}^{21} bool2int(winners_i = p)$, $num\_losses_p = \sum_{i=1}^{21} bool2int(losers_i = p)$, $num\_wins_{Kasparov} > num\_wins_{Fisher}$, $num\_wins_{Kasparov} > num\_wins_{Karpov}$, $num\_losses_{Karpov} < num\_losses_{Fisher}$, $num\_losses_{Karpov} < num\_losses_{Kasparov}$, $total_{Fisher} > total_{Kasparov}$, $total_{Fisher} > total_{Karpov}$ - These constraints ensure that the outcome of each match is valid, the winner and loser of each match are correctly assigned, the total points and number of wins and losses for each player are accurately calculated, and the given conditions (Kasparov wins the most games, Karpov loses the least games, and Fisher wins the tournament) are satisfied.
objective: satisfy - The objective of this problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
