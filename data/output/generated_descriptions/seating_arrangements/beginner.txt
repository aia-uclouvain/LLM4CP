Seating Arrangement Problem
The problem is about organizing a party with multiple tables, each having a certain number of chairs. There are guests who like each other and want to sit together, and guests who dislike each other and want to sit apart. 

The main variables are: 
- The number of tables (n) and the number of chairs at each table (m), which determine the total number of guests (k). 
- The "likes" and "dislikes" lists, which are pairs of guests who want to sit together or apart, respectively. 
- The "x" variable, which assigns each guest to a table. 
- The "tables" variable, which keeps track of the guests assigned to each table.

The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- Each table can only hold a certain number of guests (m). 
- Guests who like each other must be assigned to the same table. 
- Guests who dislike each other must be assigned to different tables.

The goal of the problem is to find a seating arrangement that satisfies all these rules, or to determine that it is impossible to do so. In other words, the problem aims to assign each guest to a table in a way that makes everyone happy, or at least does not make anyone unhappy, according to their likes and dislikes.
