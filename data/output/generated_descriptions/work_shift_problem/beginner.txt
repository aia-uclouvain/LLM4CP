Work-shift scheduling problem
The problem is about assigning six workers to three different work shifts, making sure each shift has the right number of workers. 
The main variables are the workers (Alan, Bob, John, Mike, Scott, and Ted) and the work shifts (1, 2, and 3) they can be assigned to. 
The basic restrictions are that the first shift needs 1-4 workers, the second shift needs 2-3 workers, and the third shift needs exactly 2 workers. Also, Alan cannot work on the first shift, and Bob can only work on the third shift. 
The goal is to find a way to assign all the workers to the shifts that satisfies these rules, which is called a "feasible solution".
