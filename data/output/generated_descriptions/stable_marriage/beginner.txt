Stable Marriage Problem
The problem is about matching men and women in a way that is stable, meaning that there is no pair of man and woman who would rather be with each other than with their current partner.
The main variables are: 
- num_men and num_women: the number of men and women
- rankMen and rankWomen: tables showing each man's and woman's preferences for partners, with lower numbers indicating a more preferred partner
- wife and husband: the partner assigned to each man and woman
The basic restrictions are: 
- each man and woman can only have one partner
- the partner assignments must be consistent, meaning that if a man is assigned to a woman, she must also be assigned to him
- the assignments must be stable, meaning that there is no pair of man and woman who would rather be with each other than with their current partner
The goal of the problem is to find a stable set of partner assignments, with no specific objective to minimize or maximize.
