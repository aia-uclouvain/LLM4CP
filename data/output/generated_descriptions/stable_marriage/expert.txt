name: Stable Marriage Problem
description: The Stable Marriage Problem is a well-known problem in computer science and operations research that involves finding a stable matching between two sets of entities, typically men and women, based on their preferences. The goal is to find a matching such that there is no pair of entities that would prefer each other to their current matches, resulting in an unstable matching.
variables: $num\_men, num\_women, rankWomen, rankMen, wife, husband$ - These variables represent the number of men and women, the preference rankings of men and women, and the assignments of wives to husbands and husbands to wives, respectively. $rankWomen$ and $rankMen$ are matrices where $rankWomen_{ij}$ represents the ranking of man $j$ by woman $i$, and $rankMen_{ij}$ represents the ranking of woman $j$ by man $i$. $wife$ and $husband$ are arrays where $wife_i$ represents the woman assigned to man $i$, and $husband_i$ represents the man assigned to woman $i$.
constraints: $\forall m \in 1..num\_men (husband[wife[m]] = m) \land \forall w \in 1..num\_women (wife[husband[w]] = w) \land \forall m \in 1..num\_men, o \in 1..num\_women (rankMen[m,o] < rankMen[m, wife[m]] \rightarrow rankWomen[o,husband[o]] < rankWomen[o,m]) \land \forall w \in 1..num\_women, o \in 1..num\_men (rankWomen[w,o] < rankWomen[w,husband[w]] \rightarrow rankMen[o,wife[o]] < rankMen[o,w])$ - These constraints ensure that the assignments of wives to husbands and husbands to wives are consistent, and that there is no pair of entities that would prefer each other to their current matches, resulting in an unstable matching.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a stable matching that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
