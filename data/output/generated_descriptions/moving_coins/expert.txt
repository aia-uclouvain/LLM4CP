name: Moving Coins Problem
description: The problem involves rearranging a triangular formation of coins to point downwards by moving as few coins as possible. The coins are initially arranged in a triangular formation with the base at the top and the apex at the bottom, and the goal is to transform this into a triangular formation with the base at the bottom and the apex at the top.
variables: $x_{i,j}, z, grid2_{i,j}, start\_positions2_{s,1}, start\_positions2_{s,2}$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the presence or absence of a coin at position $(i,j)$ in the final arrangement, $z$ represents the number of coins that remain in the same position, $grid2_{i,j}$ represents the initial position of the coins, and $start\_positions2_{s,1}$ and $start\_positions2_{s,2}$ represent the initial positions of the coins.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..n2, j \in 1..n2: grid2_{i,j} = 1 \leftrightarrow \exists s \in 1..n: start\_positions2_{s,1} = i \wedge start\_positions2_{s,2} = j$, $\forall j \in 1..n2: \sum_{i=1}^{n2} x_{i,j} = \sum_{i=1}^{n2} grid2_{i,j}$, $\exists i,j \in n div 2..n2-(n div 2): \forall a \in 1..k: \forall b \in 2..a*2: b mod 2 = 0 \rightarrow x_{i-a,j+a-b} = 1$, where the first constraint creates the problem instance from the start positions, the second constraint ensures that the number of coins in each column remains the same, and the third constraint places the coins in the final triangular formation.
objective: Maximize $z$, which represents the number of coins that remain in the same position, thereby minimizing the number of coins that need to be moved.
