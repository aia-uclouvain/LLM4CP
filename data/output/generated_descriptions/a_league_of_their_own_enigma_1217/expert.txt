name: A League of Their Own
description: The problem describes a round-robin football tournament between four teams, where each team plays each other twice, once at home and once away. The final league table is given, with the teams finishing in alphabetical order. The problem requires finding the matches that were drawn, given certain conditions such as the number of wins, draws, and losses for each team, and the total points scored.
variables: $games_{t1,t2,k}, scores_{t1,t}$, where $games_{t1,t2,k}$ represents the outcome of the match between team $t1$ and team $t2$ at location $k$ (home or away), and $scores_{t1,t}$ represents the score of team $t1$ for category $t$ (won, drawn, lost, or points). These variables are used to determine the outcome of each match and the final scores of each team.
constraints: $\forall t1 \in 1..n, t \in \{won,drawn,lost\} (scores_{t1,t} \leq 6)$, $scores_{t1,won} = \sum_{t2,k} (games_{t1,t2,k} = win)$, $scores_{t1,drawn} = \sum_{t2,k} (games_{t1,t2,k} = draw)$, $scores_{t1,lost} = \sum_{t2,k} (games_{t1,t2,k} = loss)$, $scores_{t1,points} = win \cdot scores_{t1,won} + draw \cdot scores_{t1,drawn}$, $\forall t1,t2,k (games_{t1,t2,k} = win \leftrightarrow games_{t2,t1,k} = loss)$, $\forall t1,t2,k (games_{t1,t2,k} = draw \leftrightarrow games_{t2,t1,k} = draw)$, $\forall t (all\_different([scores_{t1,t} | t1 \in 1..n]))$, $scores_{2,won} > scores_{1,won}$, $\sum_{t1,t2} (games_{t1,t2,away} = win) > \sum_{t1,t2} (games_{t1,t2,home} = win)$. These constraints ensure that the scores are consistent with the match outcomes, that the teams finish in alphabetical order, and that the given conditions are met.
objective: satisfy, the objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
