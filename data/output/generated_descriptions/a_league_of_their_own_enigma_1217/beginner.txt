A League of Their Own
The problem is about a football tournament between four teams: Fagan's, George, Harlequin, and Inkerman. Each team played each other twice, once at home and once away. The goal is to figure out which matches were drawn.

The main variables are the number of wins, draws, and losses for each team, as well as the points each team scored. In simple terms, these variables mean: 
- wins: the number of games a team won
- draws: the number of games a team tied
- losses: the number of games a team lost
- points: the total score of a team, with 2 points for a win and 1 point for a draw

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- each team played each other twice
- the teams finished in alphabetical order
- each column in the table (won, drawn, lost, points) has four different numbers
- George won more games than Fagan's, even though Fagan's won the tournament
- there were more away wins than home wins

The goal of the problem is to identify which matches were drawn, given the rules and restrictions above.
