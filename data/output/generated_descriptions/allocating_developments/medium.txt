**Problem Name:** Allocating Developments in a Mall

**Description:** The problem involves allocating four establishments - a Japanese restaurant, a hairstylist, a clothing store, and a toy store - in a 3x3 grid representing the floor plan of a new mall. The goal is to position these establishments while satisfying certain constraints based on marketing research, considering the existing positions of a fish and chips stand and a health store.

**Decision Variables:** The main decision variables are represented by the 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` denotes the establishment placed in cell `(i, j)` of the grid. Each cell can be assigned one of the six establishments (including the fish and chips stand and the health store) or left empty.

**Constraints:**

* The fish and chips stand is fixed in cell `(2, 2)`, and the health store is fixed in cell `(1, 0)`.
* The hairstylist and clothing store should not be close to the fish and chips stand.
* The Japanese restaurant should be close to the health store.
* The hairstylist and clothing store should be close to the Japanese restaurant.
* The hairstylist and clothing store should not be close to the toy store.
* Each establishment should be placed exactly once in the grid.

**Objective:** The objective is to find a feasible allocation of the establishments that satisfies all the given constraints. If some constraints cannot be satisfied, the model uses soft constraints to maximize the number of satisfied constraints.
