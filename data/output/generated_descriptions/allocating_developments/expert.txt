name: Allocating Developments in a Mall
description: The problem involves allocating four establishments - a Japanese restaurant, a hairstylist, a clothing store, and a toy store - in a 3x3 grid representing the floor plan of a new mall. The goal is to position these establishments while satisfying certain constraints based on marketing research, considering the positions of existing establishments like a fish and chips stand and a health store.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ where $i \in \{0,1,2\}$ and $j \in \{0,1,2\}$, which represent the type of establishment (Japanese restaurant, hairstylist, clothing store, toy store, fish and chips stand, health store, or empty) placed in cell $(i,j)$ of the grid. 
constraints: 
- $x_{2,2} = 5$ (fish and chips stand is in cell (2,2))
- $x_{1,0} = 6$ (health store is in cell (1,0))
- $\neg close(x, 2, 5)$ and $\neg close(x, 3, 5)$ (hairstylist and clothing store should not be close to the fish and chips stand)
- $close(x, 1, 6)$ (Japanese restaurant should be close to the health store)
- $close(x, 2, 1)$ and $close(x, 3, 1)$ (hairstylist and clothing store should be close to the Japanese restaurant)
- $\neg close(x, 2, 4)$ and $\neg close(x, 3, 4)$ (hairstylist and clothing store should not be close to the toy store)
- $alldifferent\_except\_0([x_{i,j} | i,j \in \{0,1,2\}])$ (each establishment is placed exactly once, and all cells are filled with one of the establishments or left empty)
objective: The objective is to satisfy all the given constraints, which can be achieved by either using hard constraints and finding a feasible solution or using soft constraints and maximizing the number of satisfied constraints ($max\_prefs$).
