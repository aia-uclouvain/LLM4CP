name: Correspondence Problem
description: The correspondence problem is a constraint satisfaction problem where we have two collections of items, FROM and TO, and a permutation that defines the correspondence between the items in the two collections. The goal is to find an assignment of items in the TO collection to the items in the FROM collection according to the given permutation.
variables: $from_1, from_2, ..., from_n, permutation_1, permutation_2, ..., permutation_n, to_1, to_2, ..., to_n$ - These are the decision variables representing the items in the FROM and TO collections and the permutation that defines the correspondence between them. The $from_i$ and $to_i$ variables represent the items in the FROM and TO collections, respectively, and the $permutation_i$ variables represent the index of the item in the TO collection that corresponds to the $i^{th}$ item in the FROM collection.
constraints: $all\_different(permutation) \wedge \forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., n\} (from_i = to_{permutation_i})$ - These constraints ensure that the permutation is a valid one (i.e., each item in the TO collection is assigned to at most one item in the FROM collection) and that the items in the FROM and TO collections are assigned according to the permutation.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of items in the TO collection to the items in the FROM collection according to the given permutation, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
