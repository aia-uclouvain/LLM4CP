Manasa and Stones problem.
The problem is about a person named Manasa who is following a trail of stones with numbers on them. Each stone has a number that is either a certain amount more than the previous stone, and this amount can be one of two values, let's call them a and b. The goal is to figure out what the number on the last stone could be, given that the first stone has the number 0.
The main variables are the number of stones (n), the two possible differences between consecutive stones (a and b), and the numbers on each stone (x). In simple terms, n is how many stones there are, a and b are the two possible steps you can take from one stone to the next, and x is the number on each stone.
The basic rules are that each stone's number must be either a or b more than the previous stone's number, and the numbers on the stones are in increasing order. This means that if you start at 0, you can only move to a higher number by adding either a or b.
The goal is to find all the possible numbers that could be on the last stone, given the rules and the values of n, a, and b. In other words, what are all the possible final steps you could take, following the rules, to get to the last stone.
