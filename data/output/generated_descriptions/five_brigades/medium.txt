The Five Brigands Puzzle
The problem is about five Spanish brigands - Alfonso, Benito, Carlos, Diego, and Esteban - who have captured a total of 200 doubloons. The puzzle states that if each brigand had a certain multiple of their original amount, they would still have a total of 200 doubloons. The goal is to find out how many doubloons each brigand had originally.

The main decision variables are A, B, C, D2, and E3, which represent the number of doubloons each brigand had. Specifically, A is the amount Alfonso had, B is the amount Benito had, C is the amount Carlos had, D2 is half the amount Diego had, and E3 is one-third the amount Esteban had.

The key constraints are: 
- The total amount of doubloons the brigands had is 200.
- If Alfonso had 12 times his original amount, Benito had 3 times his original amount, Carlos had the same amount, Diego had half his original amount, and Esteban had one-third his original amount, the total would still be 200 doubloons.
- Each brigand had at least 8 doubloons and no more than 160 doubloons.

The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find all possible combinations of doubloons that each brigand could have had, given the constraints. The problem is a feasibility problem, where the goal is to find all feasible solutions that satisfy the given conditions.
