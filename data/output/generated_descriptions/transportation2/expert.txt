name: Transportation Problem
description: The Transportation Problem is a classic problem in operations research and optimization, which involves finding the most cost-effective way to transport goods from a set of sources (plants) to a set of destinations (clients) while satisfying the demand of each client and not exceeding the capacity of each plant.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the amount of goods transported from plant $i$ to client $j$. These variables are decision variables that determine the optimal transportation plan.
constraints: $\forall i, j: x_{i,j} \geq 0$, $\forall i: \sum_{j} x_{i,j} \leq capacity_i$, $\forall j: \sum_{i} x_{i,j} = demand_j$, where the first constraint ensures that the amount of goods transported is non-negative, the second constraint ensures that the total amount of goods transported from each plant does not exceed its capacity, and the third constraint ensures that the total amount of goods transported to each client meets its demand.
objective: minimize $z = \sum_{i,j} x_{i,j} \cdot cost_{i,j}$, which represents the total transportation cost. The goal is to find the optimal transportation plan that minimizes the total cost while satisfying all the constraints.
