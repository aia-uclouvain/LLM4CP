The Gunport problem
The Gunport problem is about covering a rectangular grid with dominoes in such a way that each domino covers two adjacent cells, and no two adjacent cells are left uncovered.
The main decision variables are x[i,j,k] and d[k], where x[i,j,k] represents whether domino k is placed in cell (i,j) and d[k] represents whether domino k is used.
The key constraints are: 
- each domino must be placed in adjacent cells, 
- each domino must cover exactly two cells, 
- each cell can be covered by at most one domino, 
- no two adjacent cells can be left vacant, 
- the placement of dominoes must be such that the output matrix rt is correctly calculated.
The objective of the problem is to minimize the number of dominoes used, represented by the variable z, which is the sum of d[k] over all k.
