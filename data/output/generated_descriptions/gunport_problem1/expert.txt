name: Gunport Problem
description: The Gunport problem is a classic puzzle where a set of dominoes need to be placed on a grid such that each domino covers exactly two cells, and no two adjacent cells are left vacant. The problem is to find the minimum number of dominoes required to cover the grid.
variables: $x_{i,j,k}, d_k, z, rt_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j,k}$ represents whether domino $k$ is placed on cell $(i,j)$, $d_k$ represents whether domino $k$ is used, $z$ is the total number of dominoes used, and $rt_{i,j}$ is the output matrix representing the placement of dominoes.
constraints: 
$\sum_{l \in j-1..j+1, l \geq 1, l \leq n, l \neq j} x_{i,l,k} + \sum_{l \in i-1..i+1, l \geq 1, l \leq m, l \neq i} x_{l,j,k} \geq x_{i,j,k}$, 
$\sum_{i \in M, j \in N} x_{i,j,k} = 2d_k$, 
$\sum_{k \in P} x_{i,j,k} \leq 1$, 
$\sum_{c \in j..j+1, k \in P} x_{i,c,k} \geq 1$, 
$\sum_{r \in i..i+1, k \in P} x_{r,j,k} \geq 1$, 
$rt_{i,j} = \sum_{k \in P} kx_{i,j,k}$, 
which ensure that each domino is placed adjacent to other cells, each domino covers exactly two cells, each cell is covered by at most one domino, no two adjacent cells are vacant, and the output matrix represents the placement of dominoes.
objective: minimize $z = \sum_{k \in P} d_k$, which represents the total number of dominoes used.
