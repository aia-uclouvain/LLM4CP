Mama's Age Problem
The Mama's Age problem is a classic puzzle that involves determining the current age of Mama based on a set of clues provided by her and her husband, Papa, in a conversation with their son, Tommy.
The main decision variables in this problem are the ages of Mama (m), Papa (p), and Tommy (t), as well as a temporary variable (i) used to represent the number of years that will pass before Tommy is half as old as Papa. These variables represent the current ages of the family members in months.
The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows: the sum of the ages of Mama, Papa, and Tommy is 70 years (or 840 months), Papa is six times as old as Tommy, and at some point in the future, Tommy will be half as old as Papa, at which time the sum of their ages will be twice what it is now. Additionally, the problem involves converting the ages from months to years.
The objective of this problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a set of values for the ages of Mama, Papa, and Tommy that satisfy all the given constraints. In other words, the goal is to determine the current age of Mama (and the other family members) based on the clues provided. The solution will be a set of values for the ages that meet all the conditions outlined in the problem.
