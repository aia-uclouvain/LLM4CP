Harry Potter's Potion Problem
The problem is about identifying two specific bottles of potion from a set of seven bottles, where each bottle contains either a nettle wine, a poison, a forward-moving potion, or a backward-moving potion.
The main decision variables are `x`, an array of seven variables, each representing the content of a bottle, which can be one of four types: wine, poison, forward, or back. The variables `dwarf` and `giant` represent the smallest and largest bottles, respectively.
The key constraints are: 
- There is exactly one forward-moving potion, one backward-moving potion, two nettle wines, and three poisons.
- The smallest and largest bottles are not poison.
- Each wine bottle has a poison bottle to its left.
- The bottles at the ends are different and neither of them is the forward-moving potion.
- The second bottle from the left and the second bottle from the right have the same content.
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of the decision variables, which represents the correct arrangement of the bottles, allowing Harry and Hermione to move forward or backward. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints.
