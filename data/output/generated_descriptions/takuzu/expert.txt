name: Takuzu
description: Takuzu is a logic-based number placement puzzle where the objective is to fill a grid with 1s and 0s, such that there is an equal number of 1s and 0s in each row and column, no more than two of either number are adjacent to each other, and there are no identical rows or columns.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ and $j$ are the row and column indices, respectively. These variables represent the decision variables in the problem, which are the values to be assigned to each cell in the grid, either 0 or 1.
constraints: 
$\forall i \in 1..n: \sum_{j=1}^{n} (1 - x_{i,j}) = m \land \sum_{j=1}^{n} (1 - x_{j,i}) = m$ 
$\forall i \in 2..n-1, \forall j \in 1..n: (x_{i-1,j} \neq x_{i,j} \lor x_{i,j} \neq x_{i+1,j}) \land (x_{j,i-1} \neq x_{j,i} \lor x_{j,i} \neq x_{j,i+1})$ 
$\forall i,j \in 1..n, i < j: \exists k \in 1..n (x_{i,k} \neq x_{j,k}) \land \exists k \in 1..n (x_{k,i} \neq x_{k,j})$ 
$x_{i,j} = problem_{i,j}$. 
These constraints ensure that each row and column has an equal number of 0s and 1s, no more than two of the same values are adjacent, there are no identical rows or columns, and the given problem is initialized.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, which means finding a feasible solution that meets all the conditions, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
