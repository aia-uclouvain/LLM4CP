name: Project Euler Problem 2
description: The problem is to find the sum of all the even-valued terms in the Fibonacci sequence that do not exceed four million. The Fibonacci sequence is a series of numbers where each new term is generated by adding the previous two terms, starting with 1 and 2.
variables: $f_1, f_2, ..., f_n, x_1, x_2, ..., x_n, res$ - These variables represent the Fibonacci sequence ($f_i$) and a binary array ($x_i$) indicating whether the corresponding Fibonacci number is even and less than four million, and $res$ is the sum of the even-valued terms.
constraints: $f_1 = 1, f_2 = 1, f_i = f_{i-1} + f_{i-2} \forall i \in \{3, ..., n\}, (f_i \mod 2 = 0 \land f_i < 4000000) \leftrightarrow (x_i = 1) \forall i \in \{1, ..., n\}$ - These constraints define the Fibonacci sequence, and ensure that $x_i$ is 1 if and only if the corresponding Fibonacci number $f_i$ is even and less than four million.
objective: The objective is to find the value of $res$, which is the sum of all the even-valued terms in the Fibonacci sequence that do not exceed four million, but since the problem is modeled as a satisfaction problem, the objective is implicitly to satisfy all the constraints and compute the value of $res$.
