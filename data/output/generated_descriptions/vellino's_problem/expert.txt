name: Vellino's Problem

description: Vellino's Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves assigning materials to bins of different colors, each with its own capacity and restrictions on the materials it can contain. The goal is to minimize the number of bins used while satisfying all the constraints.

variables: $c, p$ 
$c$ is a function that assigns a color to each bin, and $p$ is a function that assigns a multiset of materials to each bin. In other words, $c$ determines the color of each bin, and $p$ determines the materials that are placed in each bin.

constraints: 
$\forall b \in Bin. b \in defined(c) \leftrightarrow b \in defined(p)$, 
$\forall m \in Material. \sum_{b \in defined(p)} freq(p(b), m) = quantity(m)$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). |p(b)| \leq capacity(c(b))$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). c(b) = red \rightarrow !(plastic \in p(b)) \land !(steel \in p(b))$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). c(b) = blue \rightarrow !(wood \in p(b)) \land !(plastic \in p(b))$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). c(b) = green \rightarrow !(steel \in p(b)) \land !(glass \in p(b))$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). c(b) = red \rightarrow freq(p(b), wood) \leq 1$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). c(b) = green \rightarrow freq(p(b), wood) \leq 2$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). wood \in p(b) \rightarrow plastic \in p(b)$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). glass \in p(b) \rightarrow !(copper \in p(b))$, 
$\forall b \in defined(c). copper \in p(b) \rightarrow !(plastic \in p(b))$ 
These constraints ensure that every bin with a color has a corresponding multiset of materials, that the total amount of each material is spread across all bins, that the capacity of each bin is not exceeded, and that the specific restrictions on the materials that can be placed in each colored bin are satisfied.

objective: minimize $|defined(c)|$ 
The objective is to minimize the number of bins that have a color assigned to them, which corresponds to minimizing the number of used bins.
