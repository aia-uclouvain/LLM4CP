Lex Chain Less Problem
The problem is about comparing and ordering sets of numbers in a specific way, like alphabetizing words. 
The main variables are sets of numbers, like lists of digits, and they mean the individual sets of numbers being compared.
The basic restrictions are that each set of numbers must be in a specific order, and each set must be "less than" the next set in a specific way, similar to how "apple" comes before "banana" in the dictionary.
The goal of the problem is to find a set of numbers that satisfies these ordering rules, and to output the sets of numbers in the correct order.
