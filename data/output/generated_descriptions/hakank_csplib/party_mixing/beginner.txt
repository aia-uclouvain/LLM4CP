Party Mixing Problem
The problem is about arranging people at tables so that each person talks to as many different people as possible. 
The main variables are the number of tables, the number of chairs at each table, and the arrangement of people at the tables. 
The basic restrictions are that each person must move to a different table, and people who were originally at the same table should not sit next to each other at the new table. 
The goal is to find an arrangement where each person talks to as many different people as possible.
