The name of the problem is the Queens problem. 
This problem is about placing as many queens as possible on a chessboard of size nxn, such that no two queens attack each other. 
The main decision variables are x[i,j], which represent whether a queen is placed in square [i,j] on the chessboard. 
The key constraints are that at most one queen can be placed in each row, column, and diagonal of the chessboard, ensuring that no two queens attack each other. 
The problem's objective is to maximize the number of queens placed on the chessboard, which is represented by the variable obj, the sum of all x[i,j] values.
