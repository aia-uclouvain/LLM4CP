Shopping Puzzle
The problem is about three husbands - Arthur, Bernard, and Charles - and their wives - Ann, Barbara, and Cynthia. We need to figure out who is married to whom and how many articles and books each person bought.
The main variables are: 
- w (a table showing which husband is married to which wife)
- x (the number of articles bought by each husband)
- y (the number of books bought by each wife, which also represents the price)
The basic restrictions are: 
- Each husband is married to only one wife
- Each wife is married to only one husband
- Arthur bought 23 more articles than Barbara
- Bernard bought 11 more articles than Ann
- Each husband spent $63 more than his wife on articles and books
The goal is to find a solution that satisfies all these rules, without trying to minimize or maximize anything. The problem is about finding a possible arrangement of marriages and purchases that meets all the given conditions.
