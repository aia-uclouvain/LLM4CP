name: The Logical Labyrinth (Smullyan) puzzle
description: The problem is a classic puzzle where a prisoner is standing in front of 9 doors, behind one of which is a lady, behind another is a tiger, and the remaining 7 doors are empty. The prisoner can see a sign on each door with a statement that may be true or false. The prisoner must figure out which door the lady is behind using the given statements. The statements on the doors are related to the contents of the other doors, and the prisoner must use logical reasoning to deduce the correct door.
variables: $x_{i,j}, t_i$ where $i \in \{1,2,...,9\}$ and $j \in \{1,2,3\}$. $x_{i,j}$ represents whether door $i$ hides prize $j$ (1 = Lady, 2 = Tiger, 3 = Empty), and $t_i$ represents whether the statement on door $i$ is true.
constraints: 
$t_1 = x_{1,1}+x_{3,1}+x_{5,1}+x_{7,1}+x_{9,1}$, 
$t_2 = x_{2,3}$, 
$t_3 = (t_5 + x_{1,1} > 1)$, 
$t_4 = 1-t_1$, 
$t_5 = (t_2 + t_4 > 1)$, 
$t_6 = 1-t_3$, 
$t_7 = 1-x_{1,1}$, 
$t_8 = (x_{8,2}+x_{9,3} = 2)$, 
$t_9 = (x_{9,2}+t_3 = 2)$, 
$\sum_{j=1}^{3} x_{i,j} = 1$ for all $i$, 
$\sum_{i=1}^{9} x_{i,1} = 1$, 
$t_i \geq x_{i,1}$ for all $i$, 
$t_i \leq 1 - x_{i,2}$ for all $i$, 
$x_{8,3} = 0$. 
These constraints model the relationships between the doors, the statements on the doors, and the contents of the doors.
