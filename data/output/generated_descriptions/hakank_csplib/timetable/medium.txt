**Timetable Problem**
The Timetable Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves scheduling lectures for a set of courses in a given number of periods, subject to certain constraints.

The main decision variables in this problem are the `timetable` variables, which represent whether a lecture of a particular course is scheduled in a specific period. These variables are binary, meaning they can take on values of either 0 (not scheduled) or 1 (scheduled).

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- **Conflicts**: If two courses have common students, their lectures must be scheduled at different times.
- **Availabilities**: Certain courses may not be available to be scheduled at specific periods.
- **Rooms**: There are a limited number of rooms available, and at most that many lectures can be scheduled at the same time.
- **Lecture Requirements**: Each course has a specific number of lectures that must be scheduled.

The objective of this problem is to find a feasible schedule that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function. In other words, the goal is to assign lectures to periods in such a way that all constraints are met, and all courses have their required number of lectures scheduled.
