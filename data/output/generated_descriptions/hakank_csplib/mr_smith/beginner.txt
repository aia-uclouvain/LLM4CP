The name of the problem is the Mr. Smith problem. 
This problem is about a family, the Smiths, who want to go on a visit together, but not all of them can make it. The problem is trying to figure out who will go and who will stay home based on some given hints. 
The main variables are Mr_Smith, Mrs_Smith, Matt, John, and Tim, which represent whether each member of the family will come on the visit or not. 
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: if Mr. Smith comes, his wife must come too; at least one of the two sons, Matt or John, must come; either Mrs. Smith or Tim can come, but not both; either Tim and John come together, or neither of them comes; and if Matt comes, then John and Mr. Smith must also come. 
The goal of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all these rules, meaning to determine which family members will go on the visit.
