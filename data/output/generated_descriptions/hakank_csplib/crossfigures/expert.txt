name: Crossfigure Problem
description: The Crossfigure problem is a numerical puzzle where a grid of numbers needs to be filled in, following certain rules and clues. The clues are given in the form of equations involving the numbers in the grid, and the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the clues.
variables: $M_{i,j}, A_1, A_4, ..., A_{30}, D_1, D_2, ..., D_{28}$, where $M_{i,j}$ represents the value in the $i^{th}$ row and $j^{th}$ column of the grid, and $A_k$ and $D_k$ represent the values of the across and down clues, respectively. These variables are decision variables that need to be assigned values to satisfy the constraints of the problem.
constraints: $across(M, A_k, len, row, col)$, $down(M, D_k, len, row, col)$, $A_k = f(A_j, D_j)$, $D_k = f(A_j, D_j)$, $M_{i,j} = 0$ for black cells, where $f$ represents a function involving addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division. These constraints ensure that the values in the grid satisfy the clues and the rules of the puzzle.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., to fill in the grid with values that make all the clues true. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
