name: Portfolio Optimization Problem
description: The Portfolio Optimization Problem is a classic problem in finance and operations research that involves selecting the optimal mix of assets to invest in, given a set of constraints and objectives. The goal is to maximize returns while minimizing risk. In this problem, we have a set of assets with given returns and covariances, and we want to find the optimal portfolio that meets certain criteria, such as a minimum return and a maximum budget.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_n, f$ where $x_i$ represents the amount invested in asset $i$ and $f$ represents the total risk of the portfolio. These variables are decision variables that we want to optimize.
constraints: $f \geq 0, f = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} coeff_{i,j} x_i x_j, \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i \leq B, \sum_{i=1}^{n} r_i x_i \geq G$ where the first constraint ensures that the risk is non-negative, the second constraint calculates the total risk of the portfolio, the third constraint ensures that the total investment does not exceed the budget $B$, and the fourth constraint ensures that the total return is at least $G$.
objective: minimize $f$ which represents the total risk of the portfolio. The goal is to find the optimal portfolio that minimizes the risk while meeting the constraints.
