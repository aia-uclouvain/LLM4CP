The problem is called the "Final Student Problem". 
It's about a teacher, Mrs. Krabappel, who has almost finished grading her students' tests, with just one test left to grade. 
The main variables are the number of students in the class (n) and the scores of the students who have already taken the test (x). 
The scores are like grades, and they can be any number from 0 to 100. 
The basic restrictions are that if the last student scores 7%, the class average will be 90%, and if they score 92%, the class average will be 95%. 
The goal is to figure out how many students are in the class.
