name: Final Student Problem
description: The problem describes a scenario where a teacher, Mrs. Krabappel, has almost finished grading her students' tests, with only one test left to grade. The problem states that if the final student scores 7%, the class average will be 90%, and if they score 92%, the class average will be 95%. The goal is to determine the number of students in the class.
variables: $n, x_1, x_2, ..., x_{n-1}$, where $n$ is the number of students in the class and $x_1, x_2, ..., x_{n-1}$ are the scores of the first $n-1$ students, represented as decision variables in the model.
constraints: $\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n-1} x_i + 7}{n} = 90$, $\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n-1} x_i + 92}{n} = 95$, and $x_1 \geq x_2 \geq ... \geq x_{n-1}$, which represent the conditions for the class average to be 90% and 95% when the final student scores 7% and 92%, respectively, and the constraint that the scores are in decreasing order.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find the value of $n$, the number of students in the class, that satisfies the given conditions, which can be considered as a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a feasible solution that meets all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
