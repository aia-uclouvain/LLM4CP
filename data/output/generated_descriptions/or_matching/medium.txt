**Stable Marriage Problem**
The Stable Marriage Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves matching two sets of entities, typically men and women, based on their preferences.
**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are the matchings `x`, which represent the pairs of individuals that are matched together. The variable `s` represents the scores of each pair, and `total` represents the sum of these scores.
**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
* Each person can only be matched once.
* The matchings must be symmetric, i.e., if person A is matched with person B, then person B is also matched with person A.
* The scores of each pair must be consistent with the given scores table.
* The matchings must be stable, i.e., there is no pair of individuals who prefer each other to their current matches.
**Objective**
The objective of this problem is to maximize the total score of all matchings, which represents the overall satisfaction of the matched individuals. In other words, the goal is to find the optimal matching that results in the highest total score.
