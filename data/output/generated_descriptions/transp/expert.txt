name: Transportation Problem
description: The Transportation Problem is a classic problem in operations research and management science that involves finding the most cost-effective way to transport goods from a set of sources (such as factories or warehouses) to a set of destinations (such as markets or customers). The problem takes into account the capacity of each source, the demand at each destination, and the cost of transporting goods between each source and destination.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the quantity of goods transported from source $i$ to destination $j$. These variables are the decision variables of the problem, and their values determine the shipping schedule.
constraints: $\sum_{j} x_{i,j} \leq a_i$, $\sum_{i} x_{i,j} \geq b_j$, $x_{i,j} \geq 0$, where $a_i$ is the capacity of source $i$, $b_j$ is the demand at destination $j$, and $x_{i,j}$ is the quantity of goods transported from source $i$ to destination $j$. These constraints ensure that the shipping schedule does not exceed the capacity of each source, meets the demand at each destination, and is non-negative.
objective: Minimize $\sum_{i,j} c_{i,j} x_{i,j}$, where $c_{i,j}$ is the cost of transporting one unit of goods from source $i$ to destination $j$. The objective is to find the shipping schedule that minimizes the total transportation cost.
