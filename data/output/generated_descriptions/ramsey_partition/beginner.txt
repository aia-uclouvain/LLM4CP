Ramsey Partition Problem
The problem is about dividing a set of numbers (1 to 23) into three groups in such a way that for no group are there three different numbers where two of the numbers add up to the third.
The main variables are the three groups of numbers, represented by x[1], x[2], and x[3], which are sets of numbers from 1 to 23.
The basic restrictions are that the three groups must contain all the numbers from 1 to 23, and for each group, there should not be three numbers where two add up to the third.
The goal of the problem is to find a way to divide the numbers into three groups that satisfy these restrictions, with no specific objective to minimize or maximize.
