name: Another Kind of Magic Square
description: This problem is a variation of the classic magic square problem, where the goal is to arrange numbers in a square array such that the sum of the numbers surrounding each number is a multiple of that number. The problem does not require that all rows, columns, and diagonals sum to the same value, making it a distinct type of magic square.
variables: $magic_{i,j}$ for $i = 1, \ldots, n$ and $j = 1, \ldots, n$, where $magic_{i,j}$ represents the number assigned to the cell at row $i$ and column $j$ in the $n \times n$ array, and is a decision variable that takes a value between $1$ and $n^2$.
constraints: $\forall i, j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}: \sum_{a, b \in \{-1, 0, 1\}}^{i+a > 0, j+b > 0, i+a \leq n, j+b \leq n} magic_{i+a, j+b} \mod magic_{i, j} = 0$ and $\forall i, j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}: magic_{i, j} \in \{1, \ldots, n^2\}$ and $\bigwedge_{i, j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}} magic_{i, j} \neq magic_{i', j'}$ for $i \neq i'$ or $j \neq j'$, where the first constraint ensures that the sum of the surrounding numbers is a multiple of the center number, the second constraint defines the domain of the decision variables, and the third constraint ensures that all numbers in the array are distinct.
objective: The objective of this problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, i.e., to solve the problem, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, and the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
