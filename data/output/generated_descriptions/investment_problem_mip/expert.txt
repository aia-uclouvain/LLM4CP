name: Investment Problem

description: The investment problem is a classic problem in operations research and finance. A portfolio manager has a fixed budget of $100 million to invest in eight different investment opportunities, each with its own return on investment. The manager must decide how much to invest in each opportunity, with the goal of maximizing the total annual return on investment. The investment amounts are restricted to intervals of $10 million, and any leftover funds can be invested in a ninth opportunity with a return of 5% per year.

variables: $x_{ij}, choices_j, the\_returns_j, not\_invested, z$ 
These variables represent the following: $x_{ij}$ is a binary variable indicating whether $i$ units of $10 million are invested in opportunity $j$, $choices_j$ is the amount invested in opportunity $j$, $the\_returns_j$ is the return on investment for opportunity $j$, $not\_invested$ is the amount of leftover funds, and $z$ is the total annual return on investment.

constraints: $\sum_{i=0}^{n} x_{ij} \leq 1, \forall j \in \{1, \ldots, m\}$, $z = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{m} returns_{ij} \cdot x_{ij} + not\_invested \cdot not\_invested\_returns$, $\sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{m} i \cdot x_{ij} + not\_invested \leq budget$, $the\_returns_j = \sum_{i=0}^{n} returns_{ij} \cdot x_{ij}, \forall j \in \{1, \ldots, m\}$, $choices_j = \sum_{i=0}^{n} i \cdot x_{ij}, \forall j \in \{1, \ldots, m\}$
These constraints ensure that: each investment opportunity is invested in at most once, the total annual return on investment is calculated correctly, the total investment amount does not exceed the budget, and the return on investment and amount invested for each opportunity are calculated correctly.

objective: Maximize $z$ 
The objective of the problem is to maximize the total annual return on investment, $z$.
