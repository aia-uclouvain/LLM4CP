The Locker Puzzle
The Locker Puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding a three-number combination for a locker, where each number is between 1 and 40, and the numbers must satisfy several conditions related to their digits and relationships between them.

The main decision variables in this problem are the three numbers `d1`, `d2`, and `d3`, which represent the first, second, and third numbers in the combination, respectively. Each of these numbers is further broken down into two digits, represented by the arrays `a_d1`, `a_d2`, and `a_d3`.

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows: 
- The second number is twice the first.
- The third number is ten more than the second.
- The first number has one even digit and one odd digit.
- The second number has either two odd or two even digits.
- The total of all the different digits used in the combination is 10.

The objective of this problem is to find a combination of three numbers that satisfies all the given constraints. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a feasible solution that meets all the conditions. The solution is a set of three numbers that can be used as a locker combination.
