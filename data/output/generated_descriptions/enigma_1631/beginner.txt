Joe's Pyramid 
The problem is about a pyramid with stones, each marked with a different number. The numbers on the stones follow a specific rule: a stone's number is the sum of the numbers on the two stones it rests on. 
The main variables are the numbers on each stone, which are represented by the array x. Each number in the array corresponds to a stone in the pyramid, with x[1] being the top stone and x[21] being one of the bottom stones. 
The basic restrictions are that each stone's number must be the sum of the two stones below it, and all numbers must be different. 
The goal is to find the number on the top stone, X, which is represented by x[1].
