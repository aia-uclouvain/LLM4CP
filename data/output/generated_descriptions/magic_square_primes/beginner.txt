Magic Square Primes
The problem is about creating a special square grid filled with prime numbers in a way that the sum of numbers in each row, column, and diagonal is the same.
The main variables are the numbers in the grid, which are all prime numbers, and the total sum of numbers in each row, column, and diagonal.
The basic restrictions are that each number in the grid must be a prime number, all numbers must be different, and the sum of numbers in each row, column, and diagonal must be the same.
The goal is to find a way to fill the grid with prime numbers that meets these restrictions while keeping the total sum as small as possible.
