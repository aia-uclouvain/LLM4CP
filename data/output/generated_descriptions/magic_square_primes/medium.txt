Magic Square Primes Problem
The Magic Square Primes problem is about creating a square grid filled with prime numbers in such a way that the sum of numbers in each row, column, and diagonal is the same.
The main decision variables in this problem are the elements of the 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` represents the prime number placed at the `i-th` row and `j-th` column of the grid. The variable `total` represents the common sum of numbers in each row, column, and diagonal.
The key constraints in this problem are: 
- each prime number can only be used once (all_different constraint), 
- the sum of numbers in each row and column must be the same (forall constraint), 
- the sum of numbers in the main diagonal and the anti-diagonal must also be the same (sum constraints).
The objective of the problem is to minimize the total sum of numbers in the grid, or equivalently, to minimize the largest prime number used in the grid.
