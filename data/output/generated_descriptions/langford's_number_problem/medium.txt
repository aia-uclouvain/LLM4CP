Langford's Number Problem
The Langford's Number Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves arranging two sets of positive integers from 1 to k in a sequence, such that each subsequent occurrence of an integer i appears i+1 indices later than the last occurrence.

The main decision variables in this problem are the positions of the integers in the sequence. In some models, the positions are represented as a single array, while in others, they are represented as two separate arrays: one for the sequence of numbers and one for the positions of the numbers.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- All numbers from 1 to k must appear exactly twice in the sequence.
- The difference between the positions of two occurrences of the same number must be equal to the number plus one.
- The positions of the numbers must be distinct.

The objective of the problem is to find a sequence that satisfies these constraints. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather a requirement to find a feasible solution that meets the given constraints.

In some models, symmetry breaking constraints are added to reduce the search space and improve the efficiency of the solver. These constraints typically involve imposing an order on the positions of the numbers, such as requiring the first occurrence of a number to be before the last occurrence.
