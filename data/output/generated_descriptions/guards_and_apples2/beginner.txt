The problem is called the Guards and Apple problem. 
It's about a boy who wants to give one apple to a girl, but to reach her, he must pass through five gates, each guarded by a person who demands half of the boy's apples plus one as a bribe. 
The main variables are the number of apples the boy has at each gate, represented by x[1] for the starting point, x[2] for the first gate, and so on, up to x[6] which is the number of apples left after passing all gates. 
The basic restrictions are that the boy must give half of his apples plus one to each guard, and he must have a whole number of apples at each step because you can't divide an apple. 
The goal of the problem is to find out how many apples the boy had at the beginning, which is represented by x[1], given that he has no apples left after giving one to the girl and passing through all the gates.
