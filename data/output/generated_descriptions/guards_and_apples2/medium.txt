The Guards and Apple problem. 
This problem is about a boy who wants to give an apple to a girl and must pass through five gates, each with a guard, to get to her. At each gate, he bribes the guard with half of his apples, plus one, and after giving the apple to the girl, he has no apples left.

The main decision variable is an array `x` of size `n+1`, where `x[i]` represents the number of apples the boy has before passing through the `i-th` gate. 

The key constraints are that the boy starts with a certain number of apples, and at each gate, he gives the guard half of his apples, plus one. This means that the number of apples he has after passing through a gate is half of the number he had before, minus one. Additionally, since he can't give a fraction of an apple, the number of apples he has before passing through a gate must be even. After passing through all the gates, he gives his last apple to the girl, leaving him with no apples.

The problem's objective is to find the initial number of apples the boy had, which is represented by `x[1]`. The model is designed to satisfy the given constraints, and the solution will provide the sequence of apple counts at each gate, as well as the initial number of apples.
