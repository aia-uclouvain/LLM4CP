Denny's Menu problem
The problem is about finding different combinations of menu items that can be purchased to total a specific amount, in this case $10, given the prices of the items.
The main decision variables are `x`, an array of variables representing the quantity of each menu item to purchase, where `x[i]` is the quantity of the item with price `y[i]`.
The key constraints are that the total cost of the purchased items must equal $10, and the quantity of each item cannot be negative.
The problem's objective is to find all possible combinations of menu items that satisfy the total cost constraint, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. In other words, the goal is to enumerate all feasible solutions that total $10, given the prices of the menu items.
