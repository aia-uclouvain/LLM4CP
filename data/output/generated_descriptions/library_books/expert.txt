name: Library Books problem
description: The problem involves three students, Arthur, Belinda, and Clarissa, who have a total of 25 overdue books. Each student is majoring in one of three languages: English, French, or German. The problem provides additional clues about the number of overdue books each student has and their majors. The goal is to determine which student is majoring in which language and how many books each student needs to return to the library.
variables: $major = [major_1, major_2, major_3], major\_inv = [major\_inv_1, major\_inv_2, major\_inv_3], returned = [returned_1, returned_2, returned_3]$ where $major_i$ represents the major of the $i^{th}$ student, $major\_inv_i$ is the inverse of $major_i$, and $returned_i$ is the number of overdue books the $i^{th}$ student has.
constraints: $all\_different(major)$, $\sum_{i=1}^{3} returned_i = 25$, $returned_{major[French]} = returned_{Arthur} + 3$, $major[French] \neq Arthur$, $returned_{Clarissa} = returned_{major[English]} + 2$, $major[English] \neq Clarissa$, $inverse(major, major\_inv)$ where these constraints ensure that each student has a unique major, the total number of overdue books is 25, the student majoring in French has 3 more overdue books than Arthur, Clarissa has 2 more overdue books than the student majoring in English, and the inverse of the major array is correctly defined.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the MiniZinc model. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
