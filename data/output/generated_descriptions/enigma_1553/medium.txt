Squares from Squares problem
The problem is about placing digits in a 4x4 grid such that each row forms a unique four-figure perfect square, no digit occurs more than once in any row, and the sum of the digits in each row and column is the same.
The main decision variables are x[i,j], which represent the digit in the ith row and jth column, and row_squares[i], which represent the four-figure perfect square formed by the digits in the ith row.
The key constraints are: 
- each row must contain unique digits, 
- the sum of the digits in each row must be the same, 
- the sum of the digits in each column must be the same, 
- each row must form a unique four-figure perfect square, 
- the four-figure perfect squares must be in increasing order.
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, with no specific objective function to minimize or maximize, as the goal is to find a feasible solution that meets all the conditions.
