name: Domino Problem
description: The Domino Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming where the goal is to place domino pieces on a board such that each piece covers two fields and the numbers on the piece match the numbers on the board. The board is set up with a number in each field that must match the number of the domino piece placed on that field.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i \in 1..r$ and $j \in 1..c$, which represent the decision variables that determine which domino piece to place in each cell of the board. These variables are used to assign a domino piece to each cell of the board.
constraints: $\forall p \in 1..m: \sum_{i=1}^{r} \sum_{j=1}^{c} (x_{i,j} = p) = 2$ and $\forall i \in 1..r, j \in 1..c: \exists a,b \in \{-1,0,1\}: (i+a \geq 1 \wedge j+b \geq 1 \wedge i+a \leq r \wedge j+b \leq c \wedge |a| + |b| = 1) \rightarrow ((pieces[x_{i,j},1] = board[i,j] \wedge pieces[x_{i+a,j+b},2] = board[i+a,j+b]) \vee (pieces[x_{i+a,j+b},1] = board[i+a,j+b] \wedge pieces[x_{i,j},2] = board[i,j])) \wedge x_{i,j} = x_{i+a,j+b}$, which ensure that each domino piece is used exactly twice and that the numbers on the domino pieces match the numbers on the board.
objective: satisfy, which means that the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
