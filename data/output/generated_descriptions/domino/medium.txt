Domino Problem
The Domino problem is a classic constraint programming problem where the goal is to place domino pieces on a board such that each piece covers two fields and has two numbers that match the numbers on the board.

The main decision variables in this problem are the `x` variables, which represent the piece to be placed in each cell of the board. The `x` variables are two-dimensional arrays where `x[i, j]` represents the piece to be placed in the cell at row `i` and column `j`.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each piece can only be used twice.
- The numbers on each piece must match the numbers on the board.
- Each cell on the board must be covered by a piece.
- The pieces must be placed horizontally or vertically, not diagonally.

The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of pieces to cells, such that all constraints are met. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
