Divisible by 7 problem. 
This problem is about arranging three cubes with numbers 6, 3, and 1 on them in a line to form a 3-digit number that is divisible by 7. 
The main variables are the numbers on each cube (x[1], x[2], x[3]) which represent the digits of the 3-digit number, and z which is the 3-digit number itself. 
The basic restrictions are that each cube must be used, the numbers on the cubes must be different, and the 3-digit number must be divisible by 7. Also, since a 6 cube could also be a 9, the problem considers both possibilities but only allows one of them to be used. 
The goal of the problem is to find an arrangement of the cubes that satisfies these restrictions, meaning to find a 3-digit number that is divisible by 7.
