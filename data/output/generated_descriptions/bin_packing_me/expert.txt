name: Bin Packing Problem
description: The Bin Packing Problem is a classic problem in computer science and operations research that involves packing a set of items of different sizes into a minimum number of bins, each with a limited capacity. The goal is to find the most efficient way to pack the items into the bins, minimizing the number of bins used.
variables: $bins_{i,j}, bin\_loads_i, num\_loaded\_bins$ - These are the decision variables, where $bins_{i,j}$ represents whether item $j$ is packed in bin $i$ (1 if yes, 0 if no), $bin\_loads_i$ represents the total size of items packed in bin $i$, and $num\_loaded\_bins$ represents the total number of bins used.
constraints: $\forall b \in 1..num\_bins (bin\_loads_b = \sum_{s \in 1..num\_stuff} (stuff_s \* bins_{b,s}))$, $\sum_{s \in 1..num\_stuff} (stuff_s) = \sum_{b \in 1..num\_bins} (bin\_loads_b)$, $\forall s \in 1..num\_stuff (\sum_{b \in 1..num\_bins} (bins_{b,s}) = 1)$, $decreasing(bin\_loads)$, $bin\_loads_1 > 0$, $num\_loaded\_bins = \sum_{b \in 1..num\_bins} (bool2int(bin\_loads_b > 0))$ - These constraints ensure that the total size of items packed in each bin does not exceed the bin capacity, all items are packed exactly once, the bins are used in a decreasing order of their loads, the first bin is always used, and the number of loaded bins is calculated correctly.
objective: Minimize $num\_loaded\_bins$ - The objective of the problem is to minimize the total number of bins used to pack all the items.
