Perfect Square Placement problem
The Perfect Square Placement problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves packing a set of squares of different sizes into a larger square container. The goal is to find a placement of the squares such that they do not overlap and the container is filled as much as possible.

Main decision variables
The main decision variables in this problem are the x and y coordinates of the bottom-left corner of each square. These variables determine the position of each square in the container.

Key constraints
The key constraints in this problem are:

*   Each square must be placed within the container, i.e., its x and y coordinates must be within the bounds of the container.
*   No two squares can overlap, i.e., the area of each square must not intersect with the area of any other square.
*   The sum of the areas of all squares must be equal to the area of the container.

Problem's objective
The objective of the problem is to find a placement of the squares that satisfies all the constraints. In other words, the goal is to pack the squares into the container in a way that minimizes empty space and does not allow any overlaps between the squares.
