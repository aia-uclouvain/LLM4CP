name: Nonogram Problem
description: The Nonogram problem is a classic puzzle where a picture is represented by a grid of cells, and the goal is to fill in the cells according to given row and column constraints. Each row and column has a set of rules that specify the lengths of consecutive blocks of filled cells.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i \in \{1, \ldots, rows\}$ and $j \in \{1, \ldots, cols\}$. These variables represent the state of each cell in the grid, where $x_{i,j} = 1$ indicates an empty cell and $x_{i,j} = 2$ indicates a filled cell.
constraints: $\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, cols\}: regular([x_{i,j} | i \in \{1, \ldots, rows\}], s_j)$ and $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, rows\}: regular([x_{i,j} | j \in \{1, \ldots, cols\}], s_i)$, where $s_j$ and $s_i$ are regular expressions constructed from the column and row rules, respectively. These constraints ensure that the filled cells in each row and column satisfy the given rules.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, i.e., to fill in the grid according to the given row and column rules. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
