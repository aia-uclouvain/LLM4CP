Triangle Problem
The problem is about filling in numbers from 0 to 9 in a triangle shape, with each number used only once, in such a way that certain sums of numbers are equal.
The main variables are A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, and J, which represent the numbers to be filled in the triangle.
The basic restrictions are that each number from 0 to 9 can only be used once, and the sums of certain groups of numbers must be equal, specifically: 
- the sum of the top, middle right, bottom right, and bottom far right numbers (A + C + F + J) must be equal to the sum of the top, top left, middle left, and bottom left numbers (A + B + D + G), 
- the sum of the top, top left, and top right numbers (A + B + C) must be equal to the sum of the middle left, bottom left, and bottom middle numbers (D + G + H), 
- and the sum of the middle left, bottom left, and bottom middle numbers (D + G + H) must be equal to the sum of the bottom right, bottom middle, and bottom far right numbers (F + I + J).
The goal of the problem is to find all possible combinations of numbers that satisfy these conditions.
