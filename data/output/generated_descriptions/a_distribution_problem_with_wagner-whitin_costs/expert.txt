name: Wagner-Whitin Distribution Problem

description: The Wagner-Whitin Distribution Problem is a classic problem in operations research and management science that involves finding the optimal production and inventory policy for a multi-period, multi-product system with time-varying demand. The problem is defined on a tree-like structure, where each node represents a product or a production facility, and the edges represent the flow of goods between them. The goal is to minimize the total cost of production, inventory holding, and ordering over a finite planning horizon.

variables: $orders_{i,t}, stock_{i,t}$ 
These are the decision variables, where $orders_{i,t}$ represents the amount of product $i$ ordered in period $t$, and $stock_{i,t}$ represents the inventory level of product $i$ at the end of period $t$. 

constraints: $stock_{i,0} = 0, stock_{i,t} = stock_{i,t-1} + orders_{i,t} - \sum_{m \in children[i]} orders_{m,t}, stock_{i,t} = stock_{i,t-1} + orders_{i,t} - demand_{i,t}$
These constraints ensure that the initial inventory level is zero, the inventory level at each period is updated correctly based on the orders and demand, and the demand at each leaf node is fulfilled.

objective: The objective is to minimize the total cost, which includes the holding cost of inventory at each node and period, and the ordering cost at each node and period, given by the expression: $\min \sum_{t=1}^{numPeriods} \sum_{i=1}^{numNodes} (holdingCost_i \cdot stock_{i,t} + procCost_i \cdot \mathbb{I}(orders_{i,t} > 0))$, where $\mathbb{I}(orders_{i,t} > 0)$ is an indicator function that equals 1 if an order is placed at node $i$ in period $t$, and 0 otherwise.
