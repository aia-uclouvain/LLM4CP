name: Post Office Problem
description: The post office problem is a classic problem in operations research and constraint programming. The problem states that a post office needs to hire full-time employees to meet its daily requirements. Each full-time employee must work for 5 consecutive days and then receive 2 days off. The post office wants to minimize the number of employees that must be hired to meet its daily requirements, which vary from day to day.
variables: $x_i$, where $i \in \{Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat, Sun\}$. These variables represent the number of full-time employees starting work on day $i$, and they are used to determine the total number of employees needed to meet the daily requirements.
constraints: $\sum_{i \in DAYS, i \neq Tue, i \neq Wed} x_i \geq Need[Mon]$, $\sum_{i \in DAYS, i \neq Wed, i \neq Thu} x_i \geq Need[Tue]$, $\sum_{i \in DAYS, i \neq Thu, i \neq Fri} x_i \geq Need[Wed]$, $\sum_{i \in DAYS, i \neq Fri, i \neq Sat} x_i \geq Need[Thu]$, $\sum_{i \in DAYS, i \neq Sat, i \neq Sun} x_i \geq Need[Fri]$, $\sum_{i \in DAYS, i \neq Sun, i \neq Mon} x_i \geq Need[Sat]$, $\sum_{i \in DAYS, i \neq Mon, i \neq Tue} x_i \geq Need[Sun]$, $x_i \geq 0$. These constraints ensure that the number of employees starting work on each day is sufficient to meet the daily requirements, and that the number of employees is non-negative.
objective: Minimize $z = \sum_{i \in DAYS} x_i$, which represents the total number of full-time employees that must be hired to meet the daily requirements.
