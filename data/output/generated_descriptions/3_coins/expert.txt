name: 3 Coins Problem
description: The 3 Coins Problem is a classic problem in the field of artificial intelligence and constraint programming. The problem states that there are three coins lying on a table, with the initial configuration being tails, heads, tails. The goal is to make all three coins face either heads or tails in exactly three moves, with the constraint that only one coin can be flipped in each move.
variables: $x_{m,i}$, where $m \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$ and $i \in \{1, 2, 3\}$, $last\_sum$. These variables represent the state of each coin at each move, where $x_{m,i} = 0$ indicates that the $i^{th}$ coin is heads at move $m$, and $x_{m,i} = 1$ indicates that the $i^{th}$ coin is tails at move $m$. The variable $last\_sum$ represents the total number of tails after the third move.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, 3\}, x_{1,i} = init_i$, $\forall m \in \{2, 3, 4\}, \sum_{i=1}^{3} (x_{m-1,i} \neq x_{m,i}) = 1$, $last\_sum = \sum_{i=1}^{3} x_{4,i}$, $last\_sum = 0 \vee last\_sum = 3$. These constraints ensure that the initial state of the coins is set correctly, that only one coin is flipped in each move, that the total number of tails after the third move is calculated correctly, and that the final state of the coins is either all heads or all tails.
objective: satisfy, which means that the objective is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
