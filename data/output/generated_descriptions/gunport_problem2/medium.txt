The Gunport Problem
The Gunport Problem is about covering a rectangular grid with dominoes, where each domino covers two adjacent cells, and ensuring that no two adjacent cells are left vacant.
The main decision variables are the `x` array, which represents whether a domino is placed in a particular cell, and the `rt` array, which represents the output matrix. The `x[i,j,k]` variable is 1 if a domino is placed in cell `(i,j)` with index `k`, and 0 otherwise.
The key constraints are: 
- each cell is covered by at most one domino, 
- each domino covers exactly two cells, 
- each domino is placed in adjacent cells, 
- no two adjacent cells are left vacant, 
- the number of vacant cells is at least 4.
The problem's objective is to satisfy all the constraints, with the additional goal of maximizing the number of vacant cells, which is represented by the `sum_zeros` variable. However, in this specific model, the objective is set to `satisfy`, which means the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than maximizing or minimizing a specific objective function.
