name: The Gunport Problem
description: The Gunport Problem is a classic puzzle where the goal is to cover a rectangular grid with dominoes in such a way that each domino covers two adjacent cells, and no two adjacent cells are left uncovered. The problem has been modeled as a constraint satisfaction problem, where the grid size can vary, and the objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
variables: $x_{i,j,k}$, where $x_{i,j,k}$ is a binary decision variable that represents whether the cell at position $(i,j)$ is covered by the $k^{th}$ domino. These variables determine the placement of dominoes on the grid.
constraints: 
$\sum_{l \in j-1..j+1, l \geq 1, l \leq n, l \neq j} x_{i,l,k} + \sum_{l \in i-1..i+1, l \geq 1, l \leq m, l \neq i} x_{l,j,k} \geq x_{i,j,k}$, 
$\sum_{i \in M, j \in N} x_{i,j,k} = 2$, 
$\sum_{k \in P} x_{i,j,k} \leq 1$, 
$\sum_{c \in j..j+1, k \in P} x_{i,c,k} \geq 1$, 
$\sum_{r \in i..i+1, k \in P} x_{r,j,k} \geq 1$, 
$\sum_{k \in P} kx_{i,j,k} = rt_{i,j}$. 
These constraints ensure that each domino covers two adjacent cells, each cell is covered by at most one domino, no two adjacent cells are left uncovered, and the output matrix $rt$ is correctly computed.
objective: satisfy, the problem is modeled as a constraint satisfaction problem, where the objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than maximizing or minimizing a specific value. However, there is a constraint $\sum_{i \in M, j \in M} (rt_{i,j} = 0) \geq 4$ that can be seen as a soft objective to maximize the number of zeros in the output matrix.
