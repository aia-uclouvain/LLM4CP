name: Subsets 100 puzzle
description: The problem is to prove that given a set of 10 different integers from the set of integers 1 to 100, it is always possible to find two disjoint non-empty subsets, S and T, such that the sum of elements in S equals the sum of elements in T.
variables: $s, t, s\_total, t\_total$ - These are the decision variables where $s$ and $t$ are sets of integers representing the two disjoint subsets, and $s\_total$ and $t\_total$ are integers representing the sum of elements in $s$ and $t$ respectively.
constraints: $card(s \cup t) \leq m$, $card(s \cup t) > 0$, $card(s) > 0$, $card(t) > 0$, $s \cap t = \emptyset$, $s\_total = \sum_{i \in s} i$, $t\_total = \sum_{i \in t} i$, $s\_total = t\_total$, $card(s) = card(t)$ - These constraints ensure that $s$ and $t$ are disjoint non-empty subsets of the given set of 10 integers, the sum of elements in $s$ equals the sum of elements in $t$, and the cardinality of $s$ is equal to the cardinality of $t$.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, proving that it is always possible to find two disjoint non-empty subsets with equal sum.
