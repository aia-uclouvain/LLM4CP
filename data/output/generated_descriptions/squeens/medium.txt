Schuh's Queen Placement Problem
The problem is about placing the maximum number of queens on an 8x8 chessboard such that no queen attacks another queen.
The main decision variables are x(i,j) and a(i,j), where x(i,j) represents whether a queen is placed on the square at row i and column j, and a(i,j) represents whether the square at row i and column j is under attack by any queen.
The key constraints are that each square on the board is either occupied by a queen or under attack, and that no more than 8 queens can be placed on the board. Additionally, a square is considered under attack if there is a queen in the same row, column, or diagonal. 
The objective of the problem is to maximize the number of queens placed on the board, which is represented by the variable numq. The model uses a constraint programming approach to find the optimal solution, which is the arrangement of queens that maximizes the number of queens while satisfying all the constraints.
