Schuh's Queen Placement Problem
The problem is about placing queens on a chessboard in such a way that no queen attacks another queen, and we want to place as many queens as possible.
The main variables are x and a, where x represents whether a square on the chessboard is occupied by a queen (1) or not (0), and a represents whether a square is attacked by a queen (1) or not (0).
The basic restrictions are that a queen can attack another queen if they are in the same row, column, or diagonal, and each square on the chessboard is either occupied by a queen or attacked by a queen, but not both.
The goal is to maximize the number of queens that can be placed on the chessboard without any queen attacking another queen.
