name: Car Painting Problem
description: The Car Painting Problem is a constraint programming problem that arises in the automobile manufacturing industry. The goal is to sequence the cars in the assembly line to minimize the number of paint changeovers, which are expensive due to the cost of wasted paint and solvents, as well as the extra time required for the purging process. There are 10 cars to be painted, each with a specific color, and each car must be painted within three positions of its original assembly order.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_{10}, num\_purgings, distance$ - These variables represent the decision variables of the problem. $x_i$ represents the position in which car $i$ is painted, $num\_purgings$ represents the total number of paint changeovers, and $distance$ represents the total distance of changed positions. 
constraints: $alldifferent(x_1, x_2, ..., x_{10}), |x_i - i| \leq 3 \forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 10\}, num\_purgings = \sum_{i=2}^{10} (colors[x_i] \neq colors[x_{i-1}]), distance = \sum_{i=1}^{10} |x_i - i|$ - These constraints ensure that each car is painted exactly once, that each car is painted within three positions of its original assembly order, that the number of paint changeovers is calculated correctly, and that the total distance of changed positions is calculated correctly.
objective: minimize $num\_purgings$ - The objective of the problem is to minimize the total number of paint changeovers, which reduces the cost and time required for the purging process.
