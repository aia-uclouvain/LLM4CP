name: Music Men Puzzle
description: The Music Men Puzzle is a classic logic puzzle where three friends, each with a different surname (King, Prince, Queen), a different first name (Leon, Mark, Rob), and a different age (24, 25, 26), have different musical preferences (Classical, Jazz, Pop). The puzzle provides several clues to determine the age, surname, and musical preference of each friend.
variables: $Age24, Age25, Age26, Leon, Mark, Rob, King, Prince, Queen, Classical, Jazz, Pop, Age = [Age24, Age25, Age26], Names = [King, Prince, Queen], Surnames = [Leon, Mark, Rob], Music = [Classical, Jazz, Pop]$ 
These variables represent the ages, first names, surnames, and musical preferences of the three friends, as well as arrays to store the ages, names, surnames, and musical preferences.
constraints: $all\_different(Age) \wedge all\_different(Names) \wedge all\_different(Surnames) \wedge all\_different(Music) \wedge Age24 = 24 \wedge Age25 = 25 \wedge Age26 = 26 \wedge Rob > Queen \wedge Queen = Classical \wedge Pop \neq Prince \wedge Pop \neq Age24 \wedge Leon \neq King \wedge Leon = Age25 \wedge Mark \neq Jazz$ 
These constraints ensure that each age, name, surname, and musical preference is unique, and they also encode the given clues: Rob is older than Queen, who likes Classical music; the Pop-music fan is not Prince and not 24; Leon is not King and is 25; and Mark's musical preference is not Jazz.
objective: The objective of this problem is to satisfy all the given constraints, which means finding a solution that determines the age, surname, and musical preference of each friend while respecting all the clues. Since this is a satisfaction problem, there is no value to minimize or maximize.
