name: Teambuilding Problem

description: The problem is to configure 10 teams of 6 people from a set of 30 new employees and 30 existing employees, working in 6 different services, while respecting certain rules. Each team must have 3 existing employees and 3 new employees, and at most 4 people from the same service. Additionally, there are constraints on which employees can be in the same team, such as employees from services A and B, and employees from services E and F, cannot be in the same team. There are also coaching relationships between some employees, where a new employee who is coached must be in the team of their coach. Finally, there are some specific constraints on which employees must be in the same team.

variables: $team[p]$, $team3[t, p]$ 
These are decision variables where $team[p]$ represents the team assigned to person $p$, and $team3[t, p]$ is a binary variable that is 1 if person $p$ is assigned to team $t$ and 0 otherwise.

constraints: 
$\forall t \in teams: \sum_{existingemployee \in persons} (bool2int(team[existingemployee] = t)) = 3$
$\forall t \in teams: \sum_{newemployee \in persons} (bool2int(team[newemployee] = t)) = 3$
$\forall t \in teams, f \in services: \sum_{person \in service[f]} (bool2int(team[person] = t)) \leq 4$
$\forall pA \in service[A], pB \in service[B]: team[pA] \neq team[pB]$
$\forall pE \in service[E], pF \in service[F]: team[pE] \neq team[pF]$
$\forall c \in coaching: team[coaching[c,1]] = team[coaching[c,2]]$
$(team[5] = team[41] \lor team[5] = team[51])$
$(team[15] = team[40] \lor team[15] = team[51])$
$(team[25] = team[40] \lor team[25] = team[50])$
$(team[20] = team[24] \lor team[22] = team[50])$
These constraints ensure that each team has 3 existing and 3 new employees, at most 4 people from the same service, and respect the coaching relationships and other specific constraints.

objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
