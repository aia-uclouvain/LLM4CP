name: Tripuzzle
description: The Tripuzzle problem is a combinatorial puzzle where 16 triangular pieces with different colored spots must be placed in a specific arrangement such that the colors on adjacent spots match. The puzzle has a set of predefined color matches on the outer and inner borders that must be satisfied.
variables: $x_{i,j,k}, y_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j,k}$ represents whether triangle $i$ is placed at position $j$ in orientation $k$, and $y_{i,j}$ represents the color of spot $j$ at position $i$.
constraints: 
$\forall j \in P: \sum_{i \in T, k \in O} x_{i,j,k} = 1$, 
$\forall i \in T: \sum_{j \in P, k \in O} x_{i,j,k} = 1$, 
$\forall i \in T, j \in P, k \in O, m \in S: y_{j,m} \geq piece\_col_{i,k,m} - color*(1-x_{i,j,k}) \wedge y_{j,m} \leq piece\_col_{i,k,m} + color*(1-x_{i,j,k})$, 
$y_{i,j} = y_{k,l}$ for specific $i, j, k, l$ (color matches).
These constraints ensure that each position is occupied by one triangle in one orientation, each triangle is placed in one position and one orientation, and the colors on adjacent spots match.
objective: satisfy, meaning the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
