name: Local Art Theft Problem

description: The problem involves six suspects who are being interviewed by the police after a local art theft. Each suspect makes a statement about who they think did not commit the crime. The police know that exactly four of the suspects told one lie each, and all the other statements are true. The goal is to determine who committed the crime based on the statements made by the suspects.

variables: $Alan, Brian, Charlie, Dave, Eddie, Freddie, AlanC, BrianC, CharlieC, DaveC, EddieC, FreddieC$ 
These variables represent the truth values of the statements made by each suspect, where $Alan, Brian, Charlie, Dave, Eddie, Freddie$ are boolean variables indicating whether each suspect is telling the truth or not, and $AlanC, BrianC, CharlieC, DaveC, EddieC, FreddieC$ are integer variables counting the number of lies told by each suspect.

constraints: $(Alan \rightarrow (\neg Brian \wedge \neg Dave \wedge \neg Eddie)) \wedge (Brian \rightarrow (\neg Alan \wedge \neg Charlie \wedge \neg Eddie)) \wedge (Charlie \rightarrow (\neg Brian \wedge \neg Freddie \wedge \neg Eddie)) \wedge (Dave \rightarrow (\neg Alan \wedge \neg Freddie \wedge \neg Charlie)) \wedge (Eddie \rightarrow (\neg Charlie \wedge \neg Dave \wedge \neg Freddie)) \wedge (Freddie \rightarrow (\neg Charlie \wedge \neg Dave \wedge \neg Alan)) \wedge (AlanC = bool2int(\neg Brian) + bool2int(\neg Dave) + bool2int(\neg Eddie)) \wedge (BrianC = bool2int(\neg Alan) + bool2int(\neg Charlie) + bool2int(\neg Eddie)) \wedge (CharlieC = bool2int(\neg Brian) + bool2int(\neg Freddie) + bool2int(\neg Eddie)) \wedge (DaveC = bool2int(\neg Alan) + bool2int(\neg Freddie) + bool2int(\neg Charlie)) \wedge (EddieC = bool2int(\neg Charlie) + bool2int(\neg Dave) + bool2int(\neg Freddie)) \wedge (FreddieC = bool2int(\neg Charlie) + bool2int(\neg Dave) + bool2int(\neg Alan)) \wedge count([AlanC,BrianC,CharlieC, DaveC, EddieC, FreddieC], 2, 4)$ 
These constraints represent the statements made by each suspect, the counting of lies for each suspect, and the condition that exactly four suspects told one lie each.

objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, which will allow us to determine who committed the crime. The problem is a satisfaction problem, not an optimization problem, so there is no value to minimize or maximize.
