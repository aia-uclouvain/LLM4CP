The Local Art Theft problem
The Local Art Theft problem is about identifying the thief among six suspects based on their statements, knowing that exactly four of them told one lie each and all other statements are true.
The main decision variables are Alan, Brian, Charlie, Dave, Eddie, and Freddie, which represent whether each suspect is telling the truth or not. The variables AlanC, BrianC, CharlieC, DaveC, EddieC, and FreddieC represent the number of lies told by each suspect.
The key constraints are that each suspect's statement must be consistent with the truth values of the other suspects, and that exactly four suspects told one lie each. For example, if Alan says it wasn't Brian, then either Alan is telling the truth and Brian is not the thief, or Alan is lying and Brian is the thief. Additionally, the total number of lies told by all suspects must be equal to 4.
The objective of the problem is to find the solution that satisfies all the constraints, which will reveal who committed the crime. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a feasible solution that meets all the conditions. The output of the model will indicate which suspect committed the crime.
