Seating Plan Problem
The Seating Plan Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves arranging candidates for an examination in a way that prevents cheating. 
The problem is about seating 20 candidates, 4 for each of 5 subjects, in a room with a specific layout, such that no two candidates for the same subject are adjacent, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

The main decision variables in this problem are the values of the 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` represents the subject of the candidate seated in the cell at position `(i, j)` in the room. The value of `x[i, j]` is an integer between 1 and 5, representing one of the 5 subjects, or 0 if the cell is empty.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each cell in the room is either empty or occupied by a candidate for one of the 5 subjects.
- No two candidates for the same subject are adjacent, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
- Each row and column in the room contains at most one candidate for each subject.
- The corners of each cell do not coincide with the corners of another cell containing a candidate for the same subject.
- There are exactly 4 candidates for each subject in the room.

The objective of the Seating Plan Problem is to find a valid arrangement of candidates in the room that satisfies all the constraints. This is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a solution that meets all the requirements, rather than optimizing a specific objective function. In other words, the problem is to find a way to seat the candidates such that all the constraints are satisfied, without trying to minimize or maximize any particular quantity.
