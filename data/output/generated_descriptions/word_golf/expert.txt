name: Word Golf
description: Word golf is a game where one word is turned into another through a process of substituting single letters, with the goal of finding the shortest sequence of words, where each word is a valid English word and differs from the previous word by only one letter.
variables: $x_{i,j}, ladder\_num_i, words_{j,k}$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the $j^{th}$ letter of the $i^{th}$ word in the ladder, $ladder\_num_i$ represents the index of the $i^{th}$ word in the ladder, and $words_{j,k}$ represents the $k^{th}$ letter of the $j^{th}$ word in the given list of words. These variables are used to represent the sequence of words and the letters in each word.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..word\_len: x_{1,i} = start_i \wedge x_{ladder\_len,i} = end_i$, $\forall i \in 1..ladder\_len: \exists j \in 1..num\_words: \forall k \in 1..word\_len: x_{i,k} = words_{j,k}$, $\forall i \in 2..ladder\_len: \sum_{j=1}^{word\_len} bool2int(x_{i-1,j} \neq x_{i,j}) = 1$, $all\_different(ladder\_num)$, where the first constraint initializes the first and last words in the ladder, the second constraint ensures that each word in the ladder is a valid word from the given list, the third constraint ensures that each word in the ladder differs from the previous word by only one letter, and the fourth constraint ensures that all words in the ladder are distinct.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, which represents a valid sequence of words from the given list, where each word differs from the previous word by only one letter, and the first and last words in the sequence are the given start and end words. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
