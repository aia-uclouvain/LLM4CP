The Square Root of Wonderful problem. 
This problem is about finding a set of unique digits (1-9) to replace the letters in the word "WONDERFUL" such that the resulting number is a perfect square, and the square root of this number can be represented by the letters "OODDF" using the same digit substitution.
The main decision variables are W, O, N, D, E, R, F, U, and L, which represent the digits to be substituted for each letter in the word "WONDERFUL". 
The key constraints are that each letter must be replaced by a unique digit, the resulting number "WONDERFUL" must be greater than 0, and the number represented by "OODDF" must be the square root of "WONDERFUL". Additionally, the digits for W and O must be greater than or equal to 1.
The objective of this problem is to find a satisfying assignment of digits to letters, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. The goal is to determine the unique set of digits that makes "WONDERFUL" a perfect square and "OODDF" its square root.
