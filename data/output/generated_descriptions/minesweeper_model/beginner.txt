Minesweeper problem
The problem is about a game where you have a grid with some hidden mines and some numbers that tell you how many mines are near each cell. 
The main variables are the grid cells, which can either have a mine or not, and the numbers that tell you how many mines are near each cell.
The basic restrictions are that the numbers on the grid must match the actual number of mines near each cell, and that a cell with a mine cannot have a number on it. 
The goal of the problem is to figure out where all the mines are on the grid, based on the given numbers and the rules of the game.
