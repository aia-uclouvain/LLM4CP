name: Minesweeper
description: Minesweeper is a classic puzzle game where the player is presented with a grid of cells, some of which contain hidden mines. The goal is to determine the location of all mines based on clues provided by the game, which indicate the number of mines adjacent to each cell.
variables: $mines_{i,j}$, where $i \in \{1, \ldots, r\}$ and $j \in \{1, \ldots, c\}$. These are decision variables that represent whether a mine is present (1) or not (0) in cell $(i, j)$ of the grid.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, r\}, j \in \{1, \ldots, c\}: (game_{i,j} \geq 0 \rightarrow game_{i,j} = \sum_{a,b \in \{-1,0,1\}} mines_{i+a,j+b}) \wedge (game_{i,j} > -1 \rightarrow mines_{i,j} = 0) \wedge (game_{i,j} = -1 \leftrightarrow mines_{i,j} = 1)$. These constraints ensure that the number of mines adjacent to each cell matches the given clues, and that cells with a clue of -1 (unknown) may or may not contain a mine.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of mines to cells, such that all constraints are met. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a feasible solution that satisfies all constraints.
