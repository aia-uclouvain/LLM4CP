name: Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem (CVRP)
description: The Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves finding the most efficient routes for a fleet of vehicles to visit a set of locations and return to a depot, while not exceeding the capacity of each vehicle. The problem has many real-world applications, such as delivery routing, logistics, and transportation management.
variables: $plan, optVar, c, d$ - These decision variables represent the routes taken by each vehicle ($plan$ and $c$), the total cost of the routes ($optVar$), and the demands collected by each vehicle ($d$). $plan$ is a set of sequences, where each sequence represents the order in which a vehicle visits the locations. $c$ is a 2D array where $c[i][j]$ represents the $j^{th}$ customer visited by the $i^{th}$ vehicle. $d$ is a 2D array where $d[i][j]$ represents the demand of the $j^{th}$ customer visited by the $i^{th}$ vehicle.
constraints: $allDiff([loc | route <- plan, (_,loc) <- route]), sum(p in plan . |p|) = numberLocations, sum (_,order) in route . orderWeights(order) <= vehicleCapacity, optVar = sum route in plan . (sum([ costs(tuple(route(i-1), route(i))) | i : int(2..numberLocations), i<=|route| ]) + costs((0, route(1))) + costs((route(|route|), 0)))$ - These constraints ensure that all locations are visited exactly once, the total number of locations visited is equal to the number of locations, the capacity of each vehicle is not exceeded, and the total cost of the routes is calculated correctly. The $allDiff$ constraint ensures that each location is visited by at most one vehicle. The $sum(p in plan . |p|) = numberLocations$ constraint ensures that all locations are visited. The $sum (_,order) in route . orderWeights(order) <= vehicleCapacity$ constraint ensures that the capacity of each vehicle is not exceeded.
objective: minimize $optVar$ - The objective of the problem is to minimize the total cost of the routes, which is represented by the decision variable $optVar$. The total cost is calculated as the sum of the costs of each route, which includes the cost of traveling from the depot to the first location, the cost of traveling between locations, and the cost of traveling from the last location back to the depot.
