name: Knight Domination Puzzle
description: The Knight Domination Puzzle is a classic problem in constraint programming where the goal is to place the minimum number of knights on a chessboard such that every square is threatened by at least one knight. A knight threatens a square if it can move to that square in a single move, following the standard rules of chess.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i \in \{3, \ldots, rows+2\}$ and $j \in \{3, \ldots, cols+2\}$. These variables represent whether a knight is placed on the square at position $(i, j)$ on the board, with $x_{i,j} = 1$ indicating a knight is present and $x_{i,j} = 0$ indicating no knight is present.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{3, \ldots, rows+2\}, j \in \{3, \ldots, cols+2\}: x_{i-2,j-1} + x_{i-1,j-2} + x_{i+1,j-2} + x_{i+2,j-1} + x_{i+2,j+1} + x_{i+1,j+2} + x_{i-1,j+2} + x_{i-2,j+1} \geq 1$ and $\sum_{i=1}^{2} \sum_{j=1}^{cols+4} x_{i,j} + \sum_{i=rows+3}^{rows+4} \sum_{j=1}^{cols+4} x_{i,j} + \sum_{j=1}^{2} \sum_{i=3}^{rows+2} x_{i,j} + \sum_{j=rows+3}^{rows+4} \sum_{i=3}^{rows+2} x_{i,j} = 0$. The first constraint ensures that every square on the board is threatened by at least one knight, while the second constraint ensures that no knights are placed on the dummy squares surrounding the board.
objective: Minimize $minnum = \sum_{i=3}^{rows+2} \sum_{j=3}^{cols+2} x_{i,j}$, which represents the total number of knights placed on the board.
