Weight Plate Combination Problem
The problem is about finding the optimal combination of weight plates to achieve a desired total weight for a barbell or dumbbell, given the available weight plates and their respective weights.
The main decision variables are `x`, an array of variables representing the number of weight plates of each weight to use, and `total`, the total weight including the barbell or dumbbell. The `x` variables represent the number of pairs of weight plates of each weight to use for the barbell, or the number of sets of four weight plates of each weight to use for the dumbbells.
The key constraints are that the number of weight plates used does not exceed the available number of weight plates for each weight, and that the total weight is equal to the desired weight. In plain language, this means that we cannot use more weight plates than we have, and that the total weight of the barbell or dumbbell and the weight plates must equal the desired weight.
The problem's objective is to either maximize the total weight, minimize the number of weight plates used, or satisfy the constraints to find a feasible solution. The objective depends on the specific goal, such as finding the maximum weight that can be achieved with the available weight plates or finding the minimum number of weight plates needed to achieve a certain total weight.
