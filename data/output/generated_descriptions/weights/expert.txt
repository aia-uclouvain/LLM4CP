name: Weight Plates Problem
description: The problem is to determine how to combine weight plates for barbells or dumbbells to achieve a certain total weight, given the availability of weight plates of different weights and the requirement that a certain number of plates of each weight must be used.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_{num\_weights}$, where $x_i$ is the number of pairs of weight plates of weight $weights_i$ to use. These variables represent the decision of how many pairs of weight plates of each weight to use to achieve the desired total weight.
constraints: $x_i \leq weight\_avail_i$ for all $i$, $total = bell\_weight + \sum_{i=1}^{num\_weights} 2 \* x_i \* weights_i$, where the first constraint ensures that we do not use more weight plates of each weight than are available, and the second constraint calculates the total weight of the barbell or dumbbell.
objective: The objective of the problem can be either to satisfy a given total weight, or to minimize the number of weight plates used, or to maximize the total weight. The specific objective is determined by the solve statement used, which can be either "solve satisfy" to find a feasible solution, "solve minimize num\_selected\_weights" to minimize the number of weight plates used, or "solve maximize total" to maximize the total weight.
