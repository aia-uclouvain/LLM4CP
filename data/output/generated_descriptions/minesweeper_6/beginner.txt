Minesweeper problem
The problem is about a game where you have to figure out where mines are hidden in a grid. 
The main variables are the grid cells, which can either have a mine or a number showing how many mines are nearby.
The basic restrictions are that each cell can only have one thing in it (a mine or a number), and the numbers in the cells must match the actual number of mines nearby.
The goal of the problem is to correctly place the mines in the grid so that the numbers in the cells are correct, essentially solving the game.
