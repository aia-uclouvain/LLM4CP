name: Warehouse Location Problem
description: The Warehouse Location Problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves finding the optimal location of warehouses to supply a set of stores with goods. Each warehouse has a limited capacity and incurs a fixed construction cost, while the cost of transportation from warehouses to stores depends on their locations. The goal is to determine which warehouses to construct and which warehouse should supply which store to minimize the overall cost, including construction and transportation costs.
variables: $open, supplier, cost$ - These are the decision variables in the problem. $open$ is a binary variable that indicates whether a warehouse is open or not, $supplier$ is a variable that assigns each store to a warehouse, and $cost$ is a variable that represents the cost of supplying each store.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{n} open_i \cdot building\_cost + \sum_{i=1}^{n\_stores} cost_i = tot$, $cost\_matrix_{i,supplier_i} = cost_i$, $\sum_{i=1}^{n\_stores} (supplier_i == j) \leq capacity_j$, $(\exists i \in 1..n\_stores) (supplier_i == j) == open_j$ - These constraints ensure that the total cost is calculated correctly, the cost of supplying each store is calculated correctly, the capacity of each warehouse is not exceeded, and a warehouse is open if and only if it supplies at least one store.
objective: minimize $tot$ - The objective of the problem is to minimize the total cost, which includes the construction cost of the warehouses and the transportation cost of supplying the stores.
