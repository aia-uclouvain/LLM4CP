Warehouse Location Problem
The Warehouse Location Problem is a classic problem in operations research and constraint programming. It involves determining the optimal location of warehouses to supply a set of stores with goods, while minimizing the total cost of transportation and warehouse construction.

The main decision variables in this problem are:
- The assignment of each store to a warehouse, which determines the transportation cost from the warehouse to the store.
- The decision to open or not open each potential warehouse location, which incurs a fixed construction cost.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each store must be assigned to exactly one warehouse.
- The total number of stores assigned to a warehouse cannot exceed its capacity.
- A warehouse can only supply stores if it is open.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the total cost, which consists of two parts:
- The fixed cost of opening each warehouse.
- The transportation cost from each warehouse to the stores it supplies.
The goal is to find the optimal assignment of stores to warehouses and the optimal set of warehouses to open, such that the total cost is minimized.
