Round Robin Tournament Scheduling
The problem involves scheduling a round robin tournament for a given number of teams, where each team plays every other team exactly once, and the schedule is divided into weeks and periods.

The main decision variables are the schedule, which represents the matches between teams at each week and period. The schedule can be represented by variables such as `sched` in the Essence model or `m`, `x`, and `y` in the PyCSP3 model, where `m` represents the match at each week and period, and `x` and `y` represent the teams playing in each match.

The key constraints are:
- Each team plays every other team exactly once.
- Each team plays at most two times in each period.
- Each week, all teams are different, meaning each team plays each week.
- The schedule is symmetric, meaning that if team A plays team B at week W and period P, then team B plays team A at the same week and period.

The problem's objective is to find a valid schedule that satisfies all the constraints, without a specific objective function to minimize or maximize. The goal is to find a feasible solution that allows all teams to play each other exactly once, while respecting the constraints on the schedule.
