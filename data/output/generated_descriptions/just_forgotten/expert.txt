name: Just Forgotten Puzzle (Enigma 1517)
description: Joe has forgotten one of his bank account numbers, but he remembers that it contains all the digits 0 to 9 in some order. He tried four different sets of numbers, but none of them were correct. However, he realized that in each set, exactly four digits were in their correct position. The goal is to find the correct bank account number.
variables: $x = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_{10}]$, where $x$ is an array of decision variables representing the correct bank account number, with $x_i$ being the digit in the $i^{th}$ position.
constraints: $\forall r \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}: \sum_{c=1}^{10} (x_c = a_{r,c}) = 4$ and $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., 10\}: x_i \neq x_j$, where $a_{r,c}$ represents the $c^{th}$ digit in the $r^{th}$ set of numbers tried by Joe. The first constraint ensures that exactly four digits in each set are in their correct position, and the second constraint ensures that all digits in the correct bank account number are distinct.
objective: The objective is to satisfy the given constraints, as the problem is a satisfaction problem rather than an optimization problem, meaning that the goal is to find a solution that meets all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
