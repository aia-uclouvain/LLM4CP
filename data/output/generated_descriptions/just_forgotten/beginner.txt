Name of the problem: Just Forgotten Puzzle (Enigma 1517)

This problem is about a person named Joe who forgot his bank account number. He remembered that the number used all the digits from 0 to 9 in some order. Joe tried four different combinations of these digits, but none of them were correct. However, he realized that in each of the combinations he tried, four of the digits were in their correct positions.

The main variables are: 
- "a" which represents the four combinations of digits that Joe tried, 
- "x" which represents the correct bank account number that Joe is trying to remember.

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- Each of the combinations that Joe tried has exactly four digits in their correct positions, 
- The correct bank account number must use each of the digits from 0 to 9 exactly once.

The goal of the problem is to find the correct bank account number, which is represented by the variable "x". In other words, we want to figure out the correct order of the digits from 0 to 9 that makes up Joe's bank account number, given the information about the four combinations he tried and the fact that each combination had four correct digits.
