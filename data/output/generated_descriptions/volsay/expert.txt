name: Volsay problem
description: The Volsay problem is a classic linear programming problem that involves finding the optimal production levels of two products, Gas and Chloride, in order to maximize profit while satisfying certain constraints on production capacity and resource availability.
variables: $Gas, Chloride, z$ - These variables represent the production levels of Gas and Chloride, and the total profit $z$, respectively, where $Gas$ and $Chloride$ are the decision variables representing the amount of each product to produce, and $z$ is the objective function value.
constraints: $z = 40.0 \cdot Gas + 50.0 \cdot Chloride$, $Gas + Chloride \leq 50.0$, $3.0 \cdot Gas + 4.0 \cdot Chloride \leq 180.0$ - These constraints represent the relationship between the production levels and the total profit, as well as the limitations on production capacity, where the first constraint defines the objective function, the second constraint limits the total production of both products, and the third constraint limits the production of both products based on their resource requirements.
objective: Maximize $z$ - The objective of the problem is to maximize the total profit $z$, which is a linear function of the production levels of Gas and Chloride.
