Volsay Problem
The Volsay problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the optimal production levels of two products, Gas and Chloride, given certain constraints on their production.

The main decision variables in this problem are Gas and Chloride, which represent the amounts of Gas and Chloride to be produced, respectively. The variable z represents the total profit, which is a function of the amounts of Gas and Chloride produced.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- The total amount of Gas and Chloride produced cannot exceed 50 units.
- The production of Gas and Chloride is also limited by a second constraint, which states that 3 times the amount of Gas plus 4 times the amount of Chloride cannot exceed 180 units.

The objective of the Volsay problem is to maximize the total profit, z, which is calculated as 40 times the amount of Gas produced plus 50 times the amount of Chloride produced. In other words, the goal is to find the optimal production levels of Gas and Chloride that result in the highest possible profit, while satisfying the given constraints.
