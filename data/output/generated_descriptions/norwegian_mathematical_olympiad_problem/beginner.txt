The problem is called the "Delightfully Fruitful Problem" from the Norwegian Math Olympiad. 
It's about buying apples, bananas, and cherries. 
The main variables are: 
- a, the cost of one apple, 
- b, the cost of one banana, 
- c, the cost of one cherry, 
- y, the total cost of one apple, two bananas, and one cherry. 
The basic restrictions are that the costs of the fruits are positive integers and that the total cost of a certain combination of fruits is $77. 
The goal is to find the total cost (y) of one apple, two bananas, and one cherry.
