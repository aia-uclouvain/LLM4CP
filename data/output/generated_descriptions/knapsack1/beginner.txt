The name of the problem is the Knapsack Problem. 
This problem is about a thief who has a bag with a limited capacity and wants to fill it with valuable items like gold, silver, and bronze ingots to maximize the total value. 
The main variables are the number of each type of ingot the thief can take, and the total value or profit. 
The basic restrictions are that the total weight of the items cannot exceed the bag's capacity, and the thief can only take a whole number of each item. 
The goal of the problem is to maximize the total value of the items in the bag, given the weight constraints.
