name: Pi Day Sudoku 2011
description: The problem is a variation of Sudoku, where a 6x12 grid is divided into three types of regions: six rings, six pairs of opposite wedges, and six adjacent pairs of wedges of the same color. The goal is to fill in the grid so that the numbers 1-12 appear exactly once in each region.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}$ and $j \in \{1, \ldots, 12\}$. These are decision variables representing the value assigned to each cell in the grid.
constraints: 
$\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}: \text{alldifferent}(\{x_{i,j} | j \in \{1, \ldots, 12\}\})$, 
$\forall j \in \{2, 4, \ldots, 10\}: \text{alldifferent}(\{x_{i,j} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}\} \cup \{x_{i,j+1} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}\})$, 
$\text{alldifferent}(\{x_{i,1} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}\} \cup \{x_{i,12} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}\})$, 
$\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}: \text{alldifferent}(\{x_{i,j} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}\} \cup \{x_{i,j+6} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}\})$. 
These constraints ensure that each row, each pair of adjacent wedges, and each pair of opposite wedges contain the numbers 1-12 exactly once.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment, i.e., to fill in the grid such that all constraints are satisfied. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
