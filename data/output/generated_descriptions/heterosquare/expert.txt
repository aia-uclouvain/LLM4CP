name: Heterosquare problem

description: The Heterosquare problem is a constraint satisfaction problem where the goal is to fill an n x n matrix with distinct integers from 1 to n^2, such that the sums of the rows, columns, and diagonals are all different.

variables: $mat_{i,j}, row\_sums_i, col\_sums_j, diag1, diag2$ 
These are the decision variables of the problem, where $mat_{i,j}$ represents the value in the i-th row and j-th column of the matrix, $row\_sums_i$ and $col\_sums_j$ represent the sum of the i-th row and j-th column respectively, and $diag1$ and $diag2$ represent the sum of the main diagonal and the anti-diagonal of the matrix respectively.

constraints: $\forall i,j \in \{1,...,n\}: mat_{i,j} \in \{1,...,n^2\}$, $all\_different(mat_{i,j})$, $all\_different(row\_sums_i ++ col\_sums_j ++ \{diag1, diag2\})$, $\forall i \in \{1,...,n\}: \sum_{j=1}^{n} mat_{i,j} = row\_sums_i$, $\forall j \in \{1,...,n\}: \sum_{i=1}^{n} mat_{i,j} = col\_sums_j$, $\sum_{i=1}^{n} mat_{i,i} = diag1$, $\sum_{i=1}^{n} mat_{i,n-i+1} = diag2$, $mat_{1,1} \leq \{mat_{1,1}, mat_{1,n}, mat_{n,1}, mat_{n,n}\}$, $mat_{1,2} < mat_{2,1}$
These constraints ensure that the matrix is filled with distinct integers, the sums of the rows, columns, and diagonals are all different, and the matrix satisfies the symmetry breaking conditions.

objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize. The problem is a constraint satisfaction problem, and the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
