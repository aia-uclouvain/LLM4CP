name: Water Buckets Problem
description: The Water Buckets Problem is a classic problem in the field of constraint programming and operations research. It involves a set of buckets with different capacities, and the goal is to find a sequence of pouring operations to transform an initial state into a target state, where the amount of water in each bucket is specified. The problem requires finding the minimum number of pouring operations to achieve the target state.
variables: $x_{ij}, state_{ij}, goal\_step, z, actions, states, nbActions$ - These variables represent the amount of water in each bucket at each time step ($x_{ij}, state_{ij}$), the minimum number of steps required to reach the goal state ($goal\_step, z$), and the sequence of actions and states ($actions, states$) that achieve the target state.
constraints: $x_{ij} \geq 0, x_{ij} \leq capacity_j, \sum_{j=1}^{nb\_buckets} x_{ij} = \sum_{j=1}^{nb\_buckets} x_{i+1,j}, \sum_{j=1}^{nb\_buckets} (x_{ij} \neq x_{i+1,j}) = 2, (x_{ij} \neq x_{i+1,j}) \rightarrow (x_{i+1,j} = 0 \lor x_{i+1,j} = capacity_j), x_{1j} = start_j, x_{goal\_step,j} = goal_j$ - These constraints ensure that the amount of water in each bucket is within its capacity, the total amount of water is preserved at each time step, exactly two buckets change their water level at each time step, and the target state is reached after the minimum number of steps.
objective: minimize $goal\_step, z, nbActions$ - The objective is to minimize the number of pouring operations required to transform the initial state into the target state.
