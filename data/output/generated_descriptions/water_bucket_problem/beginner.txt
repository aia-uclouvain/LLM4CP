Water Bucket Problem
The problem is about pouring water between three buckets to achieve a specific goal. 
Imagine you have three buckets with different capacities, and you want to divide the water in one bucket into two other buckets. 
You can pour water from one bucket to another, but you can only pour as much water as the destination bucket can hold.

The main variables are the amounts of water in each bucket at each step. 
These variables represent how much water is in each bucket after each pouring operation.

The basic restrictions are that each bucket can only hold a certain amount of water, and when pouring water from one bucket to another, either the source bucket becomes empty or the target bucket becomes full. 
Additionally, the total amount of water remains the same throughout the process.

The goal of the problem is to find the minimum number of pouring operations required to achieve the desired distribution of water in the buckets. 
In other words, you want to divide the water into two buckets in the fewest number of steps possible.
