Water Bucket Problem
The Water Bucket Problem is a classic problem in the field of constraint programming. 
It involves a set of buckets with different capacities, and the goal is to find a sequence of pouring operations to achieve a target distribution of water among the buckets.

The main decision variables in this problem are the amount of water in each bucket at each time step. 
These variables represent the state of the system at each point in time and are used to determine the possible pouring operations that can be performed.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The amount of water in each bucket cannot exceed its capacity.
- The total amount of water in all buckets remains constant throughout the process.
- At each time step, exactly two buckets are involved in the pouring operation, and the amount of water in these buckets changes.
- After each pouring operation, either the source bucket is empty or the target bucket is full.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the number of pouring operations required to achieve the target distribution of water among the buckets. 
This is typically done by finding the shortest sequence of pouring operations that satisfies all the constraints and achieves the desired goal state.
