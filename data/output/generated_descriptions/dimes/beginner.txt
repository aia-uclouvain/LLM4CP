Dimes puzzle
The problem is about a person who needs to buy stamps of different values with a limited amount of money, exactly enough to buy the required stamps. 
The main variables are the number of stamps of each type (one-cent, two-cent, three-cent, five-cent, and ten-cent) and the total amount of money spent. 
The basic restrictions are that the person needs to buy four of two types of stamps and three of the other types, the total amount of money spent must be a multiple of 10 (since a dime is worth 10 cents), and the values of the stamps are one-cent, two-cent, three-cent, five-cent, and ten-cent. 
The goal of the problem is to find the number of stamps of each type that the person wants to buy, given the total amount of money spent.
