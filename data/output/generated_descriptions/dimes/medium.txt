Dimes Puzzle
The Dimes Puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the number of stamps of each type that a person wants to buy, given certain conditions. 
The main decision variables in this problem are `values`, `amount`, and `amount_gcc`. The `values` variable represents the values of the stamps, `amount` represents the number of stamps of each type, and `amount_gcc` represents the global cardinality of the `amount` variable, which means the number of times each value appears in `amount`.
The key constraints in this problem are: all stamp values must be different and in increasing order, the stamp values must be 1, 2, 3, 5, and 10 cents, the total cost of the stamps must be a multiple of 10 cents (i.e., the total cost can be paid with dimes), and there must be four stamps of two types and three stamps of the other types. 
The objective of this problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific variable. However, the problem can be modified to maximize the total cost of the stamps or to find the maximum number of possible solutions.
