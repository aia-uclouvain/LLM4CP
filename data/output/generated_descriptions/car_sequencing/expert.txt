name: Car Sequencing
description: The Car Sequencing problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves sequencing the production of cars on an assembly line, subject to certain constraints. Each car has a set of options, such as sunroofs or air conditioning, and each option has a limited capacity. The goal is to sequence the production of the cars such that the capacity constraints for each option are satisfied.
variables: $c, o, slot, setup, seq$ - These are the decision variables in the problem. $c$ represents the class of each car, $o$ represents whether each car has a particular option, $slot$ represents the sequence of cars, $setup$ represents whether a particular option is installed in a particular car, and $seq$ represents the sequence of cars in another formulation.
constraints: $\forall c \in C, \sum_{s \in S} (slot[s] = c) = demand[c]$ - This constraint ensures that the right number of cars of each class are produced. $\forall o \in O, s \in S, \sum_{j \in s..s + windowSize[o] - 1} setup[o, j] \leq optMax[o]$ - This constraint ensures that the capacity constraints for each option are satisfied. $\forall o \in O, s \in S, setup[o, s] = optionsRequired[slot[s], o]$ - This constraint links the setup variables to the slot variables. $\forall o \in O, i \in 1..optionDemand[o], \sum_{s \in 1..(nCars - i * windowSize[o])} setup[o, s] \geq (optionDemand[o] - i * optMax[o])$ - This constraint ensures that the demand for each option is met.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible sequence of cars that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
