name: Timeslot Problem
description: The Timeslot Problem is a combinatorial problem where seven consecutive time slots need to be filled with six song tapes and one news tape, with certain restrictions on their order. Each tape is unique and must be assigned to a different time slot. The restrictions include: L must be played immediately before O, the news tape must be played after L, and there must be exactly two time slots between G and P.
variables: $G, H, L, O, P, S, News, x, timeline$ - These variables represent the time slots assigned to each song tape (G, H, L, O, P, S) and the news tape (News), as well as the decision variables $x$ which is an array of the time slots for each tape, and $timeline$ which is an array representing the order of the tapes in the time slots. 
constraints: $O-L = 1, News > L, abs(G-P) = 3, alldifferent(x), inverse(x, timeline)$ - These constraints ensure that: L is played immediately before O, the news tape is played after L, there are exactly two time slots between G and P, each tape is assigned to a unique time slot, and the $x$ and $timeline$ arrays are inverses of each other.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find all possible assignments of the tapes to the time slots that satisfy the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
