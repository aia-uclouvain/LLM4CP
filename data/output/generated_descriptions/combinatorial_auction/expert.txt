name: Combinatorial Auction Problem

description: The Combinatorial Auction Problem is a problem where bidders can place bids on combinations of items, and the auctioneer must select a subset of bids to maximize revenue, ensuring that no two selected bids contain the same item.

variables: $x = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_{num\_bids}], total$ 
These are decision variables where $x_i$ represents whether bid $i$ is selected (1) or not (0), and $total$ represents the total revenue.

constraints: $total = \sum_{i=1}^{num\_bids} x_i \* bids_i$, $\forall j \in 1..num\_items: \sum_{i=1}^{num\_bids} x_i \* bool2int(j \in packages_i) \leq 1$ 
These constraints ensure that the total revenue is calculated correctly and that each item is selected at most once.

objective: Maximize $total$
