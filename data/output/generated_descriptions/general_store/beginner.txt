The General Store problem. 
This problem is about finding the correct digits to replace letters in a mathematical puzzle where several words add up to a total, "ALL WOOL". 
The main variables are letters like C, H, E, S, A, B, O, W, P, and L, which represent different digits from 0 to 9. 
The basic restrictions are that each letter must be a different digit, and when the words are added together, they must equal the total "ALL WOOL". 
The goal is to find the correct digits for each letter so that the equation is satisfied, which means finding a solution that makes the sum of the words equal to "ALL WOOL" with each letter replaced by a unique digit.
