Generalized Knapsack Problem
The problem is about a person named Tom who is going on a hike and wants to pack items in his knapsack to maximize the total benefit he gets from them. 
The main variables are the number of copies of each item Tom takes, the total benefit he gets, and the total weight of the items in his knapsack. 
The basic restrictions are that Tom's knapsack can only hold up to 20 pounds, he can take a limited number of copies of each item, and each item has a certain weight and benefit associated with it. 
The goal is to maximize the total benefit Tom gets from the items he packs, without exceeding the weight limit of his knapsack.
