The problem is called Queen Attacking. 
It's about placing a queen and some other values on a chessboard in such a way that the queen doesn't attack certain special numbers, called prime numbers. 
The main variables are the position of the queen (q) and the positions of the other values (x). 
The basic restrictions are that each value must be placed in a different cell, and each value must be placed in a cell that is a knight's move away from the previous value. 
The goal is to minimize the number of prime numbers that are not attacked by the queen.
