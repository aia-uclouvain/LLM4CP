**The Knight's Tour with a Queen Problem**
The problem is about finding a knight's tour on an n x n chessboard with a queen placed on one of the cells, such that the number of prime numbers not attacked by the queen is minimized.

**Main Decision Variables:**
- `q`: represents the cell where the queen is placed.
- `x`: an array of variables representing the cells where the knight's tour values are placed.

**Key Constraints:**
- All values in the knight's tour must be placed in different cells.
- The knight must move in an L-shape (two spaces in one direction, then one space in a perpendicular direction) between each pair of successive values.
- The queen attacks a cell if it is in the same row, column, or diagonal as the cell.

**Objective:**
The objective is to minimize the number of prime numbers that are not attacked by the queen. In other words, the goal is to place the queen such that it attacks as many prime numbers as possible, while still allowing the knight to complete its tour.
