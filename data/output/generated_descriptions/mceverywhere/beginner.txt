McEverywhere problem
The problem is about deciding where to build fast food restaurants in a town so that every resident can reach one within a certain distance, in this case, 4km. 
The main variables are the locations of homes and restaurants in the town, represented by a grid where each cell can have a home, a restaurant, or both. 
The basic restrictions are that each resident must be able to reach a restaurant within 4km, and restaurants can be built on any cell, including ones with homes. The distance between cells is calculated using a direct line, with adjacent cells being 1km apart and diagonal cells being 1.41km apart.
The goal is to minimize the number of restaurants needed to ensure every resident is within 4km of one.
