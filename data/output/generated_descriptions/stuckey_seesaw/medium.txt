Seesaw Problem
The problem is about balancing on a seesaw with three people, Liz, Fi, and Sara, who have different weights.
The main decision variables are Liz, Fi, and Sara, which represent the positions of each person on the seesaw, with negative values indicating a position to the left of the fulcrum and positive values indicating a position to the right.
The key constraints are: the total weight of the people on the seesaw must be balanced, and each person must be at least 3 units away from the others. Additionally, a symmetry-breaking constraint is included to ensure that Sara is on the right side of the fulcrum.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of positions for Liz, Fi, and Sara that meets the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function. The goal is to find a feasible solution that balances the seesaw while respecting the distance constraints between the people.
