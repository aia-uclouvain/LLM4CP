name: Calvin Puzzle
description: The Calvin Puzzle is a constraint satisfaction problem where the goal is to fill a 10x10 grid with numbers from 1 to 100, following specific movement rules. The puzzle has two types of movements: Type I, where the next number is placed vertically or horizontally, exactly three squares away from the previous number, with a two-square gap in between; and Type II, where the next number is placed diagonally, exactly two squares away from the previous number, with a one-square gap in between.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ for $i = 1, \ldots, n$ and $j = 1, \ldots, n$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the number placed in the $i$-th row and $j$-th column of the grid, and $n$ is the size of the grid. These variables are decision variables that determine the placement of numbers in the grid.
constraints: $\bigwedge_{i=1}^{n} \bigwedge_{j=1}^{n} x_{i,j} \in \{1, \ldots, n^2\}$, $\bigwedge_{k=1}^{n^2-1} (k = x_{i,j} \land k+1 = x_{i+a,j+b} \land (|a|=2 \land |b|=2 \lor |a|=3 \land b=0 \lor |b|=3 \land a=0))$, and $\bigwedge_{i=1}^{n} \bigwedge_{j=1}^{n} \bigwedge_{i'=1}^{n} \bigwedge_{j'=1}^{n} (x_{i,j} = x_{i',j'} \rightarrow i=i' \land j=j')$, which represent the constraints that each cell contains a number between 1 and $n^2$, the movement rules, and the all-different constraint, respectively.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, which means finding a valid placement of numbers in the grid that follows the movement rules. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
