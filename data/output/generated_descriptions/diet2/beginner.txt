Stigler's Nutrition Model 
This problem is about finding the cheapest way to buy food that meets a person's daily nutritional needs. 
The main variables are the amount of each food to buy, and the total cost of the food. 
The basic restrictions are that the food must provide enough of each nutrient, such as calories, protein, and vitamins, and that the amount of each food bought must be non-negative. 
The goal is to minimize the total cost of the food, while still meeting the nutritional requirements.
