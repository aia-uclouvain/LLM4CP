**Diet Problem**
The diet problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the most cost-effective diet that meets the daily nutritional requirements of an individual.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are the amounts of each food item to be purchased daily, represented by the array `x`. Each element `x[f]` represents the amount of food `f` to be purchased, where `f` ranges from 1 to the total number of food items (`num_foods`).

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
- The total amount of each nutrient consumed must meet the daily recommended allowance. This is represented by the constraint `sum(f in F) (a[f,n] * x[f]) = b[n]`, where `a[f,n]` is the amount of nutrient `n` in food `f`, and `b[n]` is the daily recommended allowance of nutrient `n`.
- The cost of the diet, represented by the variable `cost`, must be minimized.

**Objective**
The objective of the diet problem is to minimize the total cost of the diet while meeting the daily nutritional requirements. This is achieved by finding the optimal amounts of each food item to purchase, represented by the array `x`, such that the total cost is minimized.

In simpler terms, the problem is trying to answer the question: "What is the cheapest combination of foods that provides all the necessary nutrients for a healthy diet?" The solution to this problem provides a list of food items and their corresponding quantities that meet the nutritional requirements at the lowest possible cost.
