name: Multi Sudoku
description: The Multi Sudoku problem is a variant of the classic Sudoku puzzle where multiple Sudoku grids overlap. The goal is to fill in the missing numbers in each grid such that the overlapping regions are consistent across all grids, and each grid satisfies the standard Sudoku rules.
variables: $s1_{1..9,1..9}, s2_{1..9,1..9}$, where $s1$ and $s2$ are 9x9 matrices representing the two overlapping Sudoku grids, with each cell $s1_{i,j}$ and $s2_{i,j}$ being a decision variable that takes a value from 1 to 9, representing the number to be placed in the corresponding cell.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..9: \text{alldifferent}(s1_{i,1..9}) \wedge \text{alldifferent}(s1_{1..9,i}) \wedge \text{alldifferent}(s2_{i,1..9}) \wedge \text{alldifferent}(s2_{1..9,i}) \wedge \forall r,c \in 0..2: \text{alldifferent}([s1_{i+r*3,j+c*3} | i \in 1..3, j \in 1..3]) \wedge \text{alldifferent}([s2_{i+r*3,j+c*3} | i \in 1..3, j \in 1..3]) \wedge \forall i,j \in 1..3: s2_{i,j} = s1_{9-3+i,9-3+j}$, which represent the standard Sudoku constraints for each grid (each row, column, and 3x3 sub-grid must contain each number exactly once), as well as the constraint that the overlapping regions of the two grids must be consistent.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, such that all constraints are satisfied, with no specific objective function to minimize or maximize.
