The problem is known as the Set Sum problem. 
It is about finding a subset of integers from a given set of integers (from 1 to n) such that the sum of the integers in the subset is equal to a certain total sum. 
The main decision variables are `s` (a set of integers) and `s_total` (the total sum of the integers in `s`), where `s` represents the subset of integers to be selected and `s_total` represents the sum of the integers in this subset. 
The key constraints are that `s` must be a subset of the integers from 1 to `n`, and the sum of the integers in `s` must be equal to `s_total`. 
The problem's objective is to find a subset `s` that satisfies the given constraints, with no specific objective function to minimize or maximize, as the problem is solved using the `solve satisfy` statement, which means the goal is to find any solution that satisfies all the constraints.
