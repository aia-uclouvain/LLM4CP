Production Planning Problem
The problem is about determining the optimal production levels of two products, X and Y, in order to maximize profit while satisfying certain constraints.
The main decision variables are XB and XC, which represent the production levels of products X and Y, respectively, measured in units.
The key constraints are: 
- Non-negativity constraints, which ensure that production levels are not negative
- Upper bound constraints, which limit the maximum production levels of each product
- A resource constraint, which limits the total amount of a shared resource used by both products, where product X requires 1/200 of the resource per unit and product Y requires 1/140 of the resource per unit, and the total available resource is 40 units.
The objective of the problem is to maximize the total profit, which is calculated as 25 times the production level of product X plus 30 times the production level of product Y.
