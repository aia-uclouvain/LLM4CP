name: Linear Programming Problem
description: This problem is a classic example of a linear programming problem, where the goal is to maximize a linear objective function subject to a set of linear constraints. The problem can be interpreted as a production planning problem, where two products, XB and XC, are produced with limited resources. The objective is to determine the optimal production levels of XB and XC to maximize profit.
variables: $XB, XC$ - These are the decision variables representing the production levels of products XB and XC, respectively.
constraints: $XB \geq 0, XC \geq 0, XB \leq 6000, XC \leq 4000, \frac{1}{200}XB + \frac{1}{140}XC \leq 40$ - These constraints represent the non-negativity of production levels, the upper limits on production capacities, and a resource constraint that limits the total amount of resources used by the production of XB and XC.
objective: Maximize $25XB + 30XC$ - The objective is to maximize the total profit, which is a linear function of the production levels of XB and XC.
