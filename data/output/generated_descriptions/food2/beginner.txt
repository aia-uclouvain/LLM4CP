Food Manufacture Problem
The problem is about a company that produces food products using different types of oils. The company needs to decide how much of each oil to buy and use each month to maximize its profit.

The main variables are: 
- buys: the amount of each oil bought each month
- stock: the amount of each oil stored each month
- production: the amount of food product produced each month
- useoil: the amount of each oil used to produce food each month

The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- The company can only store a limited amount of each oil
- The company must have a minimum amount of each oil in stock at the beginning and end of the period
- The company can only use a certain amount of each oil to produce food each month
- The food product must have a certain level of hardness, which is determined by the types of oils used
- The company must not use more than three types of oils in a month
- If a certain type of oil is used, the company must use at least 20 tons of it

The goal of the problem is to maximize the total profit, which is calculated by subtracting the cost of buying and storing oils from the revenue generated by selling the food product.
