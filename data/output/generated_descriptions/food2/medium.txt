Food Manufacture Problem
The Food Manufacture Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the optimal production plan for a food manufacturing company. 

The main decision variables in this problem are: 
- `buys`: the amount of each oil bought in each month
- `stock`: the amount of each oil stored in each month
- `useoil`: the amount of each oil used in production in each month
- `production`: the total amount of product produced in each month

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- The initial stock of each oil is 500 tons, and the final stock must be at least 500 tons.
- The amount of each oil bought, stored, and used in production must be non-negative.
- The amount of each oil used in production cannot exceed the available stock and bought amount.
- The stock of each oil in each month is updated based on the previous month's stock, bought amount, and used amount.
- The total hardness of the oils used in production must be within a certain range.
- The production amount in each month is the sum of the amounts of all oils used.
- There are refining constraints that limit the amount of certain oils that can be used together.
- Additional conditions such as the maximum number of oils used in each month, the minimum amount of each oil used, and the dependency between certain oils are also considered.

The objective of this problem is to maximize the total profit, which is calculated as the total revenue from selling the product minus the total cost of buying the oils and storing them.
