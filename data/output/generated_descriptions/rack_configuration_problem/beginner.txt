Rack Configuration Problem
The problem is about plugging electronic cards into racks with connectors in a way that minimizes the total cost. 
The main variables are: 
- the model of rack used for each rack, 
- the number of cards of each type plugged into each rack, 
- the power and number of connectors of each rack, 
- the cost of each rack model, 
- the power required and demand for each card type.
The basic restrictions are: 
- each rack can only hold a certain number of cards based on its connectors, 
- the total power of cards in a rack cannot exceed the rack's power capacity, 
- all cards of each type must be plugged into some rack, 
- each rack must use a valid rack model.
The goal is to minimize the total cost of the rack models used.
