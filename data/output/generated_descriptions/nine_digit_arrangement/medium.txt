The problem is called the "Nine-Digit Arrangement" or "Gardner's Problem". 
It is about arranging the digits 1 through 9 in two different multiplication equations, such that both equations yield the same product. 
The main decision variables are A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, and I, which represent the digits 1 through 9, each used exactly once. 
The key constraints are that each digit is used only once, the two multiplication equations must have the same product, and the product must be equal to 7448. 
Additionally, a symmetry-breaking constraint is used to ensure that the first multiplicand in the second equation is less than or equal to the second multiplicand. 
The objective of the problem is to find all possible arrangements of the digits that satisfy these constraints, with a particular focus on finding the arrangement that yields the maximum product, which is 7448.
