name: Enigma 248: Add or Multiply
description: The problem involves five boys who are given different amounts of money to spend on goodies at a corner shop. Each goodie must cost a different exact number of half-pennies. The boys are instructed to spend all their money in such a way that when they multiply the prices of their purchases, the result is the same as if they added them. Additionally, in one case, each of the prices is an exact whole number of pence. The goal is to find the total amount of money spent by the boys.
variables: $n, m, total_i, x_{i,j}$, where $n$ is the number of boys, $m$ is the number of goodies, $total_i$ is the total amount spent by boy $i$, and $x_{i,j}$ is the number of half-pennies for goodie $j$ bought by boy $i$. These variables represent the number of boys, the number of goodies, the total amount spent by each boy, and the cost of each goodie in half-pennies.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..n: total_i = \sum_{j=1}^{m} 0.5x_{i,j}$, $\forall i \in 1..n: total_i = \prod_{j=1}^{m} 0.5x_{i,j}$, $\forall i \in 1..n, j \in 2..m: 0.5x_{i,j-1} \neq 0.5x_{i,j}$, $\forall i \in 1..n, j \in 2..m: 0.5x_{i,j-1} \leq 0.5x_{i,j}$, $\sum_{i=1}^{n} (\sum_{j=1}^{m} (x_{i,j} \mod 2 = 0) = m) = 1$, $\forall i \in 2..n: total_{i-1} \neq total_i$, $\forall i \in 2..n: total_{i-1} \leq total_i$. These constraints ensure that the total amount spent by each boy is equal to the sum and product of the prices of the goodies, that each goodie has a unique price, that the prices are in ascending order, that in one case all prices are whole numbers, and that the total amounts spent by the boys are distinct and in ascending order.
objective: The objective is to find the total amount spent by the boys, which is given by $\sum_{i=1}^{n} total_i$.
