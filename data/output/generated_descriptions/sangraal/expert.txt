name: Scheduling Problem from Sangraal
description: The problem is about freeing 8 knights from their imprisonment and preparing them for the arrival of the Sangraal within 20 minutes. Each knight has a specific time to be freed and prepared, and the goal is to maximize the number of knights that can be freed and prepared within the given time frame.
variables: $x_{i,j}, d_j, t_j, maxk$ where $x_{i,j}$ represents whether knight $i$ is in position $j$, $d_j$ represents whether the knight in position $j$ is freed and prepared within 20 minutes, $t_j$ represents the finish time for each position, and $maxk$ represents the maximum number of positions finished within 20 minutes. These variables are used to determine the optimal schedule for freeing and preparing the knights.
constraints: $\sum_{j \in posit} x_{i,j} = 1, \sum_{i \in knight} x_{i,j} = 1, \sum_{i \in knight, l \in 1..j-1} (free_i \cdot x_{i,l}) + \sum_{i \in knight} ((free_i + prep_i) \cdot x_{i,j}) = t_j, t_j \geq 21 - 15 \cdot d_j, t_j \leq 53 - 33 \cdot d_j$ where the first two constraints ensure that each knight is in one position and each position has one knight, the third constraint computes the finish time for each position, and the last two constraints ensure that the finish time is within the given bounds if the knight is freed and prepared within 20 minutes.
objective: Maximize $maxk$, which represents the maximum number of positions finished within 20 minutes, i.e., the maximum number of knights that can be freed and prepared before the arrival of the Sangraal.
