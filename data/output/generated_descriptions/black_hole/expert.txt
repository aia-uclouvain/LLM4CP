name: Black Hole Solitaire
description: The problem is a solitaire card game where the goal is to remove all cards from 17 stacks of 3 cards each onto a central pile, called the black hole. The black hole accepts a card if its rank is one greater or one less than the rank of the card currently on top of it.
variables: $x, y, blackHole, cardSequence$ - These are the decision variables where $x$ and $y$ represent the value and position of each card in the built stack, $blackHole$ represents the sequence of cards played on the black hole, and $cardSequence$ represents the position of each card in the sequence.
constraints: $blackHole[0] = 0, cardSequence[0] = 0, allDiff(cardSequence), allDiff(blackHole), (blackHole[step-1], blackHole[step]) \in CONSECUTIVE\_CARD\_TABLE, cardSequence[initialStacks[card]] < cardSequence[initialStacks[card+1]]$ - These constraints ensure the initial state of the game, that all cards are played in a different order, that the sequence of cards on the black hole follows the rules of the game, and that cards are played in the order they appear in the stacks.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a sequence of cards that can be played on the black hole, removing all cards from the stacks, which can be viewed as a feasibility problem where the goal is to satisfy all the constraints.
