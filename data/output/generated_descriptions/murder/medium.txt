The Murder Puzzle Problem
The Murder Puzzle problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves solving a murder mystery by analyzing clues and piecing together the events surrounding the crime.
The main decision variables in this problem are the suspects, evidence objects, actions, and motives. The suspects are represented by variables Ablaze, Burner, Curious, Dulles, and Evilson, which are assigned to integers 1 through 5. The evidence objects, including pistol, umbrella, cigarette, diary, and letter, are represented by variables that take on values from the set of suspects. The actions, such as argue, leave, rang, walk, and murder, are also represented by variables that take on values from the set of suspects. The motives, including harassed, abandoned, sacked, promotion, and hate, are represented by variables that take on values from the set of suspects.
The key constraints in this problem can be described in plain language as follows: each evidence object belongs to a different suspect, each action is performed by a different suspect, and each motive is associated with a different suspect. Additionally, specific clues are provided, such as the cigarette belonging to Mr. Burner, Ms. Curious not owning the pistol, and Mrs. Dulles walking past the house several times. These constraints are used to narrow down the possible solutions and ultimately identify the murderer.
The objective of this problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, thereby identifying the murderer, their motive, and the evidence object and action associated with each suspect. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a feasible solution that meets all the constraints. The output of the model provides the assignments of suspects to actions, objects, and motives, allowing us to reconstruct the events surrounding the murder.
