Agricultural Pricing
The problem is about finding the best prices for milk, butter, and two types of cheese to maximize revenue, considering various production costs and constraints.
The main variables are: 
- milk, butt, cha, chb: prices of milk, butter, and two types of cheese
- xm, xb, xca, xcb: production amounts of milk, butter, and two types of cheese
- q: a free variable representing a relationship between cheese production and price
- lmilk, lbutt, lcha, lchb, lq, mq: variables representing the proportion of each product at different price points
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- production amounts and prices must be non-negative
- there are limited resources for production
- there are relationships between the production amounts and prices of different products
- the total proportion of each product at different price points cannot exceed 1
The goal is to maximize revenue, which is calculated based on the prices and production amounts of the products.
