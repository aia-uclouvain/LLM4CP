name: Diamond-free Degree Sequences
description: The problem is to find all unique degree sequences for a given number of nodes in a graph, such that the graph is diamond-free, the degree of each node is greater than zero and modulo 3, and the sum of the degrees is modulo 12.
variables: $x_{ij}, degrees_i$ where $x_{ij}$ is the adjacency matrix of the graph and $degrees_i$ is the degree of the ith node. These variables represent the structure of the graph and the degree of each node.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^n x_{ij} + x_{ik} + x_{il} + x_{jk} + x_{jl} + x_{kl} \leq 4 \ \forall i,j,k,l \in \{1,...,n\}$ where $i < j < k < l$, $x_{ij} = x_{ji}$, $degrees_i = \sum_{j=1}^n x_{ij}$, $degrees_i \mod 3 = 0$, $\sum_{i=1}^n degrees_i \mod 12 = 0$, $x_{ii} = 0$, and $degrees_i > 0$. These constraints ensure that the graph is diamond-free, undirected, and satisfies the given properties.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find all feasible solutions, i.e., to produce all unique degree sequences that satisfy the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
