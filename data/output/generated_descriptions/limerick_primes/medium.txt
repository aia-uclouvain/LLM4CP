Limerick Primes problem
The problem is about finding prime numbers that follow the rhyme scheme of a limerick, which is AABBA, where A is an odd digit and B is any digit other than A.
The main decision variables are p, a variable representing the prime number, and x, an array of variables representing the digits of the prime number. The variable p is a 5-digit number, and the array x has 5 elements, each representing a digit in the prime number.
The key constraints are that the prime number p must be a valid 5-digit number, it must be prime, and its digits must follow the AABBA pattern. Specifically, the first, second, and fifth digits must be the same odd digit (A), the third and fourth digits must be the same and different from A (B), and A and B must be different. Additionally, the number must be a valid prime number, meaning it must be greater than 1 and have no divisors other than 1 and itself.
The objective of the problem is to find a prime number that satisfies the limerick rhyme scheme constraints. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
