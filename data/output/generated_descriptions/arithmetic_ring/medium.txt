Arithmetic Ring problem
The Arithmetic Ring problem is about arranging the digits 1 to 8 in a ring such that no two adjacent numbers are consecutive, with the exception of 6 and 7 which must be adjacent.
The main decision variables are the array x, where x[i] represents the digit placed at position i in the ring.
The key constraints are that no two adjacent numbers can be consecutive, unless they are 6 and 7, and that 6 and 7 must be adjacent. Additionally, all digits must be used exactly once, and the solution must satisfy these conditions when wrapping around the ring from the last position to the first.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying arrangement of the digits, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. In other words, the goal is to find a solution that meets all the given constraints.
