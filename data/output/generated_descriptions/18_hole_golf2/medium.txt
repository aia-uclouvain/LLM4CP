Golf Course Problem
The Golf Course problem is about generating a random 18-hole golf course with specific rules. 
The main decision variables are the hole lengths, represented by the array `x`, where each `x[i]` is a variable that can take a value of 3, 4, or 5, representing the length of the `i-th` hole. 
The key constraints are that each hole must have a length of 3, 4, or 5, and the total length of all 18 holes must add up to 72. 
The problem's objective is to find a valid configuration of hole lengths that satisfies these constraints, with the goal of being able to produce every possible hole configuration with some non-zero probability.
