The name of the problem is the Numeric Keypad problem. 
This problem is about rearranging the key caps of the numbers 1 through 9 on a numeric keypad so that no cap is on its correct key, and each of the three rows forms a 3-digit perfect square.

The main decision variables are the elements of the 2D array `x`, where `x[i,j]` represents the number on the key cap at row `i` and column `j` in the rearranged keypad.

The key constraints are: 
- each number from 1 to 9 is used exactly once, 
- no number is on its original key, and 
- the numbers in each row form a 3-digit perfect square.

The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of numbers to key caps, which means finding a rearrangement that meets all the constraints. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints.
