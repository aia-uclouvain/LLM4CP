name: Numeric Keypad Problem
description: The Numeric Keypad Problem is a constraint satisfaction problem where the goal is to rearrange the key caps of the numbers 1 through 9 on a numeric keypad such that no cap is on its correct key, and each of the three rows forms a 3-digit perfect square.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ for $i, j = 1, 2, 3$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the number assigned to the key at row $i$ and column $j$, and is a decision variable that takes a value from the set $\{1, 2, \ldots, 9\}$. 
constraints: $\bigwedge_{i=1}^3 \bigwedge_{j=1}^3 x_{i,j} \neq orig_{i,j}$, $\bigwedge_{i=1}^3 toNum10([x_{i,j}]_{j=1}^3, t_i)$, $\bigwedge_{i=1}^3 \exists y_i \in \{10, 11, \ldots, 32\} (y_i^2 = t_i)$, and $\bigwedge_{i=1}^9 \bigwedge_{j=1}^9 \bigwedge_{k=1}^9 \bigwedge_{l=1}^9 (i \neq k \lor j \neq l) \rightarrow x_{i,j} \neq x_{k,l}$, where the first constraint ensures that no key cap is on its correct key, the second and third constraints ensure that each row forms a perfect square, and the last constraint ensures that all numbers are assigned to exactly one key.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment, i.e., an assignment that satisfies all the constraints, and there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
