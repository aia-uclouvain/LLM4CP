name: De Bruijn Matrix Problem
description: The De Bruijn Matrix Problem is a constraint satisfaction problem that involves finding a matrix of size dim x dim, where each cell can take a value from 0 to base-1, such that all sub-matrices of size m x m are distinct when converted to a number in base 'base'. The problem also involves additional constraints such as requiring all rows and columns to be distinct, and all sub-grids to be distinct.
variables: $x_{i,j}, y_k$ where $i,j \in \{0,1,...,dim-1\}$ and $k \in \{0,1,...,dim^2-1\}$. $x_{i,j}$ represents the value of the cell at position (i,j) in the matrix, and $y_k$ represents the converted value of the k-th sub-matrix.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{0,1,...,dim-1\}, \forall j \in \{0,1,...,extra-1\}: x_{i,dim+j} = x_{i,j} \wedge x_{dim+j,i} = x_{j,i}$, $\forall i,j \in \{0,1,...,dim-1\}: toNum([x_{i+k_1,j+k_2} | k_1,k_2 \in \{0,1,...,m-1\}], y_{i*dim+j}, base)$, $alldifferent(y)$, and additional constraints such as $alldifferent([x_{i,j} | j \in \{0,1,...,dim-1\}])$ and $alldifferent([x_{j,i} | j \in \{0,1,...,dim-1\}])$. These constraints ensure that the first column and row are mirrored, each sub-matrix is converted to a distinct number, and all rows and columns are distinct.
objective: satisfy
