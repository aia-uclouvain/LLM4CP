description: This problem is a classic example of a nonlinear optimization problem, where the goal is to maximize the area of a rectangle with a given perimeter, subject to certain constraints. The problem can be interpreted as finding the optimal dimensions of a rectangle with a fixed perimeter, where the length of the rectangle is limited and the height is subject to a minimum value.
variables: $L, w, h, wh$ - These variables represent the length of the rectangle ($L$), the width of the rectangle ($w$), the height of the rectangle ($h$), and the area of the rectangle ($wh$), respectively. $L$, $w$, and $h$ are decision variables, while $wh$ is a derived variable that represents the product of $w$ and $h$.
constraints: $L \geq 0, w \geq 0, h \geq 0, wh \geq 625, 2(w+h) = L, L \leq 100, h \geq 2$ - These constraints ensure that the length, width, and height of the rectangle are non-negative, the area is greater than or equal to 625, the perimeter of the rectangle is equal to $L$, the length is less than or equal to 100, and the height is greater than or equal to 2.
objective: Maximize $wh$ - The objective of the problem is to maximize the area of the rectangle, which is represented by the product of the width and height ($wh$).
