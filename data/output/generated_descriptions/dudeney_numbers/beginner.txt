Dudeney Numbers problem. 
This problem is about finding special numbers called Dudeney Numbers, which are perfect cubes where the sum of their digits equals the cube root of the number. 
The main variables are: x, which represents the digits of the number, nb, which is the number itself, and s, which is the sum of the digits and also the cube root of the number. 
The basic restrictions are that the number must be a perfect cube, the sum of its digits must equal the cube root, and the digits must be between 0 and 9. 
The goal of the problem is to find all possible Dudeney Numbers by satisfying these conditions.
