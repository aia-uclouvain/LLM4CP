Dudeney Number Problem
The problem is about finding positive integers that are perfect cubes, where the sum of their decimal digits equals the cube root of the number.
The main decision variables are: 
- `x`, an array representing the decimal digits of the number, 
- `nb`, the number itself, and 
- `s`, the sum of the decimal digits of the number, which also represents the cube root of `nb`.
The key constraints are: 
- the number `nb` must be a perfect cube, 
- the number `nb` must be equal to the value represented by its decimal digits `x`, 
- the sum of the decimal digits `x` must be equal to `s`.
The problem's objective is to find values of `x`, `nb`, and `s` that satisfy these constraints, effectively finding Dudeney numbers.
