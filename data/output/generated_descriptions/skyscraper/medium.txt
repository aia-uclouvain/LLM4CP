Skyscraper Puzzle
The Skyscraper puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the heights of buildings in a grid, given certain clues about the number of buildings that can be seen from the outside.

The main decision variables in this problem are the heights of the buildings, represented by the 2D array `x`, where `x[i,j]` is the height of the building at row `i` and column `j`. The variables `cu`, `cl`, `rl`, and `rr` represent the number of visible buildings from the top, bottom, left, and right sides of the grid, respectively.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The grid must be a Latin square, meaning that each row and column contains each height exactly once.
- The number of visible buildings from each side of the grid must match the given clues.
- The heights of the buildings must be between 1 and `n`, where `n` is the size of the grid.

The objective of the Skyscraper puzzle is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function. The goal is to determine the heights of the buildings in the grid, given the clues about the number of visible buildings from each side.
