Steel Mill Slab Design Problem
The problem is about a steel mill that needs to cut steel slabs to fulfill customer orders. Each order requires a certain amount of steel, and the steel mill has a limited number of slabs with different capacities. The goal is to assign the orders to the slabs in a way that minimizes the amount of wasted steel.

The main variables are: 
- the slab used to produce each order (sb)
- the load of each slab (ld), which is the total amount of steel used by the orders assigned to it
- the loss of each slab (ls), which is the difference between the slab's capacity and the load

The basic restrictions are: 
- each order can only be assigned to one slab
- the total load of each slab cannot exceed its capacity
- each slab can have at most two different colors of steel (to simplify the production process)
- the total amount of steel used by all orders must equal the total capacity of all slabs

The goal is to minimize the total amount of wasted steel (loss) by finding the optimal assignment of orders to slabs.
