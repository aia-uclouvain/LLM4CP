name: Minesweeper
description: Minesweeper is a classic puzzle game where the player is presented with a grid of cells, some of which contain hidden mines. The player's goal is to clear all non-mine cells without detonating a mine. The game provides clues in the form of numbers in some cells, which indicate how many mines are adjacent to that cell. The puzzle is solved when all non-mine cells have been cleared, and the location of all mines has been identified.
variables: $x_{i,j} \in \{0,1\}$ for $i \in \{1,\ldots,r\}$ and $j \in \{1,\ldots,c\}$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents whether a mine is present in cell $(i,j)$ or not, and $y_{i,j} \in \mathbb{Z}$ for $i \in \{1,\ldots,r\}$ and $j \in \{1,\ldots,c\}$, which represents the number of mines adjacent to cell $(i,j)$.
constraints: $\sum_{(i',j') \in N(i,j)} x_{i',j'} = y_{i,j}$ for $i \in \{1,\ldots,r\}$ and $j \in \{1,\ldots,c\}$, where $N(i,j)$ denotes the set of neighboring cells of $(i,j)$, and $x_{i,j} \in \{0,1\}$ for $i \in \{1,\ldots,r\}$ and $j \in \{1,\ldots,c\}$, which ensures that the number of mines adjacent to each cell matches the given clues, and the mine presence variables are binary.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, as the goal is to solve the puzzle, not to optimize a specific value.
