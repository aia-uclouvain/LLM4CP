name: Congress Puzzle
description: The Congress Puzzle is a classic problem in constraint programming where five researchers of different nationalities are giving talks on different days. The goal is to determine the date and nationality of each researcher based on given clues.
variables: $talkp = [talkp_1, talkp_2, ..., talkp_5], talkn = [talkn_1, talkn_2, ..., talkn_5]$ - These are arrays of decision variables representing the choice of day for each person and the choice of day for each nationality, respectively.
constraints: 
$\begin{aligned}
&all\_different(talkp) \\
&all\_different(talkn) \\
&talkp[Michael] \neq talkn[Japanese] \\
&talkp[Eric] = talkn[French] \wedge talkp[Eric] \leq 9 \\
&talkp[Arabinda] = 9 \\
&talkp[Hitoshi] \neq talkn[Chinese] \wedge talkn[Chinese] = 8 \wedge talkp[Michael] \geq 9 \\
&talkp[Hitoshi] \geq talkn[Indian] + 1 \wedge talkp[Hitoshi] \leq talkn[American] - 1
\end{aligned}$ 
- These constraints ensure that each researcher talks on a different day, each nationality is assigned exactly once, and the given clues are satisfied.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, as there is no specific value to minimize or maximize.
