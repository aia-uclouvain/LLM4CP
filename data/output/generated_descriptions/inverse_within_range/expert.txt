name: Inverse Within Range Problem
description: The Inverse Within Range Problem is a constraint satisfaction problem that involves finding an assignment of values to two arrays, x1 and x2, such that the inverse relationship between the arrays is satisfied within a certain range. The problem is defined by the global constraint inverse_within_range, which ensures that if the ith variable of x1 is assigned to j and j is within the range of x2, then the jth variable of x2 is assigned to i, and vice versa.
variables: $x1 = [x1_1, x1_2, ..., x1_{n1}], x2 = [x2_1, x2_2, ..., x2_{n2}]$. These are two arrays of decision variables, where $x1_i$ and $x2_j$ represent the values assigned to the ith and jth positions in the arrays x1 and x2, respectively.
constraints: $x1_i <= n2 \leftrightarrow \exists j \in [1..n2] (x2_j = i \wedge x1_i = j), x2_j <= n1 \leftrightarrow \exists i \in [1..n1] (x1_i = j \wedge x2_j = i), x1 = [9,4,2], x2 = [9,3,9,2]$. These constraints ensure that the inverse relationship between x1 and x2 is satisfied within the range of the arrays, and that x1 and x2 are assigned the given values.
objective: satisfy. The objective of the problem is to find an assignment of values to x1 and x2 that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
