Zebra Puzzle
The problem is about figuring out the characteristics of five houses in a row, where each house has a different color, is owned by a person of a different nationality, has a different animal, the owner drinks a different beverage, and smokes a different brand of cigarette.
The main variables are the colors of the houses (red, green, ivory, yellow, blue), the nationalities of the owners (Englishman, Spaniard, Ukrainian, Norwegian, Japanese), the animals in each house (dog, snail, fox, horse, zebra), the drinks consumed by the owners (coffee, tea, milk, orange juice, water), and the cigarette brands they smoke (Old Gold, Kool, Chesterfield, Lucky Strike, Parliament).
The basic restrictions are that each house is unique in terms of color, nationality, animal, drink, and cigarette brand, and there are specific rules about the relationships between these characteristics, such as the green house being next to the ivory house, the Norwegian living in the first house, and the person who smokes Chesterfields living next to the person with the fox.
The goal of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given restrictions and rules, essentially solving a puzzle to determine the characteristics of each house.
