Nurse Rostering Problem
The Nurse Rostering Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves creating a schedule for nurses to work at a hospital. The goal is to assign nurses to shifts while satisfying various constraints, such as ensuring that each shift has a minimum number of nurses, respecting nurse availability, and adhering to rules governing consecutive shifts.

The main decision variables in this problem are the assignments of nurses to shifts, represented by the `x` array, where `x[i, j]` denotes the shift assigned to nurse `i` on day `j`. The shift can be one of three types: day shift, night shift, or off shift.

The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows: 
- Each nurse's schedule must follow a regular pattern, defined by a finite state automaton, to ensure that the nurse is not overworked or underworked.
- Each day, there must be at least 3 nurses assigned to the day shift and at least 2 nurses assigned to the night shift.
- Nurses who are not available on a given day must be assigned to the off shift.
- The number of nurses assigned to each shift on each day must be tracked, and the schedule must satisfy the minimum shift requirements.

The objective of the Nurse Rostering Problem is to find a schedule that satisfies all the constraints, rather than optimizing a specific objective function. In other words, the goal is to find a feasible solution that meets all the requirements, rather than minimizing or maximizing a particular quantity.
