The name of the problem is the Five Puzzle, also known as the 5x5 puzzle. 
This problem is about finding the minimum number of moves to light all 25 squares in a 5x5 grid, where each move toggles the state of a square and its orthogonal neighbors. 
The main decision variables are x[i,j], which represent the state of the square at position (i,j) in the grid, with 0 indicating an unlit square and 1 indicating a lit square, and d[i,j], which are auxiliary variables used to model the constraints of the problem.
