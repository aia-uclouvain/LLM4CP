name: Minimum Vertex Cover Problem (MVCP)
description: The Minimum Vertex Cover Problem is a classic problem in graph theory and computer science, which involves finding a subset of vertices in a graph such that each edge in the graph is incident to at least one vertex in the subset, and the total weight of the vertices in the subset is minimized.
variables: $x = (x_1, x_2, ..., x_{num\_nodes})$, where $x_i$ is a binary decision variable that represents whether vertex $i$ is included in the vertex cover ($x_i = 1$) or not ($x_i = 0$).
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., num\_edges\}, x_{E[i,1]} + x_{E[i,2]} \geq 1$, which ensures that each edge $(i, j)$ in the graph has at least one of its endpoints included in the vertex cover.
objective: Minimize $z = \sum_{i=1}^{num\_nodes} w_i \cdot x_i$, where $z$ represents the total weight of the vertices in the vertex cover, and $w_i$ is the weight of vertex $i$.
