The problem is called "max_n". 
It's about finding the maximum value among a group of numbers, but with a twist: we want to find the maximum value that is at a certain "rank" or position when the numbers are arranged in order from largest to smallest, without counting duplicates. 
The main variables are "variables" which is a list of numbers, "t_max" which is the maximum value we're looking for, and "rank" which tells us the position of "t_max" in the list. 
The basic restrictions are that "t_max" must be one of the numbers in the list, and "rank" is calculated by counting how many numbers in the list are larger than "t_max". 
The goal is to find the values of "t_max" and "rank" that satisfy these conditions, given a specific list of numbers.
