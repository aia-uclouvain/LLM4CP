name: Sort Permutation Problem
description: The Sort Permutation Problem is a constraint satisfaction problem where we have three collections of variables: FROM, PERMUTATION, and TO. The goal is to find a permutation of the TO collection such that the variables of the FROM collection correspond to the variables of the TO collection according to the permutation, and the variables of the TO collection are sorted in increasing order.
variables: $from_1, from_2, ..., from_n, permutation_1, permutation_2, ..., permutation_n, to_1, to_2, ..., to_n$ - These are the decision variables of the problem, where $from_i$, $to_i$ are the $i^{th}$ elements of the FROM and TO collections respectively, and $permutation_i$ is the $i^{th}$ element of the PERMUTATION collection, representing the index of the $i^{th}$ element of the TO collection that corresponds to the $i^{th}$ element of the FROM collection.
constraints: $all\_different(permutation) \land increasing(to) \land \forall i (from_i = to_{permutation_i} \land to_i = from_{permutation_i})$ - These constraints ensure that the permutation is valid (all elements are distinct), the TO collection is sorted, and the correspondence between the FROM and TO collections according to the permutation is satisfied.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
