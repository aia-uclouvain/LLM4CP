Perfect Shuffle Problem
The problem is about shuffling a deck of cards in a specific way, called a perfect shuffle or out-shuffle, and finding out how many times you need to do this shuffle to get the cards back in their original order.

The main variables are:
- n: the number of pairs of cards (since each shuffle involves splitting the deck into two halves and reassembling them in a specific order)
- t: the total number of cards, which is twice the number of pairs (2*n)
- x: a matrix that represents the order of the cards after each shuffle

The basic restrictions or rules are:
- The deck is split into two halves, with the first half containing the first n cards and the second half containing the last n cards
- The two halves are then reassembled in a specific order, with the cards from the first half and the second half alternating in the new order

The goal of the problem is to find the minimum number of shuffles (num_shuffles) needed to get the cards back in their original order, and to demonstrate this by showing the order of the cards after each shuffle.
