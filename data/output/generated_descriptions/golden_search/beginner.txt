The problem is called Golden Search. 
It's about finding the best value of something, let's call it "x", that makes another value, "z", as big as possible. 
The main variables are "x" and "z". "x" is a number that we can choose, and "z" is a result that depends on "x". 
The basic restriction is that "x" must be between -1.0 and 3.0. 
The goal is to maximize "z", which means finding the value of "x" that makes "z" the largest, and "z" is calculated by subtracting a special number (called exp(x)) from "x".
