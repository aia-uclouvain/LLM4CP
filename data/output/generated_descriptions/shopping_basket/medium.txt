**Optimal Shopping Basket Problem**
The Optimal Shopping Basket Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the optimal way to purchase a set of items from different shops with varying prices and delivery costs.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are represented by a 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` is a binary variable that indicates whether item `i` should be purchased from shop `j`. The variable `total` represents the total cost of the optimal shopping strategy.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem can be described in plain language as follows:
- Each item must be purchased from exactly one shop.
- The total cost of purchasing all items from a shop, plus the delivery cost if the total cost is below a certain limit, must be calculated.
- The delivery cost is only added if the total cost of purchasing items from a shop is greater than zero and less than the specified limit.

**Objective**
The objective of the Optimal Shopping Basket Problem is to minimize the total cost of purchasing all items, which includes the cost of the items themselves and any applicable delivery costs. The goal is to find the optimal shopping strategy that results in the lowest total cost.
