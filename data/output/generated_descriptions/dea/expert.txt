name: Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA)
description: Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA) is a method used to quantify the relative efficiency of decision-making units (DMUs) by finding the efficient frontier in multiple input and multiple output data. The inputs are resources, and the outputs are productive outputs. The goal is to evaluate the efficiency of each DMU by comparing its inputs and outputs to those of other DMUs.
variables: $\theta_{td}, \lambda_{d,td}$, where $\theta_{td}$ represents the efficiency score of DMU $td$, and $\lambda_{d,td}$ represents the weight assigned to DMU $d$ in the comparison with DMU $td$. These variables are used to determine the efficient frontier and calculate the efficiency scores of the DMUs.
constraints: $\theta_{td} \geq 0, \lambda_{d,td} \geq 0, \sum_{d \in DMUs} \lambda_{d,td} = 1, \sum_{d \in DMUs} \lambda_{d,td} \cdot input\_data_{d,i} \leq \theta_{td} \cdot input\_data_{td,i}, \sum_{d \in DMUs} \lambda_{d,td} \cdot output\_data_{d,o} \geq output\_data_{td,o}$, which ensure that the efficiency scores are non-negative, the weights are non-negative and sum up to 1, and the inputs and outputs of each DMU are correctly compared to those of other DMUs.
objective: minimize $\sum_{td \in DMUs} \theta_{td}$, which aims to minimize the sum of the efficiency scores of all DMUs, thereby finding the efficient frontier and evaluating the relative efficiency of each DMU.
