name: Enigma 290: Dice with a difference
description: The problem involves finding the values of letters on two special dice, one with 9 sides (IMAGINING) and the other with 4 sides (GAGS), such that the probability distribution of the sum of the numbers on the two dice is the same as the probability distribution of the sum of the numbers on two standard 6-sided dice (ENIGMA). The goal is to interpret the meanings of the letters on the special dice.
variables: $I, M, A, G, N, S, E$ - These variables represent the numerical values of the letters on the special dice, where each letter corresponds to a unique number from 1 to 12.
constraints: $\forall p \in [2..12] \left( probs[p] = \sum_{i=1}^{9} \sum_{j=1}^{4} (dice9[i]+dice4[j] == p) \land probs[p] = \sum_{i=1}^{6} \sum_{j=1}^{6} (dice6[i]+dice6[j] == p) \right)$ - These constraints ensure that the probability distribution of the sum of the numbers on the two special dice is the same as the probability distribution of the sum of the numbers on the two standard dice.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the variables such that all the constraints are met, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
