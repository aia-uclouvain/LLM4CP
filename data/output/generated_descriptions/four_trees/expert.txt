name: Four Trees Problem
description: The problem involves determining the order in which four trees (oak, elm, ash, and fir) are passed when walking around three sides of a square. The given conditions are that the oak and ash are not adjacent, the fir is passed before either the elm or the oak, and the walk does not start or finish at the ash.
variables: $oak, elm, ash, fir, position, order$ - These decision variables represent the positions of the trees (oak, elm, ash, and fir) and the order in which they are passed. The $position$ array assigns a position to each tree, and the $order$ array represents the order in which the trees are passed.
constraints: $all\_different(position) \wedge abs((oak \mod (n+1)) - (ash \mod (n+1))) > 1 \wedge (fir < elm \wedge fir < oak) \wedge (ash > 1 \wedge ash < n) \wedge inverse(position,order)$ - These constraints ensure that each tree has a unique position, the oak and ash are not adjacent, the fir is passed before either the elm or the oak, the walk does not start or finish at the ash, and the order of the trees is consistent with their positions.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of positions and order to the trees, given the constraints. There is no explicit value to minimize or maximize.
