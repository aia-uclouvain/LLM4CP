The Four Trees Problem
The Four Trees problem is about determining the order in which four trees (oak, elm, ash, and fir) are passed when walking around three sides of a square, given certain constraints on their positions.

The main decision variables in this problem are the positions of the four trees, represented by the variables `oak`, `elm`, `ash`, and `fir`, which can take values from 1 to 4, indicating the order in which they are passed.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The oak and ash trees are not adjacent to each other.
- The fir tree is passed before both the elm and oak trees.
- The walk does not start or end at the ash tree.

The objective of this problem is to find a satisfying assignment of positions to the trees, such that all the given constraints are met. In other words, the goal is to determine the order in which the trees are passed, given the constraints on their positions. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
