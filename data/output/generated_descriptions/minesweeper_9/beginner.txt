Minesweeper problem. 
This problem is about a game where you have a grid with some hidden mines and some numbers that tell you how many mines are nearby. 
The main variables are the grid cells, which can either be a mine or a number showing how many mines are around it. 
The basic restrictions are that the numbers in the grid must match the actual number of mines around them, and there are rules about how the mines are placed. 
The goal is to figure out where all the mines are in the grid, using the given numbers as clues.
