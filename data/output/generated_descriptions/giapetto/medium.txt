Giapetto's Problem
The problem is about finding the optimal production levels of two products, soldiers and trains, to maximize profit while satisfying various constraints such as limited resources and demand.

The main decision variables are x1 and x2, which represent the number of soldiers and trains produced, respectively. These variables determine the production levels of the two products.

The key constraints are: 
- Non-negativity constraints, which ensure that the production levels of both products are not negative.
- A finishing constraint, which limits the total time spent on finishing the products, with soldiers requiring twice as much time as trains.
- A carpentry constraint, which limits the total time spent on carpentry work for both products.
- A demand constraint, which limits the production level of soldiers to meet the demand.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the total profit, which is calculated as 3 times the number of soldiers produced plus 2 times the number of trains produced. The goal is to find the optimal production levels of soldiers and trains that result in the highest profit while satisfying all the constraints.
