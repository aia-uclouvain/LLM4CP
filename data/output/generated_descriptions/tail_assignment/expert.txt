name: Tail Assignment Problem
description: The Tail Assignment Problem is a combinatorial optimization problem that involves assigning a sequence of flights to each aircraft in a fleet, subject to various constraints such as maintenance requirements, flight networks, and aircraft capabilities. The goal is to find a feasible assignment of flights to aircraft that satisfies all the constraints.
variables: $route: function (total) Plane --> function int(1..n_flights) --> Flight$, which represents the route for each aircraft, i.e., the sequence of flights assigned to each aircraft.
constraints: 
$\forall s \in range(route) \forall i \in defined(s) : i=1 \lor (i-1) \in defined(s)$, 
$\forall p : Plane : (carry_in(p), route(p)(1)) \in network$, 
$\forall p : Plane \forall a \in range(route(p)) : tuple (a) \in can_fly(p,_)$, 
$\forall s \in range(route) \forall i : int(1..n_flights) , i \leq |s|-1 : (s(i),s(i+1)) \in network$, 
$\forall f : Flight \exists p : Plane : f \in range(route(p))$, 
$\forall p1,p2 : Plane : |range(route(p1)) \cap range(route(p2))| = 0$, 
$\forall s \in range(route) \forall i : int(1..n_flights - maint_freq) , i \leq |s| - maint_freq : \exists j : int(i..i+maint_freq) : s(j) \in maint$, 
which represent the following constraints: valid sequences, route beginnings, aircraft capabilities, flight order, flight coverage, route disjointness, and maintenance frequency.
objective: The problem does not have an explicit objective function, the goal is to find a feasible assignment of flights to aircraft that satisfies all the constraints.
