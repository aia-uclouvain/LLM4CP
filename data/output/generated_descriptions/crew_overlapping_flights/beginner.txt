Crew Scheduling Problem
The problem is about scheduling flights for a group of pilots, making sure that each flight has the right number of pilots with the right qualifications, and that no pilot is assigned to two flights that overlap in time.
The main variables are: 
- Pilots: a list of pilots with their qualifications (captain or first officer)
- Flights: a list of flights with their start and end times, and the number of pilots required
- Crew: a table that assigns pilots to flights
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- Each flight must have the right number of pilots with the right qualifications
- No pilot can be assigned to two flights that overlap in time
- The start and end times of each flight are fixed
The goal of the problem is to minimize the number of pilots who are assigned to at least one flight, which means finding the smallest group of pilots that can cover all the flights.
