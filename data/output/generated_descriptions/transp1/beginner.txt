Transportation Problem
The problem is about transporting products from one city to another while minimizing the total cost. 
The main variables are: 
- Cities: the different cities involved in the transportation
- Products: the types of products being transported, such as bands, coils, and plates
- Supply: the amount of each product available in each city
- Demand: the amount of each product needed in each city
- Cost: the cost of transporting each product from one city to another
- Trans: the amount of each product to be transported from one city to another
- z: the total cost of transportation. 
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- The amount of each product transported from a city cannot exceed the supply available in that city
- The amount of each product transported to a city must meet the demand in that city
- The total amount of all products transported between two cities cannot exceed the capacity of the transportation link between them
The goal of the problem is to minimize the total cost of transportation, z.
