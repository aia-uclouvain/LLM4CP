Seseman's Convent Problem
The problem is about finding a solution to a mathematical puzzle where a set of numbers are arranged in an n x n matrix, with certain constraints on the sums of the numbers in the borders and the total sum of all numbers.
The main decision variables are the elements of the n x n matrix, represented by the array x, where x[i,j] represents the value at row i and column j. The variable total_sum represents the total sum of all numbers in the matrix.
The key constraints are: the sum of the numbers in each border (top, bottom, left, and right) must be equal to the border sum; all numbers in the middle cells must be 0; all numbers in the border cells must be greater than or equal to 1; and the total sum of all numbers in the matrix must be equal to the variable total_sum.
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, with no specific objective function to minimize or maximize, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the Minizinc model. The goal is to find a feasible solution that meets all the constraints, rather than optimizing a specific objective function.
