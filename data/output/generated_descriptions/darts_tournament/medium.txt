The problem is the Nurse Scheduling Problem. 
It is about creating schedules for nurses to work shifts at a hospital, ensuring that all shifts are covered and that each nurse works a reasonable number of hours. 
The main decision variables represent which nurse is assigned to which shift, with each variable typically being a binary value (1 if the nurse is assigned, 0 if not) or an integer indicating the nurse assigned to a particular shift. 
The key constraints include ensuring every shift has a nurse assigned, not overworking any nurse by assigning them too many shifts in a row or too many hours in a week, and adhering to any nurse's personal preferences or availability. 
The objective is typically to minimize the total number of nurses required to cover all shifts, or to maximize the satisfaction of the nurses based on their preferences, while ensuring all constraints are met.
