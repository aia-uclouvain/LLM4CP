name: Blending Problem

description: The blending problem is a classic problem in operations research and optimization, which involves finding the optimal mixture of raw materials to produce a final product that meets certain quality and cost requirements. In this specific problem, we have multiple metals, raw materials, scraps, and ingots, each with its own cost and percentage composition. The goal is to determine the optimal amounts of these materials to blend together to produce a final alloy that meets the required quality standards while minimizing the total cost.

variables: $p, r, s, i, metal$ 
These are the decision variables, where $p$ represents the amount of pure metal used, $r$ represents the amount of raw material used, $s$ represents the amount of scrap metal used, $i$ represents the amount of ingot used, and $metal$ represents the amount of each metal in the final alloy.

constraints: $metal[j] \geq Low[j] * Alloy$, $metal[j] \leq Up[j] * Alloy$, $metal[j] = p[j] + \sum_{k \in Raws} (PercRaw[j,k] * r[k]) + \sum_{k \in Scraps} (PercScrap[j,k] * s[k]) + \sum_{k \in Ingos} (PercIngo[j,k] * i[k])$, $\sum_{j \in Metals} (metal[j]) = Alloy$ 
These constraints ensure that the amount of each metal in the final alloy is within the required range, that the amount of each metal is calculated correctly based on the amounts of raw materials, scraps, and ingots used, and that the total amount of metal in the final alloy meets the required standard.

objective: minimize $z = \sum_{j \in Metals} (CostMetal[j] * p[j]) + \sum_{j \in Raws} (CostRaw[j] * r[j]) + \sum_{j \in Scraps} (CostScrap[j] * s[j]) + \sum_{j \in Ingos} (CostIngo[j] * i[j])$ 
The objective is to minimize the total cost of producing the final alloy, which is calculated as the sum of the costs of all the materials used.
