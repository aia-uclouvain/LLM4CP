name: M12 Puzzle Problem
description: The M12 puzzle is a mathematical puzzle where the goal is to transform an initial configuration into a sorted configuration by applying a sequence of operations, specifically merge (shuffle) and reverse, with the constraint that no two reverse operations can be applied consecutively.
variables: $x_{i,j}, init_j, check_i, operations_i, check\_ix$ 
These variables represent the following: $x_{i,j}$ is the value in the $i^{th}$ row and $j^{th}$ column, $init_j$ is the initial configuration, $check_i$ is a binary variable indicating whether the $i^{th}$ row is the sorted configuration, $operations_i$ is the operation applied to get the $i^{th}$ row, and $check\_ix$ is the index of the first row that is the sorted configuration.
constraints: $x_{1,j} = init_j, check_{i} \leftrightarrow same([x_{i,j} | j in 1..t], [j | j in 1..t]), same([x_{i-1,j} | j in 1..t], [x_{i,j} | j in 1..t]) \lor merge([x_{i-1,j} | j in 1..t], [x_{i,j} | j in 1..t], n) \lor reverse([x_{i-1,j} | j in 1..t], [x_{i,j} | j in 1..t]), operations_i = 0 \lor operations_i = 1 \lor operations_i = 2, not(operations_i = 2 \land operations_{i-1} = 2), check\_ix = i \land check_i = 1 \land \forall j \in 2..i-1 (check_j \neq 1) \land \forall j \in i+1..rows (check_j \neq 1)$ 
These constraints ensure that the initial configuration is set correctly, each row is either the same as the previous row or the result of applying an operation, the operations are correctly applied, no two reverse operations are applied consecutively, and $check\_ix$ is the index of the first row that is the sorted configuration.
objective: minimize $check\_ix$
