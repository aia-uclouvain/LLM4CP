Dudeney's Tea Mixing Problem
The problem is about mixing different types of tea to get a certain total amount and total cost. 
The main variables are the amount of each type of tea to mix, and the total cost of the mix. 
The basic restrictions are that you can't mix a negative amount of tea, the total cost of the mix must be exactly 570, and the total amount of tea must be exactly 20 pounds. 
The goal is to find the cheapest way to mix the teas, by minimizing the total cost.
