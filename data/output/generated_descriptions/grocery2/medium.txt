The name of the problem is the Grocery problem. 
The problem is about finding the prices of four items that a kid buys at a grocery store, given that the total price is $7.11, whether the prices are added or multiplied together. 
The main decision variables are A, B, C, and D, which represent the prices of the four items in cents. 
The key constraints are that the sum of the prices of the four items equals $7.11, the product of the first two items and the product of the last two items, when multiplied together, equal $7.11^3, and the prices are positive integers. Additionally, symmetry breaking constraints are used to ensure that A is less than or equal to B, B is less than or equal to C, and C is less than or equal to D, which reduces the search space by avoiding duplicate solutions. 
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of prices to the four items, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the model.
