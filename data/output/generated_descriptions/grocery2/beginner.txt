The problem is called the Grocery problem. 
It's about a kid who buys four items at a store and the total cost is $7.11. The cashier made a mistake by multiplying the prices instead of adding them, but surprisingly, the total is still $7.11 when the prices are added. 
The main variables are A, B, C, and D, which represent the prices of the four items in cents. 
The basic restrictions are that each item must cost between 1 cent and 711 cents, the prices must be added to get 711 cents, and when the prices are multiplied in a certain way, the result must be a very large number that equals 711 times 100 cubed. The prices also have to follow a certain order, where A is less than or equal to B, B is less than or equal to C, and C is less than or equal to D. 
The goal is to find the prices of the four items, which are the values of A, B, C, and D.
