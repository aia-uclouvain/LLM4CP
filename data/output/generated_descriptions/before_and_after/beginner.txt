Before and After puzzle
The problem is about four kids - Alice, Boris, Claire, and David - who have different numbers of jellybeans. They give some of their jellybeans to each other in a specific order, and after all the transfers, they again have different numbers of jellybeans. 
The main variables are the number of jellybeans each kid has at the start and after each transfer, and the number of jellybeans given in each transfer. In plain language, these variables mean: 
- x[t,i] is the number of jellybeans kid i has at time t (at the start, after the first transfer, and after the second transfer)
- gifts[j] is the number of jellybeans given in the jth transfer
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are: 
- Each kid starts with a different number of jellybeans between 2 and 9
- The kids give each other jellybeans in a specific order (David to Alice, Claire to Boris, Boris to Alice, David to Claire)
- The number of jellybeans given in each transfer is different and less than 7
- After all the transfers, each kid has a different number of jellybeans between 2 and 9, and no kid has the same number as they did at the start
The goal of the problem is to find out how many jellybeans each kid has at the start and after each transfer, given the rules and restrictions. Specifically, the problem wants to find the values of x[t,i] and gifts[j] that satisfy all the constraints.
