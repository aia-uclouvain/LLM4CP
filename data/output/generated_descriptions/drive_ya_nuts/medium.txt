Drive Ya Nuts puzzle
The Drive Ya Nuts puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem where the goal is to place seven nuts in a specific arrangement such that the numbers on each side of a nut match the numbers on the adjoining nut.
The main decision variables are: 
- `x`: a 2D array representing the final solution, where `x[i, j]` is the number on the `j-th` side of the `i-th` nut.
- `pos`: an array representing the permutation of nuts, where `pos[i]` is the index of the nut at position `i`.
- `start_ix`: an array representing the starting index for each nut, where `start_ix[i]` is the starting index for the `i-th` nut.
The key constraints are:
- Each nut has a unique set of numbers on its sides.
- The numbers on each side of a nut must match the numbers on the adjoining nut.
- The center nut (nut 7) starts with the number 1, which serves as a symmetry-breaking constraint.
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of nuts and their rotations such that all constraints are met, with no specific minimization or maximization objective. The goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
