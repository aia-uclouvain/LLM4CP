name: Discrete Lot Sizing Problem

description: The Discrete Lot Sizing Problem is a production planning problem that involves determining a minimum cost production schedule to satisfy demands for multiple items without exceeding machine capacities. The problem considers a single machine with limited production capacity, sequence-dependent changeover costs, and storage costs. The goal is to find a production schedule that minimizes the sum of stocking costs and changeover costs while satisfying all demands.

variables: $d_{it}, x_{ij}, s_i$
$d_{it}$ represents the date of production for item $i$ at time $t$, $x_{ij}$ represents the successor of item $i$ in the production sequence, and $s_i$ represents the storage cost for item $i$. These decision variables determine the production schedule and the order in which items are produced.

constraints: $\sum_{t=0}^{T} d_{it} \leq 1, \forall i$, $x_{ij} \in \{0,1\}, \forall i,j$, $\sum_{j=0}^{n} x_{ij} = 1, \forall i$, $d_{it} \geq 0, \forall i,t$, $s_i \geq 0, \forall i$
These constraints ensure that each item is produced at most once, the production sequence is valid, and the storage costs are non-negative. The constraints also enforce the limited production capacity and the sequence-dependent changeover costs.

objective: Minimize $\sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{t=0}^{T} s_i \cdot (d_{it} - t) + \sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} c_{ij} \cdot x_{ij}$
The objective is to minimize the total cost, which includes the storage costs and the changeover costs. The storage costs are proportional to the number of days between the production date and the demand time, and the changeover costs are sequence-dependent.
