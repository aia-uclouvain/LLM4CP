**Discrete Lot Sizing Problem**
The Discrete Lot Sizing Problem is a classic problem in production planning and scheduling. It involves determining a minimum cost production schedule to satisfy demands for multiple items without exceeding machine capacities.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- **Production dates**: The date at which each item is produced, represented by the `date` variable.
- **Successors**: The successor of each production, representing the next item to be produced, denoted by the `successors` variable.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- **Production before demand**: Each item must be produced before its demand date.
- **Capacity constraint**: Only one item can be produced per period.
- **Changeover costs**: There are sequence-dependent changeover costs when switching from producing one item to another.
- **Stocking costs**: There are costs associated with holding inventory, which are proportional to the number of days between the production date and the demand date.

**Objective**
The objective of the Discrete Lot Sizing Problem is to minimize the sum of stocking costs and changeover costs. This is achieved by finding the optimal production schedule that satisfies all demands while minimizing the total cost. The objective function is represented by the `obj` variable, which is the sum of the stocking costs and the transition costs.
