Discrete Lot Sizing Problem
The problem is about planning the production of items in a factory to meet customer demands while minimizing costs. 
The main variables are: 
- production dates: when each item should be produced
- item demands: how many of each item are needed and by when
- changeover costs: the cost of switching from producing one item to another
- stocking costs: the cost of storing items before they are needed
The basic restrictions are: 
- each item must be produced before its demand date
- the factory can only produce one item at a time
- there are costs associated with switching between items and storing items
The goal is to minimize the total cost, which includes the cost of switching between items and the cost of storing items before they are needed.
