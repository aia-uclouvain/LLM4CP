name: Ola's Kraftwerk Problem
description: The problem is about a group of people who want to buy tickets to a series of concerts, with certain constraints on the number of tickets they can buy and the number of concerts they can attend. Each person has a preference for each concert, and the goal is to maximize the total happiness of all people by attending the most preferred concerts.
variables: $buy\_concert_p, buy\_num\_tickets_p, attending_{p,c}$ where $p$ represents each person and $c$ represents each concert. These variables represent the decision variables in the problem: $buy\_concert_p$ is the concert that person $p$ buys tickets for, $buy\_num\_tickets_p$ is the number of tickets person $p$ buys, and $attending_{p,c}$ is a binary variable indicating whether person $p$ attends concert $c$.
constraints: $\forall p, buy\_num\_tickets_p \leq attending\_budget_p$, $\forall c, \sum_{p} attending_{p,c} = \sum_{p} buy\_num\_tickets_p \* (buy\_concert_p == c)$, $\forall p, buy\_num\_tickets_p = \sum_{c} attending_{p,c}$, $alldifferent(buy\_concert_p)$. These constraints represent the limitations on the number of tickets each person can buy, the relationship between the number of people attending a concert and the number of tickets bought for that concert, the requirement that each person attends the same number of concerts as they buy tickets for, and the constraint that each person can only buy tickets for one concert.
objective: Maximize $z = \sum_{p,c} prefs_{p,c} \* attending_{p,c}$, where $prefs_{p,c}$ is the preference of person $p$ for concert $c$. The objective is to maximize the total happiness of all people by attending the most preferred concerts.
