The Third Trial puzzle
The problem is about finding the arrangement of a lady and a tiger behind two doors, based on given statements about their locations.
The main decision variables are x(i,j) and t(i), where x(i,j) represents whether door i hides prize j (1 for the lady and 2 for the tiger), and t(i) represents whether the statement on door i is true.
The key constraints are: each door hides one prize, the statement on door 1 is true if the lady is behind door 2 or the tiger is behind door 1, the statement on door 2 is true if the lady is behind door 1, and either both statements are true or both are false.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, such that all constraints are met, which is achieved by using the "solve satisfy" statement in the Minizinc model.
