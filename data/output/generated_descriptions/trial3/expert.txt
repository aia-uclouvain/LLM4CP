name: The Third Trial (Smullyan) puzzle

description: The problem is a classic puzzle where there are two doors, behind one of which is a lady and behind the other is a tiger. The doors have statements on them, and the goal is to figure out which door hides the lady and which door hides the tiger based on the statements. The statement on door 1 is "The lady is behind the other door" and the statement on door 2 is "The lady is behind this door". The puzzle requires finding the correct assignment of the lady and the tiger to the doors such that the statements on the doors are consistent.

variables: $x_{i,j}, t_i$ where $i \in \{1,2\}$ and $j \in \{1,2\}$, $x_{i,j}$ represents whether door $i$ hides prize $j$ (1 = Lady, 2 = Tiger), and $t_i$ represents whether the statement on door $i$ is true.

constraints: $\forall i \in \{1,2\}: \sum_{j=1}^2 x_{i,j} = 1$, $x_{1,2} + x_{2,1} - t_1 \leq 0$, $x_{1,2} + x_{2,1} - t_1 \geq 0$, $t_2 = x_{1,1}$, $t_1 = t_2$, which ensure that each door hides exactly one prize, the statements on the doors are consistent, and either both statements are true or both are false.

objective: satisfy
