Problem name: Minimum and Maximum Values in a Set

This problem is about finding the smallest and largest numbers in a group of numbers. Imagine you have a bunch of numbers in a box, and you want to know what the smallest and largest numbers are.

The main variables are: 
- S, which is the group of numbers (like the numbers in the box)
- mn, which is the smallest number in the group
- mx, which is the largest number in the group

The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- The group of numbers (S) must have exactly 4 numbers.
- The smallest number (mn) must be one of the numbers in the group, and it must be smaller than or equal to all the other numbers in the group.
- The largest number (mx) must be one of the numbers in the group, and it must be larger than or equal to all the other numbers in the group.

The goal of the problem is to find a group of 4 numbers that satisfies these rules, and then identify the smallest and largest numbers in that group. The problem doesn't try to minimize or maximize anything, it just tries to find a solution that works.
