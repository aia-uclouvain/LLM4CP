name: Non-Dominating Queens Problem
description: The Non-Dominating Queens Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming where the goal is to place a certain number of queens on a chessboard such that no queen attacks any other queen, and the number of squares that are either occupied or attacked by a queen is minimized. In this problem, a queen attacks a square if the square is in the same row, column, or diagonal as the queen.
variables: $x_{i,j}, a_{i,j}$ where $i,j \in S$, $x_{i,j}$ is a binary variable that represents whether square $(i,j)$ is occupied by a queen, and $a_{i,j}$ is a binary variable that represents whether square $(i,j)$ is attacked or occupied by a queen. 
constraints: $\sum_{i,j \in S} x_{i,j} = size$, $\forall i,j \in S: (\sum_{m \in S, m \neq i, m-i+j \geq 1, m-i+j \leq size} x_{m,m-i+j} + \sum_{m \in S, m \neq i, i+j-m \geq 1, i+j-m \leq size} x_{m,i+j-m} + \sum_{m \in S} x_{m,j} + \sum_{m \in S, m \neq j} x_{i,m} + x_{i,j}) \leq size \cdot a_{i,j}$, $\forall i,j \in S: (\sum_{m \in S, m \neq i, m-i+j \geq 1, m-i+j \leq size} x_{m,m-i+j} + \sum_{m \in S, m \neq i, i+j-m \geq 1, i+j-m \leq size} x_{m,i+j-m} + \sum_{m \in S} x_{m,j} + \sum_{m \in S, m \neq j} x_{i,m} + x_{i,j}) \geq a_{i,j}$. These constraints ensure that the number of queens placed equals the size of the board, and that a square is attacked or occupied if and only if it is in the same row, column, or diagonal as a queen, or if it is occupied by a queen.
objective: minimize $\sum_{i,j \in S} a_{i,j}$, which represents the total number of squares that are either occupied or attacked by a queen.
