Freight Transfer Problem
The Freight Transfer Problem is about transporting a certain amount of freight using a fleet of trucks with different capacities and costs, with the goal of minimizing the total cost.

The main decision variables are `x[i]`, which represent the number of trucks of each size `i` to use. The `x` array has `n` elements, where `n` is the number of different truck sizes available.

The key constraints are: 
- the total amount of freight transported must be greater than or equal to the target amount (`transported >= transport`), 
- the number of trucks of each size used cannot exceed the number of trucks of that size available (`x[i] <= available[i]`).

The problem's objective is to minimize the total cost `z`, which is the sum of the costs of all trucks used, calculated as `z = sum(i in 1..n) (x[i]*cost[i])`. In other words, the goal is to find the cheapest way to transport the required amount of freight using the available trucks.
