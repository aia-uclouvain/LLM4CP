Bus Scheduling Problem
The problem is about scheduling buses to meet the demand for transportation at different time slots while minimizing the total number of buses used.

The main decision variables are `x[i]`, which represent the number of buses that start their schedule at time slot `i`. The variable `z` represents the total number of buses used.

The key constraints are: 
- Each time slot must have at least 0 buses scheduled.
- The total number of buses scheduled for the current and next time slot must meet the demand for the current time slot.
- The last time slot's demand must be met by buses scheduled for the last and first time slots, ensuring the schedule works "around the clock".

The objective of the problem is to minimize the total number of buses used, represented by the variable `z`. This is achieved by finding the optimal schedule that meets the demand at each time slot while using the fewest buses possible.
