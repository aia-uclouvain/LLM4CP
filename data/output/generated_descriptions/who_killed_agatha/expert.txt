name: The Dreadsbury Mansion Murder Mystery

description: The problem is a classic logic puzzle where someone in Dreadsbury Mansion killed Aunt Agatha. The puzzle states that Agatha, the butler, and Charles are the only ones living in the mansion. A killer always hates and is no richer than their victim. The puzzle provides additional information about the relationships between the characters, such as who hates whom and who is richer than whom. The goal is to determine who killed Agatha.

variables: $the\_killer \in \{1, 2, 3\}, the\_victim \in \{1, 2, 3\}, hates \in \{0, 1\}^{3 \times 3}, richer \in \{0, 1\}^{3 \times 3}$ 
These variables represent the decision variables in the problem: $the\_killer$ and $the\_victim$ are the person who killed Agatha and the victim, respectively, $hates$ is a matrix where $hates_{i, j} = 1$ if person $i$ hates person $j$, and $richer$ is a matrix where $richer_{i, j} = 1$ if person $i$ is richer than person $j$.

constraints: 
$hates[the\_killer, the\_victim] = 1 \land richer[the\_killer, the\_victim] = 0, 
richer_{i, i} = 0 \forall i, 
richer_{i, j} = 1 \leftrightarrow richer_{j, i} = 0 \forall i \neq j, 
hates_{charles, i} = 0 \leftarrow hates_{agatha, i} = 1 \forall i, 
hates_{agatha, charles} = 1, 
hates_{agatha, agatha} = 1, 
hates_{agatha, butler} = 0, 
hates_{butler, i} = 1 \leftarrow richer_{i, agatha} = 0 \forall i, 
hates_{butler, i} = 1 \leftarrow hates_{agatha, i} = 1 \forall i, 
\sum_{j} hates_{i, j} \leq 2 \forall i, 
the\_victim = agatha$ 
These constraints represent the rules of the puzzle: a killer hates their victim and is not richer, the concept of richer is defined, Charles' hatred is defined, Agatha's hatred is defined, the butler's hatred is defined, no one hates everyone, and the victim is Agatha.

objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, which will determine who killed Agatha.
