name: N-Queens Problem
description: The N-Queens problem is a classic problem in the field of computer science and chess, where the goal is to place N queens on an NxN chessboard such that no two queens attack each other, meaning no two queens are in the same row, column, or diagonal.
variables: $q_1, q_2, ..., q_n$, where $q_i$ represents the column index of the queen placed in row $i$. These variables are decision variables that determine the positions of the queens on the chessboard.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}, q_i \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}$, $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}, i \neq j, q_i \neq q_j$, $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}, i \neq j, q_i + i \neq q_j + j$, $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}, i \neq j, q_i - i \neq q_j - j$. These constraints ensure that each queen is placed in a valid column, no two queens are in the same column, and no two queens are on the same diagonal.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding a way to place N queens on an NxN chessboard such that no two queens attack each other. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
