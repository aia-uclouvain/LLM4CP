name: Are You The One? Problem
description: The problem is based on a reality TV show where a group of men and women are secretly paired into male-female couples via a matchmaking algorithm. The contestants try to identify all of these "perfect matches" by choosing one couple to go to the "truth booth" and asking if that couple is a perfect match or not, and by pairing up in a "matching ceremony" to get to know how many couples they got right. The goal is to find all possible solutions (couples matches) that satisfy certain constraints, such as the number of correct matches in each ceremony and the outcome of the truth booth.
variables: $assign_{b,g}$, where $b \in boys$ and $g \in girls$. These are decision variables that represent whether boy $b$ is assigned to girl $g$ in the matching, with $assign_{b,g} = 1$ if they are assigned together and $assign_{b,g} = 0$ otherwise.
constraints: 
$\forall b \in boys: \sum_{g \in girls} assign_{b,g} = 1$
$\forall g \in girls: \sum_{b \in boys} assign_{b,g} = 1$
$\forall w \in 1..num\_weeks: \sum_{b \in 1..num\_boys} assign_{pairs[w,b,2],pairs[w,b,3]} = correct\_matches[w]$
$\forall w \in 1..num\_weeks: assign_{truth\_booth[w,1],truth\_booth[w,2]} = truth\_booth[w,3]$. 
These constraints ensure that each boy is assigned to exactly one girl, each girl is assigned to exactly one boy, the number of correct matches in each ceremony is satisfied, and the outcome of the truth booth is satisfied.
