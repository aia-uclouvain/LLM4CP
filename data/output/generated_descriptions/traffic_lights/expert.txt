name: Traffic Lights Problem
description: The Traffic Lights Problem is a classic constraint satisfaction problem that involves finding a valid configuration of traffic lights at a four-way junction. The junction has eight traffic lights, four for vehicles and four for pedestrians, and the goal is to find an assignment of colors (red, red-yellow, green, and yellow for vehicles, and red and green for pedestrians) that satisfies certain safety constraints.
variables: $V_1, V_2, V_3, V_4 \in \{r, ry, g, y\}$ and $P_1, P_2, P_3, P_4 \in \{r, g\}$, where $V_i$ represents the color of the traffic light for vehicles at junction $i$ and $P_i$ represents the color of the traffic light for pedestrians at junction $i$.
constraints: $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$ where $j = (1+i) \mod 4$, $(V_i, P_i, V_j, P_j) \in \{(r, r, g, g), (ry, r, y, r), (g, g, r, r), (y, r, ry, r)\}$, which ensures that the traffic lights are configured in a safe and valid way.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, i.e., to find a valid configuration of traffic lights that satisfies all the constraints. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that meets all the safety constraints.
