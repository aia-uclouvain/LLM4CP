name: Full Adder Problem
description: The Full Adder Problem is a classical problem in digital electronics and computer science, which involves designing a digital circuit that can add three binary digits (bits) and produce a two-bit output, consisting of a sum and a carry. The problem is modeled using Boolean variables and logical operations to represent the behavior of the full adder circuit.
variables: $I1, I2, I3, O1, O2$ - These are Boolean decision variables representing the input bits ($I1$, $I2$, $I3$) and output bits ($O1$, $O2$) of the full adder circuit, where $I1$ and $I2$ are the two input bits to be added, $I3$ is the carry-in bit, $O1$ is the sum bit, and $O2$ is the carry-out bit.
constraints: $X1 = I1 \oplus I2$, $Y1 = I1 \land I2$, $O1 = X1 \oplus I3$, $Y2 = I3 \land X1$, $O2 = Y1 \lor Y2$, $I3 = \text{false}$, $O2 = \text{true}$ - These constraints represent the logical operations performed by the full adder circuit, including the XOR and AND operations, as well as the given conditions for the input and output bits.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
