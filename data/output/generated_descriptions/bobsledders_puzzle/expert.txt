name: Bobsledders Puzzle
description: The problem involves determining the partners, countries, and sled colors of 10 bobsledders, given certain constraints and hints about their relationships and preferences. The goal is to find a consistent assignment of partners, countries, and sled colors that satisfies all the given conditions.
variables: $pairs_{1..10}, countries_{1..5}, sleds_{1..5}, partners_{1..10}$ - These decision variables represent the pair assignments of the bobsledders, the country of each pair, the sled color of each pair, and the partner of each bobsledder, respectively.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..5: countries_i \neq countries_j \wedge sleds_i \neq sleds_j \wedge partners_i \neq partners_j, \forall i,j \in 1..10: pairs_i = pairs_j \leftrightarrow partners_i = j \wedge partners_j = i, \forall i \in 1..5: \sum_{j=1}^{10} (pairs_j = i) = 2, pairs_{Betsy} = pairs_{Phil} \wedge sleds_{pairs_{Betsy}} = green, \forall i \in 1..5: sleds_i \neq red \vee countries_i \neq Romanian, sleds_{pairs_{Bobby}} \neq black, countries_{pairs_{Phil}} \neq Canada \wedge countries_{pairs_{Phil}} \neq Romanian, countries_{pairs_{Connie}} = Germany \wedge sleds_{pairs_{Connie}} \neq red \wedge sleds_{pairs_{Connie}} \neq blue, sleds_{pairs_{Ted}} = white \wedge countries_{pairs_{Ted}} \neq USA \wedge countries_{pairs_{Ted}} \neq Germany \wedge countries_{pairs_{Ted}} \neq Romanian, countries_{pairs_{Lulu}} = USA, sleds_{pairs_{Jane}} \neq blue, countries_{pairs_{Lance}} \neq Germany \wedge sleds_{pairs_{Lance}} \neq red$ - These constraints ensure that each country and sled color is assigned to at most one pair, each pair has exactly two bobsledders, and the given hints and conditions are satisfied.
objective: The objective of this problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, as there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize. The goal is to determine the partners, countries, and sled colors of the bobsledders in a way that is consistent with the given information.
