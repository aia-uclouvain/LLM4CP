**One Repetition Maximum (1RM) Problem**

The One Repetition Maximum (1RM) problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves calculating the maximum weight a person can lift for a single repetition, given the weight and number of repetitions they can lift for a specific exercise. The problem also considers age adjustments to the weights.

**Decision Variables**

The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `weight1` and `weight2`: the weights lifted for two different exercises or sets of repetitions.
- `reps1` and `reps2`: the number of repetitions performed for each exercise or set.
- `oneRM`: the one repetition maximum weight, which is the maximum weight that can be lifted for a single repetition.
- `weight1Age`, `weight2Age`, and `oneRMAge`: the age-adjusted weights for each exercise or set.

**Constraints**

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The relationship between the weight lifted, the number of repetitions, and the one repetition maximum, which is defined by a formula such as Brzycki's formula: `oneRM = weight * (36/(37 - reps))`.
- The age adjustment coefficients, which are used to adjust the weights based on the person's age.
- The constraints that define the relationships between the weights, repetitions, and one repetition maximum for each exercise or set.

**Objective**

The objective of this problem is to calculate the one repetition maximum weight (`oneRM`) and the age-adjusted weights (`weight1Age`, `weight2Age`, and `oneRMAge`) given the weights and repetitions for two different exercises or sets. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function, but rather finding a solution that satisfies the constraints and calculates the desired values.
