name: One Repetition Maximum (1RM) Calculation Problem
description: The One Repetition Maximum (1RM) Calculation Problem is a problem in the field of weight training and fitness, where the goal is to calculate the maximum weight that can be lifted for one repetition, given the weight and number of repetitions that can be lifted for a certain exercise. The problem also involves age adjustment of the calculated weights.
variables: $weight1, weight2, reps1, reps2, oneRM, weight1Age, weight2Age, oneRMAge$ - These variables represent the weights and number of repetitions for two different exercises ($weight1$ and $weight2$, $reps1$ and $reps2$), the calculated one repetition maximum ($oneRM$), and the age-adjusted weights ($weight1Age$, $weight2Age$, $oneRMAge$).
constraints: $oneRM = weight1 * (36/(37 - reps1))$, $oneRM = weight2 * (36/(37 - reps2))$, $weight1Age = weight1*AgeCoeffs[age-30+1]$, $weight2Age = weight2*AgeCoeffs[age-30+1]$, $oneRMAge = oneRM*AgeCoeffs[age-30+1]$ - These constraints represent the relationships between the weights, repetitions, and one repetition maximum, as well as the age adjustment of the weights using the provided age coefficients.
objective: The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to calculate the one repetition maximum ($oneRM$) and age-adjusted weights ($weight1Age$, $weight2Age$, $oneRMAge$) given the weights and repetitions for two different exercises.
