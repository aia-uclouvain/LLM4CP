The Zoo, Buses, and Kids problem. 
This problem is about finding the most cost-effective way to transport 300 kids to the London zoo by renting buses of two different sizes, with 30 and 40 seats, at prices of 400 and 500 pounds respectively. 
The main decision variables are the number of buses of each size, represented by the `buses` array, where `buses[1]` is the number of 30-seat buses and `buses[2]` is the number of 40-seat buses. 
The key constraint is that the total number of seats on all the buses must be at least 300, to accommodate all the kids. 
The objective is to minimize the total cost, which is the sum of the costs of all the buses, represented by the variable `z`.
