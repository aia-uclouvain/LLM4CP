Kidney Exchange Problem
The Kidney Exchange Problem is about finding the maximum number of possible kidney exchanges between a group of people, where each person can donate a kidney to someone else, but only if they are compatible.

The main decision variables in this problem are represented by the array `x`, where `x[i]` represents the person who receives a kidney from person `i`. If `x[i]` is 0, it means person `i` does not receive a kidney. The variable `z` represents the total number of people who receive a kidney.

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- Each person can only receive a kidney from one other person, or not receive a kidney at all.
- A person can only receive a kidney from someone who is compatible with them, as defined by the `compatible` array.
- If a person receives a kidney, the person who donates the kidney must also receive a kidney, to ensure fairness and maximize the number of exchanges.

The objective of the Kidney Exchange Problem is to maximize the number of people who receive a kidney, which is represented by the variable `z`. The goal is to find the largest possible set of exchanges where everyone who donates a kidney also receives a kidney, while respecting the compatibility constraints.
