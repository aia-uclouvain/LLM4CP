name: Enigma 1293 - Reverse Fahrenheit
description: The problem is about finding two temperature equivalents in Celsius and Fahrenheit, where the conversion between the two scales can be done by simply reversing the digits of the temperatures when expressed in a certain base. The goal is to find two such examples where the Celsius temperature lies between 0 and 100 degrees.
variables: $C, Base, D1, D2, F$ - These variables represent the Celsius temperature ($C$), the base in which the temperatures are expressed ($Base$), and the two digits of the temperatures in the given base ($D1$ and $D2$), as well as the corresponding Fahrenheit temperature ($F$).
constraints: $5*F = 9*C + 160, D1 \geq 0, D1 < Base, D2 \geq 0, D2 < Base, C = Base*D1 + D2, F = Base*D2 + D1$ - These constraints represent the standard Celsius to Fahrenheit conversion rule, the bounds on the digits $D1$ and $D2$ in the given base, and the relationships between the temperatures $C$ and $F$ and their digit representations $D1$ and $D2$ in the given base.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
