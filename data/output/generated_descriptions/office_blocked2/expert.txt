name: Office Blocked problem

description: The Office Blocked problem is a classic constraint programming problem where six employees (Arthur, Bella, Craig, Duncan, Elizabeth, and Francesca) are assigned to offices on two floors of a building, with three offices on each floor. The problem provides several constraints about the relative positions of the employees' offices, and the goal is to determine the office assignment for each employee.

variables: $floor = [floor_1, floor_2, ..., floor_6], room = [room_1, room_2, ..., room_6]$ 
These are decision variables representing the floor and room assignments for each of the six employees, where $floor_i$ is the floor number (1 or 2) and $room_i$ is the room number (1, 2, or 3) assigned to employee $i$.

constraints: 
$\begin{aligned}
& floor_{arthur} = 2 \land floor_{bella} = 1 \land room_{arthur} = room_{bella} \\
& floor_{bella} = floor_{duncan} \land |room_{bella} - room_{duncan}| = 1 \\
& room_{elizabeth} < room_{francesca} \\
& floor_{craig} = floor_{francesca} \land |room_{craig} - room_{francesca}| = 1 \\
& \text{all\_different}([(floor_i-1) \cdot 3 + room_i | i \in 1..6])
\end{aligned}$
These constraints represent the given conditions: Arthur is directly above Bella, who works next to Duncan; Elizabeth's office number is smaller than Francesca's; Craig and Francesca are in adjacent rooms; and each office is assigned to at most one employee.

objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of offices to employees that meets all the given constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
