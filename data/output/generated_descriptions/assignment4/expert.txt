name: Assignment Problem
description: The assignment problem is a classic problem in computer science and operations research that involves finding the optimal assignment between two sets of entities, A and B, such that each entity in A is assigned to exactly one entity in B and vice versa, while satisfying certain constraints.
variables: $A_1, A_2, A_3, A_4, A_5, B_1, B_2, B_3, B_4, B_5, x_{i,j}$ where $i \in \{1,2,...,n\}$ and $j \in \{1,2\}$. These variables represent the values of the entities in sets A and B, and the assignment of entities in A to entities in B, where $x_{i,1}$ represents the index of the entity in A and $x_{i,2}$ represents the index of the entity in B.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1,2,...,n\} (x_{i,1} = i \wedge A_{x_{i,1}} = B_{x_{i,2}}) \wedge \forall j \in \{1,2\} (all\_different([x_{i,j} | i \in \{1,2,...,n\}]))$. These constraints ensure that each entity in A is assigned to exactly one entity in B, and that the assignment is consistent, i.e., the index of the entity in A is equal to $i$, and the value of the entity in A is equal to the value of the entity in B. The $all\_different$ constraint ensures that each entity in A and B is assigned to at most one other entity.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible assignment that satisfies all the constraints, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
