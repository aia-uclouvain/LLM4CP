**Maximum Independent Set Problem**

The Maximum Independent Set Problem is a classic problem in graph theory and computer science. It involves finding the largest subset of vertices in a graph such that no two vertices in the subset are connected by an edge.

**Decision Variables**

The main decision variable in this problem is `x`, an array of binary variables where `x[i]` represents whether vertex `i` is included in the independent set (1) or not (0).

**Constraints**

The key constraints in this problem are:

* For each edge in the graph, the sum of the `x` values of the two vertices connected by the edge must be less than or equal to 1. This ensures that if an edge exists between two vertices, at most one of them can be included in the independent set.
* The `x` values are binary, meaning they can only take on the values 0 or 1.

**Objective**

The objective of the problem is to maximize the cardinality of the independent set, which is represented by the variable `obj`. This is calculated as the sum of all `x` values. In other words, the goal is to find the largest possible subset of vertices that are pairwise non-adjacent.

In plain language, the problem is trying to find the largest group of vertices in the graph such that no two vertices in the group are directly connected. This has applications in various fields, including computer networks, social networks, and scheduling.
