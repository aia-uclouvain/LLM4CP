name: Tripuzzle
description: The Tripuzzle problem is a combinatorial puzzle where the goal is to arrange a set of triangular pieces into a larger triangle, such that the colors on the edges of the pieces match. Each piece has three edges, and each edge has a specific color. The larger triangle has three sides, and each side has a specific color sequence that must be matched by the edges of the pieces. The problem requires finding an arrangement of the pieces that satisfies these color matching constraints.

variables: $x_{i,j,k}, y_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j,k}$ represents whether piece $i$ is placed at position $j$ in orientation $k$, and $y_{i,j}$ represents the color of the $j$th edge of the piece at position $i$. These variables determine the placement and orientation of each piece in the larger triangle.

constraints: $\forall i \in P, j \in S: y_{i,j} \geq 0$, $\forall j \in P: \sum_{i \in P, k \in S} x_{i,j,k} = 1$, $\forall i \in P: \sum_{j \in P, k \in S} x_{i,j,k} = 1$, $\forall i \in P, j \in P, k \in S, m \in S: y_{j,m} \geq piece\_col_{i,k,m} - color \cdot (1 - x_{i,j,k})$ and $y_{j,m} \leq piece\_col_{i,k,m} + color \cdot (1 - x_{i,j,k})$, $\forall i \in N: y_{(i-1)^2+1,1} = left_i$, $y_{i^2,2} = right_i$, $y_{(n-1)^2+2(i-1)+1,3} = base_i$, $\forall i \in 1..n-1, j \in 1..i: y_{-2i+2j+i^2,3} = y_{2j+i^2,1}$, $y_{-8i+6j+i^2+j^2-2ij+16,2} = y_{-8i+6j+i^2+j^2-2ij+17,3}$, $y_{-8i+6j+i^2+j^2-2ij+17,2} = y_{-8i+6j+i^2+j^2-2ij+18,1}$. These constraints ensure that each position is occupied by one piece in one orientation, each piece is placed in one position and one orientation, the colors of the edges of the pieces match the required colors, and the colors of the edges of adjacent pieces match.

objective: satisfy, meaning that the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than optimizing a specific objective function.
