Magic Square Problem
The Magic Square problem is about filling a square grid with distinct numbers, such that the sum of numbers in each row, column, and diagonal is the same.
The main decision variables in this problem are the values of the grid cells, represented by variables X1 to X16, which can take any integer value from 1 to 16.
The key constraints are: 
- each number can only be used once (all numbers are distinct), 
- the sum of numbers in each row is 34, 
- the sum of numbers in each column is 34, and 
- the sum of numbers in the two diagonals is 34.
The objective of the Magic Square problem is to find an assignment of numbers to the grid cells that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding a valid magic square configuration, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
