Reversed Cryptogram Problem
The Reversed Cryptogram Problem is about finding the minimum construction of a sequence of digits, where the sequence and its reverse have distinct digits, and their sum is also a sequence with distinct digits.
The main decision variables are x and z, where x represents the original sequence of digits and z represents the sum of x and its reverse. The variable vx represents the numerical value of x, and vz represents the numerical value of z.
The key constraints are: 
- all digits in x are distinct, 
- all digits in z are distinct, 
- the sum of x and its reverse equals z, 
- the first and last digits of x are non-zero, and 
- the first digit of x is less than the last digit of x (symmetry breaking constraint).
The objective of the problem is to find the minimum construction of x that satisfies all the constraints, which is equivalent to finding the smallest possible numerical value of x.
