Donald Puzzle
The Donald puzzle is a classic alphametic problem where the goal is to find the numerical values of letters in an equation such that the equation holds true.
The main decision variables in this problem are the letters D, O, N, A, L, G, E, R, B, and T, each representing a digit from 0 to 9.
The key constraints are that each letter must represent a unique digit, and the equation DONALD + GERALD = ROBERT must be satisfied when the letters are replaced with their corresponding numerical values. Additionally, the letters D, G, and R must not be zero, as they are the first letters of the names and therefore represent the most significant digits.
The objective of this problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a satisfying assignment of digits to letters such that the equation holds true, which is achieved by using the "solve satisfy" statement in the Minizinc model.
