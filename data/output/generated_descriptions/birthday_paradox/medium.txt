The Birthday Problem
The Birthday Problem is about finding the probability that at least two people in a group of randomly chosen people share the same birthday. 
The main decision variables in this problem are `prob_no_dup` and `prob_dup`, which represent the probability of no duplicate birthdays and the probability of at least one duplicate birthday, respectively, for each number of people from 1 to `p`.
The key constraints are that the probability of no duplicate birthdays for the first person is 1 (since there's no one to share a birthday with), and for each subsequent person, the probability of no duplicate birthdays is the previous probability multiplied by the fraction of remaining days in the year that don't match any previous birthdays. The probability of duplicates is simply 1 minus the probability of no duplicates.
The objective of the problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to calculate the probability of duplicate birthdays for a given number of people, with the goal of satisfying the constraints that define these probabilities. In other words, the problem aims to determine the probability that at least two people in a group share the same birthday, given the number of people in the group.
