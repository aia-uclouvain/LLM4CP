name: Birthday Paradox Problem
description: The Birthday Paradox Problem is a classic problem in probability theory that deals with the probability of at least two people sharing the same birthday in a group of randomly chosen people. The problem states that in a group of 23 or more people, there is more than 50% probability that some pair of them will have been born on the same day of the year, and this probability tends towards 100% as the number of people grows.
variables: $prob\_no\_dup_i, prob\_dup_i$ for $i = 1..p$ 
These are arrays of decision variables representing the probability of no duplicate birthdays and the probability of at least one duplicate birthday for each number of people from 1 to $p$, respectively.
constraints: $prob\_no\_dup_1 = 1.0$, $prob\_no\_dup_i = prob\_no\_dup_{i-1} \* \frac{n-i+1}{n}$ for $i = 2..p$, $prob\_dup_i = 1.0 - prob\_no\_dup_i$ for $i = 1..p$ 
These constraints calculate the probability of no duplicate birthdays for each number of people and then use this to calculate the probability of at least one duplicate birthday. The first constraint initializes the probability of no duplicate birthdays for one person to 1.0. The second constraint calculates the probability of no duplicate birthdays for $i$ people based on the probability for $i-1$ people. The third constraint calculates the probability of at least one duplicate birthday as 1 minus the probability of no duplicate birthdays.
objective: The objective of this problem is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to calculate the probability of at least one duplicate birthday for a given number of people, which is a satisfaction problem.
