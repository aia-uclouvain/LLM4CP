name: Enigma Number 1555 (Not a square)

description: The problem is to fill a 3x4 grid with digits such that the two four-digit numbers that can be read down, the two two-digit numbers that can be read down at the sides, and the two-digit number that can be read across at the bottom are all different perfect squares, while the two-digit number that can be read across at the top is not a perfect square.

variables: A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K, L, AB 
These are decision variables representing the digits in the grid, where A and B form the top two-digit number, C, D, E, and F form the first four-digit number, G, H, I, and J form the second four-digit number, and K and L form the bottom two-digit number.

constraints: 
A > 0, C > 0, G > 0, K > 0, B > 0, F > 0, 
square(C*1000+D*100+E*10+F), square(G*1000+H*100+I*10+J), 
square(C*10+G), square(F*10+J), square(K*10+L), 
square(A*1000+D*100+H*10+K), square(B*1000+E*100+I*10+L), 
not exists(tmp in 1..10) (tmp*tmp = AB)
These constraints ensure that the leading digits are non-zero, the numbers formed by the digits are perfect squares, and the top two-digit number is not a perfect square.

objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, with no specific value to minimize or maximize.
