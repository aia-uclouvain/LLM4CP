The problem is called "Not a square" (Enigma Number 1555). 
It involves filling a 12-cell grid with digits so that certain combinations of these digits form perfect squares. 
The main decision variables are A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K, and L, which represent the digits to be placed in the grid. 
The key constraints are that the two four-digit numbers, two two-digit numbers at the sides, and the two-digit number at the bottom must all be different perfect squares, with no leading zeros. 
The objective is to find the two-digit number at the top (represented by AB) that is not a perfect square, while satisfying all the constraints related to perfect squares.
