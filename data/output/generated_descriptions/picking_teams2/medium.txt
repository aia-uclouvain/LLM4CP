Team Formation Problem
The Team Formation Problem is about dividing a group of people into two teams, with the goal of making the teams as balanced as possible in terms of the total "strength" or ability of each team. 

The main decision variables are:
- `x`: an array of variables representing the team assignment for each person, where `x[i]` is 1 if person `i` is assigned to team 1 and 2 if person `i` is assigned to team 2.
- `d`: a variable representing the difference in total strength between the two teams.

The key constraints are:
- Each team must have the same number of players.
- The first person is assigned to team 1 to break symmetry.
- The difference in total strength between the two teams must be calculated correctly.
- The difference in total strength must be consistent with the total strength of all players.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the difference in total strength between the two teams, `d`. This means that the solver will try to find a team assignment that makes the total strength of the two teams as close as possible.
