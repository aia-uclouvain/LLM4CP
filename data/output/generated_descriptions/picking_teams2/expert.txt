name: Team Formation Problem
description: The problem is about forming two teams from a set of people with different strengths, with the goal of making the teams as balanced as possible. The strength of each person is measured by a single metric, and the objective is to minimize the difference in total strength between the two teams.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_n, d$ where $x_i$ represents the team to which person $i$ is assigned (1 or 2), and $d$ represents the difference in total strength between the two teams. These variables determine the team assignments and the resulting strength difference.
constraints: $n \mod 2 = 0$, $d = | \sum_{i=1}^{n} s_i \cdot \mathbb{1}_{x_i = 1} - \sum_{i=1}^{n} s_i \cdot \mathbb{1}_{x_i = 2} |$, $\sum_{i=1}^{n} \mathbb{1}_{x_i = 1} = \frac{n}{2}$, $x_1 = 1$, $d \mod 2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} s_i \mod 2$ where $s_i$ represents the strength of person $i$, and $\mathbb{1}_{x_i = j}$ is an indicator function that is 1 if $x_i = j$ and 0 otherwise. These constraints ensure that the number of people is even, the difference in strength is calculated correctly, the teams have the same size, the first person is assigned to team 1, and the difference in strength has the same parity as the total strength.
objective: minimize $d$
