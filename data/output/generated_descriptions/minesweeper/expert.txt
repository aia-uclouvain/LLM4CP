name: Minesweeper
description: The Minesweeper problem is a classic puzzle where a rectangular grid is given with some cells containing numbers and others being blank. The numbers represent the number of mines adjacent to that cell, and the blank cells may or may not contain a mine. The goal is to determine the location of all mines in the grid.
variables: $r, c, X, game, mines$ - $r$ and $c$ are the number of rows and columns in the grid, $X$ is a constant representing an unknown cell, $game$ is a 2D array representing the given grid with numbers and unknown cells, and $mines$ is a 2D array of decision variables where $mines[i,j] = 1$ if there is a mine in cell $(i,j)$ and $mines[i,j] = 0$ otherwise.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..r, j \in 1..c: (game[i,j] \geq 0 \rightarrow game[i,j] = \sum_{a,b \in \{-1,0,1\}} mines[i+a,j+b]) \wedge (game[i,j] > X \rightarrow mines[i,j] = 0)$ - These constraints ensure that the number of mines adjacent to each cell matches the given number in the grid, and that cells with a given number cannot contain a mine.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of mines to the grid, such that all constraints are met. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
