name: Rogo Problem
description: The Rogo problem is a constraint programming problem where the goal is to find a loop through a grid of fixed length that contains as many reward points as possible. The grid consists of cells with different point values, and the loop must be constructed by moving horizontally or vertically to adjacent cells. The problem requires finding a path that maximizes the total points collected while satisfying the given constraints.
variables: $x[1..max\_steps], y[1..max\_steps], points[1..max\_steps], sum\_points$ - These decision variables represent the coordinates of the cells in the path ($x$ and $y$), the points collected at each cell ($points$), and the total sum of points collected ($sum\_points$).
constraints: 
$\forall s, t \in 1..max\_steps: x[s] \neq x[t] \vee y[s] \neq y[t]$, 
$alldifferent([(x[i]-1) \* max\_steps + y[i] | i \in 1..max\_steps])$, 
$\forall s \in 1..max\_steps: points[s] = problem[x[s], y[s]]$, 
$\forall s \in 1..max\_steps: problem[x[s], y[s]] \neq B$, 
$\forall s \in 1..max\_steps-1: abs(x[s] - x[s+1]) + abs(y[s] - y[s+1]) = 1$, 
$abs(x[max\_steps] - x[1]) + abs(y[max\_steps] - y[1]) = 1$, 
$table([(x[s]-1) \* cols+y[s], (x[s+1]-1) \* cols+y[s+1]], valid\_connections)$, 
$\forall i \in 2..max\_steps: (x[1]-1) \* cols+y[1] < (x[i]-1) \* cols+y[i]$, 
$(x[1]-1) \* cols+y[1] < (x[2]-1) \* cols+y[2]$ - These constraints ensure that all coordinates in the path are unique, the points collected are calculated correctly, the path only moves to adjacent cells, the path forms a loop, and the path only visits cells with non-negative point values. Additionally, symmetry-breaking constraints are included to reduce the search space.
objective: Maximize $sum\_points$ - The objective of the problem is to maximize the total sum of points collected in the path.
