name: Social Golfer Problem
description: The Social Golfer Problem is a scheduling problem where a group of golfers need to be scheduled to play in groups of a certain size over a number of weeks, such that no two golfers play together more than once.
variables: $g_{w,p}, x_{w,g,p}, tw_{p1,p2,w}, twg_{p1,p2,w,g}, assign_{g,w}, round\_group\_golfers_{r,g}, round\_place\_golfer_{r,p}, golfer\_golfer\_round_{a,b}, round\_group\_i\_golfer_{r,g,i}, assign_{g,w}$ 
These variables represent the group assignments for each golfer in each week, with $g_{w,p}$ being the group assigned to golfer $p$ in week $w$, $x_{w,g,p}$ indicating whether golfer $p$ is in group $g$ in week $w$, $tw_{p1,p2,w}$ and $twg_{p1,p2,w,g}$ indicating whether golfers $p1$ and $p2$ play together in week $w$ and group $g$ respectively, $assign_{g,w}$ being the group assignment for golfer $g$ in week $w$, $round\_group\_golfers_{r,g}$ being the set of golfers in group $g$ in round $r$, $round\_place\_golfer_{r,p}$ being the golfer assigned to place $p$ in round $r$, $golfer\_golfer\_round_{a,b}$ being the round in which golfers $a$ and $b$ play together, and $round\_group\_i\_golfer_{r,g,i}$ being the golfer assigned to group $g$ and position $i$ in round $r$.
constraints: $\forall w, g: |round\_group\_golfers_{w,g}| = size, \forall w: alldifferent(round\_group\_golfers_{w,g}), \forall a, b: \sum_{w,g} (bool2int(\{a, b\} \subseteq round\_group\_golfers_{w,g})) \leq 1, \forall w, g, p: x_{w,g,p} \in \{0,1\}, \forall w, p: \sum_{g} x_{w,g,p} = 1, \forall w, g: \sum_{p} x_{w,g,p} = size$ 
These constraints ensure that each group has the correct size, each golfer plays with each other golfer at most once, and each golfer is assigned to exactly one group each week.
objective: maximize the number of weeks for which a schedule can be found such that no pair of golfers plays together more than once.
