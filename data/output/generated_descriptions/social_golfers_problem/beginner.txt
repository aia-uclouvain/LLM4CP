Social Golfer Problem
The Social Golfer Problem is about creating a schedule for golfers to play in groups over several weeks. 
The main variables are the number of golfers, the number of groups, the size of each group, and the number of weeks. 
The basic restrictions are that each group must have the same number of players, and no two players can be in the same group more than once. 
The goal is to find a schedule that lasts as many weeks as possible without violating these rules, essentially maximizing the number of weeks the golfers can play together under these conditions.
