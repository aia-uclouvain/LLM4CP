The Sixth Trial puzzle
The Sixth Trial puzzle is about finding the arrangement of a lady and a tiger behind two doors, where each door has a statement that may be true or false, and the goal is to satisfy all the given constraints.
The main decision variables are x, t, and d. The variable x is a 2x2 array where x[i,j] represents whether door i hides prize j (1 for the lady and 2 for the tiger). The variable t is an array where t[i] represents whether the statement on door i is true. The variable d is an array of dummy variables used to model the conditional statements.
The key constraints are: each door hides one prize, the statement on each door is consistent with the prize it hides, and the statements on the doors are related to each other in a specific way. Specifically, the statement on door 1 is true if and only if the lady is behind door 1 or the tiger is behind door 2, and the statement on door 2 is true if and only if the lady is behind door 1.
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of the variables, i.e., to find an arrangement of the lady and the tiger behind the doors that satisfies all the given constraints. The problem is a satisfaction problem, meaning that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
