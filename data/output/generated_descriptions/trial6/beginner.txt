The Sixth Trial puzzle. 
This problem is about two doors, one with a lady and one with a tiger, and the statements on the doors that may or may not be true. 
The main variables are x, which shows what is behind each door, t, which shows if the statement on each door is true, and d, which are helper variables. 
The basic restrictions are that each door has one thing behind it, and the statements on the doors must follow certain rules, like if a door says it has the lady, then it must be true or false based on what is behind the other door. 
The goal of the problem is to figure out what is behind each door and if the statements on the doors are true or not, in a way that satisfies all the given rules.
