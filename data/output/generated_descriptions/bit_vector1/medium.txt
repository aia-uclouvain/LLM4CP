Bit Vector Problem
The Bit Vector problem is about finding a number that, when multiplied by two and then having its last few bits inverted, results in the original number.
The main decision variables are `num_x`, `num2_x`, and `num3_x`, which represent the binary digits of the numbers `num`, `num2`, and `num3`, respectively. `num` is the original number picked by Alice, `num2` is the result of multiplying `num` by two, and `num3` is the result of inverting the last few bits of `num2`.
The key constraints are that `num2` is twice `num`, the last few bits of `num2` are inverted to get `num3`, and `num` is equal to `num3`. In plain language, this means that when you multiply the original number by two and flip the last few bits, you should get back the original number.
The objective of the problem is to find all possible values of `num` that satisfy these constraints, with no specific minimization or maximization goal. The problem is solved using a satisfaction-based approach, where the model is designed to find all solutions that meet the given constraints.
