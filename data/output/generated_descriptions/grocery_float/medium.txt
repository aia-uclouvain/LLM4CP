The name of the problem is the Grocery problem. 
The problem is about finding the prices of four items that a kid buys at a grocery store, given that the total price is $7.11, whether the prices are added or multiplied together. 
The main decision variables are the prices of the four items, represented by the array "item" of var floats, where each float is a price in dollars. 
The key constraints are that the sum of the prices of the four items must be $7.11 and the product of the prices of the four items must also be $7.11. Additionally, the prices must be in increasing order to break symmetry in the solution. 
The problem's objective is to find a set of prices that satisfy these constraints, rather than to minimize or maximize a specific value. The goal is to determine the prices of the four items that meet the given conditions.
