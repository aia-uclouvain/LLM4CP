name: Nonogram Puzzle
description: The Nonogram puzzle is a classic puzzle where a picture is encoded as a set of rules for filling in a grid with black and white cells. The rules are given as a set of numbers that represent the lengths of consecutive blocks of black cells in each row and column. The goal is to fill in the grid according to these rules to reveal the hidden picture. In this specific problem, there are also some fixed black squares that must be taken into account.
variables: $x_{i,j} \in \{1,2\}$, where $x_{i,j} = 1$ represents a white cell and $x_{i,j} = 2$ represents a black cell at position $(i,j)$ in the grid.
constraints: $\forall j \in \{1,\ldots,cols\}: make\_automaton([x_{i,j} | i \in \{1,\ldots,rows\}], [col\_rules_{j,k} | k \in \{1,\ldots,col\_rule\_len\}]) \wedge \forall i \in \{1,\ldots,rows\}: make\_automaton([x_{i,j} | j \in \{1,\ldots,cols\}], [row\_rules_{i,k} | k \in \{1,\ldots,row\_rule\_len\}]) \wedge \forall i \in \{1,\ldots,num\_blacks\}: x_{blacks_{i,2},blacks_{i,1}} = 2$, which ensure that the grid is filled in according to the rules for each row and column, and that the fixed black squares are correctly placed.
objective: satisfy, which means that the goal is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, without any specific objective function to minimize or maximize.
