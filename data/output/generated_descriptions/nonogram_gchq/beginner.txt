Name of the problem: Nonogram Puzzle

The problem is about solving a puzzle called a Nonogram. Imagine you have a piece of paper with a grid of squares, and you need to fill in some of the squares with black ink. The puzzle gives you clues about which squares to fill in, and you have to figure out the correct pattern.

The main variables are:
- x: a grid of squares that can be either empty (represented by 1) or filled with black ink (represented by 2)
- row_rules and col_rules: these are the clues that tell you how many consecutive black squares should be in each row and column
- blacks: these are specific squares that are already known to be filled with black ink

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- Each row and column must have the correct number of consecutive black squares, as specified by the clues
- The squares that are already known to be filled with black ink must be filled in correctly

The goal of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the clues and rules, and fills in the grid with the correct pattern of black and empty squares. The solution will be a completed grid with the correct pattern of black ink.
