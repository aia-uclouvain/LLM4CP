Project Euler Problem 39
The problem is about finding the perimeter of a right-angled triangle with integral length sides that has the maximum number of solutions, given that the perimeter is less than or equal to 1000.

The main decision variables are represented by a 2D array `s` where each row `i` represents a potential solution, and each column `j` represents a side of the triangle (with `s[i,1]`, `s[i,2]`, and `s[i,3]` being the lengths of the sides and `s[i,4]` being the perimeter). 

The key constraints are: 
- each side of the triangle must have a positive integral length, 
- the triangle must satisfy the Pythagorean theorem (`s[i,3]*s[i,3] = s[i,1]*s[i,1] + s[i,2]*s[i,2]`), 
- the perimeter of the triangle is the sum of its sides (`s[i,1] + s[i,2] + s[i,3] = s[i,4]`), 
- the perimeter must be less than or equal to 1000, 
- and the solutions must be ordered in a specific way to avoid duplicates.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the number of solutions (`num_solutions`) for a given perimeter, which is achieved by finding the perimeter that has the most right-angled triangles with integral length sides.
