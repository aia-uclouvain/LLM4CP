name: Vessel Loading
description: The Vessel Loading problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves loading containers of different sizes and classes onto a ship's deck, subject to certain constraints such as the deck's dimensions, the containers' dimensions, and minimum separation requirements between containers of different classes.
variables: $x_i, y_i, w_i, h_i, r_i$ where $i$ represents each container, $x_i$ and $y_i$ are the coordinates of the container's position, $w_i$ and $h_i$ are the container's width and height, and $r_i$ is a binary variable indicating whether the container is rotated by 90 degrees. These variables represent the position, size, and orientation of each container on the ship's deck.
constraints: $x_i + w_i \leq deck\_width$, $y_i + h_i \leq deck\_length$, $(x_i + w_i + sep \leq x_j) \vee (x_j + w_j + sep \leq x_i) \vee (y_i + h_i + sep \leq y_j) \vee (y_j + h_j + sep \leq y_i)$, $w_i = width_i$ or $w_i = height_i$, $h_i = width_i$ or $h_i = height_i$, where $sep$ is the minimum separation required between containers of different classes, and $width_i$ and $height_i$ are the dimensions of container $i$. These constraints ensure that the containers are placed within the deck's boundaries, do not overlap, and meet the minimum separation requirements.
objective: The objective of the Vessel Loading problem is to find a feasible placement of all containers on the ship's deck, satisfying all the given constraints. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather a satisfaction problem where the goal is to find a valid solution that meets all the constraints.
