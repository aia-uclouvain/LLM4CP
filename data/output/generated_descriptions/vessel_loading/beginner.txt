Vessel Loading problem
The Vessel Loading problem is about loading containers of different sizes and classes onto a ship's deck in a way that maximizes the use of space while respecting certain rules. 
The main variables are the positions and orientations of the containers on the deck. 
The basic restrictions are that each container must be placed within the deck's boundaries, containers cannot overlap, and certain classes of containers must be separated by a minimum distance. 
The goal of the problem is to find a valid arrangement of containers on the deck that satisfies all the constraints, without necessarily optimizing a specific objective function, such as minimizing unused space or maximizing the number of containers loaded.
