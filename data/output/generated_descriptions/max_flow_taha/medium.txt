Max Flow Problem
The Max Flow problem is about finding the maximum amount of flow that can be sent through a network from a source node to a sink node, without exceeding the capacity of any edge in the network.

The main decision variables are:
- `x[i, j]`: the amount of flow sent from node `i` to node `j`.
- `outFlow[i]`: the total amount of flow sent out of node `i`.
- `inFlow[i]`: the total amount of flow sent into node `i`.
- `total`: the total amount of flow sent from the source node to all other nodes.

The key constraints are:
- The flow sent from one node to another cannot exceed the capacity of the edge between them.
- The total amount of flow sent out of a node must be equal to the total amount of flow sent into that node, except for the source and sink nodes.
- The source node sends flow to other nodes, but does not receive any flow.
- The sink node receives flow from other nodes, but does not send any flow.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the total amount of flow sent from the source node, which is represented by the `total` variable. In other words, the goal is to find the maximum amount of flow that can be sent through the network without exceeding the capacity of any edge.
