Nadel's Construction Problem
The problem is about assigning different developments (recreation area, apartments, houses, cemetery, and dumpsite) to eight lots, taking into account various constraints such as proximity to the lake, steep slopes, poor soil, and visibility from other developments.
The main decision variables are the developments (recreation, apartments, houses, cemetery, and dump), which represent the assignment of each development to one of the eight lots.
The key constraints are:
- The recreation area should be near the lake.
- Steep slopes should be avoided for all developments except the recreation area.
- Poor soil should be avoided for developments that involve construction (apartments and houses).
- The highway should not be near the apartments, houses, or recreation area.
- The dumpsite should not be visible from the apartments, houses, or lake.
The objective of the problem is to minimize the number of broken constraints, with the goal of finding a solution that satisfies all constraints. However, since it is not possible to satisfy all constraints, the problem is relaxed to allow for at most one broken constraint.
