name: Sudoku
description: Sudoku is a classic puzzle where the goal is to fill a 9x9 grid with numbers from 1 to 9, such that each row, column, and 3x3 sub-grid contains each number exactly once. The puzzle starts with some numbers already filled in, and the task is to fill in the remaining empty cells.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ for $i,j = 1, \ldots, 9$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the number in the $i$-th row and $j$-th column of the grid, and is a decision variable that can take values from 1 to 9.
constraints: $\forall i,j \in \{1, \ldots, 9\}: x_{i,j} = hints_{i,j}$ if $hints_{i,j} > 0$, $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, 9\}: \text{all\_different}([x_{i,j} | j \in \{1, \ldots, 9\}])$, $\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, 9\}: \text{all\_different}([x_{i,j} | i \in \{1, \ldots, 9\}])$, $\forall i,j \in \{0, \ldots, 2\}: \text{all\_different}([x_{r,c} | r \in \{i \cdot 3 + 1, \ldots, i \cdot 3 + 3\}, c \in \{j \cdot 3 + 1, \ldots, j \cdot 3 + 3\}])$, which ensure that the numbers in each row, column, and 3x3 sub-grid are distinct, and that the given hints are respected.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, i.e., to solve the puzzle, rather than to minimize or maximize a specific value. The model uses the `satisfy` keyword to indicate that the goal is to find a feasible solution.
