**Problem Name:** Sum of Fifth Powers of Digits

**Description:** This problem involves finding numbers that can be expressed as the sum of fifth powers of their digits. For example, the number 1634 can be written as 1^4 + 6^4 + 3^4 + 4^4. The goal is to find all such numbers and calculate their sum.

**Decision Variables:**
- `digits`: a 2D array representing the digits of the numbers, where `digits[i, j]` is the jth digit of the ith number.
- `x`: an array representing the numbers themselves, where `x[i]` is the ith number.

**Key Constraints:**
- Each number `x[i]` must be equal to the sum of the fifth powers of its digits.
- Each number `x[i]` must be greater than 1 (to exclude trivial cases like 1 = 1^5).
- The numbers `x[i]` must be distinct and in increasing order (to avoid duplicates and ensure a unique solution).

**Objective:**
The objective is to find the sum `s` of all numbers that can be written as the sum of fifth powers of their digits. In other words, the goal is to maximize the sum `s` by finding all possible numbers that satisfy the given constraints.
