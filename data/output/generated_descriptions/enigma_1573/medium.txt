**Enigma Number 1573 (Sat, Sun, Mon, Tue) Problem**

The problem is about finding two five-digit numbers, represented by the multiplication of "SAT" and "MON" with two-digit numbers, such that the results are "SUNab" and "TUEcd" respectively. The letters represent different digits, and the same letter stands for the same digit.

The main decision variables are:
- S, A, T, M, O, N, U, E: representing the digits in the "SAT" and "MON" numbers
- a, b, c, d: representing the last two digits in the "SUNab" and "TUEcd" numbers
- H: an auxiliary variable

The key constraints are:
- Each letter represents a unique digit
- The first digit of "SAT" and "MON" cannot be zero
- The multiplication of "SAT" with a two-digit number results in "SUNab"
- The multiplication of "MON" with a two-digit number results in "TUEcd"
- The month represented by the digits M, O, N, T, and H is exactly divisible by 28, 30, and 31

The objective is to find the numerical total of the two five-digit products, "SUNab" and "TUEcd".
