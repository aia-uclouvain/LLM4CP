Uniform Dice Problem
The problem is about labeling a blank six-sided die with numbers from 0 to 6 so that when rolled with a standard die, the sum of the two dice shows each whole number from 1 to 12 with equal chance.
The main decision variables are x1, representing the faces of the standard die, and x2, representing the faces of the blank die. The variable dist represents the distribution of the sums of the two dice, and z represents the number of possible sums.
The key constraints are that the sum of the two dice must show each whole number from 1 to 12 with equal chance, which is achieved by ensuring that the distribution of the sums (dist) is the same for all possible sums. Additionally, all elements in dist must be greater than 0, and the sum of dist must be equal to the total number of possible outcomes (n*n). The constraint "increasing(x2)" is used to break symmetry in the solution.
The objective of the problem is to find a labeling of the blank die that satisfies the constraints, which is achieved by solving the "satisfy" problem. In other words, the goal is to find a solution that makes the distribution of the sums (dist) uniform, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
