name: Configuration Problem

description: The configuration problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves configuring a set of components to meet certain requirements while minimizing cost. In this specific problem, we have a set of models, each with its own power, connector, and price characteristics, and a set of cards, each with its own power and quantity requirements. The goal is to assign the models to a set of racks and distribute the cards among the racks such that the total cost is minimized and all the requirements are met.

variables: $rack_r, counters_{r,c}, cost$ where $r \in Racks$ and $c \in Cards$. These variables represent the model assigned to each rack ($rack_r$), the number of cards of each type assigned to each rack ($counters_{r,c}$), and the total cost of the configuration ($cost$).

constraints: 
$\forall r \in Racks: \sum_{c \in Cards} counters_{r,c} \cdot car_{c,1} \leq powerData_{rack_r}$, 
$\forall r \in Racks: \sum_{c \in Cards} counters_{r,c} \leq connData_{rack_r}$, 
$\forall c \in Cards: \sum_{r \in Racks} counters_{r,c} = car_{c,2}$, 
$cost = \sum_{r \in Racks} priceData_{rack_r}$. 
These constraints ensure that the total power consumption of the cards assigned to each rack does not exceed the power capacity of the model assigned to that rack, the total number of cards assigned to each rack does not exceed the connector capacity of the model assigned to that rack, the total number of cards of each type assigned to all racks equals the required quantity, and the total cost is calculated correctly.

objective: minimize $cost$
