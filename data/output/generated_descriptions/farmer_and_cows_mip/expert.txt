name: Farmer and Cows Problem
description: A farmer has 81 cows, each producing a different amount of milk per day, and he wants to distribute them among his 9 sons. The goal is to give each son 9 cows such that the total amount of milk produced by the cows given to each son is the same.
variables: $sons = [s_1, s_2, ..., s_9]$ where $s_i$ is the total amount of milk produced by the cows given to son $i$, and $cows = [c_{i,j}]$ where $c_{i,j}$ is a binary variable indicating whether cow $i$ is given to son $j$. These variables represent the decision variables in the problem, where $sons$ determines the total amount of milk each son should have, and $cows$ determines the assignment of cows to sons.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{9} s_i = \frac{81 \cdot 82}{2}$, $s_i = s_{i-1}$ for $i = 2, ..., 9$, $s_i = \sum_{j=1}^{81} j \cdot c_{j,i}$ for $i = 1, ..., 9$, $\sum_{i=1}^{9} c_{j,i} = 1$ for $j = 1, ..., 81$, and $\sum_{j=1}^{81} c_{j,i} = 9$ for $i = 1, ..., 9$. These constraints ensure that the total amount of milk is distributed evenly, each son gets the same amount of milk, each cow is assigned to exactly one son, and each son gets exactly 9 cows.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, which means finding a feasible solution that distributes the cows among the sons such that each son gets 9 cows and the total amount of milk produced by the cows given to each son is the same. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
