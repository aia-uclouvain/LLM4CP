Farmer and Cow Problem
The problem is about a farmer who has 81 cows that produce different amounts of milk, and he wants to distribute these cows among his 9 sons. 
The main variables are the amount of milk each son should get and which cows each son should receive. 
The basic restrictions are that each son must get 9 cows, each cow can only be given to one son, and the total amount of milk from the cows given to each son must be the same. 
The goal is to find a way to distribute the cows so that each son gets the same amount of milk, which is one-ninth of the total milk produced by all the cows.
