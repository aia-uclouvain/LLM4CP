Stock Control Problem
The problem is about managing the stock of a company that produces goods. Imagine you have a factory that makes products, and you need to decide how many products to make each day to meet customer demand while keeping costs low.

The main variables are:
- x: the number of products made each day
- y: the number of products stored in the warehouse

The basic restrictions or rules are:
- You can't make a negative number of products or store a negative number of products.
- The number of products made and stored must meet customer demand.
- You start with a certain amount of products already in the warehouse.
- You can't make more products than the factory can handle.

The goal is to minimize the total cost, which includes the cost of making products and the cost of storing them. In other words, the company wants to find the best way to make and store products to keep costs as low as possible while still meeting customer demand.
