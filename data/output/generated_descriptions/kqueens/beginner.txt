Kraitchik's Queen Placement Problem
The problem is about placing queens on a chessboard in such a way that every square on the board is either occupied by a queen or under attack by a queen.
The main variables are: 
- x(i,j) which means whether a queen is placed on the square at row i and column j (1 if a queen is placed, 0 if not)
- a(i,j) which means whether the square at row i and column j is under attack by a queen (1 if under attack, 0 if not)
- numq which is the total number of queens placed on the board.
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- A queen can attack horizontally, vertically, or diagonally
- Every square on the board must be either occupied by a queen or under attack by a queen
The goal of the problem is to minimize the number of queens (numq) needed to cover the entire board.
