Kraitchik's Queen Placement Problem
The problem is about placing the minimum number of queens on a chessboard such that every square is either occupied by a queen or under attack by a queen.
The main decision variables are x(i,j) and a(i,j), where x(i,j) represents whether a queen is placed on the square at row i and column j (1 if occupied, 0 otherwise), and a(i,j) represents whether the square at row i and column j is under attack by a queen (1 if attacked, 0 otherwise).
The key constraints are that each square is either occupied by a queen or under attack by a queen, and that a square is under attack if there is a queen in the same row, column, or diagonal. 
The objective of the problem is to minimize the total number of queens placed on the board, which is represented by the variable numq.
