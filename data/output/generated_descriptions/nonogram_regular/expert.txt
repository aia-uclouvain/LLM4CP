name: Nonogram
description: A Nonogram is a puzzle where a picture is encoded as a set of numbers that describe the sequence of filled and empty cells in each row and column. The goal is to recreate the original picture by filling in the cells according to the given sequences.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j}$ is a decision variable that represents the state of the cell at row $i$ and column $j$, with $x_{i,j} = 1$ indicating a filled cell and $x_{i,j} = 2$ indicating an empty cell. These variables are used to determine the final state of each cell in the puzzle.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..row\_num\_patterns: regular([x_{i,j} | j \in 1..col\_max], row\_num\_states[i], 2, array2d(1..row\_num\_states[i], 1..2, [row\_states[k,j] | k \in row\_start\_where[i]..row\_start\_where[i]+row\_num\_states[i]-1, j \in 1..2]), 1, \{row\_num\_states[i]\}) \wedge \forall i \in 1..col\_num\_patterns: regular([x_{j,i} | j \in 1..row\_max], col\_num\_states[i], 2, array2d(1..col\_num\_states[i], 1..2, [col\_states[k,j] | k \in col\_start\_where[i]..col\_start\_where[i]+col\_num\_states[i]-1, j \in 1..2]), 1, \{col\_num\_states[i]\})$, which are regular constraints that ensure the sequence of filled and empty cells in each row and column matches the given patterns.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, i.e., to find a solution that fills in the cells according to the given sequences, with no specific value to minimize or maximize. The goal is to recreate the original picture by finding a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
