The problem is called Conditional Lexicographic Constraints. 

This problem is about comparing two sets of values, called vectors, based on a set of rules or preferences. Imagine you have two lists of numbers and you want to decide which list is "better" according to some predefined rules. 

The main variables are: 
- arr: a table of preferences, where each row represents a possible combination of values.
- v1 and v2: the two vectors being compared.

The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- The vectors v1 and v2 must match two rows in the preference table arr.
- The comparison between v1 and v2 is done based on the order of the rows in the preference table.

The goal of the problem is to find values for v1, v2, and arr that satisfy the given constraints, specifically the conditional lexicographic constraints (less, less or equal, greater, greater or equal) between v1 and v2 based on the preference table arr.
