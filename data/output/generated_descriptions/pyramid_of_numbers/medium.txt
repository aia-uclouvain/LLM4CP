Pyramid of Numbers
The Pyramid of Numbers problem involves finding the values of a pyramid where each brick is the sum of the two bricks situated below it. The pyramid has a specific structure with some given numbers and some missing numbers that need to be determined.

The main decision variables in this problem are the values of the bricks in the pyramid, represented by the array `x`. The variables `X`, `Y`, and `Z` represent the three missing numbers at the base of the pyramid.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each brick in the pyramid is the sum of the two bricks below it.
- The middle missing number `Y` is the sum of the other two missing numbers `X` and `Z`.
- The given numbers in the pyramid must be matched with the corresponding values in the `x` array.
- The indices of the numbers to add are calculated based on the triangle numbers.

The objective of this problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding the values of the bricks in the pyramid that meet the given conditions. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
