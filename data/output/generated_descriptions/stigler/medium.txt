**Stigler's Diet Problem**
The Stigler's Diet Problem is a classic problem in the field of operations research and optimization. 

**Problem Description**
The problem is to determine the optimal daily intake of 77 different foods to meet the daily nutritional requirements of a moderately active man, while minimizing the total cost of the diet. The nutritional requirements include calories, protein, calcium, iron, vitamin A, thiamine, riboflavin, niacin, and ascorbic acid.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are the quantities of each food to be consumed daily, represented by the array `x`. Each element `x[c]` represents the quantity of food `c` to be consumed. Additionally, there are variables `x_cost` and `quant` that represent the cost and quantity of each food, respectively.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem are:
- The total amount of each nutrient consumed must be greater than or equal to the recommended daily allowance.
- The quantity of each food consumed must be non-negative.
- The cost of each food is calculated based on its quantity and price.
- The total cost of the diet is the sum of the costs of all foods.

**Objective**
The objective of the problem is to minimize the total cost of the diet, represented by the variable `cost`. This is achieved by finding the optimal quantities of each food that meet the nutritional requirements while minimizing the total cost.
