Magic Square Problem
The problem is about filling a 3x3 grid with numbers from 1 to 9 in a special way. 
The main variables are X1 to X9, which represent the numbers to be placed in each cell of the grid.
The basic restrictions are that each number can only be used once, and the numbers in each row, column, and two diagonals must add up to 15.
The goal of the problem is to find a way to fill the grid that satisfies all these rules, which is known as a "magic square".
