Word Design Problem
The problem is about designing a set of words for DNA computing on surfaces. 
In simple terms, it's like creating a collection of special codes using four letters: A, C, G, and T. 
These codes must follow certain rules to work properly.

The main variables are the words, which are like the special codes made up of the letters A, C, G, and T. 
Each word has eight positions, and each position can be one of the four letters.

The basic restrictions or rules are:
- Each word must have exactly four positions that are either C or G.
- Any two words in the collection must be different in at least four positions.
- When comparing a word with the reverse of another word, and also considering the Watson-Crick complement (a specific way of matching the letters), they must be different in at least four positions.

The goal of the problem is to find the largest possible collection of words that satisfy all these rules. 
In other words, the goal is to maximize the number of words in the collection while ensuring that all the rules are followed.
