name: Home Improvement Problem
description: The problem is about identifying the most profitable home improvement to increase the selling price of a house. The problem provides data on recently sold houses in the neighborhood, including their selling prices and the improvements they had. The goal is to determine which improvement will add the most profit to the sale of the house, considering the installation costs of the improvements.
variables: $x_j, rest_{ij}$ where $j = 1, \ldots, 5$ and $i = 1, \ldots, 9$. These variables represent the added value to the house price for each improvement ($x_j$) and the error term for each house and improvement ($rest_{ij}$), which accounts for the difference between the predicted and actual selling price of each house.
constraints: $\sum_{j=1}^{5} t_{ij}(x_j - installation\_cost_j + rest_{ij}) = house\_price_i$ for $i = 1, \ldots, 9$. These constraints ensure that the sum of the added values of the improvements for each house, minus the installation costs and plus the error term, equals the actual selling price of the house.
objective: minimize $\sum_{i=1}^{9} \sum_{j=1}^{5} rest_{ij}$, which means minimizing the sum of the error terms for all houses and improvements, indicating a better fit between the predicted and actual selling prices.
