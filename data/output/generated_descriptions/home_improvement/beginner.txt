Home Improvement Problem
The problem is about deciding which home improvement to make to a house to increase its selling price. 
The main variables are: 
- house prices: the prices at which similar houses in the neighborhood were sold
- improvements: whether a house had a new bathroom, kitchen, pool, wood floors, or new paint
- installation costs: the cost of making each improvement
- x: the amount of profit each improvement adds to the house price
- rest: the difference between the actual and expected selling price of each house.
The basic restrictions are: 
- each house can have multiple improvements, but the installation cost is subtracted from the profit
- the total profit from all improvements plus any extra factors (rest) equals the actual selling price of the house.
The goal is to find the improvement that adds the most profit to the house, while also considering the installation costs and other factors that affect the selling price.
