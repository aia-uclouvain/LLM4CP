Max Activity Problem
The problem is about assigning kids to activities they prefer, without exceeding the maximum number of participants for each activity. 
The main variables are the kids, the activities, and the preferences of each kid for the activities. In simple terms, the variables mean: 
- "x" represents which activity each kid is assigned to
- "scores" represents how happy each kid is with their assigned activity, based on their preferences
- "prefs" represents the order of preference for each kid, with 1 being their first choice, 2 being their second choice, and 3 being their third choice
- "activity_size" represents the maximum number of kids that can be assigned to each activity
The basic restrictions or rules are: 
- each kid can only be assigned to one activity
- the number of kids assigned to each activity cannot exceed the maximum size of that activity
- each kid's assignment is based on their preferences, with the goal of making them as happy as possible
The goal of the problem is to maximize the total happiness of all kids, which is represented by the "total_score" variable. This means finding an assignment of kids to activities that makes the most kids happy, while still following the rules about activity sizes and kid preferences.
