name: Max Activity Problem
description: The Max Activity Problem is a constraint programming problem where a group of kids need to be assigned to a set of activities. Each kid has a preference list of three activities in order of preference, and each activity has a maximum number of participants. The goal is to assign kids to activities while respecting their preferences as much as possible without exceeding the maximum size of each activity.
variables: $x_k, scores_k$ for $k = 1, \ldots, num\_kids$, where $x_k$ represents the selected activity for kid $k$ and $scores_k$ represents the score for the selected activity, which is calculated based on the kid's preference list.
constraints: $\forall k \in \{1, \ldots, num\_kids\}, \exists p \in \{1, 2, 3\}: x_k = prefs_{k, p} \land scores_k = 4 - p$ (each kid is assigned to one of their preferred activities and scores are calculated accordingly), $\forall a \in \{1, \ldots, num\_activities\}: \sum_{k=1}^{num\_kids} (x_k = a) \leq activity\_size_a$ (the number of kids assigned to each activity does not exceed its maximum size).
objective: Maximize $\sum_{k=1}^{num\_kids} scores_k$ (maximize the total score, which represents the overall satisfaction of the kids with their assigned activities).
