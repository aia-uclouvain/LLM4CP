name: Peaceable Army of Queens

description: The problem involves placing two equally-sized armies of queens (white and black) on a chessboard without attacking each other, with the goal of maximizing the size of the armies. The queens can move horizontally, vertically, or diagonally, and the objective is to find the largest number of queens that can be placed on the board without any queen attacking another queen of the opposite color.

variables: $n, N, board_{i,j}, amountOfQueens$ 
These variables represent the following: $n$ is the size of the chessboard, $N$ is the set of integers from 1 to $n$, $board_{i,j}$ is a 2D array representing the state of each cell on the board (0: empty, 1: white queen, 2: black queen), and $amountOfQueens$ is the number of queens of each color placed on the board.

constraints: $board_{1,1} = 1, \sum_{i,j \in N} (board_{i,j} = 1) = amountOfQueens, \sum_{i,j \in N} (board_{i,j} = 2) = amountOfQueens, \forall row, col \in N: \forall q \in \{1,2\}: \forall i \in N: (board_{row,col} = q \rightarrow (board_{i,col} \neq opposite(q) \wedge board_{row,i} \neq opposite(q) \wedge board_{row+i,col+i} \neq opposite(q) \wedge board_{row-i,col-i} \neq opposite(q) \wedge board_{row+i,col-i} \neq opposite(q) \wedge board_{row-i,col+i} \neq opposite(q)))$
These constraints ensure that: the top-left corner of the board is occupied by a white queen, the number of white queens is equal to the number of black queens, and no queen attacks another queen of the opposite color.

objective: Maximize $amountOfQueens$
