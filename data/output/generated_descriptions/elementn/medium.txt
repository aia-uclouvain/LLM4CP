The problem is called the Element Constraint Problem. 
It is about finding a subsequence of fixed length within a given sequence of elements. 
The main decision variables are: elements, an array representing the sequence of elements; entries, an array representing the subsequence to be found; and t_index, a variable representing the starting index of the subsequence in the sequence. 
The key constraints are: the sequence of elements must match a given sequence; the subsequence must match a given subsequence; the starting index of the subsequence must be within the bounds of the sequence; and the subsequence must be a contiguous part of the sequence, as defined by the elementn constraint, which ensures that each element of the subsequence matches the corresponding element in the sequence. 
The problem's objective is to satisfy these constraints, i.e., to find a solution where all constraints hold, rather than to minimize or maximize a specific value.
