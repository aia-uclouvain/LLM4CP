name: Cutting Stock Problem
description: The Cutting Stock Problem is a classic problem in operations research and constraint programming. It involves a company that sells lumber of different lengths and needs to cut these pieces from larger boards to meet customer demand while minimizing waste. The goal is to determine the optimal number of cuts for each possible combination of board lengths to satisfy customer demand and minimize the total number of boards used.
variables: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_6$ - These are decision variables representing the number of 17-ft boards cut according to each of the six possible combinations. Each combination yields a specific number of 3-ft, 5-ft, and 9-ft boards.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 6\}: x_i \geq 0, \forall j \in \{1, 2, 3\}: \sum_{i=1}^{6} combinations_{i,j} \cdot x_i \geq demand_j$ - The first set of constraints ensures that the number of boards cut according to each combination is non-negative. The second set of constraints guarantees that the total number of 3-ft, 5-ft, and 9-ft boards produced meets or exceeds customer demand.
objective: Minimize $\sum_{i=1}^{6} x_i$ - The objective is to minimize the total number of 17-ft boards used to satisfy customer demand, which indirectly minimizes waste and reduces costs.
