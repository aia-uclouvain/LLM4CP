Two-Dimensional Constrained Channels Problem
The Two-Dimensional Constrained Channels Problem is about finding all possible configurations of a grid where each interior cell has a specific number of neighboring cells with a certain value.
The main decision variables are the values of the cells in the grid, represented by the 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` is 1 if the cell at position `(i, j)` is black and 0 if it is white.
The key constraints are that for each interior cell, the number of neighboring cells that are black must be equal to a given number `m`. The neighboring cells are the eight cells surrounding the current cell, and the constraint only applies to interior cells, not cells on the boundary of the grid.
The objective of the problem is to find all possible configurations of the grid that satisfy the constraint, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value. The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find all solutions that satisfy the given constraints.
