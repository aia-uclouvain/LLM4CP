name: Circular Chain Problem
description: The problem involves finding the weights of eight linked gold rings in a circular chain, where each ring is a whole number of ounces in weight, and the total weight is 57 ounces. The chain is designed such that any whole number of ounces can be removed from the chain, either as a single link or as a chain of linked rings. There are no 3-ounce or 6-ounce rings.
variables: $chain = [chain_1, chain_2, ..., chain_8]$, where $chain_i$ represents the weight of the $i^{th}$ ring in the chain, and is a decision variable that takes a value between 1 and 57.
constraints: 
$chain_1 + chain_2 + ... + chain_8 = 57$,
$chain_i \neq 3 \forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 8\}$,
$chain_i \neq 6 \forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 8\}$,
$chain_1 = 1$,
$chain_2 < chain_8$,
$chain_i \neq chain_j \forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., 8\}, i \neq j$,
$\forall s \in \{1, 2, ..., 57\}, \exists i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., 8\}$ such that either $\sum_{k=i}^{j} chain_k = s$ or $\sum_{k=i}^{8} chain_k + \sum_{k=1}^{j} chain_k = s$.
These constraints ensure that the total weight of the chain is 57 ounces, there are no 3-ounce or 6-ounce rings, the first ring weighs 1 ounce, the second ring weighs less than the eighth ring, all rings have different weights, and any whole number of ounces can be removed from the chain.
objective: The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the given constraints.
