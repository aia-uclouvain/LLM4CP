name: Spy Girls Puzzle
description: The Spy Girls Agency consists of five female agents, each with a unique first name (Jane, Sylvia, Hillary, Kate, Amanda) and a unique last name (Brookes, Adams, Darling, Cooper, Miller). The agents are ranked from 1 (highest) to 5 (lowest) based on their secret agent number. The problem is to determine each agent's rank, first name, and last name using the given clues.
variables: $jane, sylvia, hillary, kate, amanda, brookes, adams, darling, cooper, miller \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}$, where $jane, sylvia, hillary, kate, amanda$ represent the ranks of the agents with the corresponding first names, and $brookes, adams, darling, cooper, miller$ represent the ranks of the agents with the corresponding last names.
constraints: 
$\begin{align*}
&all\_different(first\_names) \\
&all\_different(last\_names) \\
&jane \neq brookes \\
&sylvia < adams \\
&sylvia > 1 \\
&hillary < darling \\
&brookes < hillary \\
&amanda < kate \\
&amanda < hillary \\
&amanda > 1 \\
&adams < miller \\
&adams > cooper \\
&miller < 5
\end{align*}$, 
where the constraints ensure that each first name and each last name is assigned a unique rank, and the given clues are satisfied.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of ranks to the agents, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize, the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
