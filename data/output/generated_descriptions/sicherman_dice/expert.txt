name: Sicherman Dice Problem
description: The Sicherman Dice Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves finding two 6-sided dice with non-standard numbering such that the probability distribution of their sum is the same as that of two standard dice. The goal is to find the numbering on the faces of the two dice that satisfies this condition.
variables: $x1 = (x1_1, x1_2, ..., x1_6), x2 = (x2_1, x2_2, ..., x2_6)$, where $x1_i$ and $x2_i$ are the values on the $i^{th}$ faces of the first and second dice, respectively. These variables represent the decision variables in the problem, which are the values assigned to each face of the two dice.
constraints: $\forall k \in [2, 12], \sum_{i,j \in [1,6]} (x1_i + x2_j == k) = standard\_dist[k]$, $x1_i \leq x2_i \forall i \in [1,6]$, $x1$ and $x2$ are increasing arrays. These constraints ensure that the probability distribution of the sum of the two dice is the same as that of two standard dice, and that the values on the faces of the dice are non-decreasing and satisfy the symmetry breaking conditions.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the faces of the two dice, rather than to minimize or maximize a specific value. The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints.
