Parallel Resistors Problem
The problem is about combining two electrical resistors in parallel to find the total current and voltage. 
The main variables are: 
- V, the total voltage 
- I, the total current 
- R1 and R2, the resistances of the two resistors.
The basic restrictions are: 
- The voltage across each resistor is the same 
- The total current is the sum of the currents through each resistor 
- The current through each resistor is determined by its resistance and the voltage across it.
The goal is to find the total current and voltage when two resistors are combined in parallel, given their individual resistances.
