Pool-ball triangles problem
The problem is about arranging numbered pool balls in a triangle shape in a specific way. 
The main variables are the positions of the pool balls, represented by the numbers 1 to 15, and the differences between the numbers of the balls above and below them.
The basic restrictions are that each ball below two balls must have a number that is the difference between the numbers of the two balls above it, and all the balls must have different numbers.
The goal of the problem is to find an arrangement of the pool balls that satisfies these restrictions, which is a classic puzzle with no clear objective to minimize or maximize, but rather to find a valid solution.
