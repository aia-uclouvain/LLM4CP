name: Pool-ball triangles problem

description: The problem involves arranging numbered pool balls in a triangular formation such that the number of each ball below two balls is the difference of the numbers of those two balls. The triangular formation has a specific structure, with each row having one more ball than the previous row, and the total number of balls is given by the formula n*(n+1)/2, where n is the number of rows.

variables: $x = (x_1, x_2, ..., x_{len})$, $subs = (subs_1, subs_2, ..., subs_{t[n-1]})$ 
These are decision variables where $x_i$ represents the number of the ball in the $i^{th}$ position, and $subs_i$ is an auxiliary variable used to calculate the positions of the balls.

constraints: 
$x[subs[i]] - x[subs[i]+1] = x[i]$ or $x[subs[i]+1] - x[subs[i]] = x[i]$ for $i = 1..t[n-1]$, 
$subs[i] = subs[i-1] + 1$ or $subs[i] = subs[i-1] + 2$ for $i = 2..t[n-1]$, 
$x[2] < x[3]$, 
$all\_different(x)$
These constraints ensure that the number of each ball below two balls is the difference of the numbers of those two balls, that the auxiliary variables $subs$ are calculated correctly, that the solution is symmetric, and that all balls have distinct numbers.

objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
