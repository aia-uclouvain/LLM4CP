Mortgage Problem
The problem is about calculating the monthly payment, interest rate, or initial amount of a mortgage based on the other two values.
The main variables are: 
- P, the initial amount borrowed
- I, the interest rate
- R, the monthly payment
- mortgage, an array representing the outstanding mortgage balance over time
The basic restrictions are: 
- the monthly payment must cover the interest and reduce the outstanding balance
- the outstanding balance cannot be negative
- the interest rate, monthly payment, and initial amount are within certain limits
The goal is to calculate one of the variables (P, I, or R) given the other two, while ensuring the mortgage is paid off over a certain period of time (in this case, 3 months), and to minimize the initial amount borrowed.
