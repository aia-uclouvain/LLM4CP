Mortgage Problem
The Mortgage problem is about calculating the monthly payment, interest rate, or initial principal of a mortgage based on given values of the other two parameters.
The main decision variables are P (initial principal), I (interest rate), and R (monthly payment), which represent the amount borrowed, the rate at which interest is charged, and the amount paid each month, respectively.
The key constraints are that the mortgage balance at each time period must be non-negative, the initial mortgage balance is the principal plus interest minus the monthly payment, and each subsequent balance is the previous balance plus interest minus the monthly payment.
The problem's objective is to minimize the initial principal P, given the interest rate I and monthly payment R, or to calculate I or R given the other two parameters, with the goal of finding a feasible solution that satisfies all constraints.
