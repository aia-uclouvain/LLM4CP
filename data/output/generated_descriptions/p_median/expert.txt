name: P-Median Problem

description: The P-Median problem is a well-known problem in Operations Research where the goal is to choose P warehouses to open that minimize the demand-weighted distance of serving all customers from those P warehouses, given a set of customers with known amounts of demand, a set of candidate locations for warehouses, and the distance between each pair of customer-warehouse.

variables: $OpenWarehouse_w, ShipToCustomer_{cw}$ 
These are decision variables where $OpenWarehouse_w$ represents whether warehouse $w$ is open (1) or not (0), and $ShipToCustomer_{cw}$ represents whether customer $c$ is served by warehouse $w$ (1) or not (0).

constraints: $\sum_{w \in Warehouses} ShipToCustomer_{cw} = 1, \sum_{w \in Warehouses} OpenWarehouse_w = P, ShipToCustomer_{cw} \leq OpenWarehouse_w$
These constraints ensure that each customer is served by exactly one warehouse, the total number of open warehouses is equal to P, and a customer can only be served by a warehouse that is open.

objective: minimize $z = \sum_{c \in Customers, w \in Warehouses} Demand_c \times Distance_{cw} \times ShipToCustomer_{cw}$ 
The objective is to minimize the total demand-weighted distance of serving all customers from the open warehouses.
