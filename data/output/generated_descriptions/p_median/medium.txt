P-Median Problem
The P-Median problem is about finding the optimal locations for a set of warehouses to serve a group of customers, given the demand of each customer and the distance between each customer and each potential warehouse location.

The main decision variables are OpenWarehouse and ShipToCustomer. OpenWarehouse is an array of binary variables, where each element represents whether a particular warehouse is open (1) or not (0). ShipToCustomer is a two-dimensional array of binary variables, where each element represents whether a particular customer is served by a particular warehouse (1) or not (0).

The key constraints are: 
- Each customer must be served by exactly one warehouse. 
- The total number of open warehouses must be equal to P, a predefined number. 
- A customer can only be served by a warehouse that is open.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the total demand-weighted distance of serving all customers from the selected warehouses. In other words, the goal is to find the locations for the warehouses that result in the shortest total distance to serve all customers, taking into account the demand of each customer. This is achieved by minimizing the variable z, which represents the total demand-weighted distance.
