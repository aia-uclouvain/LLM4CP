name: Pi Day Sudoku 2008
description: The problem is a variation of the classic Sudoku puzzle, where each row, column, and jigsaw region must contain exactly the first twelve digits of pi, including repeats. The puzzle has a 12x12 grid, divided into 12 regions, and some numbers are already given as hints. The goal is to fill in the missing numbers so that each row, column, and region contains the digits 3, 1, 4, 1, 5, 9, 2, 6, 5, 3, 5, and 8, with the correct frequency.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ and $j$ are indices ranging from 1 to 12, representing the value of the cell at row $i$ and column $j$. These variables are decision variables that represent the numbers to be filled in the grid.
constraints: 
$\forall i,j \in \{1,2,...,12\}: x_{i,j} = problem_{i,j}$ if $problem_{i,j} > 0$,
$\forall i \in \{1,2,...,12\}: global\_cardinality([x_{i,j} | j \in \{1,2,...,12\}], cover, occ)$,
$\forall i \in \{1,2,...,12\}: global\_cardinality([x_{j,i} | j \in \{1,2,...,12\}], cover, occ)$,
$\forall k \in \{1,2,...,12\}: global\_cardinality([x_{i,j} | i,j \in \{1,2,...,12\} \wedge regions_{i,j} = k], cover, occ)$.
These constraints ensure that the given hints are respected, and that each row, column, and region contains the correct frequency of each digit.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, i.e., to find a solution that respects all the rules of the puzzle. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather a satisfaction problem where the goal is to find a feasible solution.
