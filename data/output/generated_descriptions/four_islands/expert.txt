name: Four Islands Puzzle
description: The Four Islands Puzzle is a classic logic puzzle where four islands, Pwana, Quero, Rayou, and Skern, are connected by bridges and each island has a unique primary export (alabaster, bananas, coconuts, and durian fruit) and a unique tourist attraction (hotel, ice skating rink, jai alai stadium, and koala preserve). The goal is to determine the name, export, and attraction of each island based on given clues.
variables: $Pwana, Quero, Rayou, Skern, alabaster, bananas, coconuts, durian\_fruit, resort\_hotel, ice\_skating\_rink, jai\_alai\_stadium, koala\_preserve, island = [Pwana, Quero, Rayou, Skern], export = [alabaster, bananas, coconuts, durian\_fruit], attraction = [resort\_hotel, ice\_skating\_rink, jai\_alai\_stadium, koala\_preserve]$ 
These variables represent the assignment of islands, exports, and attractions to each other, where $Pwana, Quero, Rayou, Skern$ are the islands, $alabaster, bananas, coconuts, durian\_fruit$ are the exports, and $resort\_hotel, ice\_skating\_rink, jai\_alai\_stadium, koala\_preserve$ are the attractions.
constraints: $\begin{aligned}
&all\_different(island) \\
&all\_different(export) \\
&all\_different(attraction) \\
&(Pwana = A \wedge koala\_preserve = C) \vee (Pwana = B \wedge koala\_preserve = D) \\
&(alabaster = A \wedge Quero = B) \vee (alabaster = C \wedge Quero = D) \\
&(durian\_fruit = A \wedge resort\_hotel = B) \vee (durian\_fruit = C \wedge resort\_hotel = D) \\
&(Skern = A \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = C) \vee (Skern = C \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = A) \vee (Skern = B \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = D) \vee (Skern = D \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = B) \\
&(Rayou = A \wedge bananas = B) \vee (Rayou = B \wedge bananas = A) \vee (Rayou = C \wedge bananas = D) \vee (Rayou = D \wedge bananas = C) \\
&(ice\_skating\_rink = A \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = D) \vee (ice\_skating\_rink = D \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = A) \vee (ice\_skating\_rink = B \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = C) \vee (ice\_skating\_rink = C \wedge jai\_alai\_stadium = B)
\end{aligned}$ 
These constraints ensure that each island, export, and attraction is assigned uniquely, and that the given clues are satisfied.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of islands, exports, and attractions, which is achieved by solving the constraints. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
