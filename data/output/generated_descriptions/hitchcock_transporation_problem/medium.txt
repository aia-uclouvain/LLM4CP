Transportation Problem
The Transportation Problem is a classic problem in operations research and management science that involves finding the most efficient way to transport goods from a set of sources to a set of destinations. 

The main decision variables in this problem are the elements of the transportation matrix `x`, where `x[i,j]` represents the amount of goods transported from source `i` to destination `j`. 

The key constraints in this problem are: 
- The total amount of goods transported from each source must equal the available supply at that source. 
- The total amount of goods transported to each destination must equal the demand at that destination. 
- The amount of goods transported from each source to each destination must be greater than or equal to a minimum value.

The objective of the Transportation Problem is to minimize the total amount of goods transported, which is represented by the variable `total`. In other words, the goal is to find the transportation plan that meets all the supply and demand constraints while transporting the smallest possible amount of goods. 

In this specific model, the problem is defined with 5 sources and 5 destinations, and the available supply at each source and the demand at each destination are given as input data. The model uses a constraint programming approach to find the optimal transportation plan that minimizes the total amount of goods transported.
