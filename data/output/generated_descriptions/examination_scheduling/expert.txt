name: Examination Scheduling Problem

description: The Examination Scheduling Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves assigning exams to time slots and rooms while satisfying certain constraints. The problem assumes that students in the same year will study the same subjects and that each subject requires only one exam. The goal is to generate a schedule that indicates the day of the week and the room assigned to each exam, taking into account practical constraints such as room capacity and the limit on the number of exams a student can take in a day.

variables: $s_{i,j}, xrooms_i, xslots_i, xdays_i, z$ 
These variables represent the following: $s_{i,j}$ is a matrix where $s_{i,j} = k$ means that exam $k$ is assigned to time slot $i$ and room $j$, $xrooms_i$ is the room assigned to exam $i$, $xslots_i$ is the time slot assigned to exam $i$, $xdays_i$ is the day of the week assigned to exam $i$, and $z$ is the number of used rooms.

constraints: $\forall i \in 1..n: \exists j \in 1..num\_slots, k \in 1..num\_rooms: s_{j,k} = i$, 
$\forall i \in large\_rooms\_ids: xrooms_i \in large\_rooms$, 
$\forall i \in 1..n: s_{xslots_i,xrooms_i} = i$, 
$\forall y \in 1..num\_years, d \in 1..num\_days: \sum_{i \in 1..n} (years_i = y \wedge xdays_i = d) \leq 2$, 
$z = \sum_{j \in 1..num\_rooms} \exists i \in 1..n: xrooms_i = j$
These constraints ensure that each exam is assigned to a time slot and room, large rooms are assigned to exams that require them, the $s$ matrix is consistent with the $xrooms$, $xslots$, and $xdays$ variables, no student takes more than two exams in a day, and the number of used rooms is calculated correctly.

objective: minimize $z$ 
The objective of the problem is to minimize the number of used rooms, which means finding a schedule that assigns exams to the fewest possible number of rooms while satisfying all the constraints.
