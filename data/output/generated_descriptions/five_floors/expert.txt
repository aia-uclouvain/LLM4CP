name: Five Floors Problem
description: The problem involves five people - Baker, Cooper, Fletcher, Miller, and Smith - who live on the first five floors of an apartment house. The task is to determine the floor on which each person lives, given certain constraints about their relative locations.
variables: $Baker, Cooper, Fletcher, Miller, Smith$ are decision variables representing the floor numbers (from 1 to 5) where each person lives. These variables are used to assign a floor to each person.
constraints: $Baker \neq 5, Cooper \neq 1, Fletcher \neq 5 \land Fletcher \neq 1, Miller > Cooper, |Smith - Fletcher| > 1, |Fletcher - Cooper| > 1, alldifferent(x)$ are the constraints of the problem. These constraints specify the restrictions on the floor assignments, such as Baker not living on the fifth floor, Cooper not living on the first floor, Fletcher not living on the first or fifth floor, Miller living on a higher floor than Cooper, Smith not living on a floor adjacent to Fletcher's, Fletcher not living on a floor adjacent to Cooper's, and all people living on different floors.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of floors to people, such that all constraints are met. Since the problem is a satisfaction problem (solve satisfy), there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
