Golomb Ruler Problem
The Golomb Ruler problem is about finding the shortest ruler with a given number of marks such that all distances between marks are unique.
The main variables are the positions of the marks on the ruler, which are typically represented as integers.
The basic restrictions are that all marks must be at distinct positions, all distances between marks must be unique, and the first mark must be at position 0.
The goal of the problem is to minimize the length of the ruler, which is the position of the last mark.
