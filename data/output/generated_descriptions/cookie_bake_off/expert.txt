name: Cookie Bake Off problem
description: The problem is about finding the best recipe for a perfect cookie based on past attempts with varying amounts of sugar, flour, and butter, and their corresponding judge's scores. The goal is to determine the recipe that has the best chance of achieving a perfect score of 100. 
variables: $x = [x_1, x_2, x_3], diffs = [diffs_1, diffs_2, ..., diffs_{15}]$ 
These are the decision variables where $x_i$ represents the coefficient of each ingredient (sugar, flour, and butter) in the optimal recipe, and $diffs_i$ represents the difference between the actual score and the predicted score for each past recipe.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 15\}: scores[i, 4] = \sum_{j=1}^{3} (x_j \cdot scores[i, j]) + diffs_i$
These constraints model the relationship between the ingredients, their coefficients, and the actual scores of the past recipes. The constraints state that the actual score of each past recipe is equal to the sum of the products of the coefficients and the amounts of each ingredient, plus the difference between the actual and predicted scores.
objective: minimize $diffs\_sum = \sum_{i=1}^{15} |diffs_i|$ 
The objective is to minimize the sum of the absolute differences between the actual scores and the predicted scores of the past recipes. This minimization problem aims to find the coefficients $x$ that best explain the relationship between the ingredients and the scores, thereby identifying the recipe with the highest chance of achieving a perfect score.
