Safe Cracking Puzzle
The problem is about finding a combination of 9 digits that satisfies a set of given constraints, representing a safe cracking puzzle.
The main decision variables are C1 to C9, which represent the 9 digits of the combination, and LD, an array of these variables. Each variable C1 to C9 can take a value from 1 to 9, representing a digit in the combination.
The key constraints are: 
- all digits in the combination must be different, 
- the difference between the 4th and 6th digits must be equal to the 7th digit, 
- the product of the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd digits must be equal to the sum of the 8th and 9th digits, 
- the sum of the 2nd, 3rd, and 6th digits must be less than the 8th digit, 
- the 9th digit must be less than the 8th digit, 
- no digit can be in its corresponding position (i.e., the 1st digit cannot be 1, the 2nd digit cannot be 2, and so on).
The objective of the problem is to find a combination that satisfies all these constraints, which is a satisfaction problem, not an optimization problem, so there is no specific value being minimized or maximized, but rather a feasible solution is being sought.
