The Fifth Trial puzzle
The problem is about finding the correct arrangement of a lady and a tiger behind two doors, based on given statements about the contents of each door.
The main decision variables are x(i,j) and t(i), where x(i,j) represents whether door i hides prize j (1 for the lady and 2 for the tiger), and t(i) represents whether the statement on door i is true.
The key constraints are: each door hides one prize, the statement on each door is consistent with its contents, and the statements on the doors are logically consistent with each other. Specifically, the statement on door 1 is true if and only if the lady is behind either door 1 or door 2, and the statement on door 2 is true if and only if the lady is behind door 1.
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, such that all constraints are met, which is achieved by using the "solve satisfy" statement in the model.
