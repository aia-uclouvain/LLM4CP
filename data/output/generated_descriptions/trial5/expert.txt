name: The Fifth Trial puzzle
description: The Fifth Trial puzzle is a classic puzzle in the field of logic and constraint programming. The puzzle states that there are two doors, behind one of which is a lady and behind the other is a tiger. In front of each door, there is a statement that may be true or false. The goal is to figure out which door hides the lady and which door hides the tiger, based on the given statements. 
variables: $x_{i,j}, t_i$ where $i \in \{1,2\}$ and $j \in \{1,2\}$, $x_{i,j}$ represents whether door $i$ hides prize $j$ (1 for lady, 2 for tiger), and $t_i$ represents whether the statement on door $i$ is true.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1,2\}: \sum_{j=1}^{2} x_{i,j} = 1$, $x_{i,i} = t_i$, $x_{1,1} + x_{2,1} - t_1 = 0$, $x_{1,1} = t_2$, which ensure that each door hides exactly one prize, the statement on each door is consistent with the prize it hides, and the statements on the doors are logically consistent with each other.
