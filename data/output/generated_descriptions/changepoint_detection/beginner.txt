Changepoint Detection Problem

The Changepoint Detection Problem is about finding the point in time when a significant change occurs in a sequence of data. In everyday terms, imagine you are monitoring the number of text messages you send each day, and you want to know if there is a day when your texting habits suddenly change.

The main variables are:
- `cp`: the changepoint, which is the point in time when the change occurs
- `mean1` and `mean2`: the average values of the data before and after the changepoint, respectively
- `mean_diff1` and `mean_diff2`: the total differences between the actual data and the average values before and after the changepoint, respectively

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- The data is divided into two segments at the changepoint
- The average values of the data before and after the changepoint are calculated
- The total differences between the actual data and the average values are calculated

The goal of the problem is to minimize the total difference between the actual data and the average values, which is represented by the variable `z`. By minimizing `z`, we can find the most likely point in time when a significant change occurs in the data.
