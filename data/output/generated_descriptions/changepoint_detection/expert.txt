name: Changepoint Detection Problem
description: The Changepoint Detection Problem is a classic problem in time series analysis and probabilistic programming. It involves identifying a point in a time series where the underlying distribution or behavior of the data changes. This change can be abrupt or gradual. The problem is often illustrated with examples such as detecting changes in coal mining disasters over time or shifts in text messaging habits.
variables: $cp, mean1, mean2, mean\_diff1, mean\_diff2, z$ - These are the decision variables in the problem. $cp$ represents the changepoint, the point at which the behavior of the time series changes. $mean1$ and $mean2$ are the means of the data before and after the changepoint, respectively. $mean\_diff1$ and $mean\_diff2$ represent the sum of absolute differences between each data point and the mean before and after the changepoint. $z$ is the total sum of these absolute differences, which is the objective function to be minimized.
constraints: $cp \in \{2, \ldots, len-1\}$, $mean1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{cp-1} data[i]}{cp}$, $mean2 = \frac{\sum_{i=cp}^{len} data[i]}{len-cp+1}$, $mean\_diff1 = \sum_{i=1}^{len} (i < cp) \cdot |data[i] - mean1|$, $mean\_diff2 = \sum_{i=1}^{len} (i \geq cp) \cdot |data[i] - mean2|$, $z = mean\_diff1 + mean\_diff2$ - These constraints define the relationships between the decision variables and ensure that $cp$ is within the valid range, and that $mean1$, $mean2$, $mean\_diff1$, $mean\_diff2$, and $z$ are calculated correctly based on $cp$ and the input data.
objective: Minimize $z$ - The objective of the problem is to find the changepoint $cp$ that minimizes the total sum of absolute differences $z$ between the data points and their respective means before and after the changepoint.
