**Changepoint Detection Problem**

The Changepoint Detection Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming and probabilistic programming. It involves identifying a point in a time series data where the underlying distribution or behavior of the data changes.

**Decision Variables**

The main decision variables in this problem are:

* `cp`: the changepoint, which represents the index in the time series data where the change occurs.
* `mean1` and `mean2`: the means of the data before and after the changepoint, respectively.
* `mean_diff1` and `mean_diff2`: the sum of absolute differences between the data points and the mean before and after the changepoint, respectively.
* `z`: the total sum of absolute differences, which is the objective function to be minimized.

**Constraints**

The key constraints in this problem are:

* The data is divided into two segments at the changepoint `cp`.
* The mean of the data before the changepoint is calculated as the sum of the data points before `cp` divided by the number of data points before `cp`.
* The mean of the data after the changepoint is calculated as the sum of the data points after `cp` divided by the number of data points after `cp`.
* The sum of absolute differences between the data points and the mean before the changepoint is calculated as the sum of `abs(data[i] - mean1)` for all `i` less than `cp`.
* The sum of absolute differences between the data points and the mean after the changepoint is calculated as the sum of `abs(data[i] - mean2)` for all `i` greater than or equal to `cp`.

**Objective**

The objective of the problem is to minimize the total sum of absolute differences `z`, which is the sum of `mean_diff1` and `mean_diff2`. This is equivalent to finding the changepoint that best separates the data into two segments with different means.
