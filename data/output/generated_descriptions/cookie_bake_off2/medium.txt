Cookie Bake Off problem
The problem is about finding the best recipe for baking cookies based on past attempts with varying amounts of sugar, flour, and butter, and their corresponding judge's scores. The goal is to determine which new recipe has the best chance of achieving a perfect score of 100.

The main decision variables are `x`, an array of three variables representing the weights of sugar, flour, and butter in the new recipe. These variables are used to calculate the predicted score of the new recipe.

The key constraints are:
- The total amount of sugar, flour, and butter in the new recipe must sum up to 8 cups, although this constraint is not explicitly stated in the provided model, it is mentioned in the problem description.
- The predicted score of each past recipe is calculated as the weighted sum of the amounts of sugar, flour, and butter, plus some error term (`diffs_plus` and `diffs_minus`).
- The error terms (`diffs_plus` and `diffs_minus`) represent the difference between the actual score and the predicted score for each past recipe.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the sum of the absolute errors (`diffs_sum`) between the actual scores and the predicted scores for all past recipes. This is done to find the best fit for the relationship between the ingredients and the scores, which can then be used to predict the score of the new recipes.
