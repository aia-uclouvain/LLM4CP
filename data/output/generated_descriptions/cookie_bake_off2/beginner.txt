Cookie Bake Off problem
The problem is about finding the best recipe for baking cookies that will get a perfect score of 100 from a judge. The recipe consists of three ingredients: sugar, flour, and butter. The total amount of these ingredients is 8 cups. 
The main variables are the amounts of sugar, flour, and butter in the recipe, represented by x. These variables determine how much of each ingredient to use in the recipe.
The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are that the total amount of the three ingredients must be 8 cups, and the amounts of sugar, flour, and butter must be non-negative. The problem also uses data from 15 previous recipes, including the amounts of sugar, flour, and butter used and the corresponding judge's scores.
The goal of the problem is to find the recipe that has the best chance of achieving a perfect score of 100, by minimizing the difference between the predicted scores and the actual scores of the previous recipes.
