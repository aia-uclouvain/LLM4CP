The Huey, Dewey, and Louie Problem
The problem is about determining the guilt status of three individuals, Huey, Dewey, and Louie, based on their statements and the assumption that they cannot tell a lie.
The main decision variables are `huey`, `dewey`, and `louie`, which are boolean variables representing whether each individual is guilty or not.
The key constraints are: 
- Huey's statement implies that Dewey and Louie have the same guilt status, i.e., if one is guilty, the other is also guilty, and if one is not guilty, the other is also not guilty.
- Dewey's statement implies that if Huey is guilty, then Dewey is also guilty.
- Louie's statement implies that Dewey and Louie cannot both be guilty at the same time.
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies all the given constraints, which is a feasibility problem, not an optimization problem. In other words, the goal is to determine the guilt status of Huey, Dewey, and Louie that is consistent with their statements, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
