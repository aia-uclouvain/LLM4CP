Hidato Puzzle
The Hidato puzzle is a logic-based puzzle where the goal is to connect numbered tiles in a grid by finding a path that visits each number in sequential order, with consecutive numbers being adjacent either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

The main decision variables in this problem are represented by the 2D array `x`, where `x[i, j]` denotes the number that should be placed at position `(i, j)` in the grid. The values of `x` range from 1 to `r*c`, where `r` and `c` are the number of rows and columns in the grid, respectively.

The key constraints in the Hidato puzzle can be described as follows: 
- Each number from 1 to `r*c` must be placed exactly once in the grid.
- The fixed tiles, represented by the `puzzle` array, must be placed at their corresponding positions in the grid.
- For each number `k` in the grid, there must exist a adjacent position (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) that contains the next number `k+1`.
These constraints ensure that the numbers in the grid form a connected path, with consecutive numbers being adjacent.

The objective of the Hidato puzzle is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, effectively connecting the numbered tiles in a sequential order. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that meets all the constraints.
