Changepoint Detection Problem

The Changepoint Detection Problem is about finding the point in time when a significant change occurs in a sequence of data. In everyday terms, imagine you are monitoring the number of text messages you send each day, and you want to know if there is a day when your texting habits suddenly change.

The main variables in this problem are:
- `cp`: the changepoint, which is the day when the change occurs
- `mean1` and `mean2`: the average number of text messages sent before and after the changepoint, respectively
- `data`: the sequence of daily text message counts
- `z`: a measure of the total difference between the actual data and the expected values based on the means before and after the changepoint

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- The changepoint `cp` must be a day within the sequence of data
- The means `mean1` and `mean2` are calculated based on the data before and after the changepoint
- The total difference `z` is calculated based on the differences between the actual data and the expected values

The goal of the problem is to minimize `z`, which means finding the changepoint that best explains the change in the data. In other words, the goal is to find the day when the change occurs, and the average values before and after the change, such that the total difference between the actual data and the expected values is as small as possible.
