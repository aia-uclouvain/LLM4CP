name: Party Mixing Problem
description: The Party Mixing Problem is a classic problem in constraint programming where a group of people are seated at multiple tables and the goal is to rearrange them in a way that maximizes the number of different people each person talks to. In this specific problem, 27 people are seated at 4 tables, but the model is generalized to handle 24 tables with 27 people in total, which is not a typical party mixing scenario, however the model provided seems to be a simplification of a more complex problem.
variables: $L = [L_1, L_2, ..., L_{Total}]$, where $L$ is an array of decision variables representing the new seating arrangement, with $L_i$ denoting the new seat of the person initially seated at seat $i$. The decision variables represent the permutation of people after rearrangement.
constraints: $\forall I \in 1..NumTables, J \in 1..NumAtTables: (1+((L[K]-1)) div NumAtTables) \neq (1+(((I-1)*NumAtTables+J)-1) div NumAtTables) \land circuit(L)$, where the first constraint ensures that people who start at table $T$ do not stay at the same table, and the second constraint ensures that there are no sub-circuits (sub-loops) in the permutation, which also implies that all values in $L$ are different. Additional constraints are imposed to prevent two neighbors from the same table from sitting beside each other in the new configuration.
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy the given constraints, which implicitly maximizes the number of different people each person talks to, as the model aims to find a valid permutation that meets the constraints, rather than explicitly optimizing a specific objective function.
