Social Golfer Problem
The Social Golfer Problem is about organizing a group of people into smaller groups, ensuring that each person interacts with as many different people as possible. 
The main decision variable is L, an array representing the seating arrangement, where L[i] denotes the position of the person initially seated at position i.
The key constraints are: 
- each person must move to a different table, 
- two people from the same table cannot sit beside each other in the new configuration, 
- the seating arrangement must form a single cycle (or circuit) with no sub-cycles, 
- each position in the seating arrangement must be unique.
The objective of the Social Golfer Problem is to find a seating arrangement that satisfies these constraints, effectively maximizing the number of different people each person interacts with.
