name: "It's a tie" problem
description: The problem involves four colleagues, each wearing a unique and flamboyant tie, with different patterns such as smiling cupids, grinning leprechauns, dancing reindeer, and yellow happy faces. Each tie was given to the colleagues by a different relative, and the task is to match each colleague with the pattern on their tie and determine the relative who presented each tie.
variables: $crow, evans, hurley, speigler, cupids, happy\_faces, leprechauns, reindeer, ties, ties\_inv, daughter, father\_in\_law, sister, uncle, relative, relative\_inv$ 
These variables represent the following: 
- $crow, evans, hurley, speigler$ are constants representing the four colleagues, 
- $cupids, happy\_faces, leprechauns, reindeer$ are decision variables representing the colleague wearing each type of tie, 
- $ties$ and $ties\_inv$ are arrays of decision variables representing the mapping between colleagues and tie patterns, 
- $daughter, father\_in\_law, sister, uncle$ are decision variables representing the relative who gave each type of tie, 
- $relative$ and $relative\_inv$ are arrays of decision variables representing the mapping between colleagues and relatives.
constraints: $alldifferent(ties) \wedge alldifferent(relative) \wedge inverse(ties, ties\_inv) \wedge inverse(relative, relative\_inv) \wedge leprechauns \neq daughter \wedge crow \neq reindeer \wedge crow \neq happy\_faces \wedge speigler \neq uncle \wedge happy\_faces \neq sister \wedge (evans = leprechauns \wedge speigler = father\_in\_law \vee speigler = leprechauns \wedge evans = father\_in\_law) \wedge hurley = sister$ 
These constraints ensure that: 
- each colleague has a unique tie and a unique relative, 
- the mapping between colleagues and tie patterns, and between colleagues and relatives, is consistent, 
- the tie with the grinning leprechauns was not given by a daughter, 
- Mr. Crow's tie does not feature dancing reindeer or yellow happy faces, 
- Mr. Speigler's tie was not given by his uncle, 
- the tie with the yellow happy faces was not given by a sister, 
- Mr. Evans and Mr. Speigler have the tie with the grinning leprechauns and the tie given by a father-in-law, 
- Mr. Hurley received his tie from his sister.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the decision variables, such that all the constraints are satisfied, which allows us to determine the pattern on each colleague's tie and the relative who presented each tie.
