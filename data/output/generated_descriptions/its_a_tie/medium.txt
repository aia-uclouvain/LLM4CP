**It's a Tie Problem**
The problem is about matching four colleagues with the patterns on their flamboyant ties and determining the relative who presented each tie. 

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables are:
- `ties`: an array representing the pattern on each man's tie (cupids, happy faces, leprechauns, or reindeer).
- `relative`: an array representing the relative who presented each tie (daughter, father-in-law, sister, or uncle).
These variables represent the assignments of tie patterns to men and relatives to ties.

**Constraints**
The key constraints are:
- Each man has a unique tie pattern and a unique relative who presented the tie.
- The tie with leprechauns was not presented by a daughter.
- Mr. Crow's tie does not feature reindeer or happy faces.
- Mr. Speigler's tie was not presented by his uncle.
- The tie with happy faces was not presented by a sister.
- Mr. Evans and Mr. Speigler own the tie with leprechauns and the tie presented by a father-in-law, in some order.
- Mr. Hurley received his tie from his sister.

**Objective**
The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of tie patterns to men and relatives to ties, given the constraints. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function; instead, it aims to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
