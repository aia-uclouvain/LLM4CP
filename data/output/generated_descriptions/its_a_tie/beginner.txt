The "It's a tie" problem. 
This problem is about four men - Crow, Evans, Hurley, and Speigler - who each wear a different ridiculous tie to work, and we need to figure out who is wearing which tie and who gave it to them. 
The main variables are the men (Crow, Evans, Hurley, and Speigler), the tie patterns (cupids, happy faces, leprechauns, and reindeer), and the relatives who gave the ties (daughter, father-in-law, sister, and uncle). 
The basic restrictions are that no two men wear the same tie pattern, and each tie was given by a different relative. There are also specific clues, such as the tie with leprechauns wasn't given by a daughter, and Mr. Crow's tie doesn't have reindeer or happy faces. 
The goal is to match each man with the correct tie pattern and the relative who gave it to him, using the given clues to solve the puzzle.
