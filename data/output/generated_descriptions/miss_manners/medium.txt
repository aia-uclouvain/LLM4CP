Miss Manners Seating Problem
The Miss Manners seating problem is about finding an acceptable seating arrangement for guests at a dinner party, where the goal is to match people with the same hobbies and seat everyone next to a member of the opposite sex.

The main decision variable is the `seating` array, which represents the seating arrangement of the guests. Each element in the array corresponds to a guest, and its value is the index of the guest who will sit in that position.

The key constraints are: 
- All guests must be seated, and no two guests can sit in the same position, ensured by the `all_different` constraint.
- Guests of the same sex cannot sit next to each other, enforced by the `mix genders` constraint.
- Guests must be seated next to someone with whom they share at least one hobby, implemented by the `match_hobbies` predicate and constraint.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the total number of common hobbies between neighboring guests, represented by the `common_hobbies` variable. This is achieved by finding a seating arrangement that maximizes the sum of the number of common hobbies between each pair of adjacent guests.
