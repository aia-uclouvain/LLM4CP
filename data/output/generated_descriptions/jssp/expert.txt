name: Job-Shop Scheduling Problem (JSSP)
description: The Job-Shop Scheduling Problem is a classic problem in operations research and computer science that involves scheduling a set of jobs on a set of machines, subject to certain constraints. Each job has a specified processing order through the machines, and each machine can handle at most one job at a time. The objective is to schedule the jobs so as to minimize the maximum of their completion times.
variables: $x_{i,a}, Y_{i,j,a}, z, K$ - These are the decision variables in the problem. $x_{i,a}$ represents the starting time of job $i$ on machine $a$, $Y_{i,j,a}$ is a binary variable that indicates whether job $i$ is scheduled before job $j$ on machine $a$, $z$ represents the maximum completion time of all jobs (also known as the makespan), and $K$ is a large constant used to model the constraints.
constraints: $z \geq 0, z \leq K, x_{i,a} \geq 0, x_{i,a} \leq K, \sigma_{j,t1} \neq \sigma_{j,t2}, x_{i,a} \geq x_{j,a} + p_{j,a} - K \cdot Y_{i,j,a}, x_{j,a} \geq x_{i,a} + p_{i,a} - K \cdot (1 - Y_{i,j,a}), x_{j, \sigma_{j,t}} \geq x_{j, \sigma_{j,t-1}} + p_{j, \sigma_{j,t-1}}, z \geq x_{j, \sigma_{j,m}} + p_{j, \sigma_{j,m}}$ - These constraints model the problem. The first set of constraints ensures that the makespan and starting times are non-negative and bounded by $K$. The second constraint ensures that the processing order of each job is a permutation of the machines. The next two constraints model the disjunctive condition that each machine can handle at most one job at a time. The fifth constraint ensures that each job is processed on each machine in the specified order. The last constraint ensures that the makespan is greater than or equal to the completion time of each job.
objective: Minimize $z$ - The objective of the problem is to minimize the makespan, which is the maximum completion time of all jobs.
