name: Portfolio Optimization Problem
description: The problem is a classic example of a portfolio optimization problem in finance, where the goal is to allocate a certain amount of capital to a set of shares in order to maximize the return on investment while satisfying certain constraints, such as limiting the percentage of high-risk shares and ensuring a minimum amount of shares from a specific region.
variables: $frac_1, frac_2, ..., frac_{10}$, where $frac_i$ represents the percentage of capital allocated to share $i$, multiplied by 100 to convert it to an integer value. These decision variables determine the proportion of each share in the portfolio.
constraints: $\sum_{s \in RISK} frac_s \leq 33$, $\sum_{s \in NA} frac_s \geq 50$, $\sum_{s \in SHARES} frac_s = 100$, $frac_s \leq 30 \forall s \in SHARES$, which represent the following constraints: the total percentage of high-risk shares should not exceed 33%, the total percentage of shares from North America should be at least 50%, the total percentage of all shares should equal 100%, and the percentage of each share should not exceed 30%.
objective: Maximize the total return on investment, represented by the variable $return = \sum_{s \in SHARES} ret_s \cdot frac_s$, where $ret_s$ is the estimated return on investment for share $s$.
