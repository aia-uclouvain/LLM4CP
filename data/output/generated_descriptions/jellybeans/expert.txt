name: Jellybeans problem

description: The Jellybeans problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves determining the ages and number of jellybeans each of four children, Alice, Boris, Claire, and David, has. The problem provides three clues: Alice's age plus her jellybeans equals Boris's age plus his jellybeans, Claire's age is two more than Alice's jellybeans, and Alice's age equals the total number of jellybeans she and Claire have. The goal is to find David's age and number of jellybeans.

variables: $age = [age_1, age_2, age_3, age_4], beans = [beans_1, beans_2, beans_3, beans_4]$ 
These are arrays of decision variables representing the ages and number of jellybeans for each of the four children, where $age_i$ and $beans_i$ denote the age and number of jellybeans of child $i$, respectively.

constraints: $age_1 + beans_1 = age_2 + beans_2 \land age_3 = beans_1 + 2 \land age_1 = beans_1 + beans_3 \land \bigwedge_{i=1}^{4} age_i \in \{6, 7, 8, 9\} \land \bigwedge_{i=1}^{4} beans_i \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\} \land \bigwedge_{i \neq j} age_i \neq age_j \land \bigwedge_{i \neq j} beans_i \neq beans_j$
These constraints ensure that the given clues are satisfied, each child has a unique age and number of jellybeans, and the ages and number of jellybeans are within the specified ranges.

objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of ages and jellybeans to the children, there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
