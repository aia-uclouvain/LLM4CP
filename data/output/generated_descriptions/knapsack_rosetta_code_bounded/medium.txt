**0-1 Knapsack Problem**
The 0-1 Knapsack Problem is a classic problem in computer science and operations research. 

**Problem Description**
The problem is about a tourist who wants to pack a knapsack for a trip with a limited capacity of 4 kg. The tourist has a list of items to choose from, each with a weight and a value. The goal is to select a subset of items to include in the knapsack such that the total weight does not exceed 4 kg and the total value is maximized.

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables are the array `x`, where `x[i]` represents the number of units of item `i` to include in the knapsack. The value of `x[i]` is an integer between 0 and the maximum number of available units of item `i`.

**Constraints**
The key constraints are:
- The total weight of the selected items must not exceed 4 kg (400 dag).
- The number of units of each item included in the knapsack must not exceed the available number of units.
- The items cannot be split, so only whole units of each item can be taken.

**Objective**
The objective is to maximize the total value of the items included in the knapsack. The solution will provide the optimal subset of items to include in the knapsack to achieve the maximum total value while satisfying the weight constraint.
