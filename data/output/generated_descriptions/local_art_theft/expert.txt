name: Local Art Theft Problem
description: The problem involves identifying the culprit of a local art theft based on statements from six suspects. Each suspect makes three statements denying the involvement of other suspects. The police know that exactly four of the suspects told one lie each, and all other statements are true. The goal is to determine who committed the crime.
variables: $b = [b_1, b_2, ..., b_6], counts = [c_1, c_2, ..., c_6]$ where $b_i$ represents whether suspect $i$ is the culprit, and $c_i$ represents the number of suspects that suspect $i$ accuses, which should be 2 for the culprit and 3 for the non-culprits who tell one lie.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., 6\} (b_i \rightarrow \forall j \in \{1, 2, ..., 6\} (noblame_{i, j} = 1 \rightarrow \neg b_j)) \wedge c_i = \sum_{j=1}^{6} (noblame_{i, j} = 1) (1 - b_j)$, $\sum_{i=1}^{6} (c_i = 2) = 4$ where the first constraint ensures that the culprit does not accuse themselves and that the number of accused suspects is consistent with the statements, and the second constraint ensures that exactly four suspects tell one lie.
objective: The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
