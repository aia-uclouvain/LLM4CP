name: ABC End View puzzle
description: The ABC End View puzzle is a classical puzzle where the goal is to fill a grid with letters such that each letter appears exactly once in each row and column, with some cells remaining empty. The puzzle is defined by a set of hints, which specify the first letter that appears in each row and column, as well as the last letter. The puzzle may also have a diagonal constraint, which requires that the letters on the diagonals of the grid are also unique.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ and $j$ are the row and column indices, respectively. These variables represent the letter that appears in the $i$-th row and $j$-th column of the grid, with $x_{i,j} = 0$ indicating an empty cell.
constraints: 
$\forall i \in 1..n: global\_cardinality([x_{i,j} | j \in 1..n], gcc, counts)$
$\forall j \in 1..n: global\_cardinality([x_{i,j} | i \in 1..n], gcc, counts)$
$\forall j \in 1..n: start\_with([x_{i,j} | i \in 1..n], row\_upper[j], dist)$
$\forall j \in 1..n: end\_with([x_{i,j} | i \in 1..n], row\_lower[j], dist)$
$\forall i \in 1..n: start\_with([x_{i,j} | j \in 1..n], col\_left[i], dist)$
$\forall i \in 1..n: end\_with([x_{i,j} | j \in 1..n], col\_right[i], dist)$
$\forall i \in 1..n: global\_cardinality([x_{i,i} | i \in 1..n], gcc, counts)$ (if diagonal constraint is true)
$\forall i \in 1..n: global\_cardinality([x_{i,n-i+1} | i \in 1..n], gcc, counts)$ (if diagonal constraint is true)
These constraints ensure that each row and column contains each letter exactly once, with some cells remaining empty, and that the hints are satisfied. The diagonal constraint ensures that the letters on the diagonals of the grid are also unique.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, with no specific value to minimize or maximize.
