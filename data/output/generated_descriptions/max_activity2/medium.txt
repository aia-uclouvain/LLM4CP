**Max Activity Problem**
The Max Activity problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves assigning a group of kids to a set of activities based on their preferences. 

The problem is about finding an optimal assignment of kids to activities, where each kid has a set of preferred activities, and each activity has a limited capacity. The goal is to assign kids to activities in a way that respects their preferences as much as possible, without exceeding the capacity of any activity.

The main decision variables in this problem are `x` and `scores`. The variable `x` represents the activity assigned to each kid, and `scores` represents the score assigned to each kid based on their preference. The score is calculated as 4 minus the preference number (1, 2, or 3), with a higher score indicating a higher preference.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each kid is assigned to one activity.
- The activity assigned to each kid is one of their preferred activities, if possible.
- The total number of kids assigned to each activity does not exceed its capacity.
- If a kid cannot be assigned to one of their preferred activities, they are assigned a score of 0.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the total score, which represents the overall satisfaction of the kids with their assigned activities. The total score is calculated as the sum of the scores of all kids. The problem is solved using a constraint programming approach, which involves finding a feasible assignment of kids to activities that satisfies all the constraints and maximizes the total score.
