**Eye Drop Distribution Problem**
The Eye Drop Distribution Problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves finding the optimal way to distribute eye drops of different types throughout the day. 

**Decision Variables**
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `x`: an array representing the time table, where `x[i]` is the type of eye drop to be taken at hour `i`.
- `dists`: an array representing the dispersion factor for each type of eye drop.
- `z`: the objective variable, which is the sum of the dispersion factors for all types of eye drops.

**Constraints**
The key constraints in this problem can be described as follows:
- Each type of eye drop must be taken a certain number of times per day, as specified by the `occ` array.
- The eye drops should be distributed as evenly as possible throughout the day.
- There can be at most one drop per hour.
- The last drop of the day must be of type 3 (for the night).
- The dispersion factor for each type of eye drop is calculated based on the differences between the times at which the drops are taken.

**Objective**
The objective of the Eye Drop Distribution Problem is to maximize the dispersion factor `z`, which represents the sum of the dispersion factors for all types of eye drops. This means that the goal is to separate the times at which the different types of eye drops are taken as much as possible, while still satisfying the constraints of the problem.
