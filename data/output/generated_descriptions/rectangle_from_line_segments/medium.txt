Largest Rectangle Problem
The problem is about constructing the largest possible rectangle using a set of given line segments of different lengths, where the segments can be added but not broken in between.

The main decision variables are:
- `x`: an array of variables representing which side of the rectangle each segment should be placed on (1: top, 2: right, 3: bottom, 4: left).
- `sum_sides`: an array of variables representing the total length of each side of the rectangle.
- `num_sides`: an array of variables representing the number of segments on each side of the rectangle.
- `area`: a variable representing the area of the constructed rectangle.

The key constraints are:
- Each segment must be assigned to one side of the rectangle.
- The total length of the top and bottom sides must be equal.
- The total length of the left and right sides must be equal.
- The area of the rectangle is calculated as the product of the lengths of two adjacent sides.

The objective of the problem is to maximize the area of the constructed rectangle. The model uses a constraint programming approach to find the optimal assignment of segments to sides that results in the largest possible rectangle.
