name: Largest Rectangle Problem

description: The problem is to construct the largest possible rectangle using a given set of line segments of different lengths, where the segments can be added but not broken in between. The goal is to find the arrangement of segments that maximizes the area of the resulting rectangle.

variables: $x_i, sum\_sides_j, num\_sides_j, area$ 
These variables represent the decision variables in the problem: $x_i$ is a variable that assigns each segment $i$ to one of the four sides of the rectangle, $sum\_sides_j$ is the total length of the segments assigned to side $j$, $num\_sides_j$ is the number of segments assigned to side $j$, and $area$ is the area of the resulting rectangle.

constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{num\_segments} s_i \* bool2int(x_i = j) = sum\_sides_j$, $sum\_sides_1 = sum\_sides_3$, $sum\_sides_2 = sum\_sides_4$, $area = sum\_sides_1 \* sum\_sides_2$ 
These constraints ensure that the total length of the segments assigned to each side is consistent, that opposite sides of the rectangle have the same length, and that the area of the rectangle is calculated correctly. Additionally, there are symmetry-breaking constraints to reduce the search space.

objective: Maximize $area$ 
The objective of the problem is to maximize the area of the resulting rectangle by finding the optimal assignment of segments to sides.
