name: Loading a Pair of Dice
description: The problem is about rigging a pair of dice to make the rolls of 2, 3, 4, …, 12 all have the same chance of occurring. This means that the probability distribution of the sum of the two dice should be uniform. The problem allows for the possibility of loading the dice with arbitrary numbers, including zero.
variables: $x, y, z, s$ where $x$ and $y$ are arrays representing the numbers on each die, $z$ is an array representing the distribution of the sum of the two dice, and $s$ is the sum of the distribution. $x$ and $y$ are decision variables that determine the numbers on each die, $z$ is a variable that represents the resulting distribution, and $s$ is a variable that ensures the distribution is not all zeros.
constraints: $\forall i \in [2..n*2] (z[i] = \sum_{j=1}^{n} \sum_{k=1}^{n} bool2int(x[j]+y[k] = i)) \wedge \forall i \in [3..n*2] (z[i] = z[i-1]) \wedge s > 0 \wedge lex\_lesseq(x,y) \wedge increasing(x) \wedge increasing(y)$ where the first constraint ensures that the distribution $z$ is calculated correctly, the second constraint ensures that the distribution is uniform, the third constraint ensures that the distribution is not all zeros, and the last three constraints are symmetry breaking constraints to reduce the search space.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment, i.e., to solve the problem such that all constraints are satisfied, which means finding a way to load the dice to make the rolls of 2, 3, 4, …, 12 all have the same chance of occurring.
