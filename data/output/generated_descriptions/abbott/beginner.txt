The Abbott's Window puzzle 
is about filling a grid with certain rules so that the total number of filled squares is as high as possible. 
The main variables are the squares in the grid (x), and the number of filled squares in each row (a), column (b), and some special diagonals (c, d, e, f). 
The basic restrictions are that each row, column, and some diagonals must have an even number of filled squares, and some specific squares must always be filled. 
The goal is to maximize the total number of filled squares in the grid.
