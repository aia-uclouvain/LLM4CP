Assignment Problem
The Assignment Problem is a classic problem in computer science and operations research that involves assigning a set of agents to a set of tasks, with each agent having a preference for each task.

The main decision variables in this problem are the binary variables `x[i,j]`, which represent whether programmer `i` is assigned to job `j`. The variable `jobs` represents the job assigned to each programmer, and `summax` represents the total preference of the group.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each programmer is assigned to exactly one job.
- Each job is assigned to at most one programmer.
- The total preference of the group is calculated by summing the preferences of each programmer for their assigned job.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the total preference of the group, which is equivalent to maximizing the total satisfaction of the programmers. In other words, the goal is to assign programmers to jobs in a way that minimizes the sum of the preferences, where a lower preference value indicates a more desirable job.
