name: Assignment Problem
description: The assignment problem is a classic problem in combinatorial optimization and operations research that involves finding the optimal way to assign a set of agents (in this case, programmers) to a set of tasks (in this case, programming jobs) such that the total preference of the group is maximized. Each programmer has a preference for each job, and the goal is to assign the programmers to the jobs in a way that minimizes the sum of the preferences.
variables: $x_{ij}, jobs_i, summax$ where $x_{ij}$ is a binary variable that represents whether programmer $i$ is assigned to job $j$, $jobs_i$ is an integer variable that represents the job assigned to programmer $i$, and $summax$ is an integer variable that represents the total preference of the group. These variables determine the assignment of programmers to jobs and the total preference of the group.
constraints: $\forall i \in \{1, \ldots, rows\} \sum_{j=1}^{cols} x_{ij} = 1$, $\forall j \in \{1, \ldots, cols\} \sum_{i=1}^{rows} x_{ij} = 1$, $summax = \sum_{i=1}^{rows} \sum_{j=1}^{cols} x_{ij} \cdot cost_{ij}$, $jobs_i = j \leftrightarrow x_{ij} = 1$ where the first two constraints ensure that each programmer is assigned to exactly one job and each job is assigned to exactly one programmer, the third constraint calculates the total preference of the group, and the fourth constraint converts the binary assignment matrix to a job assignment array.
objective: minimize $summax$ which represents the total preference of the group. The goal is to find an assignment that minimizes the sum of the preferences, which is equivalent to maximizing the total preference of the group.
