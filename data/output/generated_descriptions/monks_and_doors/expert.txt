name: Monks and Doors Problem

description: The Monks and Doors problem is a classic puzzle in the field of logic and constraint programming. It involves a room with four doors, where one or more doors may be an exit, and eight monks who make statements about the doors and each other's truthfulness. The goal is to determine which door is an exit, regardless of who is telling the truth and who is lying.

These constraints represent the relationships between the doors and the monks' statements, including the statements made by each monk and the condition that exactly one door is an exit. The constraints ensure that the solution satisfies all the given conditions and statements.

objective: satisfy 
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding the state of the doors and the truthfulness of the monks' statements that is consistent with all the given conditions.
