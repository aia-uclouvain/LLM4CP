The Monks and Doors problem.
The problem involves determining which door is an exit in a room with four doors, given statements from eight monks, where each monk is either telling the truth or lying.
The main decision variables are A, B, C, and D, representing whether doors A, B, C, and D are exits, and M1 to M8, representing whether monks 1 to 8 are telling the truth.
The key constraints are: 
- Monk 1's statement that door A is the exit must be true if and only if door A is indeed the exit.
- Monk 2's statement that at least one of doors B and C is the exit must be true if and only if at least one of them is indeed the exit.
- The statements of the other monks are linked by logical constraints, such as implications and equivalences, to the truth values of the doors and other monks' statements.
The objective of the problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, which will identify the door that is an exit, regardless of which monks are lying or telling the truth.
