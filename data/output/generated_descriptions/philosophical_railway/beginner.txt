The Philosophical Railway problem. 
It's about figuring out the jobs of five railway employees: James, Kant, Locke, Mill, and Nietzsche, who are the driver, guard, porter, signalman, and stationmaster. 
The main variables are the names of the employees and their corresponding jobs. 
The basic restrictions are the statements made by each employee, where each statement is either true or false, and each employee makes two statements. 
The goal is to find the job of the driver, by satisfying all the given statements and finding a valid assignment of jobs to the employees.
