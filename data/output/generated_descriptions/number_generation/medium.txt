The Devil's Word problem.
The Devil's Word problem is about finding a sequence of numbers and operations that satisfy certain conditions, specifically generating a target sum by applying a series of operations to a given set of numbers.
The main decision variables are `x`, an array of numbers, and `ops`, an array of operations to be applied between the numbers in `x`. The `x` array represents the numbers to be used in the calculation, and the `ops` array represents the operations to be applied between these numbers, such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.
The key constraints are that the numbers in `x` and the operations in `ops` must be used to generate a target sum, which is 666 in this case. Additionally, the numbers in `x` must match a given sequence of numbers, which is [72, 229, 107, 97, 110, 32, 75, 106, 101, 108, 108, 101, 114, 115, 116, 114, 97, 110, 100]. The operations in `ops` are limited to addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division, and are applied sequentially to the numbers in `x`.
The objective of the problem is to find a sequence of numbers and operations that satisfy the given constraints, specifically generating the target sum of 666 using the given sequence of numbers. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather seeks to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
