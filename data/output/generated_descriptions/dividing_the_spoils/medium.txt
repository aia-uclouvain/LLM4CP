The name of the problem is Dividing the Spoils. 
This problem is about dividing a certain number of chestnuts among three girls, Mary, Nellie, and Susie, in proportion to their ages. 
The main decision variables are mary, nellie, and susie, which represent the number of chestnuts each girl receives. 
The key constraints are that the ratio of Mary's chestnuts to Nellie's chestnuts is 4:3, the ratio of Mary's chestnuts to Susie's chestnuts is 6:7, and the total number of chestnuts the girls receive is 770. 
The problem's objective is to find a satisfying assignment of chestnuts to the girls, such that all the given constraints are met, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
