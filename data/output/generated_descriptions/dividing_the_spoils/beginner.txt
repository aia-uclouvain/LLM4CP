Dividing the Spoils 
The problem is about three girls - Mary, Nellie, and Susie - who want to divide 770 chestnuts among themselves in a way that the amount each girl gets is related to her age. 
The main variables are mary, nellie, and susie, which represent the number of chestnuts each girl gets. 
The basic restrictions are that the girls get chestnuts in a specific ratio: for every 4 chestnuts Mary gets, Nellie gets 3, and for every 6 chestnuts Mary gets, Susie gets 7, and together they get a total of 770 chestnuts. 
The goal of the problem is to find out how many chestnuts each girl gets, given these conditions.
