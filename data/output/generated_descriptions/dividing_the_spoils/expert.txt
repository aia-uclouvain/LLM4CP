name: Dividing the Spoils Puzzle
description: The problem describes a situation where three girls, Mary, Nellie, and Susie, divide a total of 770 chestnuts among themselves in a proportion related to their ages. The problem states that as often as Mary takes four chestnuts, Nellie takes three, and for every six that Mary receives, Susie takes seven. The goal is to find out how many chestnuts each girl gets.
variables: $mary, nellie, susie$ - These variables represent the number of chestnuts each girl receives, with $mary$ being the number of chestnuts Mary gets, $nellie$ being the number of chestnuts Nellie gets, and $susie$ being the number of chestnuts Susie gets.
constraints: $3 \cdot mary = 4 \cdot nellie \ \wedge \ 7 \cdot mary = 6 \cdot susie \ \wedge \ mary + nellie + susie = 770$ - These constraints represent the relationships between the number of chestnuts each girl receives. The first constraint states that the ratio of Mary's chestnuts to Nellie's chestnuts is 4:3, the second constraint states that the ratio of Mary's chestnuts to Susie's chestnuts is 6:7, and the third constraint states that the total number of chestnuts received by all girls is 770.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a satisfying assignment of values to the variables $mary, nellie, susie$ that satisfies all the given constraints, as indicated by the "solve satisfy" statement in the model.
