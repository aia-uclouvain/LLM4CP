name: Latin Square Puzzle with Additional Constraints
description: The problem is a 4x4 grid puzzle where the rows, columns, and two diagonals have given sums, and some clues are provided. The goal is to fill in the grid with numbers from 1 to 9 such that all the given sums are satisfied and the clues are respected.
variables: $x_{i,j}$ for $i, j = 1, \ldots, 4$, where $x_{i,j}$ represents the value in the $i$-th row and $j$-th column of the grid, which is a decision variable that can take any integer value from 1 to 9.
constraints: 
$\begin{aligned}
&x_{i,j} = \text{clues}_{i,j} \text{ for } i, j = 1, \ldots, 4 \text{ where } \text{clues}_{i,j} > 0 \\
&\sum_{j=1}^{4} x_{i,j} = \text{rowsums}_i \text{ for } i = 1, \ldots, 4 \\
&\sum_{i=1}^{4} x_{i,j} = \text{colsums}_j \text{ for } j = 1, \ldots, 4 \\
&\sum_{i=1}^{4} x_{i,i} = \text{diagsums}_1 \\
&\sum_{i=1}^{4} x_{i,4-i+1} = \text{diagsums}_2
\end{aligned}$ 
These constraints ensure that the given clues are respected, the row and column sums are satisfied, and the diagonal sums are also satisfied.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints, which is a satisfaction problem, so there is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
