name: Word Chain
description: The problem is to find a chain of words where each word starts with the last letter of the previous word.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $x_{i,j} = 1$ if the $i^{th}$ word is in the $j^{th}$ position of the chain, and $0$ otherwise. These variables represent the presence or absence of a word in a particular position in the chain.
constraints: $\sum_{i=1}^{4398} x_{i,j} = 1$ for all $j$, $\sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{i,j} \leq 1$ for all $i$, $lastletter(i) = firstletter(j)$ for all $i, j$ where $x_{i,j} = 1$ and $x_{j,i} = 1$. The first constraint ensures that each position in the chain contains exactly one word. The second constraint ensures that each word is used at most once. The third constraint ensures that the last letter of a word is the same as the first letter of the next word in the chain.
objective: Maximize $\sum_{i=1}^{4398} \sum_{j=1}^{n} x_{i,j}$, which is equivalent to maximizing the length of the chain.
