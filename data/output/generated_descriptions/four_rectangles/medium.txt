Rectangle Area Puzzle
The problem is about finding the area of a fourth rectangle in a large rectangle that is divided into four smaller rectangles, given the areas of the other three rectangles.
The main decision variables are `area1`, `area2`, `area3`, `area4`, `len`, `height`, `x`, and `y`, which represent the areas of the four rectangles and the dimensions of the large rectangle. Specifically, `x` and `y` represent the width and height of the first rectangle, `len` represents the total length of the large rectangle, and `height` represents the total height of the large rectangle.
The key constraints are that the areas of the first three rectangles are 13, 39, and 16, and that the area of each rectangle is equal to the product of its width and height. Additionally, the total area of the large rectangle is equal to the sum of the areas of the four smaller rectangles. In plain language, the constraints are: the area of the top-left rectangle is 13, the area of the top-right rectangle is 39, the area of the bottom-left rectangle is 16, and the area of the bottom-right rectangle is unknown; the width and height of each rectangle must be positive; and the total area of the large rectangle is the sum of the areas of the four smaller rectangles.
The objective of the problem is to find the area of the fourth rectangle, which is represented by the variable `area4`. The problem is a satisfaction problem, meaning that the goal is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific objective function.
