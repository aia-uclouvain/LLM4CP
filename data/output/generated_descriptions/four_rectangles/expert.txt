name: Rectangle Area Puzzle
description: The problem involves finding the area of a fourth rectangle in a large rectangle that is divided into four smaller rectangles, where the areas of three of the smaller rectangles are given as 16, 13, and 39.
variables: $x, y, len, height, area1, area2, area3, area4$ - These variables represent the dimensions of the rectangles and the areas of the four smaller rectangles, where $x$ and $y$ are the dimensions of the first smaller rectangle, $len$ and $height$ are the dimensions of the large rectangle, and $area1, area2, area3, area4$ are the areas of the four smaller rectangles.
constraints: $area1 = 13, area2 = 39, area3 = 16, x*y = area1, (len-x)*y = area2, (height-y)*x = area3, (height-y)*(len-x) = area4, (len*height) = area1+area2+area3+area4$ - These constraints represent the given areas of the three smaller rectangles, the relationships between the dimensions and areas of the rectangles, and the fact that the total area of the large rectangle is the sum of the areas of the four smaller rectangles.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find the value of $area4$, which is the area of the fourth rectangle, by satisfying all the given constraints.
