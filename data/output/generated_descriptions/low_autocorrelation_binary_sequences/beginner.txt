Low Autocorrelation Binary Sequences
The problem is about creating a sequence of numbers that have a special property. Imagine you have a long list of 1s and -1s, and you want to make sure that when you compare the list to itself, but shifted by a certain number of positions, the similarity between the two is as low as possible. 
The main variables are the sequence of numbers (called "Bits" or "x" or "seq") and the correlations between the sequence and its shifted versions (called "Correlations" or "c"). The sequence is made up of 1s and -1s, and the correlations are numbers that measure how similar the sequence is to its shifted versions.
The basic restrictions are that the sequence can only contain 1s and -1s, and the correlations are calculated by comparing the sequence to its shifted versions. The problem also has a symmetry restriction, which means that the first two numbers in the sequence must be -1.
The goal of the problem is to find a sequence that minimizes the sum of the squares of the correlations. In other words, we want to find a sequence where the similarity between the sequence and its shifted versions is as low as possible, and we measure this similarity by calculating the correlations and summing their squares.
