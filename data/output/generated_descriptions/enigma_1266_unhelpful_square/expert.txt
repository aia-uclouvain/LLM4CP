name: Unhelpful Squares (Enigma puzzle 1266)
description: The problem is a cross-figure puzzle where each answer is a different perfect square. The puzzle has a 3x5 grid with some digits already filled in. The task is to fill in the remaining blank cells with digits such that each answer (across and down) forms a perfect square. The constraint is that when a digit is placed in a box with an existing digit, it should be different from the existing digit, and in half the cases, it should be more than the existing digit, and in half the cases, it should be less.
variables: $x_{i,j}, y_k, nums_b$ where $i \in [1,3], j \in [1,5], k \in [1,15], b \in [1,7]$. These variables represent the matrix of digits ($x_{i,j}$), the digits in a one-dimensional array ($y_k$), and the perfect square numbers ($nums_b$) for each answer.
constraints: $\forall i \in [1,3], j \in [1,5]: y_{(i-1)*5+j} = x_{i,j}$, $\forall b \in [1,7]: nums_b = to\_num([y_k | k \in boxes_b]) \land nums_b \in perfect\_squares$, $\forall b \in [1,7]: nums_b \neq nums_{b'} \land b \neq b'$, $\forall k \in clue\_numbers: y_k \neq k$, $\sum_{k \in clue\_numbers} (y_k < k) = 3$. These constraints ensure that the matrix and the one-dimensional array of digits are consistent, each answer forms a perfect square, all answers are distinct, and the digits in the clue numbers are different and satisfy the condition of being more or less than the existing digit in half the cases.
objective: satisfy
