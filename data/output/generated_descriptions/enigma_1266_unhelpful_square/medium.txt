Unhelpful Squares
The Unhelpful Squares problem is a classic constraint programming puzzle where the goal is to fill in a grid with digits such that each row and column forms a perfect square, with some additional constraints.

The main decision variables in this problem are the values of the grid cells, represented by the `x` matrix, and the digits, represented by the `y` array. The `nums` array represents the perfect squares formed by the digits in each row and column.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each row and column must form a perfect square.
- All perfect squares must be distinct.
- The digits in the grid cells must be different from the given clue numbers.
- Exactly half of the digits should be less than the clue number, and the other half should be greater.

The objective of this problem is to find a solution that satisfies all the constraints, with no specific minimization or maximization goal. The problem is a satisfaction problem, where the goal is to find a feasible solution that meets all the requirements.
