**Social Golfer Problem**
The Social Golfer problem is a classic constraint programming problem that involves scheduling golf games between groups of golfers over several weeks. 
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `r[i][w]`: the visitor (golfer) for the ith visitee (group) at week w
- `e[i][w]`: the visitee (group) for the ith visitor (golfer) at week w
- `rg[i][w]`: the visitor group for the ith visitee at week w
- `eg[i][w]`: the visitee group for the ith visitor at week w
The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each week, all visitors must be different, and all visitees must be different, to ensure that no golfer plays with the same group twice in the same week.
- The visitor groups must be different for each visitee, and the visitee groups must be different for each visitor, to ensure that golfers from the same group do not play together.
- The channeling constraints ensure that the visitor and visitee arrays are consistent, i.e., if a visitor is assigned to a visitee, then the visitee must also be assigned to the visitor.
- The symmetry-breaking constraints ensure that the solution is unique and that the search space is reduced.
The objective of this problem is to find a schedule that satisfies all the constraints, which means finding a way to assign golfers to groups over several weeks such that all constraints are met. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints.
