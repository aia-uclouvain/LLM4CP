name: Social Golfer Problem
description: The Social Golfer problem is a classic problem in constraint programming that involves scheduling golfers into groups over a period of weeks, ensuring that each golfer plays with a different set of golfers each week.
variables: $r_{i,w}, e_{i,w}, rg_{i,w}, eg_{i,w}$, where $r_{i,w}$ represents the visitor (golfer) for the $i^{th}$ visitee (group) at week $w$, $e_{i,w}$ represents the visitee (group) for the $i^{th}$ visitor (golfer) at week $w$, $rg_{i,w}$ represents the visitor group for the $i^{th}$ visitee at week $w$, and $eg_{i,w}$ represents the visitee group for the $i^{th}$ visitor at week $w$.
constraints: $\forall w: Alldifferent(r_{1..n,w}), \forall w: Alldifferent(e_{1..n,w}), \forall i: Alldifferent(rg_{i,1..nWeeks}), \forall i: Alldifferent(eg_{i,1..nWeeks}), \forall w: Channel(r_{1..n,w}, e_{1..n,w}), (rg_{i,w}, r_{i,w}) \in vr\_table, (eg_{i,w}, e_{i,w}) \in ve\_table$, which ensure that each week all visitors are different, each week all visitees are different, the visitor groups are different for each visitee, the visitee groups are different for each visitor, the visitor and visitee arrays are linked, and the visitor and visitee groups are correctly linked.
objective: The problem is a feasibility problem, where the goal is to find a schedule that satisfies all the constraints, rather than minimizing or maximizing a specific value.
