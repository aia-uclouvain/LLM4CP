The problem is the Nurse Scheduling Problem. 
It is about creating schedules for nurses to work shifts at a hospital, ensuring that all shifts are covered and that each nurse works a reasonable number of hours. 
The main decision variables represent which nurse is assigned to which shift, and they can be thought of as a matrix where each row corresponds to a nurse and each column corresponds to a shift. 
The key constraints are that each shift must be covered by exactly one nurse, no nurse can work more than a certain number of consecutive shifts, and each nurse must have a minimum number of days off per week. 
The objective is to minimize the total number of shifts worked by all nurses, while also ensuring that the schedule is fair and reasonable for each nurse.
