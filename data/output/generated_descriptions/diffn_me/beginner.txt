Name of the problem: Non-Overlapping Rectangles Problem

The problem is about placing a set of rectangles in a way that none of them overlap with each other. Imagine you have a bunch of boxes of different sizes, and you want to put them on a table without any of them touching or covering each other.

The main variables are:
- `r`: a 2D array that represents the coordinates of the rectangles. Each row in the array corresponds to a rectangle, and the columns represent the x and y coordinates of the rectangle's corners.
- `areas`: an array that stores the areas of the rectangles.
- `n`: the number of rectangles.
- `m`: the number of dimensions (in this case, 4 for 2D rectangles, representing x and y coordinates).

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- No rectangle can overlap with any other rectangle.
- The rectangles are placed in a 2D space, with x and y coordinates.
- The size and position of each rectangle are defined by its coordinates.

The goal of the problem is to find a placement of the rectangles such that none of them overlap, given their areas. The problem can be solved in different ways, depending on the representation of the rectangles (e.g., by their corners, sizes, or a combination of both). The solution involves finding the coordinates of the rectangles that satisfy the non-overlap constraint.
