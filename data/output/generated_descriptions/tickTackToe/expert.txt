name: Tic Tac Toe
description: The problem is a classic game of Tic Tac Toe, where two players, black and white, take turns marking a space on a 3x3 grid, with the goal of getting three of their marks in a row, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally, while preventing their opponent from doing the same.
variables: $tickTackToe_{i,j,k}$, where $i \in \{0, \ldots, steps\}$, $j \in \{1, 2, 3\}$, and $k \in \{1, 2, 3\}$. These variables represent the state of the game board at each step, with $tickTackToe_{i,j,k}$ being the status of the field at row $j$ and column $k$ at step $i$, which can be either 0 (black), 1 (white), or 2 (empty).
constraints: 
$\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{0,j,k} = 2$ (initial state),
$\forall step \in \{1, \ldots, steps\}: \sum_{row, col \in \{1, 2, 3\}} tickTackToe_{step-1,row,col} = \sum_{row, col \in \{1, 2, 3\}} tickTackToe_{step,row,col}$ or $\sum_{row, col \in \{1, 2, 3\}} tickTackToe_{step-1,row,col} - ((step \mod 2) + 1) = \sum_{row, col \in \{1, 2, 3\}} tickTackToe_{step,row,col}$ (either nothing changes or there is just one change),
$\forall step \in \{1, \ldots, steps\}: \exists row, col \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{step-1,row,col} = 2 \land tickTackToe_{step,row,col} = (step \mod 2) + 1 \land \forall i, j \in \{1, 2, 3\}: (i \neq row \lor j \neq col) \rightarrow tickTackToe_{step,i,j} = tickTackToe_{step-1,i,j}$ (if a field is empty, it may be occupied in the next step),
$\forall step \in \{1, \ldots, steps\}: \forall row, col \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{step-1,row,col} \neq 2 \rightarrow tickTackToe_{step,row,col} = tickTackToe_{step-1,row,col}$ (if a player already took the field, then it belongs to the player forever),
$\exists row \in \{1, 2, 3\}: \forall col \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{steps,row,col} = WINNER \lor \exists col \in \{1, 2, 3\}: \forall row \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{steps,row,col} = WINNER \lor \forall i \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{steps,i,i} = WINNER \lor tickTackToe_{steps,1,3} = WINNER \land tickTackToe_{steps,2,2} = WINNER \land tickTackToe_{steps,3,1} = WINNER$ (goal state: WINNER wins),
$| \sum_{j,k \in \{1, 2, 3\}} bool2int(tickTackToe_{steps,j,k} = WINNER) - \sum_{j,k \in \{1, 2, 3\}} bool2int(tickTackToe_{steps,j,k} = LOOSER) | \leq 1$ (the number of WINNER's marks and LOOSER's marks must be almost equal),
$\sum_{j,k \in \{1, 2, 3\}} bool2int(tickTackToe_{steps,j,k} < 2) = steps$ (the total number of marks must be equal to the number of steps),
$\forall row \in \{1, 2, 3\}: \exists col \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{steps,row,col} \neq LOOSER \lor \forall col \in \{1, 2, 3\}: \exists row \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{steps,row,col} \neq LOOSER \lor \exists i \in \{1, 2, 3\}: tickTackToe_{steps,i,i} \neq LOOSER \lor tickTackToe_{steps,1,3} \neq LOOSER \lor tickTackToe_{steps,2,2} \neq LOOSER \lor tickTackToe_{steps,3,1} \neq LOOSER$ (LOOSER has not won).
objective: The objective of the problem is to satisfy all the constraints, which means finding a sequence of moves that leads to a winning state for the specified player (WINNER) in the given number of steps.
