Traveling Tournament Problem with Predefined Venues
The problem is about organizing a tournament where several teams play against each other in a series of rounds. Each team has to play every other team exactly once, and the venue of each game (home or away) has already been decided. The goal is to find a schedule that minimizes the total distance traveled by all teams.

The main variables are:
- opponent: a matrix that determines which team plays against which team in each round
- venue: a matrix that indicates whether a team plays at home or away in each round
- travel: a matrix that calculates the distance traveled by each team in each round

The basic restrictions or rules of the problem are:
- A team cannot play against itself
- If team A plays against team B, then team B must play against team A
- Each team must play against every other team exactly once
- The venue of each game is already decided and must be respected
- There can be at most 3 consecutive home games and at most 3 consecutive away games for each team

The goal of the problem is to minimize the total distance traveled by all teams. This is achieved by finding a schedule that respects all the rules and restrictions while keeping the total travel distance as low as possible.
