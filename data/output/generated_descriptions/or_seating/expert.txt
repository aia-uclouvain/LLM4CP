name: Seating Problem of OR'ers
description: The problem involves seating 19 people around a circular table to maximize the total compatibility score of adjacent individuals. The compatibility scores are given in a 19x19 matrix, where each entry represents the score between two people. The goal is to find the seating arrangement that maximizes the sum of the compatibility scores of adjacent individuals.
variables: $x, z, s, total$ 
These variables represent the following: 
- $x$ is an array of decision variables representing the circuit of people around the table, 
- $z$ is an array of decision variables representing the actual seating arrangement, 
- $s$ is an array of decision variables representing the compatibility scores of adjacent individuals, 
- $total$ is the total compatibility score of all adjacent individuals.
constraints: $circuit\_me(x, z), \forall i \in 1..n (s[i] = scores[i, x[i]])$ 
These constraints represent the following: 
- $circuit\_me(x, z)$ ensures that the seating arrangement forms a circuit, 
- $\forall i \in 1..n (s[i] = scores[i, x[i]])$ ensures that the compatibility score of each individual and their adjacent person is correctly calculated based on the given scores matrix.
objective: maximize $total$
