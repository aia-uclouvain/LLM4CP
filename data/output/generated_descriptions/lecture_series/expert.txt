name: Lecture Series Puzzle
description: The problem is to determine the lecturer and subject for each day of the week, given certain constraints about the lecturers and subjects. There are five days (Monday to Friday), five lecturers (two women and three men), and five subjects (choosing a college, physical hygiene, modern art, nutrition, and study habits). The constraints include information about the lecturers' names, the subjects they taught, and the days on which they taught.
variables: $choosing\_a\_college, physical\_hygiene, modern\_art, nutrition, study\_habits, Alice, Bernadette, Charles, Duane, Eddie, Felicidad, Garber, Haller, Itakura, Jeffreys$ - These variables represent the day of the week for each subject and lecturer. The subjects are assigned to a day of the week (1-5), and the lecturers are also assigned to a day of the week.
constraints: $all\_different(lectures) \wedge all\_different(first\_name) \wedge all\_different(last\_name) \wedge Alice = Monday \wedge Charles = physical\_hygiene \wedge Charles \neq Friday \wedge Jeffreys = nutrition \wedge (modern\_art = Charles \vee modern\_art = Duane \vee modern\_art = Eddie) \wedge (Itakura = Alice \vee Itakura = Bernadette) \wedge |Itakura - study\_habits| = 1 \wedge Itakura \neq study\_habits \wedge Haller > Eddie \wedge Duane = Felicidad \wedge Duane < modern\_art$ - These constraints ensure that each subject and lecturer is assigned to a unique day of the week, and that the given conditions are satisfied. The conditions include constraints on the lecturers' names, subjects, and days, as well as constraints on the relative order of certain lecturers and subjects.
objective: satisfy - The objective of the problem is to find an assignment of subjects and lecturers to days of the week that satisfies all the given constraints. There is no explicit objective function to minimize or maximize.
