name: Ramsey Problem
description: The Ramsey problem is a classic problem in graph theory and combinatorics that deals with the coloring of edges in a complete graph. The goal is to find the minimum number of vertices in a complete graph such that when the edges are colored with two colors, there will always be a monochromatic subgraph of a certain size. In other words, it is a problem of finding the smallest complete graph that guarantees the existence of either a group of vertices where all pairs of vertices know each other or a group of vertices where no pairs of vertices know each other.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}$ and $i < j$, $graph((i,j))$ 
These are decision variables representing the color of the edge between vertices $i$ and $j$ in the complete graph, where $n$ is the number of vertices.
constraints: $\forall i, j, k \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}$, $NValues(x_{i,j}, x_{i,k}, x_{j,k}) > 1$, $\forall i, j \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}$, $i < j$, $(i,j) \in defined(graph)$, $\forall i \in \{1, 2, ..., n\}$, $!(i,i) \in defined(graph)$, $\forall subgraph\_vertices \subseteq \{1, 2, ..., n\}$, $|subgraph\_vertices| = k$, $!(\forall \{i,j\} \subseteq subgraph\_vertices$, $graph((i,j)) = red)$, $\forall subgraph\_vertices \subseteq \{1, 2, ..., n\}$, $|subgraph\_vertices| = l$, $!(\forall \{i,j\} \subseteq subgraph\_vertices$, $graph((i,j)) = blue)$
These constraints ensure that there are no monochromatic triangles in the graph, the graph is complete, and there are no monochromatic subgraphs of size $k$ or $l$.
objective: minimize $Maximum(x)$, which is equivalent to minimizing the maximum color value used in the graph. However, the main objective of the Ramsey problem is to find the smallest complete graph that guarantees the existence of a monochromatic subgraph of a certain size, which is achieved by finding a coloring that satisfies the given constraints.
