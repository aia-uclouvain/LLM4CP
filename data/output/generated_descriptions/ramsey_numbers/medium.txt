**Ramsey Problem**
The Ramsey problem is about finding the minimum number of vertices in a complete graph such that, when the edges are colored with two colors, there is always a monochromatic subgraph of a certain size. 

The main decision variables in this problem are the colors assigned to the edges between vertices. In the given models, these variables are represented as `x[i][j]` in the PyCSP3 model and `graph` in the Essence model, which denote the color of the edge between vertices `i` and `j`.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- The graph is complete, meaning every vertex is connected to every other vertex.
- The graph does not contain a monochromatic subgraph of a certain size, where a monochromatic subgraph is a subset of vertices where all edges between them have the same color.
- The colors assigned to the edges are symmetric, meaning the color of the edge from vertex `i` to vertex `j` is the same as the color of the edge from vertex `j` to vertex `i`.
- There are no self-loops, meaning a vertex is not connected to itself.

The objective of the Ramsey problem is to minimize the maximum color used, which corresponds to minimizing the number of different colors needed to color the edges such that there is no monochromatic subgraph of a certain size. However, the traditional objective of the Ramsey problem is to find the smallest number of vertices such that any coloring of the edges will result in a monochromatic subgraph of a certain size. The given models seem to be focused on finding a coloring that avoids monochromatic subgraphs of certain sizes, rather than directly computing the Ramsey number.
