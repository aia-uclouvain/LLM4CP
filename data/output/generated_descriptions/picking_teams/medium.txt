Team Formation Problem
The Team Formation Problem is about dividing a set of people with different strengths into two teams, with the goal of making the teams as balanced as possible. 

The main decision variables are `x`, which represents the team assignment for each person, and `d`, which represents the difference in total strength between the two teams. 

The key constraints are: 
- Each person must be assigned to one of the two teams.
- The two teams must have the same number of people.
- The first person is assigned to team 1 to break symmetry.
- The difference in total strength between the two teams must be calculated correctly.

The objective of the problem is to minimize the difference in total strength between the two teams, `d`. In other words, the goal is to form two teams that are as balanced as possible in terms of total strength.
