Hanging Weights Puzzle
The Hanging Weights Puzzle is a classic constraint programming problem where a system of bars and weights must be balanced according to specific rules. The goal is to find the integer values of the weights, ranging from 1 to 13, that satisfy the balance conditions.

The main decision variables in this problem are the weights represented by the variables a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h, i, j, k, l, and m. Each variable corresponds to a specific weight in the system and takes on an integer value between 1 and 13.

The key constraints in the Hanging Weights Puzzle can be described in plain language as follows: 
- The weights on either side of a pivot point must be equal when weighted by their distance from the pivot.
- A bar hanging beneath another contributes its total weight as if it were a single weight.
- The total weight of the system is the sum of all individual weights.
- Each weight must have a unique value.

The objective of the Hanging Weights Puzzle is not to minimize or maximize a specific value, but rather to find a feasible solution that satisfies all the constraints. In other words, the goal is to determine the values of the weights that balance the system according to the given rules. The problem is solved when all constraints are satisfied, and a valid assignment of weights is found.
