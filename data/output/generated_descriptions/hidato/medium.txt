Hidato Puzzle
The Hidato puzzle is a logic-based puzzle where the goal is to fill in a grid with consecutive numbers, starting from 1 and ending at a predetermined number, such that each number is connected to the next one either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

The main decision variables in this problem are the values of the grid cells, represented by the `x` array, where `x[i, j]` denotes the value of the cell at row `i` and column `j`. These variables represent the numbers that need to be filled in the grid.

The key constraints in this problem are:
- Each cell in the grid must contain a unique number between 1 and the total number of cells.
- The numbers in the grid must be consecutive, meaning that each number is connected to the next one either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
- The fixed numbers in the grid, represented by the `puzzle` array, must remain in their original positions.

The objective of the Hidato puzzle is to find a valid solution that satisfies all the constraints, meaning that the numbers in the grid are consecutive and connected as required. The problem does not have a specific objective function to minimize or maximize, but rather aims to find a feasible solution that meets all the constraints.
