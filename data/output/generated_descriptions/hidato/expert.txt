name: Hidato Puzzle
description: The Hidato puzzle is a logic puzzle where a grid of numbers is given, with some numbers already filled in and the goal is to fill in the remaining numbers such that consecutive numbers are adjacent to each other, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
variables: $x_{i,j}$, where $i$ and $j$ are the row and column indices of the grid, respectively. These variables represent the numbers to be filled in at each position in the grid.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..r, \forall j \in 1..c, x_{i,j} \in \{1, 2, ..., r \cdot c\}$, $\forall i \in 1..r, \forall j \in 1..c, puzzle_{i,j} > 0 \rightarrow x_{i,j} = puzzle_{i,j}$, $\forall k \in 1..r \cdot c - 1, \exists i \in 1..r, \exists j \in 1..c, \exists a \in \{-1, 0, 1\}, \exists b \in \{-1, 0, 1\}, k = x_{i,j} \wedge i + a \geq 1 \wedge j + b \geq 1 \wedge i + a \leq r \wedge j + b \leq c \wedge (a, b) \neq (0, 0) \wedge k + 1 = x_{i+a, j+b}$, and $\forall i \in 1..r, \forall j \in 1..c, \forall k \in 1..r \cdot c, x_{i,j} \neq x_{k,l} \forall (k, l) \neq (i, j)$. These constraints ensure that each position in the grid is filled with a unique number, the given numbers are respected, consecutive numbers are adjacent, and all numbers from 1 to $r \cdot c$ are used exactly once.
