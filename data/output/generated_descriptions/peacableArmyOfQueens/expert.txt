name: Peacable Army of Queens
description: The problem involves placing two equally-sized armies of queens (white and black) on a chessboard without attacking each other, with the goal of maximizing the size of the armies.
variables: $n, N, board_{i,j}, amountOfQueens$ - These variables represent the size of the chessboard ($n$), the set of rows and columns on the board ($N$), the state of each square on the board ($board_{i,j}$, which can be empty (0), occupied by a white queen (1), or occupied by a black queen (2)), and the total number of queens of each color ($amountOfQueens$).
constraints: $board_{1,1} = 1, \sum_{i,j \in N} (board_{i,j} = 1) = amountOfQueens, \sum_{i,j \in N} (board_{i,j} = 2) = amountOfQueens, (board_{i,j} = 1) \rightarrow \forall k \in N ((k \neq i) \rightarrow board_{k,j} < 2) \wedge ((k \neq j) \rightarrow board_{i,k} < 2) \wedge ... , (board_{i,j} = 2) \rightarrow \forall k \in N ((k \neq i) \rightarrow (board_{k,j} < 1 \vee board_{k,j} > 1)) \wedge ((k \neq j) \rightarrow (board_{i,k} < 1 \vee board_{i,k} > 1)) \wedge ...$ - These constraints ensure that a white queen is placed in the top-left corner, that the number of white and black queens is equal to $amountOfQueens$, and that no queen attacks another queen of the opposite color.
objective: Maximize $amountOfQueens$
