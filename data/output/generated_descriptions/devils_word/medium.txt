The Devil's Word problem. 
It is about finding a way to add or subtract a set of given numbers to reach a specific total, in this case, 666. 
The main decision variables are `plus` and `minus`, which are arrays of binary variables representing whether each number in the array `arr` should be added or subtracted to reach the total. 
The key constraints are that each number can only be either added or subtracted, and the sum of the numbers with their respective signs must equal the total. 
The problem's objective is to find a solution that satisfies these constraints, with the option to minimize the number of subtractions.
