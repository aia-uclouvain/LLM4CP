name: Appointment Scheduling Problem
description: The problem involves scheduling interviews between an employer and N people, where each person has a free-busy schedule for N interview slots. The goal is to assign each person to a slot such that the schedule is feasible, i.e., each person is assigned to a slot where they are available.
variables: $x = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_n]$, where $x_i$ is a decision variable representing the assignment of person $i$ to a slot (appointment number $1..n$). These variables determine the schedule of interviews between the employer and the N people.
constraints: $\forall i \in 1..n, x_i \in s_i \wedge \text{alldifferent}(x)$, where $s_i$ is the set of available slots for person $i$. The first constraint ensures that each person is assigned to a slot where they are available, and the second constraint ensures that each slot is assigned to at most one person.
objective: The objective of the problem is to find a feasible schedule, i.e., an assignment of people to slots that satisfies the availability constraints. The problem does not involve minimizing or maximizing a specific value, but rather finding a solution that satisfies the given constraints.
