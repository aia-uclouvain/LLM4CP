Employee Classification Problem
The Employee Classification Problem is about analyzing employee data to calculate various statistics such as minimum, maximum, and average salaries, as well as identifying high-paid employees based on a given salary threshold.
The main decision variables in this problem are:
- `max_salaries`, `min_salaries`, and `avg_salaries`, which represent the maximum, minimum, and average salaries of all employees, respectively.
- `high_paid`, a set of employee names who have a salary above the threshold of 85000.
- `num_high_paid`, the number of high-paid employees.
The key constraints in this problem are:
- The calculation of `max_salaries`, `min_salaries`, and `avg_salaries` is based on the salaries of all employees.
- An employee is considered high-paid if their salary is greater than 85000.
The objective of this problem is to calculate and display various statistics about employee salaries, including the number of high-paid employees, without any specific minimization or maximization goal, as the `solve satisfy` statement is used to find a solution that satisfies all constraints.
