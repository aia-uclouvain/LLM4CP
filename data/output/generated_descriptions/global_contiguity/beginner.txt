The problem is called Global Contiguity. 
It's about arranging a series of 0s and 1s in a line so that all the 1s are together. 
The main variables are x, which is a list of 0s and 1s, and start and end, which mark the beginning and end of the group of 1s. 
The basic rules are that each position in the list can be either 0 or 1, and all the 1s must be next to each other. 
The goal is to find a way to arrange the 0s and 1s that satisfies these rules, without trying to minimize or maximize anything in particular, just to find a valid arrangement.
